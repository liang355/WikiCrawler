<p><b>Remscheid</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈʁɛmʃaɪt]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Remscheid.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/88/De-Remscheid.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Remscheid.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, Germany. It is, after <a href="/wiki/Wuppertal" title="Wuppertal">Wuppertal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Solingen" title="Solingen">Solingen</a>, the third largest municipality in <a href="/wiki/Bergisches_Land" title="Bergisches Land">Bergisches Land</a>, being located on the northern edge of the region, on south side of the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr area">Ruhr area</a>.</p>
<p>Remscheid had around 109,000 inhabitants in 2015.</p>
<p>Its highest point is the <a href="/wiki/Brodtberg" title="Brodtberg">Brodtberg</a> (378 m).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remscheid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Remscheid was founded in the 12th century, but remained a small village until the 19th century. Early spellings for the city included <i>Remissgeid</i> (1217), <i>Rymscheyd</i> (1351), <i>Reymscheyd</i> (1487) and <i>Rembscheid</i> (1639). The economic growth of the entire Rhine-Ruhr region led to an increase of the population of Remscheid. Mechanical engineering and toolmaking were the main industries practised within the town. This is carried on today with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hazet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hazet (page does not exist)">Hazet</a> tool company which has two factories in Remscheid. Remscheid was part of the <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussian</a> <a href="/wiki/Rhine_Province" title="Rhine Province">Rhine Province</a> from 1822–1945.</p>
<p>On 31 July 1943, during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">second World War</a>, Remscheid was almost completely destroyed during a British <a href="/wiki/Area_bombing" class="mw-redirect" title="Area bombing">bombing raid</a> which caused a <a href="/wiki/Firestorm" title="Firestorm">firestorm</a>. During 14 and 15 April 1945, Remscheid was captured by the <a href="/wiki/78th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="78th Infantry Division (United States)">78th Infantry Division (United States)</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/A-10_Thunderbolt_II" class="mw-redirect" title="A-10 Thunderbolt II">A-10 Thunderbolt II</a> <a href="/wiki/1988_Remscheid_A-10_crash" title="1988 Remscheid A-10 crash">crashed in the city on 8 December 1988</a>. Six people died.</p>
<p>Today, Remscheid comprises four boroughs, <i>Alt-Remscheid</i>, <i>Remscheid-Süd</i>, <i>Lennep</i>, and <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCttringhausen" title="Lüttringhausen">Lüttringhausen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remscheid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCngsten_Bridge" title="Müngsten Bridge">Müngstener Brücke</a></i> is a railway bridge crossing a valley and connecting Remscheid with the neighbouring town of <a href="/wiki/Solingen" title="Solingen">Solingen</a>. It is 107 m above the ground, making it the highest railway bridge in Germany. It was constructed in 1897 and originally named the <i>Kaiser-Wilhelm-Brücke</i> after <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_I_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilhelm I of Germany">Wilhelm I</a> (whose 100th birthday would have been in 1897).</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Eschbachtalsperre" class="mw-redirect" title="Eschbachtalsperre">Eschbachtalsperre</a></i>, the first dam built in Germany for the supply of drinking water is located here. It was built in 1891.</li>
<li>The old city of the borough Lennep consists of 116 houses from 1756.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Famous_people">Famous people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remscheid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Famous people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><a href="/wiki/Hans_Stammreich" title="Hans Stammreich">Hans Stammreich</a>, (1902-1969), Raman spectroscopist later living in Brazil, was born in Remscheid.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Teo_Otto" title="Teo Otto">Teo Otto</a>, (1904-1968), <a href="/wiki/Scenic_design" title="Scenic design">stage designer</a>, was born in Remscheid</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Br%C3%B6tzmann" title="Peter Brötzmann">Peter Brötzmann</a>, (born 1941), <a href="/wiki/Free_jazz" title="Free jazz">free jazz</a> saxophonist, born in Remscheid</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Michael_Bacht" title="Michael Bacht">Michael Bacht</a> (born 1947), German artist</li>
