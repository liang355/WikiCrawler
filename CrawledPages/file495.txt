<p><b>Kulmbach</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is bounded by (from the north and clockwise) the districts of <a href="/wiki/Kronach_(district)" title="Kronach (district)">Kronach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hof_(district)" title="Hof (district)">Hof</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bayreuth_(district)" title="Bayreuth (district)">Bayreuth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lichtenfels_(district)" title="Lichtenfels (district)">Lichtenfels</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kulmbach_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The historical centre of the region was the castle of <a href="/wiki/Plassenburg" title="Plassenburg">Plassenburg</a> in the city of Kulmbach. In 1135 this castle was first mentioned. The rulers of the castle and the surrounding lands were the counts of Andechs (1135–1248, from 1135 to 1180 Dießen-Andechs, from 1180 to 1248 Andechs-Meranien) and the Thuringian counts of Orlamünde (1260–1340). After the death of the last count of Orlamünde the region fell to the <a href="/wiki/Hohenzollern" class="mw-redirect" title="Hohenzollern">Hohenzollern</a> state.</p>
<p>In 1398 the Hohenzollern state was divided, and in the Franconian area the two states of <a href="/wiki/Ansbach" title="Ansbach">Ansbach</a> and Kulmbach were founded. When the Hohenzollern family was awarded the margravate of <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, these states were called Brandenburg-Ansbach and Brandenburg-Kulmbach, but not united with Brandenburg proper. In 1603 the castle of Plassenburg was abandoned, and the capital was moved to the city of <a href="/wiki/Bayreuth" title="Bayreuth">Bayreuth</a>. The importance of the city of Kulmbach declined hereafter, although the margraves (and sometimes the population of Kulmbach) often used to take shelter on the castle during the years of the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>.</p>
<p>The last margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth (as Brandenburg-Kulmbach was called now) abdicated in 1792 in order to cede his property to his cousin, the king of <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a>. However, this was a short episode. In 1806 Kulmbach surrendered to <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon</a>, who ordered the razing of Plassenburg castle. In 1810 the region fell to Bavaria and remained so to date.</p>
<p>The district of Kulmbach was founded soon after. The city of Kulmbach did not belong to the district until 1972, when city and district were merged.</p>
<p>According to a tour guide in Kulmbach, the castle Plassenburg was used by Adolf Hitler as the headquarters for his Highway Department. The highway "A9" was built under Nazi rule, connecting Berlin and Munich, and running past Kulmbach.</p>
<p>After the Second World War, the castle was used as a camp for German prisoners of war under the US military control. One of those stayed employed later as a keeper of natural and biological resources of the castle.</p>
<p>The city is also known for a brewery, producing the strongest beer (11.8% alcohol) available in Germany<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kulmbach_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district, surrounding the ancient city of Kulmbach, is located on the southern edge of <a href="/wiki/Franconian_Forest" title="Franconian Forest">Franconian Forest</a> (<i>Frankenwald</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Fichtelgebirge" class="mw-redirect" title="Fichtelgebirge">Fichtelgebirge</a>. The two headstreams of the <a href="/wiki/Main_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Main River">Main River</a>, White Main and Red Main, meet in the centre of the district.</p>
