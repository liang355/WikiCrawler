<p><b>Bamberg</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It surrounds but does not include the town of <a href="/wiki/Bamberg" title="Bamberg">Bamberg</a>. The district is bounded by the districts of (from the north and clockwise) <a href="/wiki/Lichtenfels_(district)" title="Lichtenfels (district)">Lichtenfels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bayreuth_(district)" title="Bayreuth (district)">Bayreuth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forchheim_(district)" title="Forchheim (district)">Forchheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erlangen-H%C3%B6chstadt" title="Erlangen-Höchstadt">Erlangen-Höchstadt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neustadt_(Aisch)-Bad_Windsheim" title="Neustadt (Aisch)-Bad Windsheim">Neustadt (Aisch)-Bad Windsheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kitzingen_(district)" title="Kitzingen (district)">Kitzingen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schweinfurt_(district)" title="Schweinfurt (district)">Schweinfurt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ha%C3%9Fberge_(district)" title="Haßberge (district)">Haßberge</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bamberg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The history of the district is linked with the history of Bamberg.</p>
<p>In 1862 the districts of Bamberg-West and Bamberg-East were established. They were merged in 1929. The present borders were established in 1972, when portions of the adjoining district of <a href="/wiki/Erlangen-H%C3%B6chstadt" title="Erlangen-Höchstadt">Erlangen-Höchstadt</a> were annexed.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bamberg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district surrounds the town of Bamberg. The western half of the district is occupied by the <b>Steigerwald</b>, a hilly forest region. In the east there is the hill chain of the <a href="/wiki/Frankish_Alb" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankish Alb">Frankish Alb</a>. Between these regions the <a href="/wiki/Main_(river)" title="Main (river)">Main</a> River enters the district from the north, turns around just before the town of Bamberg and leaves to the northwest. The smaller <a href="/wiki/Regnitz" title="Regnitz">Regnitz</a> River comes from the south and meets the Main behind Bamberg.</p>
