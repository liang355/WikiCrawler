			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Henry Ware (Unitarian)</h1>

<p><b>Henry Ware</b> (April 1, 1764 – July 12, 1845) was a <a href="/wiki/Preacher" title="Preacher">preacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Theologian" class="mw-redirect" title="Theologian">theologian</a> influential in the formation of <a href="/wiki/Unitarianism" title="Unitarianism">Unitarianism</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Unitarian_Association" title="American Unitarian Association">American Unitarian Association</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</p>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Sherborn,_Massachusetts" title="Sherborn, Massachusetts">Sherborn, Massachusetts</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Sewall-Ware_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Sewall-Ware House">a house</a> that survived into the 20th century), Ware was educated at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>, earning his A.B. in 1785. He was from 1787 to 1805 the minister of the First Parish in <a href="/wiki/Hingham,_Massachusetts" title="Hingham, Massachusetts">Hingham, Massachusetts</a>. He was elected a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="American Academy of Arts and Sciences">American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a> in 1804.<sup id="cite_ref-AAAS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AAAS-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1805 he was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Hollis_Chair_of_Divinity" title="Hollis Chair of Divinity">Hollis Chair</a> at Harvard, precipitating a controversy between Unitarians and more conservative <a href="/wiki/Calvinist" class="mw-redirect" title="Calvinist">Calvinists</a>. He took part in the formation of the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Divinity_School" title="Harvard Divinity School">Harvard Divinity School</a> and the establishment of Unitarianism there in the following decades, publishing his debates with eminent Calvinists in the 1820s. His son, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ware,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Henry Ware, Jr.">Henry Ware, Jr.</a>, followed his father as a Harvard Divinity professor and Unitarian theologian. He is also the grandfather of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Lee_Ware" title="Mary Lee Ware">Mary Lee Ware</a> through his other son, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Eliot_Ware" title="Charles Eliot Ware">Charles Eliot Ware</a> - Mary and her mother (his daughter-in-law) being the patron sponsors of Harvard's famed <a href="/wiki/Glass_Flowers" title="Glass Flowers">Glass Flowers</a> exhibit.</p>
