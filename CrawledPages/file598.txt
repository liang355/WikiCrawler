<p><b>John Leverett</b> (August 25, 1662 – May 3, 1724) was an early American <a href="/wiki/Lawyer" title="Lawyer">lawyer</a>, politician, educator, and President of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Leverett_the_Younger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>John Leverett was the son of Hudson Leverett, an attorney, and Sarah (Payton) Leverett, (and grandson of <a href="/wiki/John_Leverett" title="John Leverett">John Leverett</a> the Governor of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Colony" title="Massachusetts Bay Colony">Massachusetts Bay Colony</a>). He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Boston_Latin_School" title="Boston Latin School">Boston Latin School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">A.B.</a>, 1680; <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">A.M.</a>, 1683).<sup id="cite_ref-RS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Leverett_the_Younger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For twelve years Leverett was a resident fellow at Harvard. He was appointed in 1685 at the same time as William Brattle. Leverett and Brattle managed <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> while Harvard's President <a href="/wiki/Increase_Mather" title="Increase Mather">Increase Mather</a> was in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> for four years (1688–1692)</p>
<p>Leverett married on November 25, 1697 the daughter of former Harvard College president <a href="/wiki/John_Rogers_(Harvard)" title="John Rogers (Harvard)">John Rogers</a>, Margaret Rogers Berry. They had nine children, six died in infancy. Margaret died on June 7, 1720. Leverett married secondly Sarah Crisp Harris. Sarah died on April 4, 1744.</p>
<p>John served in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Massachusetts_Bay" title="Province of Massachusetts Bay">Province of Massachusetts Bay</a> as a justice of the peace (1699), a judge in the Court of Admiralty (1705), a justice of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Superior_Court_of_Judicature" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts Superior Court of Judicature">Superior Court</a> (1702–1708), judge of Probate Court for <a href="/wiki/Middlesex_County,_Massachusetts" title="Middlesex County, Massachusetts">Middlesex County</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge</a> (1702–1708), legislator(1696–1702) and Speaker of the <a href="/wiki/Colonial_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonial Massachusetts">Colonial Massachusetts</a> House of Representatives (1700–1702), and provincial councillor for eastern <a href="/wiki/District_of_Maine" title="District of Maine">Maine</a> (1706–1708).</p>
<p>Leverett acted as an Indian commissioner from Massachusetts during <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne%27s_War" title="Queen Anne's War">Queen Anne's War</a> (1701–1713). He was unable to persuade the Iroquois to enter the war on the side of the British at a conference in 1704. Leverett raised and commanded a company of volunteers (as a lieutenant in the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_and_Honorable_Artillery_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient and Honorable Artillery Company">Military Company of Massachusetts</a>, which he joined in 1704) in the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Port_Royal_(1707)" title="Siege of Port Royal (1707)">failed assaults</a> on French <a href="/wiki/Habitation_at_Port-Royal" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitation at Port-Royal">Port Royal, Acadia</a> in 1707.</p>
<p>John served for sixteen years as <a href="/wiki/President_of_Harvard" class="mw-redirect" title="President of Harvard">President of Harvard</a> from January 14, 1708 till his death in 1724. In 1709, Leverett served as an emissary from Massachusetts Governor <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Dudley" title="Joseph Dudley">Joseph Dudley</a> to New York's Governor <a href="/wiki/John_Lovelace,_4th_Baron_Lovelace" title="John Lovelace, 4th Baron Lovelace">John Lovelace</a> in negotiations for the establishment of military cooperation between Massachusetts and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_New_York" title="Province of New York">New York</a> on the frontier and for an aborted invasion of <a href="/wiki/Canada_(New_France)" title="Canada (New France)">Canada</a>.</p>
<p>In 1719 Leverett helped to form the Lincolnshire Company which attempted to develop land in the <a href="/wiki/Muscongus_Patent" class="mw-redirect" title="Muscongus Patent">Muscongus Patent</a> in <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, then part of Massachusetts. Leverett had inherited a share of this patent from his grandfather <a href="/wiki/John_Leverett" title="John Leverett">John Leverett</a>. Nothing was accomplished and the grant was later taken over by <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Waldo" title="Samuel Waldo">Samuel Waldo</a>, a Boston merchant.</p>
<p>He was elected a <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">Fellow of the Royal Society</a> in 1714.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On his death he was buried in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Burying_Ground_(Cambridge,_Massachusetts)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Old Burying Ground (Cambridge, Massachusetts) (page does not exist)">Old Burying Ground</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>.</p>
