<p>A <b>chancellor</b> is a leader of a college or university, usually either the executive or ceremonial head of the university or of a university campus.</p>
<p>In most <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> and former Commonwealth nations, the chancellor is usually a ceremonial non-resident head of the university. In such institutions, the chief executive of a university is the <b>vice-chancellor</b>, who may carry an additional title, such as "president &amp; vice-chancellor". The chancellor may serve as chairman of the governing body; if not, this duty is often held by a chairman who may be known as a pro-chancellor.</p>
<p>In many countries, the administrative and educational head of the university is known as the president, <a href="/wiki/Principal_(academia)" title="Principal (academia)">principal</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a>. In the United States, the head of a university is most commonly a university president. In U.S. university systems that have more than one affiliated university or campus, the executive head of a specific campus may have the title of chancellor and report to the overall system's president, or vice versa.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chancellor">Chancellor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chancellor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia_and_New_Zealand">Australia and New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Australia and New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In both Australia and New Zealand, a chancellor is the chairman of a university's governing body; thus, as well as having ceremonial duties, the chancellor participates in the governance of the university (but not its active management). The chancellor is assisted by a deputy chancellor (known as the pro-chancellor in some universities). The chancellor and deputy chancellor are frequently drawn from the senior ranks of business or the judiciary (it is one of the few jobs considered compatible with judicial service). <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">Some universities have a <a href="/wiki/Visitor" title="Visitor">visitor</a> who is senior to the chancellor.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> University disputes can be appealed from the governing board to the visitor (as is still the case in the UK), but nowadays, such appeals are generally prohibited by legislation, and the position has only ceremonial functions (unlike the chancellor and deputy chancellor, who frequently preside at functions such as graduations, the visitor rarely attends university functions). The vice-chancellor usually serves as the chief executive of the university.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Macquarie_University" title="Macquarie University">Macquarie University</a> in Sydney is a noteworthy anomaly as it once had the unique position of <i>Emeritus Deputy Chancellor</i>, a post created for <a href="/wiki/John_Lincoln" title="John Lincoln">John Lincoln</a> upon his retirement from his long-held post of deputy chancellor in 2000. The position was not merely an honorary title, as it also retained for Lincoln a place in the University Council until his death in 2011.</p>

<p>Canadian universities and British universities in Scotland have a titular chancellor similar to those in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a>, with day-to-day operations typically handled by a <i>principal</i>. In Scotland, for example, the chancellor of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a> is <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">Anne, Princess Royal</a>, whilst the current chancellor of the University of Aberdeen is <a href="/wiki/Camilla,_Duchess_of_Cornwall" title="Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall">Camilla, Duchess of Rothesay</a>.</p>
<p>In Canada, the vice-chancellor usually carries the joint title of "<a href="/wiki/University_president" class="mw-redirect" title="University president">president</a> and vice-chancellor" or "<a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a> and vice-chancellor." Scottish principals generally carry the title of "<a href="/wiki/University_principal" class="mw-redirect" title="University principal">principal</a> and vice-chancellor."</p>
<p>In Scotland, the title and post of <a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a> is reserved to the third ranked official of university governance. The position exists in common throughout the five ancient universities of Scotland with rectorships in existence at the universities of <a href="/wiki/University_of_St_Andrews" title="University of St Andrews">St Andrews</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Aberdeen" title="University of Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Dundee" title="University of Dundee">Dundee</a>, which is also considered to have ancient status as a result of its early connections to the University of St Andrews. The position of Lord Rector was given legal standing by virtue of the Universities (Scotland) Act 1889.</p>
<p>Rectors appoint a rector's assessor, effectively a deputy or stand-in, who may carry out their functions when they are absent from the university. The Rector chairs meetings of the university court, the governing body of the university, and is elected by the matriculated student body at regular intervals (usually every three years to enable every undergraduate who obtains a degree to vote at least once). An exception exists at Edinburgh, where the (Lord) Rector is elected by both students and staff.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finland">Finland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Finland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Finland, if the university has a chancellor (Finnish: <i>Kansleri</i>, Swedish: <i>Kansler</i>), he is the leading official in the university. The duties of the chancellor are mainly to promote sciences and to look after the best interests of the university. As the <a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a> of the university (Finnish: <i>rehtori</i>, Swedish: <i>rektor</i>) remains the de facto administrative leader and chief executive official, the role of the chancellor is more of a social, political and even historical nature. However some administrative duties still belong to the chancellor's jurisdiction despite their often arguably ceremonial nature. Examples of these include the appointment of new <a href="/wiki/Professor#Finland" title="Professor">professors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Docent" title="Docent">docents</a>.</p>
<p>The chancellor of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Helsinki" title="University of Helsinki">University of Helsinki</a> (the oldest, largest and most prestigious in Finland) has also the notable right to be present and to speak in the plenary meetings of the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Finland" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet of Finland">Council of State</a> when matters regarding the university are discussed. Despite his role as the chancellor of only one university, he is often regarded as the political representative of Finland's entire university institution when he exercises his rights in the Council of State.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Finland" title="History of Finland">history of Finland</a> the office of the chancellor dates all the way back to the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Empire" title="Swedish Empire">Swedish Empire</a>, and later the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>. Historically the chancellor's duty was to function as the official representative of the monarch in the autonomous university.</p>
<p>The number of chancellors in Finnish universities has declined over the years, and in vast majority of Finnish universities the highest official is the rector. The remaining universities with chancellors are University of Helsinki, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Turku" title="University of Turku">University of Turku</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tampere" title="University of Tampere">University of Tampere</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%85bo_Akademi_University" title="Åbo Akademi University">Åbo Akademi University</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In France, chancellor (<i>chancelier</i>) is one of the titles of the <a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a> (<i>recteur</i>), a senior civil servant of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Education_(France)" title="Ministry of National Education (France)">Ministry of Education</a> serving as manager of a regional educational district (<i>académie</i>). In his capacity as chancellor, the rector awards <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">academic degrees</a> to the university's graduates, oversees the <a href="/wiki/Legality" title="Legality">legality</a> of the universities executive acts and channels funding from the ministry. The rector has no executive function in any university, but remains a member <i>ex officio</i> of the board of every public university in his district.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany_and_Poland">Germany and Poland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Germany and Poland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Germany (der Kanzler) and Poland (kanclerz), the chancellor is the head of many universities' administration and the leader of the non-academic staff while the rector is the academic head. In Poland, the main academic bodies of the university consists of: rektor (the head of the university), prorektor (deputy rektor), dziekan (the head of the faculty), prodziekan (deputy dziekan), senat (the main council of the university). In universities with presidential constitution, the university's president holds both the functions of chancellor and rector.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong">Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Hong Kong, the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive_of_Hong_Kong" title="Chief Executive of Hong Kong">Chief Executive of Hong Kong</a> (and before 1997, <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Hong_Kong" title="Governor of Hong Kong">Governor of Hong Kong</a>) acts as the chancellor of all chartered universities, which includes all <a href="/wiki/Higher_education_in_Hong_Kong#UGC_funded_universities" title="Higher education in Hong Kong">eight public universities</a> and <a href="/wiki/Open_University_of_Hong_Kong" title="Open University of Hong Kong">Open University of Hong Kong</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Day-to-day operation is in the hands of either a vice-chancellor (older and established institutions) or a president (in newer institutions), depending on the institution.</p>
<h3><span id="Turkey,_Russia,_and_Ukraine"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Turkey.2C_Russia.2C_and_Ukraine">Turkey, Russia, and Ukraine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Turkey, Russia, and Ukraine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Turkey, Russia, and Ukraine, the chancellor is the head of the university and is called "rector." Some universities in Russia and Ukraine have figurehead "presidents."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In India, almost all universities have a chancellor as their titular head whose function is largely ceremonial. The governor of the state, appointed as the union's representative of state by the president, acts as the chancellor of the university. The de facto head of the university is the vice-chancellor. In private non-profit universities, normally the head of the foundation who has established the university is the chancellor of the university and is the head of the university. His equivalent for engineering institutes is the director, even for those engineering institutes that are university equivalents like the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Institutes_of_Technology" title="Indian Institutes of Technology">Indian Institutes of Technology</a>.</p>
<p>For private university unlike the chancellor who heads the conventional Indian 'state university', the private university is headed by a president or chairman of private organization and have other posts like vice-chancellors, deans of faculties, registrar and controller of examinations.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Ireland, the four universities all have a chancellor as their figurehead leader. However, day-to-day operations of the universities are under the directorship of a president (a <a href="/wiki/Provost_(education)" title="Provost (education)">provost</a> in the case of <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>). The <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Ireland" title="National University of Ireland">National University of Ireland</a>'s constituent universities do not have a chancellor each; rather, the president of each constituent university has the title of <a href="/wiki/Pro-Vice-Chancellor" class="mw-redirect" title="Pro-Vice-Chancellor">Pro-Vice-Chancellor</a> of the NUI. In <a href="/wiki/Dublin_City_University" title="Dublin City University">Dublin City University</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Limerick" title="University of Limerick">University of Limerick</a>, the chancellor is also the chairman of the university's governing authority.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Malaysia">Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Malaysia, the chancellor position is given to dignitaries such as royalty or prominent politicians by universities to represent the universities in the political arena. For example, the chancellor of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Malaya" title="University of Malaya">University of Malaya</a>, the oldest university in Malaysia is <a href="/wiki/Nazrin_Shah_of_Perak" title="Nazrin Shah of Perak">Sultan Nazrin Shah</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sultan_of_Perak" class="mw-redirect" title="Sultan of Perak">Sultan of Perak</a>. His father, <a href="/wiki/Azlan_Shah_of_Perak" title="Azlan Shah of Perak">Sultan Azlan Shah</a> also served as chancellor at the same university until his death in 2014.</p>
<p>The chancellor of <a href="/wiki/Universiti_Putra_Malaysia" title="Universiti Putra Malaysia">Universiti Putra Malaysia</a> is the current <a href="/wiki/Sultan_of_Selangor" class="mw-redirect" title="Sultan of Selangor">Sultan of Selangor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Sharafuddin_Idris_Shah" class="mw-redirect" title="Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah">Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah</a>, while the current <a href="/wiki/Yang_di-Pertuan_Besar" title="Yang di-Pertuan Besar">Yang di-Pertuan Besar</a> of <a href="/wiki/Negeri_Sembilan" title="Negeri Sembilan">Negeri Sembilan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tunku_Muhriz" class="mw-redirect" title="Tunku Muhriz">Tunku Muhriz</a> is the chancellor of <a href="/wiki/Universiti_Kebangsaan_Malaysia" class="mw-redirect" title="Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia">Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia</a>.</p>
<p>The chancellor of <a href="/wiki/Universiti_Tun_Hussein_Onn_Malaysia" class="mw-redirect" title="Universiti Tun Hussein Onn Malaysia">Universiti Tun Hussein Onn Malaysia</a>, a university situated in the state of Johor, is the current <a href="/wiki/Sultan_of_Johor" title="Sultan of Johor">Sultan of Johor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Ibrahim_of_Johor" class="mw-redirect" title="Sultan Ibrahim of Johor">Sultan Ibrahim</a>.</p>
<p>Datin Seri <a href="/wiki/Jeanne_Abdullah" title="Jeanne Abdullah">Jeanne Abdullah</a>, the wife of the former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia" title="Prime Minister of Malaysia">Prime Minister of Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Datuk_Seri_Abdullah_Ahmad_Badawi" class="mw-redirect" title="Datuk Seri Abdullah Ahmad Badawi">Datuk Seri Abdullah Ahmad Badawi</a> was appointed as the new chancellor of <a href="/wiki/Open_University_Malaysia" title="Open University Malaysia">Open University Malaysia</a> to take over the role from the first chancellor, the late YBhg Datin Paduka Seri <a href="/wiki/Endon_Mahmood" title="Endon Mahmood">Endon Mahmood</a> (Abdullah Badawi's first wife).</p>
<p>Recently, <a href="/wiki/UCSI" class="mw-redirect" title="UCSI">UCSI</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a> has given recognition to Tan Sri Datuk Seri Panglima Dr. Abdul Rahman Arshad as its first chancellor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Philippines, the <a href="/wiki/De_La_Salle_University" title="De La Salle University">De La Salle University</a> designates the head of its university as the chancellor. For the University of the Philippines, the entire system is headed by a president, while the eight constituent universities under the system is each headed by a chancellor. The chancellor designates the different vice-chancellors for different areas of concern of the university: academic affairs, finance, and community affairs, among others. Some more universities like <a href="/wiki/University_of_Santo_Tomas" title="University of Santo Tomas">University of Santo Tomas</a> and other colleges, institutions have chancellors. Its Chancellor is the incumbent Master of the Order of Preachers (Dominicans) meanwhile, the Vice Chancellor is the Prior Provincial of the Dominican Province of the Philippines. Their roles are largely ceremonial. The University of Santo Tomas is governed mainly by its <a href="/wiki/Rector_Magnificus_of_the_University_of_Santo_Tomas" title="Rector Magnificus of the University of Santo Tomas">Rector Magnificus</a> in overseeing its academic, financial and other affairs.</p>
<p>Other universities in the Philippines (such as state universities like Mindanao State University where each constituent campus is headed by chancellor) are mostly headed by their respective university presidents. Meanwhile, private (esp. Catholic) institutions are headed by a Rector.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistan">Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Pakistan, chancellor is normally the figurehead of the university, who is normally the provincial governor where that university exists. Day-to-day business of the university is run by the vice chancellor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chancellor is a titular position in Bangladesh always held by the incumbent <a href="/wiki/President_of_Bangladesh" title="President of Bangladesh">President of Bangladesh</a> under the Private Universities Act 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-act_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-act-3">[3]</a></sup> The position in public universities is not fixed for the president under any acts or laws (since the erection of a state university in Bangladesh requires an act to be passed in itself),<sup id="cite_ref-moe_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-moe-4">[4]</a></sup> but it has been the custom so far to name the incumbent president of the country as chancellor of all state universities thus established. The day-to-day business of the university is run by the vice chancellor. He has a deputy called the pro-vice-chancellor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nepal">Nepal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Nepal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Nepal, universities have a chancellor as ceremonial head. The de facto head of the university is the vice-chancellor. The chancellor is primarily responsible for attending the convocation programmes and accepting the resignation and appointment letter of a new vice-chancellor. Generally, the prime minister is considered the chancellor, and in his absence, the minister of education acts as the chancellor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_longest_serving_higher_education_presidents" class="mw-redirect" title="List of longest serving higher education presidents">List of longest serving higher education presidents</a></div>
<p>In the United States, heads of colleges and universities are typically called "<a href="/wiki/University_president" class="mw-redirect" title="University president">president</a>." A multi-campus <a href="/wiki/University_system" title="University system">university system</a> may be headed by a chancellor who serves as system-wide chief, with presidents governing individual institutions: for example, the <a href="/wiki/City_University_of_New_York" title="City University of New York">City University of New York</a>. There are also some university systems, such as the <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina" title="University of North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois_system" title="University of Illinois system">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Missouri_System" title="University of Missouri System">Missouri</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts" title="University of Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_California" title="University of California">California</a> university systems, in which those two titles are reversed. At <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_University" title="Rutgers University">Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey</a>, which is a single unified university with three campuses, the chief officers of the two smaller campuses at <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_Camden" class="mw-redirect" title="Rutgers Camden">Camden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rutgers%E2%80%93Newark" class="mw-redirect" title="Rutgers–Newark">Newark</a> are called chancellors, a renaming from "<a href="/wiki/Provost_(education)" title="Provost (education)">provost</a>." Rutgers University itself has a president as the chief officer.</p>
<p>Presidents are the functional chief executive officers of most standalone U.S. universities; however, a few universities, such as <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pittsburgh" title="University of Pittsburgh">University of Pittsburgh</a>, have a chancellor as the chief executive officer. There are occasional other uses of the title chancellor. <a href="/wiki/The_College_of_William_%26_Mary" class="mw-redirect" title="The College of William &amp; Mary">The College of William &amp; Mary</a> uses chancellor in the British sense, as a figurehead leader, but the actual executive of the school is the "president," not a "vice-chancellor." Some schools, such as <a href="/wiki/Lubbock_Christian_University" title="Lubbock Christian University">Lubbock Christian University</a>, give the ceremonial title of "chancellor" to a retiring university president. <a href="/wiki/The_Catholic_University_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="The Catholic University of America">The Catholic University of America</a> is headed by a <a href="/wiki/The_Catholic_University_of_America#University_rectors_and_presidents" class="mw-redirect" title="The Catholic University of America">president</a> (formerly "rector"), with the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Washington" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Washington">Archbishop of Washington</a> serving as chancellor, a ceremonial position but one which does require the archbishop to represent the university before the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>. This scenario, while not always exactly duplicated, is typical in other Catholic universities due to the Catholic hierarchy. In some schools run by Catholic religious orders, the rector of the community supersedes the president when the individual is a member of that religious order.</p>
<p>The title "chancellor" is sometimes used in <a href="/wiki/K%E2%80%9312_(education)" class="mw-redirect" title="K–12 (education)">K-12</a> education in a sense similar to <a href="/wiki/Superintendent_(education)" title="Superintendent (education)">superintendent of schools</a>, particularly in urban school districts. The <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Schools_Chancellor" title="New York City Schools Chancellor">New York City Schools Chancellor</a> is the chief executive officer of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Department_of_Education" title="New York City Department of Education">New York City Department of Education</a>, which manages the city's public school system (the largest in the United States). The leader of the <a href="/wiki/District_of_Columbia_Public_Schools" title="District of Columbia Public Schools">District of Columbia Public Schools</a> system is also referred to as the chancellor.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="University_president">University president</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: University president">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>University president is the title of the highest-ranking officer within the <a href="/wiki/Academic_administration" title="Academic administration">academic administration</a> of a university, within university systems that prefer that appellation over other variations such as chancellor or <a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a>. The relative seniority varies between institutions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France_2">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In France, the president is the elected chair of the board and chief executive officer in universities. The president is always elected by the board among the professors of the university. He serves a four-year term.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The chancellor is a servant of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Education" title="Ministry of Education">Ministry of Education</a> who supervises regional educational districts. There is no hierarchical relation between the president and the chancellor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Northern Ireland, the president is the chief academic and administrative officer of the university and is usually also the vice-chancellor of the university. The private London based liberal-arts university <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_The_American_International_University_in_London" title="Richmond, The American International University in London">Richmond, The American International University in London</a> utilises the same system as in the United States but also with a ceremonial Chancellor as figurehead.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_2">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In most stand-alone universities and colleges in the United States, the chief executive officer is called the president, while the second in command is called the <a href="/wiki/Provost_(education)" title="Provost (education)">provost</a>. In some multi-campus state university systems, the chancellor has authority over all universities in the system, and therefore ranks higher than the presidents of individual universities within the system. In other state university systems, the president has authority over multiple campuses, each of which is headed by a chancellor who is under the authority of the president.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vice-chancellor">Vice-chancellor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Vice-chancellor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A "vice-chancellor" (commonly called a "VC") of a university in England, Wales, Northern Ireland, New Zealand, Australia, India, Bangladesh, Malaysia, Nigeria, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, South Africa, other <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> countries, and <a href="/wiki/List_of_higher_education_institutions_in_Hong_Kong" title="List of higher education institutions in Hong Kong">some universities in Hong Kong</a> is the chief executive of the university. In Scotland, Canada, and Ireland, the chief executive of a university is usually called <a href="/wiki/University_principal" class="mw-redirect" title="University principal">principal</a> or <a href="/wiki/University_president" class="mw-redirect" title="University president">president</a> with <i>vice-chancellor</i> being an honorific associated with this title, allowing the individual to bestow degrees in absence of the chancellor.</p>
<p>Strictly speaking, the VC is only a deputy to the chancellor of the university, but the chancellor is usually a prominent public figure who acts as a ceremonial figurehead only (e.g., the chancellor of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a> for 36 years was <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Prince Philip</a>), while the vice-chancellor acts as the day-to-day chief executive. An assistant to a vice-chancellor is called a <a href="/wiki/Pro-vice-chancellor" title="Pro-vice-chancellor">pro-vice-chancellor</a> or deputy vice-chancellor; these were traditionally academics who were elected to take on additional responsibilities in addition to their regular teaching and research for a limited time, but are now increasingly commonly permanent appointments. In some universities (e.g. in Australian universities: <a href="/wiki/Deakin_University" title="Deakin University">Deakin University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_University" title="Macquarie University">Macquarie University</a>), there are several deputy vice-chancellors subordinate to the vice-chancellor, with <i>pro-vice-chancellor</i> being a position at executive level ranking below <i>deputy vice-chancellor</i>.</p>
<p>There are a few exceptions within England. For example, the charter of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Manchester" title="University of Manchester">University of Manchester</a> provides for the vice-chancellor to also use the title president, and the first vice-chancellor, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Gilbert_(Australian_academic)" title="Alan Gilbert (Australian academic)">Alan Gilbert</a> (2004–10), used president as his main title. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Warwick" title="University of Warwick">University of Warwick</a> now officially uses "vice-chancellor and president" (VCP), although the holder is usually still known as the vice-chancellor in all but official documents. The chief executives of the constituent colleges of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a>, many of which are now functionally independent universities, generally use the title "Principal", although the chief executive of <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Imperial College">Imperial College</a> has the title <a href="/wiki/Rector_of_Imperial_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Rector of Imperial College">Rector</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia_and_New_Zealand_2">Australia and New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Australia and New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The executive head of an Australian university is the vice-chancellor, who serves as the university equivalent of a chief executive officer.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The vice-chancellor is responsible for the day-to-day operations of the university and reports directly to the University Council, which the chancellor heads.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Assisting the vice-chancellor, the roles of deputy vice-chancellors and pro vice-chancellors have emerged to better manage the administrative overhead of the position.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada_and_the_United_Kingdom">Canada and the United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Canada and the United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Canadian university vice-chancellors almost always carry the title of "<a href="/wiki/University_president" class="mw-redirect" title="University president">president</a> (or equivalent) and vice-chancellor"; likewise, in Scotland, they hold the position of "<a href="/wiki/University_principal" class="mw-redirect" title="University principal">principal</a> and vice-chancellor," as do a few Canadian universities such as Queen's and McGill. In the Scottish practice, the one individual may have two sets of official robes, reflecting a continuing division of responsibilities between the two posts. The vice-chancellor's robes, therefore, should not be worn in the presence of the chancellor but should only be worn when deputizing for the chancellor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India_2">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In India, most central and state level universities have a titular head called chancellor who is either an eminent person appointed by the Government of India (in central universities) or provincial governor (in state universities). The de facto head of a university is the vice-chancellor, the highest paid official of the university. Next in command are more than one pro-vice-chancellor in charge of academic as well as administrative and financial affairs. In deemed universities and institutes of national importance, the head of the institution is either called <a href="/wiki/Director_general" title="Director general">director general</a> or <a href="/wiki/Director_(education)" class="mw-redirect" title="Director (education)">director</a>, the latter designation being more commonly used in academic terms in the subcontinent.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bangladesh_2">Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Bangladesh" title="President of Bangladesh">President of Bangladesh</a> is the titular chancellor of all universities in Bangladesh, public or private. The vice chancellor is the executive head, and his deputy, the pro-vice chancellor holds a full-time administrative office.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Sri Lanka, all the government universities are administered by the vice-chancellor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sudan">Sudan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Sudan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Sudan, universities are administered by the vice-chancellor .</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kenya">Kenya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Kenya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Kenya, Chancellors are titular heads of public universities, either appointed by the head of state (president) directly, or, in newly introduced legislation, at the recommendation of senate and alumni of the university. The day-to-day running of universities is the responsibility of the vice-chancellors. "Rector" and "president" are not commonly used terms in university administration.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria">Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Nigeria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, chancellors are ceremonial heads of public universities (mostly traditional monarchs), appointed by the head of state (president), governor of a state (in the case of state-owned universities) or assumed by the owner of a private university. The day-to-day running of universities is the responsibility of the vice-chancellors. "Rector" and "president" are not commonly used terms in university administration.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland_2">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Ireland, day-to-day operations of the universities are under the directorship of a president (a <a href="/wiki/Provost_(education)" title="Provost (education)">provost</a> in the case of <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College_Dublin" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity College Dublin">Trinity College Dublin</a>). However, the president of each constituent university of the <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Ireland" title="National University of Ireland">National University of Ireland</a> also has the title of <a href="/wiki/Pro-vice-chancellor" title="Pro-vice-chancellor">pro-vice-chancellor</a> of the NUI.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines_2">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Philippines, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Santo_Tomas" title="University of Santo Tomas">University of Santo Tomas</a> the day-to-day head of the university, as mandated by his duty as the <a href="/wiki/Prior" title="Prior">Prior</a> Provincial of the Philippine Dominican Province, the Dominican province that has majority control over the university.</p>
<p>As said earlier, the vice-chancellor or the "grand vice-chancellor of the University of Santo Tomas" is only the deputy to the chancellor of the university, but the chancellor is usually a prominent public figure who is not always in the country (e.g., the chancellor of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Santo_Tomas" title="University of Santo Tomas">University of Santo Tomas</a> is the current <a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Order_of_Preachers" title="Master of the Order of Preachers">Master of the Order of Preachers</a>, the current being Very Rev. Fr. Bruno Cadoré, OP, while the rector acts as the day-to-day chief executive). The current vice chancellor of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Santo_Tomas" title="University of Santo Tomas">UST</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Prior" title="Prior">Prior</a> Provincial of the Philippine Dominican Province, Very Rev. Fr. Napoleon Sipalay, OP. The current rector of the university is Rev. Fr. Herminio V. Dagohoy, OP.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Philippines" title="University of the Philippines">University of the Philippines</a>, the chancellor assigns different vice-chancellors to handle different aspects of running the university. There is one for instruction, administration, and community affairs, among others.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Sweden, the rektor (<a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)#Sweden" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a>) is the head of a Swedish university, but the word <i>vice-chancellor</i> (<i>vicekansler</i>) is often used as the English translation of rektor. The <i>vice-chancellor</i> (<i>vicekansler</i>) is also an honorary title given to the <i><a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)#Sweden" title="Rector (academia)">rectores magnifici</a></i> at the universities of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Lund" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Lund">Lund</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Uppsala" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Uppsala">Uppsala</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_3">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the United States, a vice-chancellor is an assistant to a chancellor, who is generally the (actual, not merely ceremonial) head of one campus of a large university which has several campuses. The head of the entire university is the president (the equivalent of a Commonwealth vice-chancellor), the chancellor is in charge of one campus, and a vice-chancellor is one of the chief assistants. Some systems, such as the <a href="/wiki/California_State_University" title="California State University">California State University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_System_of_Higher_Education" title="Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education">Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education</a>, and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Mississippi" title="University of Mississippi">University of Mississippi</a> invert this ranking so that the chancellor is the head of the entire university.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_South" class="mw-redirect" title="University of the South">University of the South</a>, the vice-chancellor is the administrative head of the university (as well as mayor of the town of Sewanee). The chancellor is a bishop of one of the 28 southeastern <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Church_in_the_United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Episcopal Church in the United States of America">Episcopal</a> <a href="/wiki/Dioceses" class="mw-redirect" title="Dioceses">dioceses</a> that own the university and is elected by the members of the Board of Trustees. The chancellor neither resides at the university nor holds administrative power; the office of chancellor is a ceremonial one.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Principal">Principal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Principal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The principal is the chief executive and the <a href="/wiki/Provost_(education)" title="Provost (education)">chief academic officer</a> of a university or college in certain parts of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a>.</p>
<p>A "director" is the chief executive officer of a university or other educational institution. Equivalent names in different countries are <a href="/wiki/Vice-Chancellor" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice-Chancellor">vice-chancellor</a> (many <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> countries), chancellor (United States), principal (Scotland and Canada), and <a href="/wiki/University_president" class="mw-redirect" title="University president">university president</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom_2">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Ancient_university_governance_in_Scotland" title="Ancient university governance in Scotland">Ancient university governance in Scotland</a></div>
<p>In Scotland, the principal is appointed by the <a href="/wiki/University_court" class="mw-redirect" title="University court">university court</a> or governing body of the university and will be chairman or president of the body of academics. In the case of the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_universities_of_Scotland" title="Ancient universities of Scotland">ancient universities of Scotland</a>, the principal is president of the <a href="/wiki/Academic_senate" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic senate">academic senate</a>. The principal also holds the title of vice-chancellor, but their powers with regard to this position extend only to the awarding of degrees, as both the vice-chancellor and chancellor are titular posts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_University" title="Queen's University">Queen's University</a> and <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a> in Canada have principals instead of <a href="/wiki/University_president" class="mw-redirect" title="University president">presidents</a> as a result of their Scottish origins. In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Military_College_of_Canada" title="Royal Military College of Canada">Royal Military College of Canada</a> also has a principal.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chancellor_(education)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_South_African_university_chancellors_and_vice-chancellors" class="mw-redirect" title="List of South African university chancellors and vice-chancellors">List of South African university chancellors and vice-chancellors</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Vice-Chancellors_and_Chancellors_of_the_University_of_Pretoria" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Vice-Chancellors and Chancellors of the University of Pretoria">List of Vice-Chancellors and Chancellors of the University of Pretoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_University_of_Cape_Town" title="Chancellor of the University of Cape Town">Chancellor of the University of Cape Town</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vice-Chancellor_of_the_University_of_Cape_Town" title="Vice-Chancellor of the University of Cape Town">Vice-Chancellor of the University of Cape Town</a></div>
<p>In South Africa, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Higher_Education_Act_101_of_1997&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Higher Education Act 101 of 1997 (page does not exist)">Higher Education Act 101 of 1997</a> defines the principal as "the chief executive and accounting officer of a public higher education institution."<sup id="cite_ref-che_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-che-10">[10]</a></sup> The definition allows for the alternative nomenclatures of vice-chancellor and a rector, and these terms are in widespread use (the term vice-chancellor is more common in English-medium universities, whilst the term rector tends to be used in <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans-medium</a> universities). The exact name in a particular university will be defined by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Higher_Education_Act_101_of_1997&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Higher Education Act 101 of 1997 (page does not exist)">Institutional Statute</a>. The same act defines the chancellor as the titular head of an institution.</p>
