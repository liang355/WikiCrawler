<p><b>Kitzingen</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is bounded by (from the north and clockwise) the districts of <a href="/wiki/Schweinfurt_(district)" title="Schweinfurt (district)">Schweinfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bamberg_(district)" title="Bamberg (district)">Bamberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neustadt_(Aisch)-Bad_Windsheim" title="Neustadt (Aisch)-Bad Windsheim">Neustadt (Aisch)-Bad Windsheim</a> and <a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg_(district)" title="Würzburg (district)">Würzburg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kitzingen_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district in its present form was established in the administrative reform of 1973. The former district of Gerolzhofen was dissolved, and half of its territory was merged with the Kitzingen district (which had been much smaller before). The city of Kitzingen lost its status as a district-free city and was incorporated into the district.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kitzingen_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Main_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Main River">Main River</a> runs through the district from south to north.</p>
