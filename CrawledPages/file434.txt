<p><b>Paderborn</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[paːdɐˈbɔʁn]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Paderborn.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/35/Paderborn.ogg" class="internal" title="Paderborn.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>)<sup id="cite_ref-Duden_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Duden-2">[2]</a></sup> is a city in eastern <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, capital of the <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_(district)" title="Paderborn (district)">Paderborn district</a>. The name of the city derives from the river <a href="/wiki/Pader_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Pader River">Pader</a> and 'born', an old German term for the source of a river. The river Pader originates in more than 200 springs near <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_Cathedral" title="Paderborn Cathedral">Paderborn Cathedral</a>, where <a href="/wiki/St._Liborius" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Liborius">St. Liborius</a> is buried.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paderborn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paderborn was founded as a <a href="/wiki/Bishopric" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishopric">bishopric</a> by <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a> in 795, although its official history began in 777 when Charlemagne built a castle near the Pader springs.<sup id="cite_ref-Heribert_Zelder_2009_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heribert_Zelder_2009-3">[3]</a></sup> In 799 <a href="/wiki/Pope_Leo_III" title="Pope Leo III">Pope Leo III</a> fled his enemies in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and reached Paderborn, where he met Charlemagne, and stayed there for three months. It was during this time that it was decided that Charlemagne would be crowned emperor. Charlemagne reinstated Leo in Rome in 800 and was crowned as <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> by Leo in return. In 836, <a href="/wiki/Liborius_of_Le_Mans" title="Liborius of Le Mans">St. Liborius</a> became the patron saint of Paderborn after his bones were moved there from <a href="/wiki/Le_Mans" title="Le Mans">Le Mans</a> by Bishop Badurad.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-4">[4]</a></sup> St. Liborius is commemorated in Paderborn every year in July with the Liborifest.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_Paderborn" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishopric of Paderborn">bishop of Paderborn</a>, Meinwerk, became a <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_the_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince of the Empire">Prince of the Empire</a> in 1100. The bishop had several large buildings built, and the area became a place for the emperors to stay.<sup id="cite_ref-Heribert_Zelder_2009_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heribert_Zelder_2009-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png/250px-Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png" width="250" height="77" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png/375px-Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png/500px-Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png 2x" data-file-width="2596" data-file-height="796" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paderborn-Kupferstich-Merian.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Paderborn in 1647.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city was taken by <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a> in 1802, then by the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Vassal_state" title="Vassal state">vassal state</a> <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Westphalia" title="Kingdom of Westphalia">Kingdom of Westphalia</a> from 1807 to 1813 and then returned to Prussia.</p>
<p>Native <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Sert%C3%BCrner" title="Friedrich Sertürner">Friedrich Sertürner</a>, a pharmacist's apprentice in Paderborn, was the first to isolate <a href="/wiki/Morphine" title="Morphine">morphine</a> from <a href="/wiki/Opium" title="Opium">opium</a> in 1804.</p>
<p>In 1930, the See of Paderborn was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Paderborn" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Paderborn">archdiocese</a>.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Paderborn was bombed by Allied aircraft in 1944 and 1945, resulting in 85% destruction, including many of the historic buildings. It was seized by the US <a href="/wiki/3rd_Armored_Division_(United_States)" title="3rd Armored Division (United States)">3rd Armored Division</a> after a pitched battle 31 March - 1 April 1945, in which tanks and <a href="/wiki/Flamethrower" title="Flamethrower">flamethrowers</a> were used during combined mechanized-infantry assaults against the city's southwestern, southern and southeastern approaches.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the city was reconstructed in the 1940s and 1950s, Paderborn became a major industrial seat in Westphalia.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-4">[4]</a></sup> The British Army has retained a significant presence in the area, and uses the nearby <a href="/wiki/Sennelager_Training_Area" title="Sennelager Training Area">Sennelager Training Area</a>.</p>

<p>Paderborn is situated at the spring of the <i>Pader</i> river, approximately 30 kilometres (19&#160;mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Lippstadt" title="Lippstadt">Lippstadt</a> and approximately 50 kilometres (31&#160;mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Bielefeld" title="Bielefeld">Bielefeld</a> on the Pader river. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paderborn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paderborn is the headquarters of the former <a href="/wiki/Nixdorf_Computer_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Nixdorf Computer AG">Nixdorf Computer AG</a>, which was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Siemens_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Siemens AG">Siemens</a> in the early 1990s and known as <a href="/wiki/Siemens-Nixdorf" class="mw-redirect" title="Siemens-Nixdorf">Siemens-Nixdorf</a> for about 10 years. The company is now known as <a href="/wiki/Wincor_Nixdorf" title="Wincor Nixdorf">Wincor Nixdorf</a> which is still located in Paderborn, but Siemens retains a considerable presence in the city.</p>
<p>Many other <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> companies as well as industrial enterprises are located in Paderborn, too:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Benteler_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Benteler AG">Benteler AG</a> (steel/tube, automotive, trade)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Claas" title="Claas">Claas</a> (farm machines)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bahn_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Deutsche Bahn AG">Deutsche Bahn AG</a> (vehicle maintenance)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/DSPACE_GmbH" title="DSPACE GmbH">dSPACE GmbH</a> (engineering tools)</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts_and_culture">Arts and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paderborn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Arts and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paderborn has the largest <a href="/wiki/Computer_museum" title="Computer museum">computer museum</a> in the world, the <i><a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinz_Nixdorf_MuseumsForum" class="extiw" title="de:Heinz Nixdorf MuseumsForum">de:Heinz Nixdorf MuseumsForum</a></i>. From 2001 to 2005 it hosted the <a href="/w/index.php?title=RoboCup_German_Open&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="RoboCup German Open (page does not exist)">RoboCup German Open</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/RoboCup_German_Open" class="extiw" title="de:RoboCup German Open">de</a>)</span>.</p>
<p>The town supports the <a href="/wiki/Nordwestdeutsche_Philharmonie" title="Nordwestdeutsche Philharmonie">Nordwestdeutsche Philharmonie</a> for regular symphony concerts in the Paderhalle.</p>
<p>The city is known today for its exhibitions in three museums: the <i>Kaiserpfalz</i>, The Diocesian Museum and the Art Museum - Städtische Galerie.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paderborn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paderborn is nationally known as a center for American Sports. The local <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team, the <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_Untouchables" title="Paderborn Untouchables">Paderborn Untouchables</a>, has won many German championships. The local <a href="/wiki/American_Football" class="mw-redirect" title="American Football">American Football</a> team, the Paderborn Dolphins, has also enjoyed considerable success. In 2006 the <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_Baskets" title="Paderborn Baskets">Paderborn Baskets</a>, the home <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> team of the city was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_Bundesliga" title="Basketball Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>.</p>
<h3><span id="Paderborn_Baskets_(Basketball)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Paderborn_Baskets_.28Basketball.29">Paderborn Baskets (Basketball)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paderborn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Paderborn Baskets (Basketball)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the past, the <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_Baskets" title="Paderborn Baskets">Paderborn Baskets</a> played multiple seasons in the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_Bundesliga" title="Basketball Bundesliga">Basketball Bundesliga</a>. They reached the playoffs in the 2008-09 season.</p>
<h3><span id="SC_Paderborn_07_(Football)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="SC_Paderborn_07_.28Football.29">SC Paderborn 07 (Football)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paderborn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: SC Paderborn 07 (Football)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/SC_Paderborn_07" title="SC Paderborn 07">SC Paderborn 07</a> is a German <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in Paderborn. Promoted from the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> due to a very successful <i>2013/2014</i> campaign, the team advanced to the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>, Germany's top flight, but remained there for just one year.</p>
<p>The club was formed out of the 1985 merger of FC Paderborn and TuS Schloß Neuhaus as TuS Paderborn-Neuhaus and took on its current, shorter name in 1997. The Neuhaus club was founded in 1907 as SV 07 Neuhaus which was joined by the local side TuS 1910 Sennelager to become TuS Schloss Neuhaus in 1970. The Neuhaus and Paderborn teams played as tier III sides for most of their histories, as has the unified club. Today Paderborn plays its home matches at the Benteler Arena.</p>

<p>Paderborn is located at the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_33" title="Bundesautobahn 33">Autobahn A 33</a>, which connects Paderborn to the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_2" title="Bundesautobahn 2">Autobahn A 2</a> in the north and the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_44" title="Bundesautobahn 44">Autobahn A 44</a> in the south.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_Hauptbahnhof" title="Paderborn Hauptbahnhof">main station</a> is a regular stop for the <a href="/wiki/InterCity" title="InterCity">InterCity</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Hamm%E2%80%93Warburg_railway" title="Hamm–Warburg railway">Hamm–Warburg line</a> and several local trains.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_Lippstadt_Airport" title="Paderborn Lippstadt Airport">Paderborn Lippstadt Airport</a> connects Paderborn to the bigger German airports and offers flights to many locations in Europe. There is a bus shuttle between the airport and the Paderborn main train station. General Aviation and gliders are based at Paderborn-Haxterberg (site of the world gliding championships in 1981).</p>
<p>In Paderborn there is a bus system served by the <i>PaderSprinter</i> for local buses and the <i>Bahnbus Hochstift</i> for regional buses.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paderborn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Uni-Paderborn_vom_Suedring.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Uni-Paderborn_vom_Suedring.jpg/220px-Uni-Paderborn_vom_Suedring.jpg" width="220" height="96" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Uni-Paderborn_vom_Suedring.jpg/330px-Uni-Paderborn_vom_Suedring.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Uni-Paderborn_vom_Suedring.jpg/440px-Uni-Paderborn_vom_Suedring.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1152" data-file-height="502" /></a>

<p>Paderborn was once the oldest academic site in Westphalia. In 1614, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paderborn" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Paderborn">University of Paderborn</a> was founded by the <a href="/wiki/Jesuits" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesuits">Jesuits</a> but was closed in 1819. It was re-founded in 1972 as Universität-Gesamthochschule and transformed into a university in its own right in 2002. Today, it is attended by about 20,000 students.</p>
<p>Additionally, there are several <a href="/wiki/Theological" class="mw-redirect" title="Theological">theological</a> and private academic institutes in Paderborn.</p>
<p>There are a number of grammar schools in the city, the most prominent of which are the <a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodorianum" class="extiw" title="de:Theodorianum">Theodorianum</a> and <a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gymnasium_St._Michael_Paderborn" class="extiw" title="de:Gymnasium St. Michael Paderborn">St. Michael Gymnasium</a>, along with others such as the <a href="/wiki/Goerdeler-Gymnasium_Paderborn" title="Goerdeler-Gymnasium Paderborn">Goerdeler-Gymnasium</a>. There are also a few British primary schools such as <a href="/wiki/John_Buchan_School" title="John Buchan School">John Buchan School</a>, which is located in <a href="/wiki/Sennelager" title="Sennelager">Sennelager</a> and mainly educates children of British military personnel and the garrison's employees.</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Karl_von_Plettenberg" title="Karl von Plettenberg">Karl von Plettenberg</a> (1852-1938), a Prussian officer and later <a href="/wiki/General_of_Infantry" class="mw-redirect" title="General of Infantry">General of Infantry</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a></li>
