<p>The <b>Harvard Museum of Natural History</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Natural_history" title="Natural history">natural history</a> <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museum</a> housed in the <a href="/wiki/University_Museum_(Harvard_University)" title="University Museum (Harvard University)">University Museum Building</a>, located on the campus of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>.</p>
<p>It displays a sampling of specimens drawn from the collections of the University's three natural history research museums:</p>
<ul>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University_Herbaria" title="Harvard University Herbaria">Harvard University Herbaria</a></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Comparative_Zoology" title="Museum of Comparative Zoology">Museum of Comparative Zoology</a></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Mineralogical_Museum" title="Harvard Mineralogical Museum">Harvard Mineralogical Museum</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>The museum is physically connected to the <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Museum_of_Archaeology_and_Ethnology" title="Peabody Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology">Peabody Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology</a> at 26 Oxford Street. One admission grants visitors access to both museums. In December 2012, Harvard announced a new consortium, the Harvard Museums of Science and Culture, whose members are the Harvard Museum of Natural History, the <a href="/wiki/Semitic_Museum" title="Semitic Museum">Semitic Museum</a>, the Peabody Museum, and the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Collection_of_Historical_Scientific_Instruments" title="Harvard Collection of Historical Scientific Instruments">Collection of Historical Scientific Instruments</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Museum_of_Natural_History&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Harvard Museum of Natural History was created in 1998 as the “public face” of three research museums — the Museum of Comparative Zoology, the Harvard Mineralogical and Geological Museum, and the Harvard University Herbaria. Its exhibitions draw on Harvard University’s natural history collections; Harvard’s research faculty provides expertise; and programs for members and the general public provide an exchange of information and ideas. With more than 210,000 visitors in 2013, the Harvard Museum of Natural History is the University’s most-visited museum. The Harvard Museum of Natural History is one of four public museums which are part of a new consortium created in 2012, the Harvard Museums of Science &amp; Culture.</p>

<p>In the Museum’s permanent galleries, visitors encounter the diversity of life on earth, from dinosaurs to <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossil</a> invertebrates and reptiles, to large <a href="/wiki/Mammals" class="mw-redirect" title="Mammals">mammals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birds" class="mw-redirect" title="Birds">birds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, and the only mounted <a href="/wiki/Kronosaurus" title="Kronosaurus">Kronosaurus</a>. The mineralogical galleries present a systematic display of <a href="/wiki/Meteorites" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorites">meteorites</a>, minerals and <a href="/wiki/Gemstones" class="mw-redirect" title="Gemstones">gemstones</a>. The galleries also house the historic <i><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lee_Ware" title="Mary Lee Ware">Ware</a> Collection of <a href="/wiki/Leopold_and_Rudolf_Blaschka" title="Leopold and Rudolf Blaschka">Blaschka</a> Glass Models of Plants</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> popularly known as the <a href="/wiki/Glass_Flowers" title="Glass Flowers">Glass Flowers</a> and a new exhibition, <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hmnh.harvard.edu/sea-creatures-glass">Sea Creatures in Glass</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> displaying some of the Harvard Museum of Comparative Zoology's collection of the <a href="/wiki/Glass_sea_creatures" title="Glass sea creatures">Blaschka models of marine invertebrates</a>. In addition, a series of changing exhibitions bring focus to new research at the University. Current exhibitions include Thoreau's Maine Woods: A Journey in Photographs with Scot Miller (through February 2015),<i>Mollusks: Shelled Masters of the Marine Realm</i>, and New England Forests in the Zofnass Family Gallery.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programs">Programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Museum_of_Natural_History&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Museum’s educational programs provides a hands-on, observation-based approach. With a growing reputation as a source of unique and effective science education and a successful partnership with Cambridge public schools, the Museum welcomes increasing numbers of school children and their families to its programs each year.</p>
<p>Public lectures are another of the Museum’s activities. The museum host regular free presentations by Harvard biologists, international conservationists, and popular authors that lead their audiences to a closer look at current issues in the world of science and nature.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Travel" title="Travel">travel</a> program complements the museum’s mission to enhance awareness of the natural world. Travelling in small groups often led by Harvard science faculty, Museum travellers experience destinations that are of particular importance as recognized hotspots of biodiversity.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Museum_of_Natural_History&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The museum is member-based, with over 3,200<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated.">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup> current members, primarily from the Boston metropolitan area. While the Museum is affiliated with the Harvard’s Faculty of Arts and Sciences and receives important support from the University, it derives most of its operating income from admissions, membership, gifts, and programmatic revenues.</p>
