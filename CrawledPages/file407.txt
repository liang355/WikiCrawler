<p><b>Oberhausen</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈoːbɐhaʊzn̩]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Oberhausen.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d0/De-Oberhausen.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Oberhausen.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is a city on the river <a href="/wiki/Emscher" title="Emscher">Emscher</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr Area">Ruhr Area</a>, Germany, located between <a href="/wiki/Duisburg" title="Duisburg">Duisburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen</a> (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr> 13&#160;km or 8.1&#160;mi). The city hosts the <a href="/wiki/International_Short_Film_Festival_Oberhausen" title="International Short Film Festival Oberhausen">International Short Film Festival Oberhausen</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Gasometer_Oberhausen" title="Gasometer Oberhausen">Gasometer Oberhausen</a> is an anchor point of the <a href="/wiki/European_Route_of_Industrial_Heritage" title="European Route of Industrial Heritage">European Route of Industrial Heritage</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oberhausen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oberhausen was named for its 1847 railway station which had taken its name from the <a href="/wiki/List_of_castles_in_North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="List of castles in North Rhine-Westphalia">Oberhausen Castle</a> (German: <i>Schloss Oberhausen</i>). The new borough was formed in 1862 following inflow of people for the local <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> mines and <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> mills. Awarded town rights in 1874, Oberhausen absorbed several neighbouring boroughs including <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstaden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alstaden (page does not exist)">Alstaden</a>, parts of <a href="/wiki/Styrum" title="Styrum">Styrum</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCmpten&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dümpten (page does not exist)">Dümpten</a> in 1910. Oberhausen became a city in 1901, and they incorporated the towns of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sterkrade&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sterkrade (page does not exist)">Sterkrade</a> and Osterfeld in 1929. The <a href="/wiki/Hoechst_AG" title="Hoechst AG">Ruhrchemie AG</a> synthetic oil plant ("Oberhausen-Holten" or "Sterkrade/Holten")<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was a bombing target of the <a href="/wiki/Oil_Campaign_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil Campaign of World War II">Oil Campaign of World War II</a>, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=295th_Ordnance_Heavy_Maintenance_Company_(FA)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="295th Ordnance Heavy Maintenance Company (FA) (page does not exist)">US Forces</a> had reached the plant by 4 April 1945.</p>
<p>In 1973 <a href="/wiki/Thyssen_AG" title="Thyssen AG">Thyssen</a> employed 14,000 people in Oberhausen in the steel industry, but ten years later the number had fallen to 6,000.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1954 the city began hosting the <a href="/wiki/International_Short_Film_Festival_Oberhausen" title="International Short Film Festival Oberhausen">International Short Film Festival Oberhausen</a>, and the 1982 <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Filmpreis" title="Deutscher Filmpreis">Deutscher Filmpreis</a> was awarded to a group that wrote the <a href="/wiki/Oberhausen_Manifesto" title="Oberhausen Manifesto">Oberhausen Manifesto</a>.</p>
