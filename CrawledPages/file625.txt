<p>This is a list of dormitories at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>. Only freshmen live in these dormitories, which are located in and around <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a>. Sophomores, juniors and seniors live in the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College#House_system" title="Harvard College">House system</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Apley_Court">Apley Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Harvard_College_freshman_dormitories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Apley Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Apley Court</b> – Rooms are the most spacious of the freshman dorms. Past notable residents include <a href="/wiki/T._S._Eliot" title="T. S. Eliot">T. S. Eliot</a>. Formerly part of Adams House, Apley Court is the only Gold Coast era freshman dormitory—constructed in 1897 as part of a series of luxurious apartments built on Holyoke Street. Accommodations include marble bathrooms, private in-suite showers, multiple walk in closets, and two common rooms in the basement.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Canaday_Hall">Canaday Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Harvard_College_freshman_dormitories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Canaday Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Canaday_Hall,_Harvard_-_IMG_6629.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Canaday_Hall%2C_Harvard_-_IMG_6629.JPG/220px-Canaday_Hall%2C_Harvard_-_IMG_6629.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Canaday_Hall%2C_Harvard_-_IMG_6629.JPG/330px-Canaday_Hall%2C_Harvard_-_IMG_6629.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Canaday_Hall%2C_Harvard_-_IMG_6629.JPG/440px-Canaday_Hall%2C_Harvard_-_IMG_6629.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>

<p><b>Canaday Hall</b> – Completed in 1974, it is the newest dormitory in <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a>. Seen from the air its seven buildings resemble a question mark. It is named after Ward M. Canaday, former president and major shareholder of the <a href="/wiki/Willys" title="Willys">Willys</a>, manufacturer of <a href="/wiki/Jeep" title="Jeep">Jeeps</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<p>Canaday's construction immediately followed the 1969 student takeover of <a href="/wiki/University_Hall_(Harvard_University)" title="University Hall (Harvard University)">University Hall</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> certain features of its design were meant to confound student organizing. There is a Muslim prayer space in the basement. <sup id="cite_ref-prayer-space_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prayer-space-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Residents have included <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lane_(journalist)" title="Charles Lane (journalist)">Charles Lane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Saverin" title="Eduardo Saverin">Eduardo Saverin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esther_Lofgren" title="Esther Lofgren">Esther Lofgren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Mezrich" title="Ben Mezrich">Ben Mezrich</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Sacks" title="David Sacks">David Sacks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mira_Sorvino" title="Mira Sorvino">Mira Sorvino</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Wylie" title="Paul Wylie">Paul Wylie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-search_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-search-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grays_Hall">Grays Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Harvard_College_freshman_dormitories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Grays Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grays_Hall_-_Harvard.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/03/Grays_Hall_-_Harvard.jpg/150px-Grays_Hall_-_Harvard.jpg" width="150" height="119" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/03/Grays_Hall_-_Harvard.jpg/225px-Grays_Hall_-_Harvard.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/03/Grays_Hall_-_Harvard.jpg/300px-Grays_Hall_-_Harvard.jpg 2x" data-file-width="713" data-file-height="564" /></a>

<p><b>Grays Hall</b> – Opened in 1863, Grays became the College's first building with water taps in the basement. (Residents of other buildings in Harvard Yard had to haul water from pumps in the Yard.) Known as "The Harvard Hilton",<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> it is considered the most luxurious dormitory in the Yard.<sup id="cite_ref-JFKslept_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JFKslept-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Past residents include <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Bingaman" title="Jeff Bingaman">Jeff Bingaman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cohrs" title="Michael Cohrs">Michael Cohrs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Doner" title="Jeremy Doner">Jeremy Doner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Fitzpatrick" title="Ryan Fitzpatrick">Ryan Fitzpatrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Julie_Hilden" title="Julie Hilden">Julie Hilden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norman_Mailer" title="Norman Mailer">Norman Mailer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malia_Obama" class="mw-redirect" title="Malia Obama">Malia Obama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Portman" title="Natalie Portman">Natalie Portman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Ransohoff" title="Joseph Ransohoff">Joseph Ransohoff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Rich" title="Frank Rich">Frank Rich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mo_Rocca" title="Mo Rocca">Mo Rocca</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Weidman" title="John Weidman">John Weidman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Weishan" title="Michael Weishan">Michael Weishan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenough_Hall">Greenough Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Harvard_College_freshman_dormitories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Greenough Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Greenough Hall</b> – Located just outside the confines of Harvard Yard, Greenough is part of a group of dormitories outside the Yard called the "Union Dormitories". Past notable residents include <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Abrams" title="Elliott Abrams">Elliott Abrams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Kristol" class="mw-redirect" title="Bill Kristol">Bill Kristol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallace_Shawn" title="Wallace Shawn">Wallace Shawn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Tribe" title="Laurence Tribe">Laurence Tribe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colin_Jost" title="Colin Jost">Colin Jost</a>.</p>

<p><b>Hollis Hall</b> – Built in 1763, Hollis is one of the oldest buildings at Harvard, and housed George Washington's troops during the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Massachusetts_Hall">Massachusetts Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Harvard_College_freshman_dormitories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Massachusetts Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Massachusetts_Hall,_Harvard_University.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Massachusetts_Hall%2C_Harvard_University.JPG/220px-Massachusetts_Hall%2C_Harvard_University.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Massachusetts_Hall%2C_Harvard_University.JPG/330px-Massachusetts_Hall%2C_Harvard_University.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Massachusetts_Hall%2C_Harvard_University.JPG/440px-Massachusetts_Hall%2C_Harvard_University.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920" /></a>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Hall,_Harvard_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts Hall, Harvard University">Massachusetts Hall</a></b> – The oldest surviving building at Harvard and the country's oldest dormitory, Massachusetts Hall is located next to <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Gate" title="Johnston Gate">Johnston Gate</a>. Designed by two Harvard Presidents, <a href="/wiki/John_Leverett_the_Younger" title="John Leverett the Younger">John Leverett</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Wadsworth" title="Benjamin Wadsworth">Benjamin Wadsworth</a>, between 1718 and 1720 for the housing of sixty-four students, the building served various functions over the years, including a refuge for American soldiers during the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Boston" title="Siege of Boston">Siege of Boston</a>, and an observatory after <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hollis_(1659%E2%80%931731)" title="Thomas Hollis (1659–1731)">Thomas Hollis</a>' donation of a twenty-four-foot telescope in 1722. Today, it houses the offices of Harvard's President, with a handful of freshmen living on the uppermost floor.</p>
<p>Five of the United States' <a href="/wiki/Founding_Fathers_of_the_United_States" title="Founding Fathers of the United States">Founding Fathers</a> lived here.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="Statement needs to be more specific about the content to which it refers.">specify</span></a></i>]</sup> Other residents have included <a href="/wiki/Zabdiel_Adams" title="Zabdiel Adams">Zabdiel Adams</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Harbison" title="John Harbison">John Harbison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jay_Lerner" title="Alan Jay Lerner">Alan Jay Lerner</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Redcliffe-Maud,_Baron_Redcliffe-Maud" title="John Redcliffe-Maud, Baron Redcliffe-Maud">John Redcliffe-Maud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elliot_Richardson" title="Elliot Richardson">Elliot Richardson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jared_Sparks" title="Jared Sparks">Jared Sparks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jones_Very" title="Jones Very">Jones Very</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Edward_Wigglesworth" title="Edward Wigglesworth">Edward Wigglesworth</a>.</p>

<p><b>Weld Hall</b> – Built in 1870, Weld was the second of two important additions to the Harvard campus designed by <a href="/wiki/William_Robert_Ware" title="William Robert Ware">Ware &amp; Van Brunt</a> (the first being <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Hall_(Harvard_University)" title="Memorial Hall (Harvard University)">Memorial Hall</a>).</p>
<p>It was a gift of <a href="/wiki/William_Fletcher_Weld" title="William Fletcher Weld">William Fletcher Weld</a>, in memory of his brother <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Minot_Weld" title="Stephen Minot Weld">Stephen Minot Weld</a>, and represented a new trend toward picturesque silhouettes that became important in American domestic architecture of the later nineteenth century, as can be seen in the <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne_Style_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Anne Style architecture">Queen Anne style</a> which was popular during the same period.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wigglesworth_Hall">Wigglesworth Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Harvard_College_freshman_dormitories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Wigglesworth Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:WigglesworthHall.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/60/WigglesworthHall.jpg/220px-WigglesworthHall.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/60/WigglesworthHall.jpg/330px-WigglesworthHall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/60/WigglesworthHall.jpg/440px-WigglesworthHall.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:WigglesworthHall.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Wigglesworth Hall</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><b>Wigglesworth Hall</b> – The second largest of the freshman dormitories, and actually three buildings, Wigglesworth is located along the southern edge of the Yard, between <a href="/wiki/Widener_Library" title="Widener Library">Widener Library</a> and Boylston Hall to the north, and Massachusetts Avenue to the south. It was constructed in 1931 as "part of President Lowell's plan to enclose the Yard from the traffic of Harvard Square."</p>
<p>Past residents include <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Bernstein" title="Leonard Bernstein">Leonard Bernstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Block" title="Melissa Block">Melissa Block</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_C._Bradlee" class="mw-redirect" title="Benjamin C. Bradlee">Benjamin C. Bradlee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Danner" title="Mark Danner">Mark Danner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jared_Diamond" title="Jared Diamond">Jared Diamond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andre_Gregory" title="Andre Gregory">Andre Gregory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donald_P._Hodel" title="Donald P. Hodel">Donald P. Hodel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aga_Khan_IV" title="Aga Khan IV">Aga Khan IV</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Lithgow" title="John Lithgow">John Lithgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Lowell" title="Robert Lowell">Robert Lowell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nowinski" title="Christopher Nowinski">Christopher Nowinski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pat_Toomey" title="Pat Toomey">Pat Toomey</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Vitter" title="David Vitter">David Vitter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naomi_Yang" class="mw-redirect" title="Naomi Yang">Naomi Yang</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Randi_Zuckerberg" title="Randi Zuckerberg">Randi Zuckerberg</a>.</p>
