			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Undergraduate education</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduates&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Undergraduates">Undergraduates</a>)</span></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Undergraduate education</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Post-secondary_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Post-secondary education">post-secondary education</a> previous to the <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_education" title="Postgraduate education">postgraduate education</a>. It includes all the academic programs up to the level of a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a>. For example, in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, an entry level university student is known as an undergraduate, while students of higher degrees are known as <a href="/wiki/Graduate_student" class="mw-redirect" title="Graduate student">graduates</a>. In some other educational systems and subjects, undergraduate education is <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">post-secondary education</a> up to the level of a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">master's degree</a>; this is the case for some science courses in Britain and some medicine courses in Europe.</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, undergraduate degrees (excluding Medicine, Medical Laboratory Science, Nursing, Engineering, Law and Architecture) are four-year-based courses. Medicine (MBBS) and Architecture normally take six years to complete studies while Medical Laboratory Science, Nursing, Law and Engineering courses take five years to complete studies, usually, all six years are taken to improve their chances.</p>

<p>The South African system usually has a three-year undergraduate bachelor's degree, with two or three majors. (There are exceptions, such as the medical qualification (MBChB), which is six years.) A fourth year, known as an Honours year, is considered a post-graduate degree. It is usually course-driven, although may include a project or thesis.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> follows the major traits of the continental European system; free public schools are available from kindergarten up to postgraduation, both as a right established in Article 6, <i>caput</i> of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian Constitution">Brazilian Constitution</a> and as a duty of the State in Article 208, Items I, IV and V, of the Brazilian Constitution.<sup id="cite_ref-Planalto_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Planalto-1">[1]</a></sup> Students choose their specific course of studies before joining the university. Admission to university is obtained by means of a competitive entrance exam known as <a href="/wiki/Vestibular" class="mw-redirect" title="Vestibular">Vestibular</a> (a concept somewhat similar to the <i><a href="/wiki/Baccalaur%C3%A9at" title="Baccalauréat">Baccalauréat</a></i> in France). There's a new system, adopted by most federal universities, that uses the high school national examination (<a href="/wiki/ENEM" class="mw-redirect" title="ENEM">ENEM</a>) result as part or a replacement of the Vestibular grade.<sup id="cite_ref-MEC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MEC-2">[2]</a></sup> Depending on the chosen course, upon graduating the student shall be granted: a technologist diploma, 3 years to complete, a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Diplom" title="Diplom">diploma</a>, which usually takes 4 or, in the case of <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">Law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Veterinary" class="mw-redirect" title="Veterinary">Veterinary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">Geology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a>, 5 years to complete; or a professional <a href="/wiki/Diplom" title="Diplom">diploma</a>, which normally require 5 or, in the case of <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a>, 6 years to complete.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_system">United States system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: United States system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a> <i>undergraduate</i> refers to those who are studying for a bachelor's degree.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The most common method consists of four years of study leading to a Bachelor of Arts (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">B.A.</a>), a Bachelor of Science (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">B.S.</a>), or sometimes another bachelor's degree such as Bachelor of Fine Arts (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">B.F.A.</a>), Bachelor of Music (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Music" title="Bachelor of Music">B. Mus.</a>), Bachelor of Social Work (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Social_Work" class="mw-redirect" title="Bachelor of Social Work">B.S.W.</a>), Bachelor of Engineering (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Engineering" title="Bachelor of Engineering">B.Eng.</a>), Bachelor of Science in Public Affairs (<a href="/w/index.php?title=B.S.P.A&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="B.S.P.A (page does not exist)">B.S.P.A</a>), Bachelor of Science in Nursing (<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science_in_Nursing" title="Bachelor of Science in Nursing">B.S.N.</a>), or Bachelor of Philosophy (<a href="/wiki/B.Phil." class="mw-redirect" title="B.Phil.">B.Phil.</a>) Five-Year Professional Architecture programs offer the Bachelor of Architecture Degree (<a href="/wiki/B.Arch." class="mw-redirect" title="B.Arch.">B.Arch.</a>) or sometimes Master of Architecture degree (<a href="/wiki/M.Arch." class="mw-redirect" title="M.Arch.">M.Arch.</a>).</p>
<p>Unlike in the British model, degrees in law and medicine are not offered at the undergraduate level and are completed as professional study after earning a bachelor's degree. Neither field specifies or prefers any undergraduate major, though medical schools have set prerequisite courses that must be taken before enrollment.</p>
<p>Some students choose to attend a <a href="/wiki/Community_college" title="Community college">community college</a> for two years prior to further study at another college or university. In most states, community colleges are operated either by a division of the state university or by local special districts subject to guidance from a state agency. Community colleges award <a href="/wiki/Associate_degree" title="Associate degree">associate degrees</a> of different types, some intended to prepare students to transfer to four-year schools (e.g. Associate of Arts (AA), Associate of Science (AS)), and others intended to provide vocational skills and training for students wishing to enter into or advance in a profession. Those seeking to continue their education may transfer to a four-year college or university after applying through a similar admissions process as those applying directly to the four-year institution called <a href="/wiki/Articulation_(education)" title="Articulation (education)">articulation</a>. Some community colleges have automatic enrollment agreements with a local four-year college, where the community college provides the first two years of study and the university provides the remaining years of study, sometimes all on one campus. The community colleges award associate degrees, while universities and colleges award the bachelor's. However, some community colleges, such as <a href="/wiki/Brazosport_College" title="Brazosport College">Brazosport College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Jackson,_Texas" title="Lake Jackson, Texas">Lake Jackson, Texas</a> offer bachelor's degrees along with associate degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong_system">Hong Kong system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Hong Kong system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, the English system is followed. Students sit for the <a href="/wiki/HKCEE" class="mw-redirect" title="HKCEE">Certificate of Education examinations</a> at around sixteen years of age, and the <a href="/wiki/HKALE" class="mw-redirect" title="HKALE">Advanced-level, or A-level examinations</a> at around eighteen, then follow by three years of undergraduate education, except for a few specific fields, such as medicine, nursing and law. This is due to be changed, with five-year secondary education and two-year <a href="/wiki/Matriculation_examination" title="Matriculation examination">matriculation examination</a> combined and shortened to six years <a href="/wiki/Matriculation" title="Matriculation">matriculation</a>, and undergraduate education lengthened to four years. Students may be able to receive <a href="/wiki/Curriculum#Higher_education" title="Curriculum">general education</a> in their first years in universities, more akin to the North American system. The first batch of students under the new system will enter universities in 2012.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Indian_system">Indian system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Indian system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> the Graduation system is classified into two parts: Undergraduation (UG) and Postgraduation (PG). It takes three or four years to complete an "undergraduate" degree. The three-year undergraduate programs are mostly in the fields of arts, commerce, science etc., and the four-year programs are mostly in the fields of technology, engineering, pharmaceutical sciences, agriculture etc. However, for medicine, law and architecture, the period has been five years.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The possessor of the first UG is referred to as graduate and that of the PG degree as post-graduate.<sup id="cite_ref-Anand2014_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Anand2014-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TOI-20141007_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOI-20141007-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistani_system">Pakistani system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Pakistani system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, it generally requires four years to complete a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in Arts, Sciences, Dentistry, Engineering or Business Administration such as <a href="/wiki/BA_degree" class="mw-redirect" title="BA degree">BA</a>, <a href="/wiki/BS_degree" class="mw-redirect" title="BS degree">BS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dental_Degree" class="mw-redirect" title="Dental Degree">BDS</a>, <a href="/wiki/B.E." class="mw-redirect" title="B.E.">BE/BS/BSc Engineering</a> or <a href="/wiki/BBA" class="mw-redirect" title="BBA">BBA</a> and five years for bachelor's degrees in Medicine (<a href="/wiki/MBBS" class="mw-redirect" title="MBBS">MBBS</a>), Physiotherapy (<a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Physical_Therapy" title="Doctor of Physical Therapy">DPT</a>), Pharmacy (<a href="/wiki/Pharm.D" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharm.D">Pharm.D</a>) and Architecture (<a href="/wiki/B.Arch" class="mw-redirect" title="B.Arch">B.Arch</a>) after successfully completing 12 years of schooling. 4 years bachelor's degree is offered in various universities of Pakistan such as <a href="/wiki/COMSATS_Institute_of_Information_Technology" title="COMSATS Institute of Information Technology">COMSATS Institute of Information Technology</a> (CIIT), <a href="/wiki/University_of_Engineering_and_Technology,_Lahore" title="University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore">University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore</a> (UET Lahore), <a href="/wiki/University_of_Engineering_and_Technology,_Taxila" title="University of Engineering and Technology, Taxila">University of Engineering and Technology, Taxila</a> (UET Taxila), <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Sciences_and_Technology_(Pakistan)" title="National University of Sciences and Technology (Pakistan)">National University of Sciences and Technology</a> (NUST), <a href="/wiki/Lahore_University_of_Management_Sciences" title="Lahore University of Management Sciences">Lahore University of Management Sciences</a> (LUMS), and <a href="/wiki/Foundation_for_Advancement_of_Science_and_Technology" title="Foundation for Advancement of Science and Technology">Foundation for Advancement of Science and Technology</a> (FAST).</p>
<p>The modern educational system comprises the following five stages: The <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">Primary school</a> lasting five years for children 5-10 years old in grades one to five; a <a href="/wiki/Middle_school" title="Middle school">Middle school</a> of three years for children 10 to 13 years old, covering grades six through eight; a two-year secondary, or <a href="/wiki/Matriculation" title="Matriculation">Matriculation</a> consists of grades nine and ten, for children 13 to 15 years old; a two-year higher secondary, or <a href="/wiki/Intermediate_college" class="mw-redirect" title="Intermediate college">Intermediate college</a>, leading to an <a href="/wiki/Faculty_in_Arts_(Certificate)" class="mw-redirect" title="Faculty in Arts (Certificate)">F.A.</a> in arts or <a href="/wiki/Faculty_in_Science_(Certificate)" class="mw-redirect" title="Faculty in Science (Certificate)">F.Sc.</a> in science; and a fifth stage covering college and university programs leading to baccalaureate, professional, master's and doctorate degrees. The pre-primary or preparatory classes, called kachi (literally, unripe) or <a href="/wiki/Nursery_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Nursery school">Nursery school</a>, were formally integrated into the education system in 1988. <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="English,_Welsh,_and_Northern_Irish_system"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="English.2C_Welsh.2C_and_Northern_Irish_system">English, Welsh, and Northern Irish system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: English, Welsh, and Northern Irish system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Students in England, Wales and Northern Ireland may usually enter university from the age of eighteen, often having studied <a href="/wiki/A-levels" class="mw-redirect" title="A-levels">A-levels</a> and thus having had thirteen to fifteen years of schooling. Occasionally students who finish A Level or equivalent qualifications early (after skipping a year in school on the grounds of academic giftedness) may enter below this age but large universities are now setting minimum age limits of 16 or 17 after a number of well publicised "child prodigies" were found to be emotionally and mentally unprepared for university life.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Applications for undergraduate courses in UK higher education are made through the <a href="/wiki/Universities_and_Colleges_Admissions_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Universities and Colleges Admissions Service">Universities and Colleges Admissions Service</a> (UCAS).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>For their first degree, most students read for the degree of <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor</a>, which usually takes three years, however in the sciences and engineering integrated courses covering both undergraduate level and advanced degree level leading to the degree of <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree_in_Europe#With_Dissertation" title="Master's degree in Europe">master</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-10">[10]</a></sup> usually taking four years and including a research project or <a href="/wiki/Dissertation" class="mw-redirect" title="Dissertation">dissertation</a> are popular. Given the integrated nature of these programs someone who gains a master's degree via an integrated program is not admitted to the degree of bachelor.</p>
<p>Master's degrees conferred after extended programs are not to be conflated with the degree of <a href="/wiki/Oxbridge_MA" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxbridge MA">Master of Arts conferred at Oxbridge and Dublin</a>, which is not a substantive qualification, but reflects the ancient practice of those three universities of promoting Bachelors of Arts to Masters of Arts (and thus full membership of the University) six or seven years after matriculation.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Honours degrees and integrated master's degrees are awarded with <a href="/wiki/British_undergraduate_degree_classification" title="British undergraduate degree classification">1st, upper 2nd, lower 2nd or 3rd class honours</a>. If a student passes the course but fails to do so sufficiently well for third class honours to be awarded he will be awarded with an ordinary degree. It is possible to use the abbreviation "Hons" after the degree <a href="/wiki/Post_nominal_letters" class="mw-redirect" title="Post nominal letters">postnominals</a> to indicate that the degree has been passed with honours and is not an ordinary degree.</p>
<p>Many universities offer sandwich courses or an extramural year, which offer work placements for a short period of time in a relevant industry before students complete their studies. Taking a sandwich course may make the course last a year longer than it would otherwise.</p>
<p>Apart from a single private university, <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Buckingham University">Buckingham</a>, all universities with the power to award degrees are heavily state financed. However, they also rely on tuition fees set by the government at a maximum index-linked level, repayable after graduation contingent on attaining a certain level of income, and with the state paying all fees for students from the poorest backgrounds. UK students are generally entitled to student loans for maintenance with repayment contingent on income.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Unlike in other European countries, the British government does not own the universities' assets and university staff are not civil servants. United Kingdom universities are therefore better described as autonomous, intellectually-independent institutions with public funding, rather than public universities per se. The crown does not control syllabi, with the exception of teacher training. The crown restricts the power to award degrees to those with a <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charter</a>, in the case of traditional universities, or authorization from the <a href="/wiki/DIUS" class="mw-redirect" title="DIUS">Secretary of State for Universities</a>, in the case of <a href="/wiki/Post-1992_universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Post-1992 universities">modern universities</a>. Universities accredited in foreign countries, such as <a href="/wiki/Richmond_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Richmond University">Richmond University</a> are, however, free to operate.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Bologna_process_systems">European Bologna process systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: European Bologna process systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In many countries, the English distinction between a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_Degree" class="mw-redirect" title="Bachelor's Degree">bachelor's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_Degree" class="mw-redirect" title="Master's Degree">master's</a> degree is being introduced by the <a href="/wiki/Bologna_process" class="mw-redirect" title="Bologna process">Bologna process</a>. Under the new Bologna reform, universities in Europe are introducing the Bachelor level (BA or BS) degree, often by dividing a 5-year Master-level program into two parts (3-year Bachelor's + 2-year Master's), where students are not obligated to continue with the second Master's-degree part. These new bachelor's degrees are similar in structure to British bachelor's degrees.</p>
<p>If there is a separate undergraduate degree, higher degrees (License, Master, Doctorat) can be gained after completing the undergraduate degree. In the traditional German system, there were no undergraduate degrees in some fields, such as engineering: students continued to Master's level education without any administrative breakpoints, and employers would not consider half-finished master's degrees.</p>
<p>The Bachelor's phase in The Netherlands can be fulfilled either at university or at the <a href="/wiki/Hogeschool#Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Hogeschool">University of Applied Sciences</a>. Except for some specific exceptions, only at universities students are able to graduate for their masters or be promoted. These two institutions differ from each other in the level students learn abstract concepts. Whereas theories are created at the university, at the University of Applied Sciences theories are taught to be applied correctly.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Scottish_system">Scottish system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Scottish system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Students in Scotland usually enter university in the year they turn eighteen (with many still being seventeen upon starting), hence courses take an extra year compared to England, Wales and Northern Ireland.</p>
<p>At the older universities the degree of <a href="/wiki/Scottish_MA" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish MA">Master of Arts</a> is conferred in the arts subjects after four years while the newer universities instead confer the degree of <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a>. The degree of <a href="/wiki/Scottish_MA" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish MA">Master of Arts conferred by the Ancient Scottish Universities</a> is equivalent to the degree of Bachelor of Arts at other universities and does not require the level of study necessitated for the other degrees of master awarded by these universities. The degree instead reflects the ancient traditions of these universities.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the sciences, students usually read for the <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">degree of bachelor</a>, which usually takes four years. However, as with the rest of the UK, integrated master's degrees are popular in science and engineering, although in Scotland they last for five years.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/British_undergraduate_degree_classification" title="British undergraduate degree classification">Degree classification is as that of the rest of the UK.</a></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_European_systems">Other European systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undergraduate_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Other European systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In many other, particularly continental European systems, an "undergraduate" degree in the American sense does not exist. Because students are expected to have received a sound general education at the secondary level, in a school such as a <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasium</a> or <a href="/wiki/Lycee" class="mw-redirect" title="Lycee">lycee</a>, students in Europe enroll in a specific course of studies they wish to pursue upon entry into a University. In the US, students only specialize in a "major" during the last years of college. Specializing in a field of study upon entry into a university means most students graduate after four to five years of study. The fields available include those only taught as graduate degrees in the US, such as <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">law</a> or <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a>.</p>
<p>In the traditional German system, there is a vocational degree (<a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">Diploma</a> <a href="/wiki/Fachhochschule" title="Fachhochschule">FH</a>) that is similar in length, and is also considered an academic degree. Though it is designed as a specialist degree, in contrast to the Diplom degree at <i>University</i>, which claims to be more generalist. Germany itself, however, is currently abolishing the legal distinction between <a href="/wiki/Fachhochschule" title="Fachhochschule">Fachhochschule</a> and University. They are both translated as university and they both provide bologna-compliant and equivalent postgraduate degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Not obligatory and sometimes applied at Universities in the Netherlands are the <a href="/wiki/Propaedeutics" title="Propaedeutics">propaedeutic</a> exams. The entire curriculum of the first two semesters of the bachelors programme is part of the propaedeutic exams. In most bachelor's studies, students are required to obtain their propaedeutic certificate within three semesters after starting the course. A propaedeutic certificate also counts as a requirement for participating in a university level bachelor's study. The propaedeutic exams have the purpose of assessing whether a student has the appropriate capacities in order to complete the course.</p>
<p>At some Swedish universities (such as the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_Technology" title="Royal Institute of Technology">Royal Institute of Technology</a>), PhD courses are sometimes referred to as "graduate courses", whereas courses for other students (up to master level) sometimes are referred to as "undergraduate courses". The system at many Finnish universities is similar.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/French_Education_System" class="mw-redirect" title="French Education System">French system</a>, the first degree of tertiary education was reached two years after the <a href="/wiki/Baccalaur%C3%A9at" title="Baccalauréat">baccalauréat</a>. Amongst these degrees the university-delivered <a href="/wiki/DEUG" class="mw-redirect" title="DEUG">DEUG</a> has disappeared, whereas <a href="/wiki/Dipl%C3%B4me_universitaire_de_technologie" class="mw-redirect" title="Diplôme universitaire de technologie">Diplôme universitaire de technologie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brevet_de_Technicien_Sup%C3%A9rieur" class="mw-redirect" title="Brevet de Technicien Supérieur">Brevet de Technicien Supérieur</a> or <a href="/wiki/Classe_pr%C3%A9paratoire_aux_grandes_%C3%A9coles" title="Classe préparatoire aux grandes écoles">classe préparatoire aux grandes écoles</a> still exist. According to the <a href="/wiki/Bologna_process" class="mw-redirect" title="Bologna process">Bologna process</a>, these two-years curriculum will be replaced by the three years <a href="/wiki/Licence" class="mw-redirect" title="Licence">licence</a>, yet existing.</p>
