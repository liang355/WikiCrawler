<p><b>Rostock</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈʁɔstɔk]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Rostock.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/dc/De-Rostock.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Rostock.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is the largest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> in the north <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">state</a> <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg-Vorpommern" title="Mecklenburg-Vorpommern">Mecklenburg-Vorpommern</a>. Rostock is on the <a href="/wiki/Warnow" title="Warnow">Warnow</a> river; the district of <a href="/wiki/Warnem%C3%BCnde" title="Warnemünde">Warnemünde</a> 12 kilometres (7 miles) north of the city centre is directly on the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> coast.</p>
<p>Rostock is home to one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_oldest_universities_in_continuous_operation" title="List of oldest universities in continuous operation">oldest universities in the world</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rostock" title="University of Rostock">University of Rostock</a>, founded in 1419.</p>
<p>The city territory of Rostock stretches for about 20&#160;km (12&#160;mi) along the Warnow to the Baltic Sea. The largest built-up area of Rostock is on the western side of the river. The eastern part of its territory is dominated by industrial estates and the forested <a href="/wiki/Rostock_Heath" title="Rostock Heath">Rostock Heath</a>. Rostock is considered as the only <a href="/wiki/Regiopolis" title="Regiopolis">regiopolis</a> in Mecklenburg-Vorpommern.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Rostock" title="Timeline of Rostock">Timeline of Rostock</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 11th century <a href="/wiki/Polabian_Slavs" title="Polabian Slavs">Polabian Slavs</a> founded a settlement at the Warnow river called <i>Roztoc</i> (*<i>ras-tokŭ</i>, Slavic for "fork of a river"); the name Rostock is derived from that designation.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> king <a href="/wiki/Valdemar_I_of_Denmark" title="Valdemar I of Denmark">Valdemar I</a> set the town aflame in 1161. Afterwards the place was settled by German traders. Initially there were three separate cities:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Altstadt</i> (Old Town) around the <i>Alter Markt</i> (Old Market) with <a href="/wiki/St._Petrikirche,_Rostock" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Petrikirche, Rostock">St. Petri</a> (St. Peter's Church),</li>
<li><i>Mittelstadt</i> (Middle Town) around the <i>Neuer Markt</i> (New Market) with St. Marien (<a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s_Church,_Rostock" title="St. Mary's Church, Rostock">St. Mary's Church</a>) and</li>
</ul>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png/175px-Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png" width="175" height="216" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png/263px-Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png/350px-Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png 2x" data-file-width="1744" data-file-height="2148" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostocker_Stadtbest%C3%A4tigung_1213.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Confirmation of <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbeck_law" title="Lübeck law">Lübeck law</a> city rights, 1218</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><i>Neustadt</i> (New Town) around the <i>Hopfenmarkt</i> (Hop Market, now University Square) with St. Jakobi (St. James's Church, now demolished).</li>
</ul>
<p>In 1218, Rostock was granted <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbeck_law" title="Lübeck law">Lübeck law</a> city rights by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Borwin_I,_Lord_of_Mecklenburg" title="Henry Borwin I, Lord of Mecklenburg">Heinrich Borwin</a>, prince of Mecklenburg.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hanseatic_League">Hanseatic League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hanseatic League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg/220px-Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg/330px-Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg/440px-Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="697" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock_Universit%C3%A4t_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/University_of_Rostock" title="University of Rostock">Rostock University</a>, one of the world's oldest universities, founded 1419.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_partition_of_Mecklenburg" class="mw-redirect" title="First partition of Mecklenburg">first partition of Mecklenburg</a> following the death of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Borwin_II,_Lord_of_Mecklenburg" title="Henry Borwin II, Lord of Mecklenburg">Henry Borwin II of Mecklenburg</a> in 1226, Rostock became the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Lordship_of_Rostock" title="Lordship of Rostock">Lordship of Rostock</a>, which survived for almost a century. In 1251, the city became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hanseatic_League" title="Hanseatic League">Hanseatic League</a>. In the 14th century it was a powerful seaport town with 12,000 inhabitants and the biggest city of <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg" title="Mecklenburg">Mecklenburg</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cog_(ship)" title="Cog (ship)">Ships</a> for cruising the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> were constructed in Rostock. The formerly independent fishing village of <a href="/wiki/Warnem%C3%BCnde" title="Warnemünde">Warnemünde</a> at the Baltic Sea became a part of Rostock in 1323, to secure the city's access to the Baltic Sea.</p>
<p>In 1419, one of the earliest universities in Europe, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rostock" title="University of Rostock">University of Rostock</a>, was founded.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="15th_to_18th_century">15th to 18th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 15th to 18th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock_1550-60.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Rostock_1550-60.jpg/220px-Rostock_1550-60.jpg" width="220" height="68" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Rostock_1550-60.jpg/330px-Rostock_1550-60.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Rostock_1550-60.jpg/440px-Rostock_1550-60.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2353" data-file-height="731" /></a>

<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock_1550-60.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

</div>
</div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock_um_1650.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Rostock_um_1650.jpg/220px-Rostock_um_1650.jpg" width="220" height="91" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Rostock_um_1650.jpg/330px-Rostock_um_1650.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Rostock_um_1650.jpg/440px-Rostock_um_1650.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3370" data-file-height="1393" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

</div>
<p>At the end of the 15th century the dukes of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Mecklenburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Duchy of Mecklenburg">Mecklenburg</a> succeeded in enforcing their rule over the town of Rostock, which had until then been only nominally subject to their rule and essentially independent. They took advantage of a riot known as <i>Domfehde</i>, a failed uprising of the <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">impoverished</a> population. Subsequent quarrels with the dukes and persistent plundering led ultimately to a loss of economic and political power.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg/220px-Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg" width="220" height="115" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg/330px-Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg/440px-Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5046" data-file-height="2635" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Plan_von_Rostock_gez_1780-1790_J.M._von_Tarnows.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rostock 1780-90</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1565 there were further clashes with Schwerin that which had far-reaching consequences. Among other things, was the introduction of a beer <a href="/wiki/Excise" title="Excise">excise</a> that favoured the dukes. <a href="/wiki/John_Albert_I,_Duke_of_Mecklenburg" title="John Albert I, Duke of Mecklenburg">John Albert I</a> advanced on the city with 500 horsemen, after Rostock had refused to take the formal oath of allegiance, and had the city wall slighted in order to have a fortress built. Not until the first <a href="/wiki/Rostock_Inheritance_Agreement" title="Rostock Inheritance Agreement">Rostock Inheritance Agreement</a> of 21 September 1573, in which the state princes were guaranteed hereditary rule over the city for centuries, binding Rostock for a long time, and recognizing them as the supreme judicial authority, was the conflict ended. The citizens slighted the fortress the following spring. From 1575 to 1577 the city walls were rebuilt, as was the <i>Lagebusch</i> tower and the <i>Stein</i> Gate in the Dutch Renaissance style. The inscription <i>sit intra te concordia et publica felicitas</i>, which can still be read on the gate, refers directly to the conflict with the Duke. In 1584 it finally came to the Second Rostock Inheritance Agreement, which resulted in a further loss of former tax privileges. At the same time, these inheritance contracts put paid to Rostock's ambition of achieving <a href="/wiki/Imperial_immediacy" title="Imperial immediacy">imperial immediacy</a> as <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbeck" title="Lübeck">Lübeck</a> had done in 1226.</p>
<p>The strategic location of Rostock provoked the envy of its rivals. <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedes</a> occupied the city twice, first during the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a> (1618–48) and again from 1700 to 1721. Later, the <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">French</a>, under <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>, occupied the town for about a decade until 1813. In nearby <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbeck" title="Lübeck">Lübeck</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ratekau" title="Ratekau">Ratekau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gebhard_Leberecht_von_Bl%C3%BCcher" title="Gebhard Leberecht von Blücher">Gebhard Leberecht von Blücher</a>, who was born in Rostock and who was one of few generals to fight on after defeat at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Jena" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Jena">Battle of Jena</a>, surrendered to the French in 1806. This was only after furious street fighting in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_L%C3%BCbeck" title="Battle of Lübeck">Battle of Lübeck</a>, in which he led some of the <a href="/wiki/Cavalry_charge" class="mw-redirect" title="Cavalry charge">cavalry charges</a> himself. By the time of the surrender, the exhausted <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Prussians</a> had neither food nor ammunition.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Rostock.jpg/220px-Rostock.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Rostock.jpg/330px-Rostock.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Rostock.jpg/440px-Rostock.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2592" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Colourful gabled houses of Rostock</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the first half of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a> Rostock regained much of its economic importance, due at first to the wheat trade, then, from the 1850s, to industry, especially to its shipyards. The first propeller-driven steamers in Germany were constructed here.</p>
<p>The city grew in area and population, with new quarters emerging in the south and west of the ancient borders of the city. Two notable developments were added to house the increasing population at around 1900:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Steintor-Vorstadt</i> in the south, stretching from the old city wall to the facilities of the new <i>Lloydbahnhof</i> (Lloyd Railway Station, now <a href="/wiki/Rostock_Hauptbahnhof" title="Rostock Hauptbahnhof">Rostock Hauptbahnhof</a>), was designed as a living quarter and consisted mostly of large single houses, once inhabited by wealthy citizens.</li>
<li><i>Kröpeliner-Tor-Vorstadt</i> in the west, was designed to house the working population as well as to provide smaller and larger industrial facilities, such as the Mahn &amp; Ohlerich's Brewery (now Hanseatische Brauerei Rostock). The main shipyard, <i>Neptun</i>, was nearby at the shore of the river.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 20th century, important <a href="/wiki/Fixed-wing_aircraft" title="Fixed-wing aircraft">aircraft</a> manufacturing facilities were situated in the city, such as the <a href="/wiki/Arado_Flugzeugwerke" title="Arado Flugzeugwerke">Arado Flugzeugwerke</a> in <a href="/wiki/Warnem%C3%BCnde" title="Warnemünde">Warnemünde</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Heinkel" title="Heinkel">Heinkel</a> Works with facilities at various places, including their secondary <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport"><i>Heinkel-Süd</i> facility in Schwechat, Austria</a> — the original Heinkel firm's Rostock facilities being named <i>Heinkel-Nord</i>. It was at their facilities in what used to be named the <i>Rostock</i>-<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marienehe" class="extiw" title="de:Marienehe"><i>Marienehe</i></a> neighborhood (today's <i>Rostock</i>-<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schmarl" class="extiw" title="de:Schmarl"><i>Schmarl</i></a> community, along the west bank of the <a href="/wiki/Unterwarnow" title="Unterwarnow">Unterwarnow</a> estuary) that the world's first <a href="/wiki/Heinkel_He_178" title="Heinkel He 178">airworthy jet plane prototype</a> made its test flights.</p>
<p>In the early 1930s, the Nazi Party began to gain among Rostock's voters, many of whom had suffered economic hardship during the 1920s. In <a href="/wiki/German_federal_election,_July_1932" title="German federal election, July 1932">elections</a> in the summer 1932, when the Nazis achieved their greatest national showing in a free election of 37.3 percent, they polled 40.3 percent in Rostock. A year later, after the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_seizure_of_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Nazi seizure of power">Nazi seizure of power</a> and the suppression of other political parties, the Rostock city council (<i>Stadtrat</i>) was composed entirely of Nazis. In the so-called "Crystal Night" <i><a href="/wiki/Kristallnacht" title="Kristallnacht">Kristallnacht</a></i> of Nov. 10, 1938, the Jewish synagogue in Rostock's Augustenstrsse was destroyed by arson and dozens of Jews were beaten and imprisoned.</p>
<p>Feverish rearmament by the Nazi regime boosted Rostock's industrial importance in the late 1930s, and employment soared at the <a href="/wiki/Heinkel" title="Heinkel">Heinkel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arado_Flugzeugwerke" title="Arado Flugzeugwerke">Arado</a> factories, and at the Neptunwerft shipyard. The city's population grew from 100,000 in 1935 to 121,192 in 1939.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Rostock was subjected to repeated and increasingly heavy bombing attacks, especially by the British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>. Targets included the Heinkel and Arado plants and the shipyard, but churches and other historic structures in the city center also were heavily damaged, among them the 14th century Nikolaikirche (St. Nicholas Church) and Jakobikirche (St. Jacob's Church). The ruins of the latter were torn down in 1960.</p>
<p>After the war, the city—now in the <a href="/wiki/German_Democratic_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="German Democratic Republic">German Democratic Republic</a>—became East Germany's largest seaport, and the now state-owned shipyards in the district of <a href="/wiki/Warnem%C3%BCnde" title="Warnemünde">Warnemünde</a> were expanded. Its population, boosted in part by resettled refugees from former German territories in the east, increased in the GDR years to a peak of 260,000. Following the <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">reunification</a> of Germany in 1990, Rostock lost its privileged position as the No. 1 port of the <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">GDR</a>, and the city's population declined to about 200,000. However, after 2006, the population increased again. Today, Rostock and Warnemünde are significant tourist destinations on the Baltic Sea.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rostock-Lichtenhagen_riots" title="Rostock-Lichtenhagen riots">Rostock-Lichtenhagen riots</a> occurred from August 22 to August 24, 1992 when violent xenophobic riots took place in the Lichtenhagen district of Rostock, Germany; these were the worst mob attacks against migrants in postwar Germany. Even though stones and petrol bombs were thrown at an apartment block where asylum seekers lived, no one was killed. At the height of the riots, several hundred militant right-wing extremists were involved, and about 3,000 neighbourhood onlookers stood by, applauding them.</p>

<p>Rostock has had three different coats of arms, known as the <i>Signum</i>, the <i>Secretum</i> and the <i>Sigillum</i>. The Signum, which can be traced back to 1367, was developed last and is to this day the coat of arms of the city.</p>
<p>The Signum depicts a golden <a href="/wiki/Griffin" title="Griffin">griffin</a> on a blue background, with bars of silver and red, the colours of the <a href="/wiki/Hanseatic_League" title="Hanseatic League">Hanseatic League</a>, below. It can be seen not only on flags and houses, and at bus stops, but also on bridges, gullies, fences, ships and restaurants.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the 13th century, the governing body of the city has been the city council (<i>Rat</i>), first consisting of ten, later of 24 <a href="/wiki/Alderman" title="Alderman">aldermen</a> (<i>Ratsherren</i>). The chairman of the city council was the city <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a>. In the 19th century there were even three mayors. Since 1925, the head of the city has borne the title of <a href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Mayor">Lord Mayor</a>. Having been elected for centuries by the city council, he is now elected directly by the citizens of Rostock, following a reform in 2002.</p>

<p>The city parliament is presided by the <i>Präsident der Bürgerschaft</i>. He heads and prepares the sessions and, together with the Lord Mayor, represents the city.</p>
<p>Roland Methling (Independent), was elected Lord Mayor of Rostock in the first round by 58.2% of the voters on 27 February 2005.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regiopolis_Rostock">Regiopolis Rostock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Regiopolis Rostock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rostock is the first city region that defines itself not only as a city in its boundaries, but as a <a href="/wiki/Regiopolis" title="Regiopolis">regiopolis</a>, with a supra-regional sphere of influence. A regiopolis can be compared to a <a href="/wiki/Metropolis" title="Metropolis">metropolis</a>, but on a smaller scale. This is a sign for the inter-regional cooperation and economic dynamics that can be found in the Rostock area. A taskforce with different actors such as the hanseatic city of Rostock, the administrative <a href="/wiki/Rostock_(district)" title="Rostock (district)">district of Rostock</a>, the <i>Regional Planning Association Middle Mecklenburg/Rostock</i> and the local business organisations are working on the promotion and advancement of the concept.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rostock is located nearly centrally on <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg-Vorpommern" title="Mecklenburg-Vorpommern">Mecklenburg-Vorpommern</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> coast. The city is crossed by the <a href="/wiki/Warnow" title="Warnow">Warnow</a>.</p>
<p>The seaside part of Rostock, Rostock-<a href="/wiki/Warnem%C3%BCnde" title="Warnemünde">Warnemünde</a>, is about 16&#160;km (10&#160;mi) to the north of the historic city centre. The west and the southeast are the most densely populated parts of town. The overseas port is to the east of Rostock. Rostock stretches 21.6&#160;km (13.4&#160;mi) from the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> to the south and 19.4&#160;km (12.1&#160;mi) from east to west.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rostock has an <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic climate</a> typical of the South Baltic Sea region, and the climate has more in common with that found in southern <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Sealand,_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="Sealand, Denmark">Sealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> than the typical German oceanic climate that usually has milder winter days.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rostock">Rostock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Rostock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>One of the most picturesque places in Rostock is the <i>Neuer Markt</i> (New Market Square), with the Town Hall - that was originally built in the 13th century in <a href="/wiki/Brick_Gothic" title="Brick Gothic">Brick Gothic</a> style, but extensively transformed in the 18th century, with the addition of a <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a> façade and a banqueting hall. The square also preserved six original, carefully restored gable houses from the 15th and 16th centuries. The other historical houses in Hanseatic style that once bordered the square were destroyed in an Allied air-raid in 1942, and rebuilt in a simplified manner.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 15th-century <i>Kerkhofhaus</i> (at Große Wasserstraße, behind the Town Hall) is considered the best preserved brick Gothic house in Rostock.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s_Church,_Rostock" title="St. Mary's Church, Rostock">St. Mary's Church</a> <i>Marienkirche</i>, on Ziegenmarkt, is an imposing <a href="/wiki/Brick_Gothic" title="Brick Gothic">Brick Gothic</a> church. Built in the 13th century, it was enlarged and modified at the end of the 14th century into the present cross-shaped basilica. The huge tower was not completed until the end of the 18th century. Inside there is an <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_clock" title="Astronomical clock">astronomical clock</a> built in 1472 by Hans Düringer.</p>

<p>The main pedestrian precinct is <i>Kröpeliner Straße</i>, that runs east from the Neuer Markt to the 14th-century <i>Kröpeliner Tor</i>, a former town gate. The main buildings of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rostock" title="University of Rostock">Rostock University</a> lie at Universitätsplatz, near the middle of the street, in front of the lively fountain of <i>zest for life (Brunnen der Lebensfreude)</i>.</p>
<p>The <i>Kloster St Katharinen</i> (Convent of St. Catherine), an old <a href="/wiki/Franciscan" class="mw-redirect" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> founded in 1243, and extended several times during the 14th and 15th centuries. Now used as the seat of the Academy of Music and Theatre (HMT-Rostock).</p>
<p>The Brick Gothic <i>Nikolaikirche</i> (St. Nicholas Church), which is the oldest church in Rostock, built in mid-13th century. Heavily damaged during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and subsequently restored, the building is now used as an exhibition centre and concert hall, due to its outstanding acoustics.</p>
<p>Some parts of the medieval <a href="/wiki/City_wall" class="mw-redirect" title="City wall">city wall</a>, with four <a href="/wiki/City_gate" title="City gate">city gates</a>, have survived to the present day.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Warnem%C3%BCnde" title="Warnemünde">Warnemünde</a> is the seaside part of Rostock and a major attraction of the city. Locals and tourists alike enjoy the maritime flair of old houses, a large beach, a <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse_Warnem%C3%BCnde" class="mw-redirect" title="Lighthouse Warnemünde">lighthouse</a> and the old fisherman port.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg/220px-Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg" width="220" height="81" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg/330px-Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg/440px-Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4998" data-file-height="1845" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostock_Botanischer_Garten_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Historical <a href="/wiki/Botanischer_Garten_Universit%C3%A4t_Rostock" title="Botanischer Garten Universität Rostock">Botanical Garden of Rostock University</a>, greenhouse</div>

<p>Rostock is home to one of the oldest universities in the world. Founded in 1419, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rostock" title="University of Rostock">University of Rostock</a> is the third oldest university in Germany in continuous operation, and one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_oldest_universities_in_continuous_operation" title="List of oldest universities in continuous operation">oldest universities of the world</a>. It also maintains a <a href="/wiki/Botanical_garden" title="Botanical garden">botanical garden</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Botanischer_Garten_Universit%C3%A4t_Rostock" title="Botanischer Garten Universität Rostock">Botanischer Garten Universität Rostock</a>.</p>
<p>The Academy of Music and Theatre (<i>Hochschule für Musik und Theater</i>) offers graduate degrees in artistic fields. Founded in 1994, the institution combined <i>Ernst Busch</i>, the former drama school, and the outpost school of the <a href="/wiki/Hochschule_f%C3%BCr_Musik_Hanns_Eisler" class="mw-redirect" title="Hochschule für Musik Hanns Eisler">Hanns Eisler Music School Berlin</a>. Today, the combined school is a member of the Association of Baltic Academies of Music (ABAM), a union of 17 music conservatories at the Baltic Sea and Israel. Unique in Europe is the postgraduate degree in piano duo performance. The school possesses a large opera stage (Katharinensaal) and two chamber music halls. There are concerts every day throughout the year.</p>
<p>Rostock also hosts the <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Institute_for_Demographic_Research" title="Max Planck Institute for Demographic Research">Max Planck Institute for Demographic Research</a> and the Leibniz Institute for Catalysis, as well as two branches of Fraunhofer Institutes, one for Computer Graphics and one for Large Structures in Production Technology.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city is home to the annual <a href="/wiki/Hanse_Sail" title="Hanse Sail">Hanse Sail</a> festival, during which many large sailing ships and museum vessels are brought out to sea, drawing over 1.5 million visitors.</p>
<p>An annual jazz festival, <i>Ostsee-Jazz</i> ("Baltic Sea Jazz"), takes place in June.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cinemas">Cinemas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Cinemas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.liwu.de/">Lichtspieltheater Wundervoll</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Art_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Art house">art house</a> cinema of Rostock. It opened in 1993 and offers a daily programme in two venues, the Metropol and the Frieda 23 with three cinemas. At Frieda 23 is the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://ifnm.de/blog/">Institut für neue Medien</a> (IFNM), Rostock's Institute for New Media, which includes a media workshop. Both Liwu and IFNM are active members of the <i>Landesverband Filmkommunikation Mecklenburg-Vorpommern</i>. Special screenings for schools, educational programmes and special programmes are offered as well. It is the central venue for Rostock's Film Festival, the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.fish-rostock.de/">Festival im Stadthafen</a> (FISH), the German Federal Festival for Young German Film.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rostock can be reached by motorway (<i><a href="/wiki/Autobahn" title="Autobahn">Autobahn</a></i>) <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_1" title="Bundesautobahn 1">A 1</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> via <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbeck" title="Lübeck">Lübeck</a> on <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_20" title="Bundesautobahn 20">A 20</a> and by <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_19" title="Bundesautobahn 19">A 19</a> from <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and A 20 from <a href="/wiki/Stettin" class="mw-redirect" title="Stettin">Stettin</a> in Poland.</p>

<p><i><a href="/wiki/Rostock_Hauptbahnhof" title="Rostock Hauptbahnhof">Rostock Hauptbahnhof</a></i> offers fast rail connections to <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and from there to almost any other European city.</p>
<p>Within the city the <a href="/wiki/Trams_in_Rostock" title="Trams in Rostock">Rostock tramway network</a> is available, along with a wide network of buses and ferries. The first privately financed <a href="/wiki/Warnow_Tunnel" title="Warnow Tunnel">tunnel</a> in Germany crosses the <a href="/wiki/Warnow" title="Warnow">Warnow</a> river and thus connects the eastern part of Rostock with the western part.</p>
<h3><span id="Ferry/ship"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Ferry.2Fship">Ferry/ship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Ferry/ship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rostock is Germany's largest Baltic port. Rostock is also home to a large <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a> <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a>. It is a main base for ferry operators <a href="/wiki/Scandlines" title="Scandlines">Scandlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/TT-Line" class="mw-redirect" title="TT-Line">TT-Line</a>, which both connect Rostock with major Scandinavian destinations. Furthermore, Rostock receives the highest number of cruise tourists in Germany every year.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plane">Plane</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rostock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Plane">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rostock%E2%80%93Laage_Airport" title="Rostock–Laage Airport">Rostock–Laage Airport</a> offers connections to major German and international destinations; regular flights to e.g. <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> are offered. The nearest larger international airports are in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>. There are also a number of airfields for smaller aircraft, such as Purkshof.</p>
