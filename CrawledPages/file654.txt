<p>The <b>Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health</b> (formerly <b>Harvard School of Public Health</b>) is the public health <a href="/wiki/Graduate_school" title="Graduate school">graduate school</a> of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Longwood_Medical_and_Academic_Area" title="Longwood Medical and Academic Area">Longwood Medical Area</a> of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> adjacent <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Medical_School" title="Harvard Medical School">Harvard Medical School</a>. The Chan School is considered a preeminent school of <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The school grew out of the Harvard-<a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">MIT</a> School for Health Officers,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the nation's first graduate training program in <a href="/wiki/Population_health" title="Population health">population health</a>, which was founded in 1913 and became the Harvard School of Public Health in 1922. <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Ann_Williams" title="Michelle Ann Williams">Michelle Williams</a>, faculty and chair of the school's Department of Epidemiology, became the school's dean in July 2016, following the departure of former dean <a href="/wiki/Julio_Frenk" title="Julio Frenk">Julio Frenk</a> and interim service of acting dean David Hunter.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She then became the first African American individual to head a Harvard faculty.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2015, the school is ranked second in the nation (after the <a href="/wiki/Johns_Hopkins_Bloomberg_School_of_Public_Health" title="Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health">Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health</a> and tied with <a href="/wiki/UNC_Gillings_School_of_Global_Public_Health" title="UNC Gillings School of Global Public Health">UNC Gillings School of Global Public Health</a>) in the <i><a href="/wiki/U.S._News_%26_World_Report" title="U.S. News &amp; World Report">U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>U.S. News</i> consistently ranks Harvard #1 in Health Policy and Management.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_T.H._Chan_School_of_Public_Health&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The School traces its origins to the Harvard-MIT School for Health Officers, founded in 1913; Harvard calls it "the nation's first graduate training program in public health." In 1922, the School for Health Officers became the Harvard School of Public Health, and in 1946 it was split off from the medical school and became a separate faculty of Harvard University.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It was renamed the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health in 2014 after receiving a $350 million donation, the largest gift in Harvard's history at the time, from the Morningside Foundation.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The Morningside Foundation is headed by Harvard School of Public Health alumnus <a href="/wiki/List_of_Hong_Kong_people_by_net_worth" title="List of Hong Kong people by net worth">Dr. Gerald Chan</a>, SM '75, SD '79, the son of T.H. Chan<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>, and younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Chan" title="Ronnie Chan">Ronnie Chan</a> who runs <a href="/wiki/Hang_Lung_Group" title="Hang Lung Group">Hang Lung Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Curriculum">Curriculum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_T.H._Chan_School_of_Public_Health&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Curriculum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Public_Health" class="mw-redirect" title="Master of Public Health">Master of Public Health Program (MPH)</a> offers eight degree fields of study:</p>
<ul>
<li>Clinical Effectiveness (CLE)</li>
<li>Epidemiology (EPI)</li>
<li>Health and Social Behavior (HSB)</li>
<li>Health Policy (HP)</li>
<li>Health Management (HM)</li>
<li>Global Health (GH)</li>
<li>Occupational and Environmental Health (OEH)</li>
<li>Quantitative Methods (QM)<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Degree programs offered by specific departments:</p>
<ul>

<li>Genetics and Complex Diseases: PhD</li>
<li>Health Policy and Management: ScM, ScD, PhD</li>
<li>Health Care Management: ScM</li>

<li>Global Health and Population (since 2009, formerly Population and International Health) (GHP):</li>

<li>Population Health Sciences (Interdisciplinary PhD within departments of EH, EPI, GHP, NUT, and SBS)</li>
</ul>
<p>The Harvard Doctor of Public Health (DrPH) was launched in 2014 as a multidisciplinary degree providing advanced education in public health along with mastery of skills in management, leadership, communications, and innovation thinking. The program is a cohort-based program emphasizing small-group learning and collaboration. The program is designed for three years – two years at Harvard, plus one year in a field-based doctoral project – although some students may take up to four years to complete the program. Academic training in the DrPH covers the biological, social, and economic foundations of public health, as well as essential statistical, quantitative, and methodological skills in the first year, an individualized course of study in your second year, and a field-based, capstone project called the DELTA (Doctoral Engagement in Leadership and Translation for Action) in the final year(s) of the program.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>PhD programs are offered under the aegis of the Harvard University Graduate School of Arts and Sciences</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_projects">Research projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_T.H._Chan_School_of_Public_Health&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Research projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Nurses%27_Health_Study" title="Nurses' Health Study">Nurses Health Study and Nurses Health Study II</a>, which have followed the health of over 100,000 nurses from 1976 to the present; its results have been used in hundreds of published papers.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Health Professionals Followup Study, a similar study of over fifty thousand male health professionals seeking to connect diet, exercise, smoking, and medications taken to frequency of cancer and cardiovascular disease.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li>The International Health Systems Program, which has provided training or technical assistance to projects in 21 countries, and conducts health policy research<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Program in Health Care Financing, which studies the economics of <a href="/wiki/Publicly-funded_health_care" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicly-funded health care">national health care programs</a>; evaluates the health care programs of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, and other countries; studies the effects of bringing <a href="/wiki/HMO" class="mw-redirect" title="HMO">HMO</a>-like hospital reimbursement practices to developing countries; and applies <a href="/wiki/Hedonimetrics" class="mw-redirect" title="Hedonimetrics">hedonimetrics</a> to health care.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.hpcrresearch.org/">Program on Humanitarian Policy and Conflict Research (HPCR)</a>, which studies public health and <a href="/wiki/International_humanitarian_law" title="International humanitarian law">humanitarian law</a> and policy in the context of conflict-torn regions like the <a href="/wiki/Gaza_Strip" title="Gaza Strip">Gaza Strip</a> and transnational issues like <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Lung Cancer S.O.S. study, examining the risk factors for and prognosis of <a href="/wiki/Lung_cancer" title="Lung cancer">lung cancer</a> in terms of genetics and environment.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
