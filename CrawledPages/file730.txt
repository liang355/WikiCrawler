<p><b>PayScale, Inc.</b> or <b>payscale.com</b> is an online <a href="/wiki/Salary" title="Salary">salary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Employee_benefit" class="mw-redirect" title="Employee benefit">benefits</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wage" title="Wage">compensation information</a> company, which launched its service on January 1, 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was founded by Joe Giordano, a former <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drugstore.com" title="Drugstore.com">drugstore.com</a> manager, and John Gaffney. On April 24, 2014 <a href="/wiki/Warburg_Pincus" title="Warburg Pincus">Warburg Pincus</a> acquired PayScale in a deal worth up to $100m.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>PayScale was developed to help people obtain accurate real-time information on job market compensation. The service works via the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> by enabling individual employees to submit their job profile and salary data, which is then compared to others. The company claims that it is able to use the volume of information statistically to determine accurate real-time salary information.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company generates revenue by selling aggregate data and <a href="/wiki/Web_service" title="Web service">web services</a> to employers, to aid in determining correct market rates for hiring, benchmarking and budgeting, and by targeted advertising to employees that visit its web site.</p>
<p>The company has published rankings of U.S. universities based on "return on investment," or the purported difference between student expenditures for a university degree and the money they earn from it.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> These rankings are dramatically different from better-known rankings published by <i>U.S. News and World Report</i>, <i>Forbes</i>, <i>The Economist</i>, and other mainstream publications (for instance, PayScale places <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> 93rd among American universities and <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_University" title="Georgetown University">Georgetown University</a> 132nd). <i>The Chronicle of Higher Education</i> has criticized PayScale for using misleading sample sizes and for omitting all alumni of universities who go on to grad school from its count.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
