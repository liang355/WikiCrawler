<p><span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Wei%C3%9Fenburg-Gunzenhausen.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9a/De-Wei%C3%9Fenburg-Gunzenhausen.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Weißenburg-Gunzenhausen.ogg"><b>Weißenburg-Gunzenhausen</b></a></span>&#160;<small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:De-Wei%C3%9Fenburg-Gunzenhausen.ogg" title="File:De-Weißenburg-Gunzenhausen.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in the west of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> with a population of 95,000. Neighbouring districts are (from the north clockwise) <a href="/wiki/Ansbach_(district)" title="Ansbach (district)">Ansbach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roth_(district)" title="Roth (district)">Roth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eichst%C3%A4tt_(district)" title="Eichstätt (district)">Eichstätt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Donau-Ries" title="Donau-Ries">Donau-Ries</a>. It is located in the south of <a href="/wiki/Middle_Franconia" title="Middle Franconia">Middle Franconia</a>, 50 kilometres south of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>. Largest city and the administrative center is <a href="/wiki/Wei%C3%9Fenburg_in_Bayern" title="Weißenburg in Bayern">Weißenburg in Bayern</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wei%C3%9Fenburg-Gunzenhausen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district is located on the <a href="/wiki/Hahnenkamm_(Altm%C3%BChl_valley)" title="Hahnenkamm (Altmühl valley)">Hahnenkamm</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/Franconian_Alb" class="mw-redirect" title="Franconian Alb">Franconian Alb</a> in the North of the <a href="/wiki/Altm%C3%BChltal" title="Altmühltal">Altmühltal</a>. In the north there are several lakes of the <a href="/wiki/Franconian_Lake_District" title="Franconian Lake District">Franconian Lake District</a>. The highest point of the district is the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCrrenberg" title="Dürrenberg">Dürrenberg</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Altm%C3%BChl" title="Altmühl">Altmühl</a> flows through the district. From here comes the Solnhofen limestone. Among its nature reserves are the <a href="/wiki/Brombachmoor" title="Brombachmoor">Brombachmoor</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wei%C3%9Fenburg-Gunzenhausen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district was formed in 1972 by a merger of the districts of <a href="/wiki/Gunzenhausen" title="Gunzenhausen">Gunzenhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wei%C3%9Fenburg_(Bavaria)" class="mw-redirect" title="Weißenburg (Bavaria)">Weißenburg</a>, and the previously independent <a href="/wiki/List_of_German_urban_districts" class="mw-redirect" title="List of German urban districts">urban district</a> of Weißenburg.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wei%C3%9Fenburg-Gunzenhausen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table>
<tr valign="top">
<td><a href="/wiki/File:Wappen_Landkreis_Weissenburg-Gunzenhausen.svg" class="image" title="Coat of arms"><img alt="Coat of arms" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Wappen_Landkreis_Weissenburg-Gunzenhausen.svg/110px-Wappen_Landkreis_Weissenburg-Gunzenhausen.svg.png" width="110" height="125" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Wappen_Landkreis_Weissenburg-Gunzenhausen.svg/165px-Wappen_Landkreis_Weissenburg-Gunzenhausen.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Wappen_Landkreis_Weissenburg-Gunzenhausen.svg/220px-Wappen_Landkreis_Weissenburg-Gunzenhausen.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="530" data-file-height="600" /></a></td>
<td>The coat of arms of the district is divided into three fields: left, right and bottom. The bottom of the coat of arms (silver <a href="/wiki/Kettle_hat" title="Kettle hat">kettle hats</a> on blue) derives from the Counts of <a href="/wiki/Pappenheim" title="Pappenheim">Pappenheim</a>, and was taken from the coat of arms of the former district Weißenburg. The half eagle (black on yellow) in the right stands for the city of Weißenburg, and the red-yellow horizontal bars in the left are taken from the coat of arms of the former district Gunzenhausen.</td>
</tr>
</table>
