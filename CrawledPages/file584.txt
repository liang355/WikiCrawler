			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of Presidents of the United States by education</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>Most <a href="/wiki/Presidents_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidents of the United States">Presidents of the United States</a> received a <a href="/wiki/College_education" class="mw-redirect" title="College education">college education</a>, even most of the earliest. Of the first seven Presidents, five were college graduates. College degrees have set the Presidents apart from the general population, and Presidents have held such a degree even when this was quite rare indeed, as well as unnecessary, for practicing most occupations, including <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">law</a>. Of the forty-five individuals to have been the President, twenty-four of them graduated from a private undergraduate college, nine graduated from a public undergraduate college, and twelve held no degree. Every President since 1953 has had a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a>, reflecting the increasing importance of <a href="/wiki/Higher_education_in_the_United_States" title="Higher education in the United States">higher education in the United States</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_by_institutions">List by institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Presidents_of_the_United_States_by_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: List by institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Undergraduate">Undergraduate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Presidents_of_the_United_States_by_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Undergraduate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some Presidents attended more than one institution. George Washington never attended college, though <a href="/wiki/The_College_of_William_%26_Mary" class="mw-redirect" title="The College of William &amp; Mary">The College of William &amp; Mary</a> did issue him a surveyor's certificate. Only three Presidents attended foreign colleges at the undergraduate level: <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> at the <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">London School of Economics</a> and John Quincy Adams at <a href="/wiki/Leiden_University" title="Leiden University">Leiden University</a> (with both transferring to <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> who was a <a href="/wiki/Rhodes_Scholar" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhodes Scholar">Rhodes Scholar</a> at <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxford University">Oxford University</a>. Three Presidents have attended the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Service_academies" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Service academies">United States Service academies</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ulysses_S._Grant" title="Ulysses S. Grant">Ulysses S. Grant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> graduated from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Military_Academy" title="United States Military Academy">United States Military Academy</a> at <a href="/wiki/West_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="West Point">West Point</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a> graduated from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Naval_Academy" title="United States Naval Academy">United States Naval Academy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Annapolis,_Maryland" title="Annapolis, Maryland">Annapolis, Maryland</a>. No Presidents have graduated from the much newer <a href="/wiki/U.S._Air_Force_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Air Force Academy">U.S. Air Force Academy</a>. Eisenhower also graduated from the Command and General Staff College, Army Industrial College and Army War College. These were not degree granting institutions when Eisenhower attended, but were part of his professional education as a career soldier.</p>

<p>Several Presidents who were lawyers did not attend law school, but became lawyers after <a href="/wiki/Reading_law" title="Reading law">independent study</a>. Some had attended college before beginning their legal studies, and several studied law without first having attended college. It was customary to study under established lawyers.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Presidents who were lawyers but did not attend law school include: <a href="/wiki/John_Adams" title="John Adams">John Adams</a>; <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a>; <a href="/wiki/James_Madison" title="James Madison">James Madison</a>; <a href="/wiki/James_Monroe" title="James Monroe">James Monroe</a>; <a href="/wiki/John_Quincy_Adams" title="John Quincy Adams">John Quincy Adams</a>; <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson" title="Andrew Jackson">Andrew Jackson</a>; <a href="/wiki/Martin_Van_Buren" title="Martin Van Buren">Martin Van Buren</a>; <a href="/wiki/John_Tyler" title="John Tyler">John Tyler</a>; <a href="/wiki/James_K._Polk" title="James K. Polk">James K. Polk</a>; <a href="/wiki/Millard_Fillmore" title="Millard Fillmore">Millard Fillmore</a>; <a href="/wiki/James_Buchanan" title="James Buchanan">James Buchanan</a>; <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>; <a href="/wiki/James_A._Garfield" title="James A. Garfield">James A. Garfield</a>; <a href="/wiki/Grover_Cleveland" title="Grover Cleveland">Grover Cleveland</a>; <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Harrison" title="Benjamin Harrison">Benjamin Harrison</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Coolidge" title="Calvin Coolidge">Calvin Coolidge</a>.</p>
<p>Presidents who were admitted to the bar after a combination of law school and independent study include; <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Pierce" title="Franklin Pierce">Franklin Pierce</a>; <a href="/wiki/Chester_A._Arthur" title="Chester A. Arthur">Chester A. Arthur</a>; <a href="/wiki/William_McKinley" title="William McKinley">William McKinley</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">Woodrow Wilson</a>.</p>
