			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Ten Thousand Men of Harvard</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>"Ten Thousand Men of Harvard"</b> is the most-frequently performed of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>'s numerous <a href="/wiki/Fight_songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Fight songs">fight songs</a>. It was written by A. Putnam, class of 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Harvard College <a href="/wiki/First_year" class="mw-redirect" title="First year">freshmen</a> become acquainted with this song early in their college careers, as the Harvard <a href="/wiki/Marching_band" title="Marching band">marching band</a> traditionally marches through <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a> and performs this song one night early each fall. It is also among the songs performed by the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Glee_Club" title="Harvard Glee Club">Harvard Glee Club</a> at its annual Football Concerts with Yale.</p>
<p>The song is the fight song of choice for the Harvard Football team. Each incoming class of players is required to memorize and perform the song to the upperclassmen. The football team sings the song in unison after each win, and following victory in <a href="/wiki/Harvard-Yale_football_games_(The_Game)" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard-Yale football games (The Game)">The Game</a> the song is sung on the 50-yard line.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University_Band" title="Harvard University Band">Harvard University Band</a> generally plays the song after every score by the Harvard football team, after every goal scored by the Harvard men's ice hockey team, as well as at the beginning and end of every period of a Harvard men's ice hockey game.</p>
<p>The "Latin" verse, written in 1953<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and subsequently adopted by the Harvard Marching Band, is <a href="/wiki/Dog_Latin" title="Dog Latin">dog Latin</a> (a pun), as "<a href="/wiki/Illegitimi_non_carborundum" title="Illegitimi non carborundum">illegitimum non carborundum</a>" loosely means, "don't let the bastards grind you down."</p>
<p>As typically performed by the Harvard University Band, the second and third verses are, respectively, another dog Latin verse with lewd lyrics (always covered up by loud drumming), and just the syllable "la" over and over again.</p>
