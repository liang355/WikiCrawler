<p>The <b>President and Fellows of Harvard College</b> (also called the <b>Harvard Corporation</b>) is the smaller of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>'s two governing boards, the other being its <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Board_of_Overseers" title="Harvard Board of Overseers">Board of Overseers</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=President_and_Fellows_of_Harvard_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1650, at the request of <a href="/wiki/President_of_Harvard_University" title="President of Harvard University">Harvard President</a> <a href="/wiki/Henry_Dunster" title="Henry Dunster">Henry Dunster</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_General_Court" title="Massachusetts General Court">Great and General Court of Massachusetts</a> issued the body's charter, making it now the oldest <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporation</a> in the Americas; the subsequent Constitution of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts confirmed that, despite the change in government, the corporation would continue to "have, hold, use, exercise and enjoy" its property and legal privileges<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>. Although the institution it governs has grown into Harvard University (of which Harvard College is one of several components), the corporation's formal title remains <i>the President and Fellows of Harvard College</i>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg/350px-FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg" width="350" height="208" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg/525px-FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg/700px-FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5104" data-file-height="3026" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FiveHarvardPresidents.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Presidents <a href="/wiki/Josiah_Quincy_III" title="Josiah Quincy III">Josiah Quincy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Everett" title="Edward Everett">Edward Everett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jared_Sparks" title="Jared Sparks">Jared Sparks</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Walker_(Harvard)" title="James Walker (Harvard)">James Walker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Conway_Felton" title="Cornelius Conway Felton">Cornelius Conway Felton</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The corporation was probably originally intended to be a body of the school's resident instructors, similar to the <a href="/wiki/Fellow#Oxford.2C_Cambridge_and_Dublin" title="Fellow">fellows</a> of an <a href="/wiki/Oxbridge" title="Oxbridge">Oxbridge</a> college. However, it early fell into the now-familiar American model of a governing board—an outside body whose members are not involved in the institution's daily life, which meets periodically to consult with the day-to-day head, the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Harvard_University" title="President of Harvard University">president</a> (whom it appoints). The Corporation is self-perpetuating, appointing new members to fill its own vacancies as they arise.</p>
<p>In recent years, the Corporation has comprised six fellows in addition to the president, but in December 2010 it announced that, as a result of a year-long governance review, its "composition, structure, and practices" would be greatly altered: the number of fellows would increase from 7 to 13, with prescribed terms of service, and several new committees would endeavor to improve the group's integration with the activities of the University as a whole, especially its long-term planning.<sup id="cite_ref-gazette_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
