<p><b>Offenbach am Main</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈɔfn̩bax ʔam ˈmaɪn]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Offenbach_(Main).ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c8/De-Offenbach_%28Main%29.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Offenbach (Main).ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, located on the left bank of the river <a href="/wiki/Main_(river)" title="Main (river)">Main</a> and part of the <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Rhein-Main_Regional_Authority" title="Frankfurt Rhein-Main Regional Authority">Frankfurt Rhein-Main urban area</a>. Offenbach has a population of 126,934.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 20th century the city's economy was dominated by the machine-building and leather industries, and it was also a major centre of the typography and design industries. Other important industries are the automobile and pharmaceutical industries. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> the city suffered heavily from bombing by the Allied Forces. More than half of the city's population have a non-German background, with <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turks</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavs" title="Yugoslavs">Yugoslavs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Poles</a> as major groups.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach_am_Main&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subdivisions">Subdivisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach_am_Main&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subdivisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The inner town of Offenbach is quite large and has only a few suburbs. In the east the three incorporated: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach-B%C3%BCrgel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Offenbach-Bürgel (page does not exist)">Bürgel</a> (incorporated 1908), <a href="/wiki/Offenbach-Bieber" title="Offenbach-Bieber">Bieber</a> (incorporated April 1, 1938), and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach-Rumpenheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Offenbach-Rumpenheim (page does not exist)">Rumpenheim</a> (incorporated April 4, 1942). In the south are the newer suburbs <a href="/wiki/Offenbach-Lauterborn" title="Offenbach-Lauterborn">Lauterborn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Offenbach-Rosenh%C3%B6he" title="Offenbach-Rosenhöhe">Rosenhöhe</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tempelsee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tempelsee (page does not exist)">Tempelsee</a>, the office town <a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach-Kaiserlei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Offenbach-Kaiserlei (page does not exist)">Kaiserlei</a> and the industrial area <a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach-Waldheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Offenbach-Waldheim (page does not exist)">Waldheim</a>. The newest suburb is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach-Mathildenviertel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Offenbach-Mathildenviertel (page does not exist)">Mathildenviertel</a> in the center near to the Main river.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>The first documented reference to a suburb of Offenbach appears in 770.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In a document of the Holy Roman <a href="/wiki/Otto_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Otto II, Holy Roman Emperor">Emperor Otto II</a> dating to 977 exists the first mention of the place of Offenbach.<sup id="cite_ref-factsfigures_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factsfigures-5">[5]</a></sup> During the Middle Ages Offenbach passed through many hands. Only in 1486 could the Count Ludwig of <a href="/wiki/County_of_Isenburg" title="County of Isenburg">Isenburg</a> finally take control of city for his family, and 1556 Count Reinhard of Isenburg relocated his Residence to Offenbach, building a palace, the Isenburger Schloß (Isenburg Palace), which was completed in 1559. It was destroyed by fire in 1564 and rebuilt in 1578.</p>
<p>In 1635 Offenbach given to the <a href="/wiki/Landgraviate_of_Hesse-Darmstadt" title="Landgraviate of Hesse-Darmstadt">Landgraves of Hesse-Darmstadt</a> but it was returned to the Isenburg-Birstein Count (later Prince) in 1642 and remained in that Principality until 1815 when the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a> gave the city to the Austrian Emperor, <a href="/wiki/Francis_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor">Francis I</a>. A year later it was given to the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Hesse" title="Grand Duchy of Hesse">Grand Duchy of Hesse-Darmstadt</a>.</p>
<p>Always very close to the city centre of Frankfurt, Offenbach was a popular location for business. The town has its own trade fair, and many companies have opened facilities here because there are fewer restrictions and no closed businesses. French Protestants (<a href="/wiki/Huguenot" class="mw-redirect" title="Huguenot">Huguenots</a>) came in the 17th century and settled in Offenbach and contributed to making Offenbach a prosperous city, e.g., bringing knowledge of tobacco with them and turning Offenbach into a centre for rolling cigars. The town was more cosmopolitan than Frankfurt; famous people such as <a href="/wiki/Goethe" class="mw-redirect" title="Goethe">Goethe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mozart" class="mw-redirect" title="Mozart">Mozart</a> visited it several times.</p>
<p>The Rumpenheim Palace and its park were a popular destination for monarchs in the 19th century. The city was thereafter ruled by <a href="/wiki/Rulers_of_Hesse" class="mw-redirect" title="Rulers of Hesse">Grand Dukes of Hesse and by Rhine</a> until the monarchy was abolished in 1918. Offenbach became the center of the traditional design with figures such as the architect <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Eberhardt" title="Hugo Eberhardt">Hugo Eberhardt</a>, the typographer <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Koch" title="Rudolf Koch">Rudolf Koch</a>, the bookbinder and designer Ignatz Wiemeler and <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Engel" title="Ernst Engel">Ernst Engel</a> and the painter <a href="/wiki/Karl_Friedrich_Lippmann" title="Karl Friedrich Lippmann">Karl Friedrich Lippmann</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> a third of the city was destroyed by Allied bombing, which claimed 467 lives. With the new district <a href="/wiki/Offenbach-Lauterborn" title="Offenbach-Lauterborn">Lauterborn</a> the city was expanded to the south in the 1960s. On the border with Frankfurt, the office district Kaiserlei was built. Offenbach is a so-called ´Sozialer Brennpunkt`(= multiple social problems area) because of unemployment, poverty, gang related crime and migration.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach_am_Main&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Offenbach has a large non-German population. In 2016, foreign nationals made up 37% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The largest communities are, in that order, from Turkey, Greece, Romania, Poland and Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> According to census data Offenbach and <a href="/wiki/Duisburg" title="Duisburg">Duisburg</a> had the highest share of muslim migrants of all German districts in 2011. <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bev%C3%B6lkerungsentwicklung_in_Offenbach.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Bev%C3%B6lkerungsentwicklung_in_Offenbach.png/220px-Bev%C3%B6lkerungsentwicklung_in_Offenbach.png" width="220" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Bev%C3%B6lkerungsentwicklung_in_Offenbach.png/330px-Bev%C3%B6lkerungsentwicklung_in_Offenbach.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Bev%C3%B6lkerungsentwicklung_in_Offenbach.png/440px-Bev%C3%B6lkerungsentwicklung_in_Offenbach.png 2x" data-file-width="949" data-file-height="589" /></a>

The development of the population in Offenbach between 1540 and 1997.</div>

<p>Until the end of the 17th century, Offenbach remained a small town with less than a thousand habitants. With the coming into power of the count <a href="/w/index.php?title=Johann_Philipp&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Johann Philipp (page does not exist)">Johann Philipp</a> in 1685, the city began to develop and the population rose steadily. In the 19th century the city became industrialized and the population increased even tenfold.<sup id="cite_ref-grossstadtmain_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grossstadtmain-12">[12]</a></sup> Offenbach is one of the German cities where Germans without migrant background make up a minority of the population. As of 31 December 2012, approx. 44.3% of residents or 55,047 people had no foreign background. In contrast to that, there were 55.7% or 69,214 people of non-German descent.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The largest of those groups are:<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turks</a>: 15,000 or 12.2%</p>
<p>People from the former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croats" title="Croats">Croats</a>): 13,000 or 10.5%</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a>: 8,000 or 6.5%</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italians</a>: 8,000 or 6.5%</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a>: 7,500 or 6.0%</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Poles</a>: 4,000 or 3.2%</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Afghan_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Afghan people">Afghans</a>: 3,600 or 3%</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pakistanis" title="Pakistanis">Pakistanis</a>: 2,700 or 2.2%</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach_am_Main&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg/220px-City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg" width="220" height="383" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg/330px-City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg/440px-City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="796" data-file-height="1386" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:City_Tower_Nordseite2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The 120-meter high "City-Tower" is mainly used as the German headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Capgemini" title="Capgemini">Capgemini</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Until the early 1970s Offenbach was dominated by the machine-building and leather industries. The city hosts the <a href="/wiki/Verband_der_Elektrotechnik,_Elektronik_und_Informationstechnik" title="Verband der Elektrotechnik, Elektronik und Informationstechnik">German Association for Electrical, Electronic and Information Technologies</a> to this day. The <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Wetterdienst" title="Deutscher Wetterdienst">Deutscher Wetterdienst</a>, commonly abbreviated as DWD, (translated from German as German Meteorological Service), residing in the Westend district.</p>
<p>Offenbach was also the European center of <a href="/wiki/Typography" title="Typography">typography</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Gebr._Klingspor" class="mw-redirect" title="Gebr. Klingspor">Gebr. Klingspor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mergenthaler_Linotype_Company" title="Mergenthaler Linotype Company">Linotype</a> (inventors of <a href="/wiki/Optima" title="Optima">Optima</a> or <a href="/wiki/Palatino" title="Palatino">Palatino</a> <a href="/wiki/Typeface" title="Typeface">typeface</a>) moving to nearby <a href="/wiki/Eschborn" title="Eschborn">Eschborn</a> in the 1970s and <a href="/wiki/MAN_Roland" class="mw-redirect" title="MAN Roland">MAN Roland</a> printing machines still a major employer today. Typography and design still remain important with a cluster of graphic design and industrial design companies, as well as the university level <a href="/wiki/Hochschule_f%C3%BCr_Gestaltung_Offenbach_am_Main" title="Hochschule für Gestaltung Offenbach am Main">Hochschule für Gestaltung Offenbach am Main</a> design school and the <a href="/wiki/Klingspor_Museum" title="Klingspor Museum">Klingspor Museum</a>.</p>
<p>In recent years Offenbach has become a popular location for a wide array of services, especially from the transport sectors. Offenbach is the host to the European headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Motors" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyundai Motors">Hyundai Motors</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Kumho_Tires" class="mw-redirect" title="Kumho Tires">Kumho Tires</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>In Offenbach there is no specific Old Town, but there are several buildings which survived bombing during the war and have been restored. One of them is the <a href="/wiki/Baroque_Revival_architecture" title="Baroque Revival architecture">Neo-baroque</a> palace <i>Büsingpalais</i> with the Büsingpark, reconstructed in the 1980s. Today it is used as a congress center close to the Sheraton hotel. Between the shopping area and the Main, is the <i>Lilipark</i> and the <i>Lilitemple</i>, named after <a href="/wiki/Johann_Wolfgang_von_Goethe" title="Johann Wolfgang von Goethe">Johann Wolfgang von Goethe</a>'s fiancee Lili Schönemann. The most important building is the <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isenburger_Schloss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Isenburger Schloss (page does not exist)">Isenburger Schloss</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isenburger_Schloss" class="extiw" title="de:Isenburger Schloss">de</a>)</span></i> (Isenburg Palace), a <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_architecture" title="Renaissance architecture">renaissance</a> palace from 1576. It is today used by the <a href="/wiki/Hochschule_f%C3%BCr_Gestaltung_Offenbach_am_Main" title="Hochschule für Gestaltung Offenbach am Main">Offenbach Design University</a> which is next to it. There is also a <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassic</a> palace in the borough Rumpenheim, the <i>Rumpenheimer Schloss</i> it now serves exclusively as domestic dwellings but the park is public.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach_am_Main&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The streets of central Offenbach are usually congested with cars during the rush hour. Some areas, especially around the shopping streets, are pedestrian-only streets. There are numerous car parks located throughout the city. The Offenbacher Kreuz is an Autobahn interchange where the Autobahnen <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_3" title="Bundesautobahn 3">A 3</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>-<a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Würzburg">Würzburg</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_661" title="Bundesautobahn 661">A 661</a> meet. The A661 crosses the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_3" title="Bundesautobahn 3">A 3</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>-<a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Würzburg">Würzburg</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_5" title="Bundesautobahn 5">A 5</a> (<a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a>-<a href="/wiki/Hannover" class="mw-redirect" title="Hannover">Hannover</a>).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transport">Public transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach_am_Main&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Public transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The city is connected by a major line of the <a href="/wiki/Rhine-Main_S-Bahn" title="Rhine-Main S-Bahn">S-Bahn railway system</a> to Frankfurt. The station in the city center is <i>Marktplatz</i>. In general, six stations are located in Offenbach: <i>Offenbach-Kaiserlei, Offenbach-Ledermuseum, Offenbach-Marktplatz, <a href="/wiki/Offenbach-Ost_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Offenbach-Ost station">Offenbach-Ost</a>, Offenbach-Bieber, Offenbach-Waldhof</i>. Trains run every 5–10 minutes between Offenbach and Frankurt. A 24 hours Service between both cities was introduced in 2013. The journey from Offenbach Marktplatz to Frankfurt Main Station takes 15 minutes, Frankfurt Airport can be reached within 26 minutes. The city's public transportation services OVB and NIO connect all city districts to downtown by bus lines. Since the construction of the S-Bahn, the central train station, the <a href="/wiki/Offenbach_Hauptbahnhof" class="mw-redirect" title="Offenbach Hauptbahnhof">Offenbach Hauptbahnhof</a>, is no longer considered important.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Frankfurt_Airport">Frankfurt Airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach_am_Main&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Frankfurt Airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The city is accessed from around the world via the <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Airport" title="Frankfurt Airport">Frankfurt Airport</a>, (Flughafen Frankfurt am Main) which is located 12 kilometres (7.5 miles) from Offenbach. The airport can be reached by car or bus and has two train stations, one for regional and one for long-distance traffic. The S-Bahn lines S8 and S9 (direction "Offenbach Ost or "Hanau"), departing from the regional traffic station, take 25 minutes from the airport to get to Offenbach.</p>
