			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Accreditation</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Accreditation</b> is the process in which <a href="/wiki/Certification" title="Certification">certification</a> of competency, authority, or credibility is presented.</p>
<p>Organizations that issue <a href="/wiki/Credential" title="Credential">credentials</a> or certify third parties against official standards are themselves formally accredited by accreditation bodies (such as <a href="/wiki/UKAS" class="mw-redirect" title="UKAS">UKAS</a>); hence they are sometimes known as "accredited certification bodies".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The accreditation process ensures that their certification practices are acceptable, typically meaning that they are competent to test and certify third parties, behave ethically and employ suitable <a href="/wiki/Quality_assurance" title="Quality assurance">quality assurance</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accreditation_standards">Accreditation standards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accreditation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accreditation standards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many accreditation bodies operate according to processes developed by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) as specified in ISO/IEC 17011.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Accredited entities in specific sectors must provide evidence to the accreditation body that they conform to other standards in the same series:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_17020&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ISO/IEC 17020 (page does not exist)">ISO/IEC 17020</a>: "General criteria for the operation of various types of bodies performing inspection" (2012)</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_17021&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ISO/IEC 17021 (page does not exist)">ISO/IEC 17021</a>: "Conformity assessment. Requirements for bodies providing audit and certification of management systems" (2011)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_17024" title="ISO/IEC 17024">ISO/IEC 17024</a>: "Conformity Assessment. General requirements for bodies operating certification of persons" (2012)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_17025" title="ISO/IEC 17025">ISO/IEC 17025</a>: "General requirements for the competence of testing and calibration laboratories" (2005)</li>
</ul>
