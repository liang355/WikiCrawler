<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pfaffenhofen_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In early medieval times the region was partially property of the powerful monasteries of <a href="/wiki/Ilmm%C3%BCnster_Abbey" title="Ilmmünster Abbey">Ilmmünster</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchsm%C3%BCnster_Abbey" title="Münchsmünster Abbey">Münchsmünster</a>, and partially divided into tiny secular states. One of those states was the county of Scheyern. The counts were ancestors of the <a href="/wiki/Wittelsbach" class="mw-redirect" title="Wittelsbach">Wittelsbach</a> family, who in 1180 became rulers of Bavaria. From that time on the region was a part of Bavaria.</p>
<p>The district of Pfaffenhofen was established in 1972 by merging several former districts.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pfaffenhofen_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district is located in the Hallertau Plains between the <a href="/wiki/Isar" title="Isar">Isar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> rivers. The Danube crosses the northernmost part of the district.</p>
