				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Fortune Global 500</b>, also known as <b>Global 500</b>, is an annual ranking of the top 500 <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporations</a> worldwide as measured by <a href="/wiki/Revenue" title="Revenue">revenue</a> and the list is compiled and published annually by <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Methodology">Methodology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortune_Global_500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Methodology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Until 1989, it listed only non-<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> industrial corporations under the title "International 500" while the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> contained and still contains exclusively United States corporations. In 1990, United States companies were added to compile a truly global list of top industrial corporations as ranked by sales. Since 1995, the list has had its current form, listing also top financial corporations and service providers by revenue.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortune_Global_500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several inconsistencies exist in Fortune's ranking of cities with the most Fortune 500 headquarters. On June 3, 2011, the <i><a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Business_Chronicle" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta Business Chronicle">Atlanta Business Chronicle</a></i> stated examples of <i>Fortune</i> including regional headquarters for some cities, not including regional headquarters for other cities and in some cases not including headquarters that are physically located inside a city limit.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_distribution">Geographical distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortune_Global_500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geographical distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2001, there has been a significant change in the geographical distribution of the companies in the Global 500 rankings. The number of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a>-based companies decreased from 215 in 2001 to 145 in 2017 and the contribution of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a>-based companies increased rapidly from 116 in 2001 to 197 in 2017. Most of this growth is accounted for by the rapid increase in the number of Chinese Global 500 companies, of which there were 109 by 2017 (the number becomes 115 if Taiwan is included), increasing from only 10 in 2001. The share of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a>-based companies also reduced, from 158 to 143, over the same period.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>As can be seen from the table above, 427 (85.4%) of the Global 500 are represented by only 10 countries: two in North America (Canada and United States), five in Western Europe (France, Germany, Netherlands, Switzerland and United Kingdom) and three in East Asia (China, Japan and South Korea). Moreover, the top six (United States, China, Japan, France, Germany and United Kingdom) are the world's largest economies as estimated by the IMF (<a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(nominal)" title="List of countries by GDP (nominal)">List of countries by GDP (nominal)</a>). Among the Fortune Global 500, 373 companies (74.6%) are from these six countries. According to the Credit Suisse Global Wealth Report 2016, the adult citizens of these six countries own approximately 67% of the world's total household wealth.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
