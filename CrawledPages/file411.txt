<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bergisch_Gladbach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bergisch Gladbach is located east of the river <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a>, approx. 10 kilometers east of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Neighbouring_municipalities">Neighbouring municipalities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bergisch_Gladbach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Neighbouring municipalities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beginning in the north clockwise the neighbouring municipalities and neighbouring towns are: <a href="/wiki/Odenthal" title="Odenthal">Odenthal</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%C3%BCrten" title="Kürten">Kürten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Overath" title="Overath">Overath</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B6srath" title="Rösrath">Rösrath</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leverkusen" title="Leverkusen">Leverkusen</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bergisch_Gladbach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Early settlements existed in the 13th century, but the town was officially founded in 1856.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg/220px-Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg" width="220" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg/330px-Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg/440px-Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="538" data-file-height="480" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bensberg_Rathaus2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Old <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">castle</a> and town hall of Bensberg</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The word <i>Bergisch</i> in the name does not originate from its location in the county of <a href="/wiki/Berg_(German_region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Berg (German region)">Berg</a> and was not added to distinguish it from <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Mönchengladbach">Mönchengladbach</a> as believed by many people, but from the counts who gave their name to the region. At the start of the 12th century the counts of Berg settled in the area and it later became the duchy (under Napoleon, the grand duchy) of Berg. This is where the first part of the name (<i>Bergisch</i>) comes from, the town being located in the former county of Berg. It is the administrative headquarters ('Kreisstadt') of the Rheinish-Bergisch district (or 'Kreis').</p>
<p>The second part of the name, <i>Gladbach</i> (cognate with English <i>Ladbrooke</i>) originates from <a href="/wiki/Low_German" title="Low German">Low German</a> (<i>Bergisches Platt</i>) and means <i>canalised stream</i>, referring to the small river (the <i>Strunde</i>) that was artificially canalised (<i>laid</i>) in early medieval times. In <i>Bergisch Platt</i>, the regional dialect, <i>laid</i> is said <i>gelaat</i>, a word which eventually evolved to <i>glad</i> (in this case the 'd' is pronounced as a 't'). The second part of the word, <i>bach</i> is the standard German word for a small stream, referring in this case to the <i><a href="/wiki/Strunde" title="Strunde">Strunde</a>.</i></p>
<p>In 1975 the town incorporated neighbouring Bensberg and when it reached a population of 100,000 in 1977 it was given city status.</p>
