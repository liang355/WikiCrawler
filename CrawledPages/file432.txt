<p><b>Oldenburg</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈɔldn̩bʊʁk]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Oldenburg.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/47/Oldenburg.ogg" class="internal" title="Oldenburg.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is an <a href="/wiki/Independent_city" title="Independent city">independent city</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Oldenburg_(district)" title="Oldenburg (district)">district of Oldenburg</a> in the state of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a>, Germany. The city is officially named <b>Oldenburg (Oldb)</b> (<i>Oldenburg in Oldenburg</i>) to distinguish from <a href="/wiki/Oldenburg_in_Holstein" title="Oldenburg in Holstein">Oldenburg in Holstein</a>.</p>
<p>During the French annexation (1811–1813) in the wake of the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic war</a> against Britain, it was also known as <i>Le Vieux-Bourg</i> in French. The city is situated at the Rivers <a href="/wiki/Hunte" title="Hunte">Hunte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haaren_(river)" title="Haaren (river)">Haaren</a>, in the northwestern region between the cities of <a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">Bremen</a> in the east and <a href="/wiki/Groningen" title="Groningen">Groningen</a> (Netherlands) in the west. It has a population of 167,633 (November 2016).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Oldenburg is part of the&#160;<a href="/wiki/Bremen/Oldenburg_Metropolitan_Region" title="Bremen/Oldenburg Metropolitan Region">Bremen/Oldenburg Metropolitan Region</a>, with 2.37 million people.</p>
<p>The city is the place of origin of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Oldenburg" title="House of Oldenburg">House of Oldenburg</a>. Before the end of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> (1918), it was the administrative centre and residence of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Oldenburg" class="mw-redirect" title="List of rulers of Oldenburg">monarchs of Oldenburg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Archaeological finds point to a settlement dating back to the 8th century. The place was first mentioned in 1108 as <i>Aldenburg</i> in connection with <a href="/wiki/Elimar_I,_Count_of_Oldenburg" title="Elimar I, Count of Oldenburg">Elimar I</a> (also known as Egilmar I) who is now commonly seen as the first count of Oldenburg. The town gained importance due to its location at a <a href="/wiki/Ford_(river)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford (river)">ford</a> of the navigable Hunte river. Oldenburg became the capital of the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Oldenburg" title="County of Oldenburg">County of Oldenburg</a> (later <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Oldenburg" title="Duchy of Oldenburg">Duchy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Oldenburg" title="Grand Duchy of Oldenburg">Grand Duchy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Oldenburg" title="Free State of Oldenburg">Free State</a>), a small state in the shadow of the much more powerful Hanseatic city of <a href="/wiki/Bremen_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bremen (city)">Bremen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 17th century, Oldenburg was a wealthy town in a time of <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">war and turmoil</a> and its population and power grew considerably. In 1667, the town was struck by a disastrous <a href="/wiki/Black_Death" title="Black Death">plague</a> epidemic and, shortly after, a fire destroyed Oldenburg. The Danish kings, who were also counts of Oldenburg at the time, were not much interested in the condition of the town and it lost most of its former importance. In 1773, Danish rule ended. It was only then that the destroyed buildings in the city were rebuilt in a <a href="/wiki/Neoclassicism" title="Neoclassicism">neoclassicist</a> style.<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-3">[3]</a></sup> (In German, the "neoclassicist style" of that period would usually be called <i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">klassizistisch</span></i>, while <i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">neoklassizistisch</span></i> specifically refers to the classicist style of the early 20th century.)</p>

<p>After German Emperor <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_II,_German_Emperor" title="Wilhelm II, German Emperor">Wilhelm II</a> was forced to abdicate following the exhaustion and defeat of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, monarchic rule ended in Oldenburg as well with the abdication of Grand Duke <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Augustus_II,_Grand_Duke_of_Oldenburg" title="Frederick Augustus II, Grand Duke of Oldenburg">Frederick Augustus II of Oldenburg</a> <i>(<span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Friedrich August II von Oldenburg</span>)</i> on 11 November 1918. The Grand Duchy now became the Free State of Oldenburg <i>(<span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Freistaat Oldenburg</span>)</i>, with the city remaining the capital.</p>
<p>In the 1928 city elections, the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi Party</a> received 9.8% of the vote, enough for a seat on the Oldenburg city council. In the September 1930 Oldenburg state elections, the Nazi Party's share of the vote rose to 27.3%, and on May 29, 1932, the Nazi Party received 48.4% of the state election, enough to put the Nazi party in charge of forming a state government and, significantly, making Oldenburg the first state in the country to put the Nazis in power based on electoral turnout. By that autumn, a campaign of <a href="/wiki/Aryanization_(Nazism)" title="Aryanization (Nazism)">Aryanization</a> began, forcing the sale of formerly Jewish-owned properties at steep discounts.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1945, after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the State of Oldenburg was part of the <a href="/wiki/Allied-occupied_Germany" title="Allied-occupied Germany">British zone of occupation</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Control_Commission_for_Germany_-_British_Element" class="mw-redirect" title="Control Commission for Germany - British Element">British military government</a> of the Oldenburg region resided in the city. Several <a href="/wiki/Displaced_persons_camp" title="Displaced persons camp">displaced persons camps</a> were set up in the city that had suffered only 1.4% destruction during the <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Bombing_During_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Strategic Bombing During World War II">bombing campaigns of World War II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> About 42,000 refugees migrated into Oldenburg, which raised the number of residents to over 100,000. In 1946, the Free State of Oldenburg was dissolved, and the area became the 'Administrative District' of Oldenburg <i>(<span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Verwaltungsbezirk Oldenburg</span>)</i> as part of the newly formed <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">federal German state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> <i>(<span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Niedersachsen</span>)</i>. The city was now capital of the district. In 1978, the district was dissolved and succeeded by the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Weser-Ems" title="Weser-Ems">Weser-Ems</a> administrative region <i>(<span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Regierungsbezirk" title="Regierungsbezirk">Regierungsbezirk</a> Weser-Ems</span>)</i>, again with the city as administrative capital. The State of Lower Saxony dissolved all of the <i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Regierungsbezirke</span></i> by the end of 2004 in the course of administrative reforms.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="City_government">City government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: City government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_and_infrastructure">Economy and infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy and infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<ul>
<li>The city centre of Oldenburg is surrounded by a ring of freeways (<a href="/wiki/German_autobahns" class="mw-redirect" title="German autobahns">autobahns</a>) consisting of <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_28" title="Bundesautobahn 28">A 28</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_29" title="Bundesautobahn 29">A 29</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_293" title="Bundesautobahn 293">A 293</a>. Because of this, Oldenburg is connected to the nationwide network of federal autobahns, as well as to the <a href="/wiki/International_E-road_network" title="International E-road network">international E-road network</a> (German: <i>Europastraßen</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oldenburg_Central_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Oldenburg Central Station">Oldenburg Central Station</a>, <i>Oldenburg (Oldb) Hauptbahnhof</i>, is at the intersection of the railway lines <a href="/wiki/Norddeich_Mole_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Norddeich Mole station">Norddeich Mole</a>—<a href="/wiki/Leer" title="Leer">Leer</a>—Oldenburg—Bremen and <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmshaven" title="Wilhelmshaven">Wilhelmshaven</a>—Oldenburg—Osnabrück, with <a href="/wiki/Intercity_(Deutsche_Bahn)" title="Intercity (Deutsche Bahn)">Intercity</a> services to <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leipzig" title="Leipzig">Leipzig</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a> and <a href="/wiki/InterCityExpress" class="mw-redirect" title="InterCityExpress">InterCityExpress</a> services to <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>.</li>
<li>Oldenburg is only about half an hour drive from <a href="/wiki/Bremen_Airport" title="Bremen Airport">Bremen Airport</a> (about 50&#160;km | 31 miles). Other international airports nearby are <a href="/wiki/Hamburg_Airport" title="Hamburg Airport">Hamburg Airport</a> (160&#160;km | 100 miles) and <a href="/wiki/Hannover-Langenhagen_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Hannover-Langenhagen Airport">Hannover-Langenhagen Airport</a> (170&#160;km | 106 miles).</li>
<li>The small <a href="/wiki/Hatten" title="Hatten">Hatten</a> Airfield, (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.flugplatz-hatten.de">Flugplatz Oldenburg-Hatten</a> <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO airport code</a>: EDWH), is located about 17&#160;km south-west of Oldenburg. It serves to small aircraft (private planes, gliders, balloons, and helicopters). A flight training school is also located there, and small planes can be chartered. Scenic flights can be booked as well.</li>
<li>Oldenburg is connected to shipping through the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%BCstenkanal" class="mw-redirect" title="Küstenkanal">Küstenkanal</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Ship_canal" title="Ship canal">ship canal</a> connecting the rivers <a href="/wiki/Ems_(river)" title="Ems (river)">Ems</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weser_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Weser River">Weser</a>. With 1.6 million tons of goods annually, it is the most important non-coastal harbour in Lower Saxony.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bicycle" title="Bicycle">Bicycles</a> play a very important part in personal transport.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city is surrounded by large agricultural areas, about 80% of which is grassland. There are farms near and even a few within city limits. Predominant agricultural activities of the region are the cultivation of livestock, especially dairy cows and other grazing animals, crops such as grains for food and animal feed, as well as asparagus, corn, and kale.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recurring_cultural_events">Recurring cultural events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Recurring cultural events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i>Kultursommer</i> (summer of culture), series of free musical and other cultural events in the city centre during summer holiday season in July.</li>
<li><i>CSD Nordwest (Christopher Street Day)</i> parade of the regional <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender</a> community in June, with up to 10,000 participants (since 1995).</li>
<li><i>Stadtfest</i>, a three-day festival of the city centre in August/September, comprises gastronomical offerings and rock and pop music performances on various stages.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Oldenburg_International_Film_Festival" title="Oldenburg International Film Festival">Oldenburg International Film Festival</a></i>, privately organised <a href="/wiki/Film_festival" title="Film festival">film festival</a> in September, focussed on <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent film</a> and film makers. The festival is funded through public subsidies and private sponsoring.</li>
<li><i>Kramermarkt</i>, <a href="/wiki/Funfair" class="mw-redirect" title="Funfair">fun fair</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Weser-Ems_Halle" title="Weser-Ems Halle">Weser-Ems Halle</a> on ten days in September/October. The tradition of this annual <a href="/wiki/Volksfest" title="Volksfest">volksfest</a> dates back to the 17th century, when the Kramermarkt was a market event at the end of the harvest.</li>
<li><i>Oldenburger Kinder- und Jugendbuchmesse (KIBUM)</i>, an exhibition of new <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German language</a> <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_literature" title="Children's literature">children's and youth literature</a>, takes place over 11 days in November. A non-commercial fair organised by the city government in cooperation with the public library and the university library. In the course of the fair, a prize, the <i>Kinder- und Jugendbuchpreis</i>, is awarded to a debuting author or illustrator.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Points_of_interest">Points of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Points of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Core city centre, a large pedestrianised shopping destination for the region.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oldenburg_State_Theatre" class="mw-redirect" title="Oldenburg State Theatre">Oldenburg State Theatre</a>, oldest mainstream theatre in Oldenburg, first opened in 1833.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Schloss_Oldenburg" title="Schloss Oldenburg">Schloss Oldenburg</a> in the city centre, until 1918 residence of the monarchic rulers of Oldenburg, today a museum. A public park, the <i>Schlossgarten</i>, is nearby.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Weser-Ems_Halle" title="Weser-Ems Halle">Weser-Ems Halle</a>, exhibition and congress centre with outdoor fair area, located in Oldenburg Donnerschwee.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Small_EWE_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Small EWE Arena">Small EWE Arena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Large_EWE_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Large EWE Arena">Large EWE Arena</a>, two sports and event halls located near the main railway station, opened in 2005 and 2013, and seating up to 4,000 and 6,852 visitors respectively. The large arena is also home to the <i><a href="/wiki/EWE_Baskets_Oldenburg" title="EWE Baskets Oldenburg">EWE Baskets Oldenburg</a></i> basketball club.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lutheran_community">Lutheran community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Lutheran community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Oldenburg is the seat of administration and bishop of the <a href="/wiki/Evangelical_Lutheran_Church_in_Oldenburg" title="Evangelical Lutheran Church in Oldenburg">Evangelical Lutheran Church in Oldenburg</a>, whose preaching venue is the <a href="/wiki/St_Lamberti_Church" title="St Lamberti Church">St Lamberti Church</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jewish_community">Jewish community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Jewish community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG/170px-Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG/255px-Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG/340px-Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="1280" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Young_Nathan_Marcus_Adler.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Nathan Marcus Adler, chief Rabbi of the Oldenburg Jewish community in the 19th century</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Oldenburg" title="History of the Jews in Oldenburg">history of the Jewish community of Oldenburg</a> dates back to the 14th century.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Towards and during the 19th century, the Jews in Oldenburg were always around 1% of the total population, and by that time had acquired their own synagogue, cemetery and school. Most of them were merchants and businessmen. On 1938 <a href="/wiki/Kristallnacht" title="Kristallnacht">Kristallnacht</a>, the town men were led to <a href="/wiki/Sachsenhausen_concentration_camp" title="Sachsenhausen concentration camp">Sachsenhausen concentration camp</a>, among them <a href="/wiki/Leo_Trepp" title="Leo Trepp">Leo Trepp</a>, the community <a href="/wiki/Rabbi" title="Rabbi">Rabbi</a> who survived and later became an honorary citizen of Oldenburg and honored by a street named after him.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Since 1981 an annual commemoration walk (Erinnerungsgang) has been held by Oldenburg citizens in memory of the deportation of the Oldenburg Jews on November 10, 1938.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Those who remained after 1938 immigrated to Canada, USA, United Kingdom, Holland or Palestine.</p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, a group of survivors returned to the city and maintained a small community until it was dissolved during the 1970s. Nevertheless, due to Jewish emigration from the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">USSR</a> to Germany in the 1990s, a community of about 340 people is now maintaining its own synagogue, cemetery and other facilities. The old Jewish cemetery, which is no longer active after the opening of a new one, was desecrated twice in 2011 and 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tertiary_education">Tertiary education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oldenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Tertiary education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>There are two <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public universities</a> in Oldenburg:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Carl_von_Ossietzky_University_of_Oldenburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Carl von Ossietzky University of Oldenburg">Carl von Ossietzky University of Oldenburg</a> was founded in 1973 based on a previous college for teacher training, the <i>Pädagogische Hochschule Oldenburg</i>, which had a history in Oldenburg dating back to 1793. The university was officially named after <a href="/wiki/Carl_von_Ossietzky" title="Carl von Ossietzky">Carl von Ossietzky</a> in 1991. As of 2014, it has almost 13,746 students, a scientific staff of 1,130, as well as 964 technical and administrative staff.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> A new faculty of medicine and health sciences was established in 2012 as part of the newly founded <i>European Medical School Oldenburg-Groningen</i>, a cooperation with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Groningen" title="University of Groningen">University of Groningen</a> (Netherlands) and local hospitals.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Jade_University_of_Applied_Sciences" title="Jade University of Applied Sciences">Jade University of Applied Sciences</a> (Jade-Hochschule) The former <i>Fachhochschule Oldenburg</i> (until 1999) was founded in 1971, a merger of the previous engineering academy with the nautical college in <a href="/wiki/Elsfleth" title="Elsfleth">Elsfleth</a>. Oldenburg already had a history of construction engineering training dating back to 1882. Starting in 2000, the Fachhochschule had been part of multiple re-organisations involving several <a href="/wiki/Fachhochschule" title="Fachhochschule">UAS (Fachhochschule)</a> in the northwestern region. A relaunch under the name <i>Jade-Hochschule</i> took place in 2009 (previously: <i>Fachhochschule Oldenburg/Ostfriesland/Wilhelmshaven</i>). The Jade-Hochschule now comprises branches in three towns: Oldenburg, Elsfleth, and <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmshaven" title="Wilhelmshaven">Wilhelmshaven</a>. Based in Oldenburg are the departments of <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Construction_engineering" title="Construction engineering">construction engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Construction_management" title="Construction management">construction management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geodesy" title="Geodesy">geodesy</a>, as well as the institute of <a href="/wiki/Hearing_aid" title="Hearing aid">hearing aid technology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Audiology" title="Audiology">audiology</a>. There are about 2,000 students in the Oldenburg branch.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> (The Elsfleth branch offers bachelor's degree courses in nautical science, international logistics, and harbour management. The Wilhelmshaven branch offers courses in engineering, business management, and media management.)</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Privately managed institutions of higher education:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Founded in 2004, the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ibs-ol.de">IBS IT &amp; Business School Oldenburg</a> (former <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://ba-oldenburg.de">Berufsakademie Oldenburg</a>), a college of <a href="/wiki/Cooperative_education" title="Cooperative education">cooperative education</a>, offers a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">B.Sc.</a> degree course in <a href="/wiki/Business_informatics" title="Business informatics">business informatics</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">B.A.</a> degree course in <a href="/wiki/Business_studies" title="Business studies">business studies</a>. The dual-system course combines practical vocational training at one of the partnering local companies with periods of academic studies.</li>
<li>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.fhwt.de/">Private Fachhochschule für Wirtschaft und Technik</a>, a regional college of <a href="/wiki/Cooperative_education" title="Cooperative education">cooperative education</a>, maintains a branch in Oldenburg offering bachelor's degree courses with integrated vocational training in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mechatronics" title="Mechatronics">mechatronics</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Other:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The Oldenburg branch of the Lower Saxony police academy (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://polizeiakademie.niedersachsen.de/">Polizeiakademie Niedersachsen</a>) maintains a study facility in Oldenburg preparing candidates for a career in higher-middle-level or higher-level police service.</li>
</ul>
