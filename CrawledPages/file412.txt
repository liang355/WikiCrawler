<p><b>Bottrop</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈbɔtʁɔp]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Bottrop.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b9/De-Bottrop.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Bottrop.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is a city in west central <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, on the Rhine-Herne Canal, in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>. Located in the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr area">Ruhr industrial area</a>, Bottrop adjoins <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oberhausen" title="Oberhausen">Oberhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gladbeck" title="Gladbeck">Gladbeck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dorsten" title="Dorsten">Dorsten</a>. The city had been a coal-mining and rail center and contains factories producing coal-tar derivatives, chemicals, textiles, and machinery. Bottrop grew as a mining center beginning in the 1860s, was chartered as a city in 1921, and bombed during the <a href="/wiki/Oil_Campaign_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil Campaign of World War II">Oil Campaign of World War II</a>. In 1975 it unified with the neighbour communities of Gladbeck and Kirchhellen, but Gladbeck left it in 1976, leading to Kirchhellen becoming a district of Bottrop as Bottrop-Kirchhellen.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boroughs">Boroughs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bottrop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Boroughs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The total area of the municipal territory is about 101 square kilometres (39 square miles). The longest north-south distance is 17 kilometres (11 miles), and from west to east 9 kilometres (5.6 miles). The highest peak within the city's territory is 78 metres (256 feet), the lowest one being 26 metres (85 feet) <a href="/wiki/Above_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above sea level">above sea level</a>.</p>
<p>Bottrop is divided into 3 boroughs, they are: <b>Bottrop-Mitte</b> (Bottrop-Center), <b>Bottrop-Süd</b> (Bottrop South) and <b><a href="/wiki/Bottrop#Kirchhellen" title="Bottrop">Bottrop-Kirchhellen</a></b>, each one having a borough representation and a borough ruler.</p>
<p>These boroughs are further subdivided into city parts, partly named after their traditional name, while the newly built parts are only recently named&#160;:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Bottrop-Mitte</b>: Eigen, Fuhlenbrock, Stadtmitte</li>
<li><b>Bottrop-Süd</b>: Batenbrock, Boy, Ebel, Lehmkuhle, Vonderort, Gartenstadt Welheim (Garden city Welheim) and Welheimer Mark</li>
<li><b>Bottrop-Kirchhellen</b>: Ekel, Feldhausen, Grafenwald, Hardinghausen, Holthausen, Im Loh, Kirchhellen, Kuhberg, Overhagen</li>
</ul>
<p>For statistical reasons, Bottrop is also divided into <i>statistical</i> boroughs. They are (with their official numbering)</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kirchhellen">Kirchhellen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bottrop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kirchhellen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1919 until 1976 Kirchhellen was its own town. From 1976 until 1978 it was a part of "GlaBotKi"(<a href="/wiki/Gladbeck" title="Gladbeck">Gladbeck</a>, Bottrop, Kirchhellen). In 1978 Kirchhellen became part of Bottrop.</p>
<p>Most of Kirchhellen is Catholic (approx. 65%) - there are three churches there. There is one Lutheran church.</p>
