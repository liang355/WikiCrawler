<p><b>Leverkusen</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'">eɪ</span><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/ər/: 'er' in 'letter'">ər</span><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/uː/: 'oo' in 'goose'">uː</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span><span title="/ən/: 'on' in 'button'">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈleːvɐˌkuːzn̩]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Leverkusen.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c7/De-Leverkusen.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Leverkusen.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> on the eastern bank of the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a>. To the South, Leverkusen borders the city of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> and to the North is the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">state</a> capital <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a>.</p>
<p>With about 161,000 inhabitants, Leverkusen is one of the state's smaller cities. The city is known for the pharmaceutical company <a href="/wiki/Bayer" title="Bayer">Bayer</a> and its associated sports club <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer 04 Leverkusen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leverkusen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The heart of what is now Leverkusen was a village called Wiesdorf, which dates back to the 12th century. During the <a href="/wiki/Cologne_War" title="Cologne War">Cologne War</a>, from 1583 to 1588 Leverkusen was ravaged by war. The entire area was rural until the late 19th century, when industry prompted the development that led to the city of Leverkusen, and to its becoming one of the most important centres of the German chemical industry.</p>
<p>The chemist <a href="/wiki/Carl_Leverkus" title="Carl Leverkus">Carl Leverkus</a>, looking for a place to build a <a href="/wiki/Dye" title="Dye">dye</a> factory, chose Wiesdorf in 1860. He built a factory for the production of artificial <a href="/wiki/Ultramarine_blue" class="mw-redirect" title="Ultramarine blue">ultramarine blue</a> at the Kahlberg in Wiesdorf in 1861, and called the emerging settlement "Leverkusen" after his family home in <a href="/wiki/Lennep" class="mw-redirect" title="Lennep">Lennep</a>. The factory was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/Bayer" title="Bayer">Bayer</a> company in 1891; Bayer moved its headquarters to Wiesdorf in 1912. After asset confiscation at the end of the First World War, it became <a href="/wiki/IG_Farben" title="IG Farben">IG Farben</a>. The city of Leverkusen proper was founded in 1930 by merging Wiesdorf, Schlebusch, Steinbüchel and Rheindorf, and was posthumously named for Carl Leverkus.</p>
<p>During the Second World War, the IG Farben factories were bombed by by the <a href="/wiki/RAF" class="mw-redirect" title="RAF">RAF</a> on 22 August 1943,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> again by the RAF during <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Berlin_(RAF_campaign)" title="Battle of Berlin (RAF campaign)">bombing campaigns</a> on 19/20 November, the USAAF <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Air_Force" title="Eighth Air Force">Eighth Air Force</a> on 1 December 1943,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and finally once again by the RAF on 10/11 December 1943.</p>
<p>In 1975, Opladen (including Quettingen and Lützenkirchen since 1930), Hitdorf and Bergisch Neukirchen joined Leverkusen. The present city is made up of several villages, originally called Wiesdorf, <a href="/wiki/Opladen" title="Opladen">Opladen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schlebusch" title="Schlebusch">Schlebusch</a>, Manfort, Bürrig, Hitdorf, Quettingen, Lützenkirchen, Steinbüchel, Rheindorf and Bergisch-Neukirchen.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leverkusen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is home to the German record holder of <a href="/wiki/German_Basketball_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="German Basketball Championship">national basketball championships</a>, which is the team <a href="/wiki/Bayer_Giants_Leverkusen" title="Bayer Giants Leverkusen">Bayer Giants Leverkusen</a>. As of 2017, the team plays in the 3rd division <a href="/wiki/ProB" title="ProB">ProB</a> and plays its home games in the <a href="/wiki/Smidt-Arena" title="Smidt-Arena">Smidt-Arena</a>.</p>
<p>The Smidt-Arena, previously known as <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Dopatka_Halle" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilhelm Dopatka Halle">Wilhelm Dopatka Halle</a>, was one of the host arenas for the FIBA <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_1985" title="EuroBasket 1985">EuroBasket 1985</a>. (the official European Basketball Championship)</p>
