<p><b>Freising</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Following a recent ranking of the German magazine <a href="/wiki/Focus_(German_magazine)" title="Focus (German magazine)"><i>Focus-Money</i></a> comparing all German districts it is number one concerning economic growth abilities. It is bounded by (from the north and clockwise) the districts of <a href="/wiki/Kelheim_(district)" title="Kelheim (district)">Kelheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landshut_(district)" title="Landshut (district)">Landshut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erding_(district)" title="Erding (district)">Erding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Munich_(district)" title="Munich (district)">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dachau_(district)" title="Dachau (district)">Dachau</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pfaffenhofen_(district)" title="Pfaffenhofen (district)">Pfaffenhofen</a>. The district is located north of the <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> metropolitan area. The <a href="/wiki/Isar" title="Isar">Isar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amper" title="Amper">Amper</a> rivers run in parallel from southwest to northeast. North of the rivers there is the <a href="/wiki/Hallertau" title="Hallertau">Hallertau</a>, a hilly region mainly used for <a href="/wiki/Hops" title="Hops">hop</a> growing.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freising_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>, Freising was a clerical state ruled by the bishops. In 1803, when the clerical states of Germany were dissolved, the region was annexed by Bavaria.</p>

<h3><span id="The_Freising_Moor's_head"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Freising_Moor.27s_head">The Freising Moor's head</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freising_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Freising Moor's head">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is a controversial <a href="/wiki/Charge_(heraldry)" title="Charge (heraldry)">charge</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_Arms" class="mw-redirect" title="Coat of Arms">Coat of Arms</a>, which could represent one of many different persons:</p>
<ul>
<li>One of the three <a href="/wiki/Biblical_Magi" title="Biblical Magi">Magi</a> (one of them is shown as a moor)</li>
<li>Saints who were, or probably were, moors
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Saint_Maurice" title="Saint Maurice">St. Mauritius</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St._Zeno" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Zeno">St. Zeno</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St._Sigismund" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Sigismund">St. Sigismund</a> (mixed up with St. Mauritius)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Or just that a black Moor King.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Corbinian" title="Corbinian">St. Corbinian</a>, who was not a moor, but whose pictures might have become darker over time.</li>
<li>Other person or meaning lost in time</li>
</ul>
<p>It is important to note the crown on the <a href="/wiki/Maure" title="Maure">moor's head</a>, which probably indicated that the territory of the Bishop of Freising was autonomous.</p>
