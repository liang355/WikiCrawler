<p><b>Rosenheim</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in the south of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Neighboring districts are, clockwise from the west, <a href="/wiki/Miesbach_(district)" title="Miesbach (district)">Miesbach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Munich_(district)" title="Munich (district)">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ebersberg_(district)" title="Ebersberg (district)">Ebersberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BChldorf_(district)" title="Mühldorf (district)">Mühldorf</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Traunstein_(district)" title="Traunstein (district)">Traunstein</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> province <a href="/wiki/Tyrol_(state)" title="Tyrol (state)">Tirol</a> across the southern border. The district entirely surrounds the city of <a href="/wiki/Rosenheim" title="Rosenheim">Rosenheim</a>, which is independently administered but hosts the district's administration; both the city and the district share the "RO" designation for their <a href="/wiki/German_car_number_plates" class="mw-redirect" title="German car number plates">license plates</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosenheim_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district was created in 1972 when the former districts Rosenheim, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Aibling" title="Bad Aibling">Bad Aibling</a>, and parts of <a href="/wiki/Wasserburg_am_Inn" title="Wasserburg am Inn">Wasserburg am Inn</a> were merged.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosenheim_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Rosenheim district is located in the foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Chiemgau" title="Chiemgau">Chiemgau</a>. The landscape is dominated by <a href="/wiki/Moraine" title="Moraine">moraines</a> created by the <a href="/wiki/Inn_Glacier" title="Inn Glacier">Inn Glacier</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">last glacial period</a>, including many lakes. To the east of the district is the largest of these lakes, the <a href="/wiki/Chiemsee" title="Chiemsee">Chiemsee</a>. The main rivers in the district are the <a href="/wiki/River_Inn" class="mw-redirect" title="River Inn">Inn</a> and the Mangfall, which meet in the city of Rosenheim.</p>
<p>Mountain ranges in the south of the district include the <i>Chiemgauer Alpen</i> and the <i>Mangfallgebirge</i>, which also contains the <i>Wendelstein</i>, at 1,838 metres (6,030&#160;ft) the highest elevation in the district.</p>
