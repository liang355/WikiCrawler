<p><b>Hagen</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈhaːɡn̩]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Hagen.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/ca/De-Hagen.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Hagen.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is the <a href="/wiki/Largest_cities_in_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Largest cities in Germany">41st-largest</a> <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. The municipality is located in the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>. It is located on the south eastern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr area">Ruhr area</a>, 15&#160;km south of <a href="/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund">Dortmund</a>, where the rivers <a href="/wiki/Lenne" title="Lenne">Lenne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volme" title="Volme">Volme</a> (met by the river <a href="/wiki/Ennepe" title="Ennepe">Ennepe</a>) meet the river <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_(river)" title="Ruhr (river)">Ruhr</a>. As of 31 December 2010, the population was 188,529. The city is home to the <a href="/wiki/FernUniversit%C3%A4t_Hagen" class="mw-redirect" title="FernUniversität Hagen">FernUniversität Hagen</a>, which is the only state funded <a href="/wiki/Distance_education" title="Distance education">distance education</a> university in Germany. Counting more than 67,000 students (March 2010), it is the largest university in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hagen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg/220px-Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg/330px-Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg/440px-Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hengsteysee_Panorama.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i><a href="/wiki/Hengsteysee" title="Hengsteysee">Hengsteysee</a></i></div>

<p>Hagen was first mentioned c.1200, presumably the name of a farm at the junction of the Volme and the Ennepe. After the conquest of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Burg_Volmarstein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Burg Volmarstein (page does not exist)">Burg Volmarstein</a> in 1324, Hagen passed to the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Mark" title="County of Mark">County of Mark</a>. In 1614 it was awarded to the <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Margraviate of Brandenburg</a> according to the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Xanten" title="Treaty of Xanten">Treaty of Xanten</a>. In 1701 it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a>.</p>
<p>After the defeat of Prussia in the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Coalition" class="mw-redirect" title="Fourth Coalition">Fourth Coalition</a>, Hagen was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Berg" title="Grand Duchy of Berg">Grand Duchy of Berg</a> from 1807–13. In 1815 it became part of the new Prussian <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Westphalia" title="Province of Westphalia">Province of Westphalia</a>.</p>
<p>The growth of the city began in the 19th century with the mining of <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> and the production of <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr Area">Ruhr Area</a>. Hagen was the scene of fighting during the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_Uprising" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr Uprising">Ruhr Uprising</a>, 13 March - 2 April 1920 and has a monument to the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_Red_Army" title="Ruhr Red Army">Ruhr Red Army</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1928, Hagen developed into a city of more than 100,000 inhabitants.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Hagen was bombed repeatedly, by both the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Air_Force#World_War_II_.281944.E2.80.931945.29" title="Eighth Air Force">Eighth Air Force</a>. On the night of 1 October 1943, 243 <a href="/wiki/Avro_Lancaster" title="Avro Lancaster">Lancasters</a> and 8 <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Mosquito" title="De Havilland Mosquito">Mosquitoes</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bomber_Command" title="Bomber Command">Bomber Command</a> attacked the city. According to the Bomber Command Campaign Diary, "This raid was a complete success achieved on a completely cloud-covered target of small size, with only a moderate bomber effort and at trifling cost."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Hagen sustained severe damage from that raid, and hundreds of <a href="/wiki/German_casualties_in_World_War_II#Air_raid_deaths" title="German casualties in World War II">civilians were killed</a>.</p>
<p>After World War II, the town became part of the new state of <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hagen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Owing to the extensive use of water power along the rivers Ruhr, Lenne, Volme and Ennepe, metal processing played an important role in the region of Hagen in and even before the 15th century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the 17th and 18th century, <a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">textile</a> and steel industries as well as <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a> production followed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Hagen is the home of the Suedwestfaelische Industrie- und Handelskammer, as well as <a href="/wiki/Sparkasse_Hagen" title="Sparkasse Hagen">Sparkasse Hagen</a>, the local <a href="/wiki/German_public_bank" title="German public bank">municipal savings bank</a>. The bank's former headquarters, the <a href="/wiki/Sparkasse_Hagen_tower" title="Sparkasse Hagen tower">Sparkasse Hagen tower</a>, was a regional landmark until its demolition in 2004.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The city is heavily indebted and in the process of cutting city services in order to balance its budget.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hagen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of the five branches of <a href="/wiki/South_Westphalia_University_of_Applied_Sciences" title="South Westphalia University of Applied Sciences">South Westphalia University of Applied Sciences</a> is located in the city (also: Fachhochschule Südwestfalen (FH SWF)) which offers various engineering programmes.The inception of this institution in the city dates back to 1824.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions">Attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hagen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hagen is home to the <i>LWL-Freilichtmuseum Hagen</i>, or <a href="/wiki/Hagen_Westphalian_Open-Air_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Hagen Westphalian Open-Air Museum">Hagen Westphalian Open-Air Museum</a>, a collection of historic industrial facilities where trades such as printing, brewing, smithing, milling, and many others are represented not simply as static displays, but as living, working operations that visitors may in some cases even be invited to participate in. It is located near the Hagen community of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eilpe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eilpe (page does not exist)">Eilpe</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Historisches_Centrum_Hagen" title="Historisches Centrum Hagen">Historisches Centrum Hagen</a> includes the <a href="/wiki/City_museum" class="mw-redirect" title="City museum">city museum</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Werdringen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Werdringen (page does not exist)">Werdringen</a> castle. In the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bl%C3%A4tterh%C3%B6hle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Blätterhöhle (page does not exist)">Blätterhöhle</a> cave in Hagen the oldest fossils of modern people in <a href="/wiki/Westphalia" title="Westphalia">Westphalia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr" title="Ruhr">Ruhr</a> were found. Some date back to the early <a href="/wiki/Mesolithic" title="Mesolithic">Mesolithic</a>, 10,700 years B.C. It seems that the descendants of Mesolithic people in this area maintained a foraging lifestyle for more than 2000 years after the arrival of farming societies.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traffic">Traffic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hagen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Traffic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg/220px-Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg/330px-Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg/440px-Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4672" data-file-height="3104" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hagen-Vorhalle_20080830.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Shunting yard Hagen-Vorhalle</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Autobahn" title="Autobahn">Autobahnen</a> <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_1" title="Bundesautobahn 1">A1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_45" title="Bundesautobahn 45">A45</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_46" title="Bundesautobahn 46">A46</a> touch Hagen.</p>
<p>Hagen has been an important rail junction for the southeastern Ruhr valley since the first rail line opened in 1848. The <a href="/wiki/Shunt_(rail)" class="mw-redirect" title="Shunt (rail)">shunting</a> yard of Hagen-Vorhalle is among Germany's largest, and the <a href="/wiki/Hagen_Hauptbahnhof" title="Hagen Hauptbahnhof">central station</a> offers connections to the <a href="/wiki/InterCityExpress" class="mw-redirect" title="InterCityExpress">ICE</a> network of <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bahn" title="Deutsche Bahn">Deutsche Bahn</a> as well as to local and <a href="/wiki/S-Bahn" class="mw-redirect" title="S-Bahn">S-Bahn</a> services. Since December 2005, Hagen has also been the starting point for a new service into <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/Abellio_Deutschland" title="Abellio Deutschland">Abellio Deutschland</a>.</p>
<p>Local traffic is handled by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hagener_Stra%C3%9Fenbahn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hagener Straßenbahn (page does not exist)">Hagener Straßenbahn</a> (Hagen Tramways), which, despite its name, offers only bus services, as the last <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tramway</a> route in Hagen was abandoned in May 1976. All local rail and bus services operate under the transport association <a href="/wiki/Verkehrsverbund_Rhein-Ruhr" title="Verkehrsverbund Rhein-Ruhr">VRR</a>.</p>
