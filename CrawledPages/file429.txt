<p><b>Mülheim an der Ruhr</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈmyːlhaɪm ʔan deːɐ̯ ˈʁuːɐ̯]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-M%C3%BClheim_(Ruhr).ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a6/De-M%C3%BClheim_%28Ruhr%29.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Mülheim (Ruhr).ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>), also described as <i>"City on the River"</i>, is a city in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr Area">Ruhr Area</a> between <a href="/wiki/Duisburg" title="Duisburg">Duisburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essen,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Essen, Germany">Essen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oberhausen" title="Oberhausen">Oberhausen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ratingen" title="Ratingen">Ratingen</a>. It is home to many companies, especially in the food industry, such as the <a href="/wiki/Aldi" title="Aldi">Aldi Süd Company</a>, the Harke Group and the <a href="/wiki/Tengelmann_Group" title="Tengelmann Group">Tengelmann Group</a>.</p>
<p>Mülheim received its town charter in 1808, and 100 years later the population exceeded 100,000, making Mülheim officially a city. At the time of the city's 200th anniversary with approximately 170,000 residents, it was counted among the smaller cities of Germany.</p>
<p>Mülheim was the first city in the Ruhr Area to become completely free of coal mines, when its last coal mine "Rosenblumendelle" was closed. The former leather and coal city had successfully made a complete transformation to a diversified economic centre. With more than 50% covered by greenery and forest, the city is regarded as an attractive place to live between <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> and the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr" title="Ruhr">Ruhr</a>. It is the home of two <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Society" title="Max Planck Society">Max Planck Institutes</a> and, since 2009, the technical college Ruhr West. It has a <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BClheim_(Ruhr)_Hauptbahnhof" title="Mülheim (Ruhr) Hauptbahnhof">station</a> on the important railway between <a href="/wiki/Witten/Dortmund%E2%80%93Oberhausen/Duisburg_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Witten/Dortmund–Oberhausen/Duisburg Railway">Dortmund and Duisburg</a> and is served by <a href="/wiki/Rhine-Ruhr_S-Bahn" title="Rhine-Ruhr S-Bahn">Rhine-Ruhr S-Bahn</a> lines <a href="/wiki/S1_(Rhine-Ruhr_S-Bahn)" title="S1 (Rhine-Ruhr S-Bahn)">S1</a> and <a href="/wiki/S3_(Rhine-Ruhr_S-Bahn)" title="S3 (Rhine-Ruhr S-Bahn)">S3</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%BClheim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_location">Geographical location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%BClheim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geographical location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mülheim an der Ruhr lies at the junction of the hill country of the Lower <a href="/wiki/Bergisches_Land" title="Bergisches Land">Bergisches Land</a>, the western <a href="/wiki/Hellweg" title="Hellweg">Hellweg</a> and the plane of the middle <a href="/wiki/Lower_Rhine_region_(Germany)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lower Rhine region (Germany)">Lower Rhine region</a>.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ruhrtalbruecke-Sonnenuntergang.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Ruhrtalbruecke-Sonnenuntergang.jpg/220px-Ruhrtalbruecke-Sonnenuntergang.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Ruhrtalbruecke-Sonnenuntergang.jpg/330px-Ruhrtalbruecke-Sonnenuntergang.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Ruhrtalbruecke-Sonnenuntergang.jpg/440px-Ruhrtalbruecke-Sonnenuntergang.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2904" data-file-height="1923" /></a>

<p>The Ruhr leaves the foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Rhenish_Massif" title="Rhenish Massif">Rhenish Massif</a> and enters the <a href="/wiki/Lower_Rhine_Plain" title="Lower Rhine Plain">Lower Rhine Plain</a> at Mülheim. The Ruhr flows through the city from south-east to north-west for a distance of 14&#160;km (9 miles) dividing it into two districts: Broich on its left (western) bank and Kirchenhügel (Church Hill) on its right (east) bank. The central city lies on both banks of the river, about 12 kilometres (7 miles) east of where the Ruhr discharges into the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a>. Life in Mülheim is intermediate between the more urban, downstream, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> and the more rural life of the upstream <a href="/wiki/Ruhr" title="Ruhr">Ruhr</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-M.C3.BClheim_Location_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M.C3.BClheim_Location-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%BClheim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The northern foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Rhenish_Massif" title="Rhenish Massif">Rhenish Massif</a> are characterised by the distinctive rock formation of the bare mountain slopes through which run coal-bearing layers which formed during the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">carboniferous</a> period. Here the Ruhr cuts more than 50 meters deep into this <a href="/wiki/Mittelgebirge" title="Mittelgebirge">Mittelgebirge</a>. This natural <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">erosion</a> partly uncovered these mineable <a href="/wiki/Bituminous_coal" title="Bituminous coal">black coal</a> deposits, which enabled their exploration and extraction using <a href="/wiki/Adit" title="Adit">adits</a>. However, the coal-rich layers became ever deeper as one progressed northward, which required setting up <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mines</a> to extract the black coal. In contrast, the broad <a href="/wiki/Bayou" title="Bayou">bayou</a> (dead arm of a river) of Styrum borough is characteristic of the features of the Lower Rhine Plain.<sup id="cite_ref-M.C3.BClheim_Geology_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M.C3.BClheim_Geology-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
