<p>A <b>religious denomination</b> is a subgroup within a <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> that operates under a common name, tradition, and identity.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:502px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Prevailing_world_religions_map.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Prevailing_world_religions_map.png/500px-Prevailing_world_religions_map.png" width="500" height="231" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Prevailing_world_religions_map.png/750px-Prevailing_world_religions_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Prevailing_world_religions_map.png/1000px-Prevailing_world_religions_map.png 2x" data-file-width="1357" data-file-height="628" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Prevailing_world_religions_map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Major denominations and religions of the world.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The term refers to the various <a href="/wiki/Christian_denomination" title="Christian denomination">Christian denominations</a> (for example, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a>, and the many varieties of <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestantism</a>). It is also used to describe the four branches of <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a> (<a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Judaism" title="Conservative Judaism">Conservative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reform_Judaism" title="Reform Judaism">Reform</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Reconstructionist_Judaism" title="Reconstructionist Judaism">Reconstructionist</a>). Within Islam, it can refer to the branches or sects (such as <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shia_Islam" title="Shia Islam">Shia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya" title="Ahmadiyya">Ahmadiyya</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> as well as their various subdivisions such as sub-sects,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Madhhab" title="Madhhab">schools of jurisprudence</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Schools_of_Islamic_theology" title="Schools of Islamic theology">schools of theology</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and religious movements.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The world's largest religious denomination is <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni Islam</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Christianity">Christianity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_denomination&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Christianity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Christian_denomination" title="Christian denomination">Christian denomination</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Christian_denominations" title="List of Christian denominations">List of Christian denominations</a></div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Christian_denomination" title="Christian denomination">Christian denomination</a> is a generic term for a distinct religious body identified by traits such as a common name, structure, leadership and doctrine. Individual bodies, however, may use alternative terms to describe themselves, such as church or fellowship. Divisions between one group and another are defined by doctrine and church authority; issues such as the nature of Jesus, the authority of apostolic succession, eschatology, and papal primacy often separate one denomination from another. Groups of denominations often sharing broadly similar beliefs, practices, and historical ties are known as branches of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judaism">Judaism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_denomination&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Judaism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Jewish_religious_movements" title="Jewish religious movements">Jewish religious movements</a></div>
<p>Jewish religious movements, sometimes called "denominations" or "branches", include different groups which have developed among <a href="/wiki/Jew" class="mw-redirect" title="Jew">Jews</a> from ancient times. Today, the main division is between the <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reform_Judaism" title="Reform Judaism">Reform</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Judaism" title="Conservative Judaism">Conservative</a> lines, with several smaller movements alongside them. This threefold denominational structure is mainly present in the United States, while in Israel the fault lines are between the religious Orthodox and the <a href="/wiki/Jewish_secularism" title="Jewish secularism">non-religious</a>.</p>
<p>The movements differ in their views on various issues. These issues include the level of observance, the methodology for interpreting and understanding <a href="/wiki/Halakha" title="Halakha">Jewish law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Historical_criticism" title="Historical criticism">biblical authorship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Textual_criticism" title="Textual criticism">textual criticism</a>, and the nature or role of <a href="/wiki/Messiah_in_Judaism" title="Messiah in Judaism">the messiah</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Messianic_age" class="mw-redirect" title="Messianic age">messianic age</a>). Across these movements there are marked differences in <a href="/wiki/Jewish_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish services">liturgy</a>, especially in the language in which services are conducted, with the more traditional movements emphasizing Hebrew. The sharpest theological division occurs between Orthodox and non-Orthodox Jews who adhere to other denominations, such that the non-Orthodox movements are sometimes referred to collectively as the "liberal denominations" or "progressive streams."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hinduism">Hinduism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_denomination&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hinduism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Hinduism" title="History of Hinduism">History of Hinduism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hindu_denominations" title="Hindu denominations">Hindu denominations</a></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a>, the major deity or philosophical belief identifies a denomination, which also typically has distinct cultural and religious practices. The major denominations include <a href="/wiki/Shaivism" title="Shaivism">Shaivism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaktism" title="Shaktism">Shaktism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaishnavism" title="Vaishnavism">Vaishnavism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smartism" class="mw-redirect" title="Smartism">Smartism</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Islam">Islam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_denomination&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Islam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Islam" title="History of Islam">History of Islam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muslim_denominations" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim denominations">Muslim denominations</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/History_of_Islam" title="History of Islam">Historically</a>, Islam was divided into three major sects well known as <a href="/wiki/Sunni" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunni">Sunni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khawarij" title="Khawarij">Khawarij</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sh%C4%AB%E2%80%98ah" class="mw-redirect" title="Shī‘ah">Shī‘ah</a>. Nowadays, <a href="/wiki/Sunnis" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunnis">Sunnis</a> constitute more than 85% of the overall <a href="/wiki/Muslim_population" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim population">Muslim population</a> while the <a href="/wiki/Shi%27as" class="mw-redirect" title="Shi'as">Shi'as</a> are slightly more than 12%.<sup id="cite_ref-PEW2009_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PEW2009-10">[10]</a></sup> Today, many of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_extinct_Shia_sects" title="List of extinct Shia sects">Shia sects are extinct</a>. The major surviving <a href="/wiki/Imamah" class="mw-redirect" title="Imamah">Imamah</a>-<a href="/wiki/Muslim_Sects" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim Sects">Muslim Sects</a> are <a href="/wiki/Usulism" class="mw-redirect" title="Usulism">Usulism</a> <i>(with nearly more than 10%),</i> <a href="/wiki/Nizari_Ismailism" class="mw-redirect" title="Nizari Ismailism">Nizari Ismailism</a> <i>(with nearly more than 1%),</i> <a href="/wiki/Alevism" title="Alevism">Alevism</a> <i>(with slightly more than 0.5%<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but less than 1%<sup id="cite_ref-Near_East.27_1997_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Near_East.27_1997-12">[12]</a></sup>).</i> The other existing groups include <a href="/wiki/Zaydi_Shi%27a" class="mw-redirect" title="Zaydi Shi'a">Zaydi Shi'a</a> of <a href="/wiki/Religion_in_Yemen" title="Religion in Yemen">Yemen</a> whose population is nearly more than 0.5% of the world's Muslim population, <a href="/wiki/Musta%E2%80%99li_Ismaili" class="mw-redirect" title="Musta’li Ismaili">Musta’li Ismaili</a> <i>(with nearly 0.1%<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> whose <a href="/wiki/Taiyabi" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiyabi">Taiyabi</a> adherents reside in <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a> state in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> city in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. There are also significant <a href="/wiki/Diaspora" title="Diaspora">diaspora</a> populations in Europe, North America, the Far East and <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a><sup id="cite_ref-paul_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paul-14">[14]</a></sup>),</i> and <a href="/wiki/Ibadis" class="mw-redirect" title="Ibadis">Ibadis</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Kharijites" class="mw-redirect" title="Kharijites">Kharijites</a> <a href="/wiki/Ibadi#Demographics" title="Ibadi">whose population has diminished to a level below 0.15%</a>. On the other hand, new <a href="/wiki/Muslim_sects" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim sects">Muslim sects</a> like <a href="/wiki/African_American_Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="African American Muslims">African American Muslims</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ahmadi_Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Ahmadi Muslims">Ahmadi Muslims</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <i>(with nearly around 1%<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>),</i> <a href="/wiki/Non-denominational_Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-denominational Muslims">non-denominational Muslims</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quranist_Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Quranist Muslims">Quranist Muslims</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wahhabis" class="mw-redirect" title="Wahhabis">Wahhabis</a> <i>(<a href="/wiki/Wahhabism#Population" title="Wahhabism">with nearly around 0.5%</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> of the world's total <a href="/wiki/Muslim_population" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim population">Muslim population</a>)</i> were later independently developed.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Multi-denominational">Multi-denominational</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_denomination&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Multi-denominational">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term "multi-denominational" may describe (for example) a <a href="/wiki/Religious" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious">religious</a> event that includes several religious denominations from sometimes unrelated religious groups. Many <a href="/wiki/Civic_engagement" title="Civic engagement">civic</a> events include religious portions led by representatives from several religious denominations to be as inclusive or representational as possible of the expected population or audience. For example: the Sunday <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving" title="Thanksgiving">thanksgiving</a> mass at Campamento Esperanza (English: <a href="/wiki/Camp_Hope" title="Camp Hope">Camp Hope</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Church_service" title="Church service">services</a> were led by both a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priest</a> and by an <a href="/wiki/Evangelical" class="mw-redirect" title="Evangelical">Evangelical</a> <a href="/wiki/Preacher" title="Preacher">preacher</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/2010_Copiap%C3%B3_mining_accident" title="2010 Copiapó mining accident">2010 Copiapó mining accident</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sundaymassbbc_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundaymassbbc-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sundaymass_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundaymass-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chaplains" class="mw-redirect" title="Chaplains">Chaplains</a> - frequently <a href="/wiki/Ordained" class="mw-redirect" title="Ordained">ordained</a> <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">clergy</a> of any <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> - are often assigned<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (January 2015)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> to <a href="/wiki/Secular" class="mw-redirect" title="Secular">secular</a> organizations to provide <a href="/wiki/Spirituality" title="Spirituality">spiritual</a> support to its members who may belong to any of many different religions or denominations. Many of these chaplains, particularly those serving with the <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> or other large secular organizations, are specifically trained to minister to members of many different faiths, even faiths with opposing religious <a href="/wiki/Ideology" title="Ideology">ideology</a> from that of the chaplain's own faith.<sup id="cite_ref-prisonchap_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prisonchap-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Military organizations that do not have large numbers of members from several individual smaller but related denominations will routinely hold multi-denominational religious services, often generically called "<a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a>" Sunday services, so minority Protestant denominations are not left out or unserved.<sup id="cite_ref-obamachurch_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obamachurch-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-forthood_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forthood-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
