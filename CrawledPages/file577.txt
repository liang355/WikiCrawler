<p><b>Allston</b> is an officially recognized <a href="/wiki/Neighborhoods_in_Boston" title="Neighborhoods in Boston">neighborhood</a> of the City of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. It was named after the American painter and poet <a href="/wiki/Washington_Allston" title="Washington Allston">Washington Allston</a>. It comprises the land covered by the zip code 02134.<sup id="cite_ref-bestplaces_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bestplaces-1">[1]</a></sup> For the most part, Allston is administered collectively with the adjacent neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Brighton,_Boston" title="Brighton, Boston">Brighton</a>. The two are often referred to together as "<a href="/wiki/Allston%E2%80%93Brighton" title="Allston–Brighton">Allston–Brighton</a>." <a href="/wiki/Boston_Police_Department" title="Boston Police Department">Boston Police Department</a> District D-14 covers the Allston-Brighton area and a Boston Fire Department Allston station is located in Union Square which houses Engine 41 and Ladder 14. Engine 41 is nicknamed "The Bull" to commemorate the historic stockyards of Allston.</p>
<p>Housing stock varies but largely consists of brick apartment buildings, especially on Commonwealth Avenue and the streets directly off it, while areas further down Brighton Avenue, close to Brighton, are largely dotted with wooden <a href="/wiki/Triple-decker" title="Triple-decker">triple-deckers</a>. Lower Allston, across the Massachusetts Turnpike from the rest of Allston, consists of mostly 1890–1920s single-family and multi-family <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian</a> homes.</p>

<p>The estimated population of Allston is 29,196, according to the 2010 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The median home cost is $317,000, a decline of 0.97% in the last year. The cost of living is 9.81% higher than the national average. The population density is 18,505/mi<sup>2</sup>, about 50% higher than the citywide average of 12,166. The median age is 29.2. 76.45% of residents list status as single.<sup id="cite_ref-bestplaces_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bestplaces-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allston is home to many immigrant populations, the largest groups being from <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a> (particularly <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>), <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> (particularly <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Young adults (age 18-34) make up 78.3% of the neighborhood's population (as compared to 39.4% for the city of Boston as a whole).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/College_town" title="College town">high concentration of students and "twenty-somethings"</a> has created tension between some long-time <a href="/wiki/Town_and_gown" title="Town and gown">residents and the student population</a> which constantly cycles in and out as students matriculate and graduate from Boston's many colleges and universities. In addition to nightly dancing and live music at area bars, house parties abound on surrounding streets, particularly during the school year. This has long been a sore point among other Allston residents.<sup id="cite_ref-Phoenix_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Phoenix-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The largest religious affiliation is <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church_in_the_United_States" title="Catholic Church in the United States">Catholic</a> (44.96%), followed by <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> (4.77%), unspecified Christian (4.62%), <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a> (3.58%), <a href="/wiki/Baptists" title="Baptists">Baptist</a> (2.10%), and <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_the_United_States" title="Islam in the United States">Muslim</a> (1.97%).<sup id="cite_ref-bestplaces_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bestplaces-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Area_description">Area description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Area description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The neighborhood of Allston is almost completely cut off from the main body of the city of Boston by the town of <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline</a>, which borders Allston on the south and southeast. It is connected to the rest of Boston only by a small portion of its eastern border that is shared with the Fenway/Kenmore neighborhood. Allston is bordered on the east and north by the <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles River</a>, separating it from the city of Cambridge, and is split in two by the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Turnpike" title="Massachusetts Turnpike">Massachusetts Turnpike</a>.</p>
<p>The area north of the turnpike near the Charles river is known as Lower Allston (or North Allston). It consists of streets north of Cambridge Street and the Turnpike, all the way to the Charles River. It extends westward to Everett Street and eastward to the Charles River. In its center is Allston Square at the crossroads of Western Avenue and North Harvard Street. Allston is named for the great painter and 1800 Harvard graduate, Washington Allston, "The Father of American Romanticism". Allston Square is appropriately located halfway between Harvard Square in the North and Allston Village, Boston's 'Greenwich Village' in the South. Allston is the only community in America named for an artist.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lower Allston is a small neighborhood that consists of a mix of young professionals, blue-collar tradesmen, members of the educational community, homeowners, and long-term residents. Unlike the rest of Allston, Lower Allston has far fewer students. The neighborhood is very quiet, has extremely low crime,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and is an easy walk to Allston Village or <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Square" title="Harvard Square">Harvard Square</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lower Allston has close proximity to Route 2, the Mass Pike, Storrow Drive, and Soldiers Field Road. Public transportation includes the Red Line at Harvard Square, the Green Line at Packard's Corner or Harvard Avenue and Commonwealth Avenue and the 57, 66, 70, 71, and 86 bus connections on North Harvard Street and Western Avenue.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the early 21st century, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> announced dramatic expansion plans that called for major building projects, including the demolition of existing businesses, to prepare for the construction of new biology and science buildings in the northern sections of Lower Allston.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> While the existing building stock was demolished and businesses were evicted, the financial crisis of 2008 and the resultant decrease in Harvard's endowment caused the university to suspend the expansion projects. In 2016, Harvard began building again, has completed two new buildings and is starting on the new, state-of-the-art Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences on Western Avenue west of Allston Square by the Charles River. Later, it will begin construction of the "Gateway" building on the northeast corner of Allston Square.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Allston was an eastern section of the former town of <a href="/wiki/Brighton,_Boston" title="Brighton, Boston">Brighton</a>.</p>
<p>In 1867 a new railroad depot for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_and_Albany_Railroad" title="Boston and Albany Railroad">Boston and Albany Railroad</a> opened. In 1868 the station and post office in Brighton's eastern portion were given the name "Allston" after <a href="/wiki/Washington_Allston" title="Washington Allston">Washington Allston</a>, the noted <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painter</a> who had lived and worked across the <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles River</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeport,_Cambridge" title="Cambridgeport, Cambridge">Cambridgeport</a> section of Cambridge.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It can even be said to have been named for a specific painting: Washington Allston's "Fields West of Boston."</p>
<p>Allston has never existed as a separate political entity in its own right. The Town of Brighton was annexed by the City of Boston in 1874. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Wadsworth_Longfellow" title="Henry Wadsworth Longfellow">Henry Wadsworth Longfellow</a> owned several properties in Allston. In 1887 the wooden depot was replaced by the station depicted at the right. In 1888 Boston's first trolley route began there, running a route through <a href="/wiki/Coolidge_Corner" title="Coolidge Corner">Coolidge Corner</a>, Brookline, to Boylston Street, to downtown Boston.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Allston community developed largely around large <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">railroad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a> operations. The <a href="/wiki/Boston_and_Albany_Railroad" title="Boston and Albany Railroad">Boston and Albany Railroad</a> (now <a href="/wiki/CSX_Transportation" title="CSX Transportation">CSX</a>) operated a major <a href="/wiki/Rail_yard" title="Rail yard">yard</a>. Stockyards and a large <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse" title="Slaughterhouse">abattoir</a> operated nearby in the northern part of Brighton. Much of the railroad yard remains in use today as the <a href="/wiki/CSX_Transportation" title="CSX Transportation">CSX Transportation</a> Beacon Park Yard, but all livestock activity ended by the mid-20th century. CSX plans to move its yard operation west, allowing the plot to be redeveloped by Harvard.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>A strip running from Brighton Avenue in Allston out <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Avenue_(Boston)" title="Commonwealth Avenue (Boston)">Commonwealth Avenue</a> toward <a href="/wiki/Kenmore_Square" title="Kenmore Square">Kenmore Square</a> was Boston's original "Automile", lined with <a href="/wiki/Car" title="Car">automobile</a> dealerships. Packard's Sales Stable and Riding School<sup id="cite_ref-Packards_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Packards-13">[13]</a></sup> gave <a href="/wiki/Packard%27s_Corner" title="Packard's Corner">Packard's Corner</a> its name, which was then perpetuated by the presence of an opulent <a href="/wiki/Packard" title="Packard">Packard</a> dealership. Only a <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">Toyota</a> dealer and a <a href="/wiki/Vespa" title="Vespa">Vespa</a> dealer remain, but the windowed buildings along the eastern end of Brighton Avenue reflect this history.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Turnpike" title="Massachusetts Turnpike">Massachusetts Turnpike</a> Extension, built largely on part of the Boston and Albany right-of-way, opened through Allston in 1964 and 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-pike_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pike-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Allston is home to numerous small businesses and restaurants. Brighton Avenue, between Packard's Corner and Allston Street, boasts various ethnic and national cuisines from around the world. Harvard Avenue hosts a number of furniture stores, thrift shops, and stores that offer items for resale, due to the large student body and high residential turnover.</p>
<p>The section of the neighborhood that lies immediately south of the turnpike and centers on the stretch of Harvard Avenue between <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Avenue_(Boston)" title="Commonwealth Avenue (Boston)">Commonwealth Avenue</a> and Cambridge Street also houses many shops, bars, and restaurants. Recent business promotion initiatives have dubbed this area "Allston Village."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Allston_&quot;Christmas&quot;"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Allston_.22Christmas.22">Allston "Christmas"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Allston &quot;Christmas&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Every year, in the days leading up to and following September 1, Allston experiences a period known as <i>Allston Christmas</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Boston.com1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boston.com1-16">[16]</a></sup> This period is referred to as such because it is the time of year when renters (many of whom are college students) move out their things so new renters (also frequently college students) can move in. The renters who are leaving put their unwanted possessions out in the streets for those who may want the items, which include couches, tables, kitchenware and clothing.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Heights_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heights-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Music venues in Allston include Brighton Music Hall (formerly <a href="/wiki/Harpers_Ferry_(nightclub)" title="Harpers Ferry (nightclub)">Harpers Ferry</a>), Great Scott, O'Brien's Pub, <a href="/wiki/Paradise_Rock_Club" title="Paradise Rock Club">Paradise Rock Club</a>, and Wonder Bar.<sup id="cite_ref-Globe1_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Globe1-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guardian1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian1-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Several recording studios are located in the neighborhood, such as Galaxy Park, established in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Allston's music scene includes a <a href="/wiki/Do_it_yourself" title="Do it yourself">DIY</a> community. The annual Allston-Brighton parade and annual <a href="/wiki/Allston_DIY_Fest" title="Allston DIY Fest">Allston DIY Fest</a> feature many of the neighborhood's musical acts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Major League Baseball's <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Boston_Braves" title="History of the Boston Braves">Boston Braves</a> played at <a href="/wiki/Braves_Field" title="Braves Field">Braves Field</a> (now Boston University's <a href="/wiki/Nickerson_Field" title="Nickerson Field">Nickerson Field</a>) at Allston's eastern edge, from 1915 to 1952. The <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_New_England_Patriots" title="History of the New England Patriots">Boston Patriots</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Football_League" title="American Football League">American Football League</a> (now the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Patriots" title="New England Patriots">New England Patriots</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a>) played four seasons in Allston: at Nickerson Field in 1960 through 1962; and at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Stadium" title="Harvard Stadium">Harvard Stadium</a> in 1970.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_schools">Public schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Public schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Public schools in Allston are part of the <a href="/wiki/School_district" title="School district">school district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Boston_Public_Schools" title="Boston Public Schools">Boston Public Schools</a>.</p>
<p>Gardner Pilot Academy (also called the Thomas Gardner School), located on Athol Street, serves Allston residents pre-kindergarten through grade five.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In April 2008 a science teacher at Gardner Pilot Academy won the "Ultimate Science Classroom", a raffle prize furnished annually by the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Teachers_Association" title="National Science Teachers Association">National Science Teachers Association</a>. The school received approximately $40,000 in science teaching materials and apparatus.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Horace_Mann_School_for_the_Deaf_and_Hard_of_Hearing" title="Horace Mann School for the Deaf and Hard of Hearing">Horace Mann School for the Deaf and Hard of Hearing</a>, located on Armington Street, is the oldest public school for the hearing impaired in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The school was attended by <a href="/wiki/Helen_Keller" title="Helen Keller">Helen Keller</a> (Shichtman, 23) and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Graham_Bell" title="Alexander Graham Bell">Alexander Graham Bell</a>'s work at the school inspired him to begin experiments in an apparatus to help deaf children hear. These experiments eventually led to the telephone (MacKenzie, 56-57). The school serves the hearing impaired in Boston from pre-kindergarten through twelfth grade.</p>

<p>Jackson Mann School, also on Armington Street, serves residents from kindergarten through eighth grade.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Colleges_and_universities">Colleges and universities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Colleges and universities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Allston lies near two major universities. A substantial part of the campus of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_School" title="Harvard Business School">Harvard Business School</a> and most athletic facilities (such as the <a href="/wiki/Bright-Landry_Hockey_Center" title="Bright-Landry Hockey Center">Bright-Landry Hockey Center</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Stadium" title="Harvard Stadium">Harvard Stadium</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Lavietes_Pavilion" title="Lavietes Pavilion">Lavietes Pavilion</a>), are in North Allston. Harvard also owns large portions of other land in North Allston, much of which it plans to develop as an academic campus, particularly as an auxiliary site for the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Medical_School" title="Harvard Medical School">Harvard Medical School</a> and other healthcare-related programs. Eventually, Harvard's Allston campus will be larger than their Cambridge campus. <a href="/wiki/Boston_University" title="Boston University">Boston University</a> lies along <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Avenue_(Boston)" title="Commonwealth Avenue (Boston)">Commonwealth Avenue</a> to the east. The <a href="/wiki/New_Balance" title="New Balance">New Balance</a> Field of Boston University symbolizes further integration of BU into the Allston community and is the first in a series of projects that will include the creation of a major new dormitory building in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Berklee_College_of_Music" title="Berklee College of Music">Berklee College of Music</a> also has a practice and rehearsal building near Commonwealth Avenue, on Fordham Road, which runs between Commonwealth Avenue and Brighton Avenue.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_%22B%22_Branch" title="Green Line &quot;B&quot; Branch">"B" Branch</a> of the Boston <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Transportation_Authority" title="Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority">MBTA</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">subway</a> <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_(MBTA)" title="Green Line (MBTA)">Green Line</a> runs through the neighborhood along Commonwealth Avenue. The former <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_%22A%22_Branch" title="Green Line &quot;A&quot; Branch">"A Line"</a> of the Green Line to <a href="/wiki/Watertown_Square_(MBTA_station)" title="Watertown Square (MBTA station)">Watertown Square</a> ran along Brighton Avenue, and was replaced by MBTA bus route 57. Other MBTA bus lines serve Allston, including routes 64, 66, 70 and 86. 43.88% of residents commute by mass transit, compared with 2% for the country as a whole.<sup id="cite_ref-bestplaces_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bestplaces-1">[1]</a></sup> Until 2013, the <a href="/wiki/CSX" class="mw-redirect" title="CSX">CSX</a> Railroad operated the large <a href="/wiki/Beacon_Park_Yard" title="Beacon Park Yard">Beacon Park freight yard</a> which runs adjacent to the Massachusetts Turnpike; the land has been purchased by Harvard. From 2014 to March 2016, the MBTA included bus route 57 in its late night service, running until 3 am.<sup id="cite_ref-57_Bus_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57_Bus-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2006, Harvard officials said that they would like to establish a <a href="/wiki/MBTA_Commuter_Rail" title="MBTA Commuter Rail">commuter rail</a> stop in Allston on the Framingham/Worcester line.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> This would restore service lacking since the closure of the long-dormant Allston train depot. As of 2009<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, there had been actions by the state legislature to restore train service in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In June 2012, plans were announced for a station to be called <a href="/wiki/Boston_Landing_(MBTA_station)" title="Boston Landing (MBTA station)">Boston Landing</a>, located in Brighton, to serve the Allston-Brighton area. Originally intended to open in 2014, the station finally opened in 2017.</p>
<p>In September 2014, plans for a $25 million commuter rail station nicknamed "West Station"<sup id="cite_ref-west_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-west-29">[29]</a></sup> were announced. The station's construction will coincide with a plan to straighten the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Turnpike" title="Massachusetts Turnpike">Massachusetts Turnpike</a> over the former CSX rail yard. The new station will initially operate exclusively on the Worcester/Framingham line, but will be constructed with four platforms to accommodate future uses that may include rapid-transit service to North Station via the existing Grand Junction rail line through Cambridge and Somerville.<sup id="cite_ref-west_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-west-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Allston <a href="/wiki/Water_gun" title="Water gun">Squirt Gun</a> Day is an unsanctioned event organized by young residents and held yearly in late August. Participants are asked to wear green and gather at the intersection of Harvard Avenue and Brighton Avenue.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The event is typically short-lived as it creates a panic among those unaware of what is going on.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 2009, the Squirt Gun festivities continued as a sanctioned event at nearby Ringer Park.</li>
<li>In 2005, the New England Foundation for the Arts selected a site in Allston for its Art &amp; Community Landscapes program.<sup id="cite_ref-NEFA_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NEFA-33">[33]</a></sup> The artist team of Legge Lewis Legge<sup id="cite_ref-Legge_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Legge-34">[34]</a></sup> was chosen to design this site which is known as the Lincoln Street Green Strip.<sup id="cite_ref-Lincoln-Street_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lincoln-Street-35">[35]</a></sup></li>
<li>Each year the community hosts the Allston Village Street Fair on Harvard Avenue between Brighton Avenue and Cambridge Street. The fair features live performances, international food, and local businesses, along with family-friendly activities.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2012, Aerosmith played a set in front of their home at 1325 Commonwealth. They were introduced by the New England Patriots players (most notably <a href="/wiki/Tom_Brady" title="Tom Brady">Tom Brady</a>), owner <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kraft" title="Robert Kraft">Robert Kraft</a>, and the cheerleaders.<sup id="cite_ref-boston.com_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boston.com-37">[37]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Allston's <a href="/wiki/ZIP_Code" title="ZIP Code">ZIP Code</a> 02134 is famously identified due to a recurring musical piece on the <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> children's series <i><a href="/wiki/Zoom_(1972_TV_series)" title="Zoom (1972 TV series)">ZOOM</a></i> — whose originating station, <a href="/wiki/WGBH-TV" title="WGBH-TV">WGBH</a>, was located on Western Avenue east of Allston Square until 2008. Allston was created in 1868 when the town of Brighton, due to the construction of the trolley, grew too large for a single post office to service its residents. Allston was created as a separate and autonomous community with its own zip code, 02134. The two communities have evolved into very different places since 1868. One of the few things they still have in common today is that the Brighton Police Department, Division 14, provides law enforcement for both communities. The border runs along Everett Street to the west, continuing along Gordon Street and Kelton Street. On the south the border is Brookline and to the east and north it is the Charles River.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Big_D_and_the_Kids_Table" title="Big D and the Kids Table">Big D and the Kids Table</a>, a ska band, makes multiple references to Allston within their songs.</li>
</ul>
