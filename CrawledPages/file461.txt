<p><b>Aichach-Friedberg</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is bounded by (from the northwest and clockwise) the districts of <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_(district)" title="Augsburg (district)">Augsburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donau-Ries" title="Donau-Ries">Donau-Ries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neuburg-Schrobenhausen" title="Neuburg-Schrobenhausen">Neuburg-Schrobenhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pfaffenhofen_(district)" title="Pfaffenhofen (district)">Pfaffenhofen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dachau_(district)" title="Dachau (district)">Dachau</a>, <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrstenfeldbruck_(district)" title="Fürstenfeldbruck (district)">Fürstenfeldbruck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Landsberg_(district)" title="Landsberg (district)">Landsberg</a>, as well as by the city of <a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aichach-Friedberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aichach-Friedberg was settled by Bavarian tribes from the seventh century on. The region is sometimes called the cradle of Bavaria, since the <a href="/wiki/Burg_Wittelsbach" class="mw-redirect" title="Burg Wittelsbach">castle of Wittelsbach</a> was located close to the present city of <a href="/wiki/Aichach" title="Aichach">Aichach</a>. It was the ancestral castle of the Wittelsbach family, who were rulers of Bavaria for thousand years. The castle was razed to the ground in 1208, and today there is nothing else left than a memorial stone at the place.</p>
<p>The town of <a href="/wiki/Friedberg,_Bavaria" title="Friedberg, Bavaria">Friedberg</a> was founded in the 13th century in order to collect a toll from people using the bridge across the <a href="/wiki/Lech_(river)" title="Lech (river)">Lech</a> River. Aichach became a town about hundred years later. In 1862 the two districts of Aichach and Friedberg were founded. They were merged in 1972 and became part of the administrative region of <a href="/wiki/Swabia" title="Swabia">Swabia</a>. Anyhow, historically Aichach-Friedberg does not belong to Swabia, but to <a href="/wiki/Old_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Old Bavaria">Old Bavaria</a>. The name of the new district was originally <i>Augsburg-Ost</i> ("Augsburg East"), but it was changed to Aichach-Friedberg in 1973.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aichach-Friedberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district is located to the east of the city of <a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a> and comprises a rural area with few major towns. The Lech River forms the western border of the district. Another river, the <a href="/wiki/Paar" title="Paar">Paar</a> (an affluent of the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>), enters the district in the southwest, runs through <a href="/wiki/Aichach" title="Aichach">Aichach</a> and leaves to the northeast.</p>
<p>The territory is also known as <b>Wittelsbacher Land</b>, due to the castle of Wittelsbach near Aichach.</p>
