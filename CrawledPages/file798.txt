				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Helen Hennessy Vendler</b> (born April 30, 1933,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">MA</a>) is an American literary critic and is the A. Kingsley Porter University Professor at Harvard University.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Helen_Vendler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vendler has written books on <a href="/wiki/Emily_Dickinson" title="Emily Dickinson">Emily Dickinson</a>, <a href="/wiki/W._B._Yeats" title="W. B. Yeats">W. B. Yeats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallace_Stevens" title="Wallace Stevens">Wallace Stevens</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Keats" title="John Keats">John Keats</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Heaney" title="Seamus Heaney">Seamus Heaney</a>. She has been a professor of English at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> since 1984; between 1981 and 1984 she taught alternating semesters at Harvard and Boston University.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1990 she was appointed to an endowed chair as the A. Kingsley Porter University Professor. She is the first woman to hold this position. She has also taught at Cornell University, Swarthmore and Smith Colleges, and Boston University. She married (then later divorced) the philosopher <a href="/wiki/Zeno_Vendler" title="Zeno Vendler">Zeno Vendler</a> with whom she had one son. In 1992 Vendler received a Litt. D. from <a href="/wiki/Bates_College" title="Bates College">Bates College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vendler did not major in English as an undergraduate. She earned an A.B. in chemistry at <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_College_(Massachusetts)" title="Emmanuel College (Massachusetts)">Emmanuel College</a>. She was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Fulbright_Fellowship" class="mw-redirect" title="Fulbright Fellowship">Fulbright Fellowship</a> for mathematics, before earning her Ph.D. in <a href="/wiki/English_Literature" class="mw-redirect" title="English Literature">English</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/American_Literature" class="mw-redirect" title="American Literature">American Literature</a> from Harvard.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She has also been a judge for the Pulitzer Prize and the National Book award, in poetry.</p>
<p>In 2004, the <a href="/wiki/National_Endowment_for_the_Humanities" title="National Endowment for the Humanities">National Endowment for the Humanities</a> selected Vendler for the <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Lecture" title="Jefferson Lecture">Jefferson Lecture</a>, the U.S. federal government's highest honor for achievement in the <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">humanities</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jefflect_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jefflect-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Vendler's lecture, entitled "The Ocean, the Bird, and the Scholar,"<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> used a number of poems by <a href="/wiki/Wallace_Stevens" title="Wallace Stevens">Wallace Stevens</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> to argue for the role of the arts (as opposed to history and philosophy) in the study of humanities.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Academy_of_Science_and_Letters" title="Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters">Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
