<p><b>Moers</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈmœʁs]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Moers.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4a/De-Moers.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Moers.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>; older form: <i>Mörs</i>; archaic <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>Murse</i>, <i>Murs</i> or <i>Meurs</i>) is a German city on the western bank of the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a>. Moers belongs to the district of <a href="/wiki/Wesel_(district)" title="Wesel (district)">Wesel</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Murs.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Murs.jpg/220px-Murs.jpg" width="220" height="222" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Murs.jpg/330px-Murs.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Murs.jpg/440px-Murs.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="553" /></a>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moers#Geschichte" class="extiw" title="de:Moers">w:de:Moers §&#160;Geschichte</a>, and <a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grafschaft_Moers" class="extiw" title="de:Grafschaft Moers">w:de:Grafschaft Moers</a></div>
<p>Known earliest from 1186, the county of Moers was an independent principality within the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years' War">Eighty Years' War</a> it was alternately captured by Spanish and Dutch troops, as it bordered the Upper Quarter of <a href="/wiki/Guelders" title="Guelders">Guelders</a>. During the war it <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Meurs_(1597)" title="Siege of Meurs (1597)">finally fell</a> to <a href="/wiki/Maurice_of_Orange" class="mw-redirect" title="Maurice of Orange">Maurice of Orange</a>. As it was separated from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> by <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Netherlands" title="Spanish Netherlands">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Upper_Guelders" title="Upper Guelders">Upper Guelders</a> it did not become an integral part of the Republic, though Dutch troops were stationed there.</p>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_England" title="William III of England">William III of Orange</a> in 1702, Moers was inherited by the king of <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a>. All Dutch troops and civil servants were expelled.</p>
<p>In 1795 it was annexed by France. At the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a>, in 1815 it was returned to Prussia and in 1871 it became part of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a>.</p>
<p>A target of the <a href="/wiki/Oil_Campaign_chronology_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil Campaign chronology of World War II">Oil Campaign of World War II</a>, the <i>Steinkohlenbergwerke</i> (English: coal mine) <i>Rheinpreussen</i> synthetic oil plant in Moers,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was partially dismantled <a href="/wiki/Post-war" title="Post-war">post-war</a>.</p>
