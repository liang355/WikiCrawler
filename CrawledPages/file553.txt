<p>The <b>Association of Independent Colleges and Universities in Massachusetts (AICUM),</b> is a grouping of <a href="/wiki/Higher_education_accreditation" title="Higher education accreditation">accredited</a>, independent, <a href="/wiki/Private_university" title="Private university">private colleges</a> and <a href="/wiki/Private_university" title="Private university">universities</a> in the state of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. The Association advocates in public policy forums on behalf of its member institutions.<sup id="cite_ref-About_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>AICUM is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>, and it was established by a panel of university presidents. Its current president is Richard Doherty.<sup id="cite_ref-Contact_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Contact-2">[2]</a></sup> AICUM is a member of the Coalition for College Cost Savings, and the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Independent_Colleges_and_Universities" title="National Association of Independent Colleges and Universities">National Association of Independent Colleges and Universities</a><sup id="cite_ref-CCCS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCCS-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NAICU_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NAICU-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
