			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Fair Harvard</h1>

<p>"<b>Fair Harvard</b>" is the <a href="/wiki/Alma_mater_(song)" title="Alma mater (song)">alma mater</a> of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>. Written by <a href="/wiki/The_Reverend" title="The Reverend">the Reverend</a> <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Gilman" title="Samuel Gilman">Samuel Gilman</a> of the class of 1811 for the university's 200th anniversary in 1836, it bids the school an affectionate farewell. Of its four verses, the first and fourth are traditionally sung and the second and third omitted. Its first line was revised to read "...we join in thy jubilee throng" between 1997 and 1998. As a side effect of the change, the word <i>throng</i>, a verb in the original lyrics, became a noun.</p>
<p>The song is set to a traditional Irish air, best known in early 19th century America as "<a href="/wiki/Believe_Me,_if_All_Those_Endearing_Young_Charms" title="Believe Me, if All Those Endearing Young Charms">Believe Me, if All Those Endearing Young Charms</a>", a popular song whose lyrics were written by the <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> poet <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Moore" title="Thomas Moore">Thomas Moore</a>. The tune is occasionally wrongly credited to <a href="/wiki/Sir_William_Davenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir William Davenant">Sir William Davenant</a>, whose library may have been a source of the music for later publishers.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (The tune is also a newer setting of "My Lodging Is In The Cold, Cold Ground".) <a href="/wiki/Horatio_Alger,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Horatio Alger, Jr.">Horatio Alger, Jr.</a>, an 1852 graduate of Harvard's Divinity School, composed his "Harvard Odes" I-IV, and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Laurence_Dunbar" title="Paul Laurence Dunbar">Paul Laurence Dunbar</a> originally wrote the lyrics of the "<a href="/wiki/Tuskegee_University" title="Tuskegee University">Tuskegee</a> Song", to the tune.</p>
<p>The music makes an appearance in the 1951 animated cartoon <i><a href="/wiki/Ballot_Box_Bunny" title="Ballot Box Bunny">Ballot Box Bunny</a></i> featuring <a href="/wiki/Bugs_Bunny" title="Bugs Bunny">Bugs Bunny</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yosemite_Sam" title="Yosemite Sam">Yosemite Sam</a>, as well as in the opening bars of the 1982-83 pop hit "<a href="/wiki/Come_on_Eileen" class="mw-redirect" title="Come on Eileen">Come on Eileen</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Dexy%27s_Midnight_Runners" class="mw-redirect" title="Dexy's Midnight Runners">Dexy's Midnight Runners</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_Text">Official Text</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Harvard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Official Text">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Below is the full text of the official lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<div class="poem">
<p>Fair Harvard! we join in thy Jubilee throng,<br />
And with blessings surrender thee o’er<br />
By these Festival-rites, from the Age that is past,<br />
To the Age that is waiting before.<br />
O Relic and Type of our ancestors’ worth,<br />
That hast long kept their memory warm,<br />
First flow’r of their wilderness! Star of their night!<br />
Calm rising thro' change and through storm.<br />
<br />
Farewell! be thy destinies onward and bright!<br />
To thy children the lesson still give,<br />
With freedom to think, and with patience to bear,<br />
And for Right ever bravely to live.<br />
Let not moss-covered Error moor thee at its side,<br />
As the world on Truth’s current glides by,<br />
Be the herald of Light, and the bearer of Love,<br />
Till the stock of the Puritans die.<br />
<br />
[Revised 1998]</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_Text">Original Text</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fair_Harvard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Original Text">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Below is the full text of the original lyrics.</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<div class="poem">
<p>Fair Harvard! thy sons to thy Jubilee throng,<br />
And with blessings surrender thee o'er<br />
By these festival rites, from the age that is past,<br />
To the age that is waiting before.<br />
O relic and type of our ancestors' worth<br />
That hast long kept their memory warm,<br />
First flow'r of their wilderness! Star of their night!<br />
Calm rising thro' change and thro' storm.<br />
<br />
To thy bow'rs we were led in the bloom of our youth,<br />
From the home of our infantile years,<br />
When our fathers had warn'd, and our mothers had pray'd,<br />
And our sisters had blest thro' their tears.<br />
Thou then wert our parent, the nurse of our soul;<br />
We were molded to manhood by thee,<br />
Till freighted with treasure thoughts, friendships and hopes,<br />
Thou didst launch us on Destiny's sea.<br />
<br />
When as pilgrims we come to revisit thy halls,<br />
To what kindlings the season gives birth!<br />
Thy shades are more soothing, thy sunlight more dear,<br />
Than descend on less privileged earth.<br />
For the good and the great, in their beautiful prime,<br />
Thro' thy precincts have musingly trod,<br />
As they girded their spirits or deepen'd the streams<br />
That make glad the fair city of God.<br />
<br />
Farewell! be thy destinies onward and bright!<br />
To thy children the lesson still give,<br />
With freedom to think, and with patience to bear,<br />
And for right ever bravely to live.<br />
Let not moss-covered error moor thee at its side,<br />
As the world on truth's current glides by<br />
Be the herald of light, and the bearer of love,<br />
Till the stock of the Puritans die.</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
