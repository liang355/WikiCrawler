<p><b>Harvard Forest</b> is an ecological research area of 3,000 acres (12&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) owned and managed by <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> and located in <a href="/wiki/Petersham,_Massachusetts" title="Petersham, Massachusetts">Petersham, Massachusetts</a>. The property, in operation since 1907, includes one of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>'s oldest managed forests, educational and research facilities, a museum, and recreation trails. Harvard Forest is open to the public.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_education">Research and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Research and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harvard University conducts forest <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conservation_biology" title="Conservation biology">conservation</a> research on the property, including two major programs: the Long Term Ecological Research <a href="/wiki/LTER" class="mw-redirect" title="LTER">LTER</a> program, funded by the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a> and established in 1988 to investigate New England's natural <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>; and the National Institute of Global Environmental Change program established and funded by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">United States Department of Energy</a> and emphasizing the study of physical and biological processes in relation to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. Harvard Forest is also slated to be the northeastern core site of the <a href="/wiki/National_Ecological_Observatory_Network" title="National Ecological Observatory Network">National Ecological Observatory Network</a>. A number of smaller research programs are also conducted in Harvard Forest.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Fisher_Museum">The Fisher Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Fisher Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Fisher Museum, open on weekends, offers exhibits on current research as well as twenty-three model <a href="/wiki/Diorama" title="Diorama">dioramas</a> portraying the history, conservation and management of <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> woodlands. A sixty-seat lecture hall is located in the museum.</p>
<p>Harvard Forest staff and students conduct educational programs and guided natural history tours for adults and children. Self-guided interpretive trails are located on the property, as well as several miles of primitive roads and trails, open to the public for non motorized recreation including hiking, wildlife observation, mountain biking, cross country skiing, hunting (in season) and similar passive pursuits. Camping, fires, and motorized vehicles are not allowed. An observation tower is located on the forest's Prospect Hill Tract.</p>
<p>The Fisher Museum and primary trailhead are located on <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Route_32" title="Massachusetts Route 32">Massachusetts Route 32</a> north of Petersham center.</p>
<p>Harvard Forest is part of an area of over 4,000 acres (16&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of protected open space including several properties owned by <a href="/wiki/The_Trustees_of_Reservations" title="The Trustees of Reservations">The Trustees of Reservations</a>, state forest land, and the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Audubon_Society" title="Massachusetts Audubon Society">Massachusetts Audubon Society</a>'s <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rutland_Brook_Sanctuary&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rutland Brook Sanctuary (page does not exist)">Rutland Brook Sanctuary</a>.</p>
