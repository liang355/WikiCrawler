<p><b>Increase Mather</b> (June 21, 1639 O.S. – August 23, 1723 O.S.) was a major figure in the early history of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Colony" title="Massachusetts Bay Colony">Massachusetts Bay Colony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Massachusetts_Bay" title="Province of Massachusetts Bay">Province of Massachusetts Bay</a> (now the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth (United States)">Commonwealth</a> of Massachusetts). He was a <a href="/wiki/Puritan" class="mw-redirect" title="Puritan">Puritan</a> <a href="/wiki/Minister_(religion)" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister (religion)">minister</a> who was involved with the government of the colony, the administration of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>, and most notoriously, the <a href="/wiki/Salem_witch_trials" title="Salem witch trials">Salem witch trials</a>. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Mather" title="Richard Mather">Richard Mather</a>, and the father of <a href="/wiki/Cotton_Mather" title="Cotton Mather">Cotton Mather</a>, both influential Puritan ministers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mather was born in <a href="/wiki/Dorchester,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Dorchester, Massachusetts">Dorchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Colony" title="Massachusetts Bay Colony">Massachusetts Bay Colony</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-colombia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colombia-1">[1]</a></sup> on June 21, 1639 to Rev. <a href="/wiki/Richard_Mather" title="Richard Mather">Richard Mather</a> and Kathrine Holt Mather,<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> following their participation in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Migration_(Puritan)" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Migration (Puritan)">Great Migration</a> from England due to <a href="/wiki/Nonconformism" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonconformism">nonconformity</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dorch_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dorch-3">[3]</a></sup> He was the youngest of six<sup id="cite_ref-bio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-4">[4]</a></sup> brothers, the others being: Samuel, Nathaniel, Eleazar, Joseph, and Timothy.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-4">[4]</a></sup> Three of his brothers (Samuel, Nathaniel and Eleazar) also became ministers.<sup id="cite_ref-1911rm_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1911rm-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1651 Mather was admitted to <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> where he roomed with and studied under Robert Massey. When he graduated in 1656 with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">B.A.</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> he began to train for the <a href="/wiki/Christian_ministry" title="Christian ministry">ministry</a> and gave his first sermon on his 18th birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup> He quickly left Massachusetts and went to Ireland, where he studied at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a> for an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">M.A.</a>. He graduated in 1659,<sup id="cite_ref-colombia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colombia-1">[1]</a></sup> and spent the next 3 years as a <a href="/wiki/Chaplain" title="Chaplain">chaplain</a> attached to a garrison in the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>. During his time at Trinity College he was licensed as a Commonwealth Minister by <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a> to the joint charge of St. Tida's Church at <a href="/wiki/Ballyscullion" title="Ballyscullion">Ballyscullion</a>, and St. Swithan's Church in <a href="/wiki/Magherafelt" title="Magherafelt">Magherafelt</a>. On Cromwell's death in 1658 his joint charge at these South Londonderry churches was quickly severed by the new authorities. Harvard later awarded him the first <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">honorary degree</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>, becoming a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Sacred_Theology" title="Doctor of Sacred Theology">Doctor of Sacred Theology</a>, in 1692.<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishing_himself_in_Massachusetts">Establishing himself in Massachusetts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Establishing himself in Massachusetts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1661, with the advent of the <a href="/wiki/English_Restoration" class="mw-redirect" title="English Restoration">English Restoration</a> and resurgence of <a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglicanism</a>, Increase returned to Massachusetts, where he married Maria Cotton. She was his step-sister by virtue of his father's marriage to Sarah Hankredge, widow of <a href="/wiki/John_Cotton_(puritan)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Cotton (puritan)">John Cotton</a> and mother of Maria.<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup> She gave birth to <a href="/wiki/Cotton_Mather" title="Cotton Mather">Cotton Mather</a> in 1663. In 1676, he published <i>A Brief History of the War with the Indians in New-England</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> a contemporary account of <a href="/wiki/King_Philip%27s_War" title="King Philip's War">King Philip's War</a>.</p>
<p>He was <a href="/wiki/Ordination" title="Ordination">ordained</a> as minister of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Church,_Boston" title="Second Church, Boston">North Church</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> whose congregation included many of the <a href="/wiki/Upper_class" title="Upper class">upper class</a> and governing class, on May 27, 1664. He held this post until he died.<sup id="cite_ref-colombia_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colombia-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Harvard_University">Harvard University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Harvard University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On June 11, 1685 he became the Acting President of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> (then Harvard College) and steadily advanced: A little over a year later on July 23, 1686 he was appointed the <a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">Rector</a>. On June 27, 1692, he became the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Harvard_University" title="President of Harvard University">President of Harvard</a>, a position which he held until September 6, 1701.<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> He was rarely present on campus or in the town, especially during his term of Rector as he was out of the Colony for all but two years of his term in that office. Despite his absences he did make some changes: re implementation of <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a> instruction, replacement of classical Roman authors with <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Biblical</a> and Christian authors in <a href="/wiki/Ethics" title="Ethics">ethics</a> classes, enactment of requirements that students attend classes regularly, live and eat on campus, and that seniors not <a href="/wiki/Hazing" title="Hazing">haze</a> other students.<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvement_in_politics">Involvement in politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Involvement in politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While politics and Puritan religion were closely related during Increase's lifetime, his first direct involvement with politics occurred as a result of <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_England" title="James II of England">James II of England</a>'s manipulation of the New England governments. In 1686 James revoked the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Charter" title="Massachusetts Charter">Charter of Massachusetts</a> in the process of creating the <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_New_England" title="Dominion of New England">Dominion of New England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-colombia_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colombia-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Dominion was headed by <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Andros" title="Edmund Andros">Edmund Andros</a>, who not only disliked puritanism and was haughty,<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> but ruled as a near absolute dictator: <a href="/wiki/Town_meeting" title="Town meeting">Town meetings</a> were outlawed, leaving the Dominion without consent of the governed, marriage was removed from the clergy, and the <a href="/wiki/Old_South_Church" title="Old South Church">Old South Church</a> was temporarily appropriated for <a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglican</a> services.<sup id="cite_ref-ish_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ish-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 1687 <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_Indulgence" title="Declaration of Indulgence">Declaration of Indulgence</a>, prohibiting discrimination against <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Catholics</a>, saw staunch opposition from the Puritan establishment. When Mather successfully roused opposition to revocation of the charter, he was nearly framed for <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a>. He traveled to London (eluding spies out to catch him) to petition the King. While engaged in petitioning he published pieces to build popular support for his positions, such as <i>A Narrative of the Miseries of New-England, By Reason of an <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship" title="Dictatorship">Arbitrary Government</a> Erected there Under Sir Edmund Andros</i> (1688) and <i>A Brief Relation for the Confirmation of Charter Privileges</i> (1691).<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>He attempted to restore the old charter<sup id="cite_ref-colombia_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colombia-1">[1]</a></sup> and obtain a royal charter for Harvard; however, he abandoned that course and changed his petitions to a new charter not lacking any of the rights previously granted. Following the <a href="/wiki/Glorious_Revolution" title="Glorious Revolution">Glorious Revolution</a> and <a href="/wiki/1689_Boston_revolt" title="1689 Boston revolt">subsequent overthrow</a> of Andros, a new charter was granted to the colony.<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> The 1692 charter was a major departure from its predecessor, granting sweeping <a href="/wiki/Home_rule" title="Home rule">home rule</a>, establishing an elective <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislature</a>, enfranchising all <a href="/wiki/Fee_simple" title="Fee simple">freeholders</a> (previously only men admitted to a congregation could vote), and uniting the Massachusetts Bay Colony and <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Colony" title="Plymouth Colony">Plymouth Colony</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> Following Andros' deposition and arrest,<sup id="cite_ref-ish_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ish-9">[9]</a></sup> he had <a href="/wiki/William_Phips" title="William Phips">William Phips</a> appointed as Royal Governor and they returned to Massachusetts, arriving on May 14, 1692.<sup id="cite_ref-UMKC_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UMKC-10">[10]</a></sup> Following his return, the administration of Harvard grew increasingly insistent that he reside nearer to the institution. Not wanting to leave his Second Church, he did not do so, and eventually resigned the Presidency.<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvement_in_the_Salem_witch_trials">Involvement in the Salem witch trials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Involvement in the Salem witch trials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As an influential member of the community, having handpicked Governor Phips and his deputy Stoughton, Mather was involved in the notorious witch hysteria of <a href="/wiki/Salem,_Massachusetts" title="Salem, Massachusetts">Salem, Massachusetts</a>. As the court of <a href="/wiki/Oyer_and_terminer" title="Oyer and terminer">oyer and terminer</a> was beginning to hear cases of suspected witchcraft, Increase's son Cotton wrote a letter known as "The Return of Several Ministers Consulted", which urged caution but failed to denounce the use of "<a href="/wiki/Spectral_evidence" title="Spectral evidence">spectral evidence</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-va_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-va-11">[11]</a></sup> In June and July 1692 as the trials and executions grew, Increase Mather made a number of sermons interpreted as a plea to cool the heated atmosphere.<sup id="cite_ref-va_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-va-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 1692 he published <i>Cases of Conscience Concerning Evil Spirits Personating Men, Witchcrafts, infallible Proofs of Guilt in such as are accused with that Crime</i> (more commonly known as just "Cases of conscience concerning evil spirits"), which defended the judges and trials, but strongly denounced the spectral evidence used by them. It said, <i>"It were better that Ten Suspected Witches should escape, than that one Innocent Person should be Condemned."</i> (A slightly altered version of this phrase would later become known as <a href="/wiki/Blackstone%27s_formulation" title="Blackstone's formulation">Blackstone's formulation</a>.) Notwithstanding this, his reputation was not improved afterwards or for posterity due to his association with the trials as well as his subsequent refusal, for whatever reasons, to denounce them.<sup id="cite_ref-UMKC_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UMKC-10">[10]</a></sup> He was also briefly mentioned in a thorough treatment of his son Cotton by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Calef" title="Robert Calef">Robert Calef</a> in his comprehensive book of the Salem Trials and their aftermath, <i>More Wonders of the Invisible World</i><sup id="cite_ref-va-rc_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-va-rc-12">[12]</a></sup> (referred to as <i>More Wonders of the Spiritual World</i> by the <a href="/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A6dia_Britannica_Eleventh_Edition" title="Encyclopædia Britannica Eleventh Edition">Encyclopædia Britannica Eleventh Edition</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup> Increase Mather was said to have burned Calef's book in <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_death">Later life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Later life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In August 1714, following the death of his wife Maria, he married, to Ann (maiden name unknown). On September 27, 1722, he <a href="/wiki/Loss_of_consciousness" class="mw-redirect" title="Loss of consciousness">fainted</a> and was bedridden thereafter. In August 1723 he suffered <a href="/wiki/Urinary_bladder" title="Urinary bladder">bladder failure</a> and died three weeks later on August 23, 1723, in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, aged 84. He was buried on <a href="/wiki/Copp%27s_Hill" title="Copp's Hill">Copp's Hill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mhs_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mhs-13">[13]</a></sup> Before his death, he took lodging at the retreat of Mineral Spring Pond to recover from his illness and drink from the famous healing waters of the springs from <a href="/wiki/Spring_Pond" class="mw-redirect" title="Spring Pond">Spring Pond</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beliefs">Beliefs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Beliefs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Throughout his life Mather was a staunch Puritan, opposing anything openly contradictory to, mutually exclusive with, or potentially "distracting" from his religious beliefs. He supported suppression of <a href="/wiki/Substance_intoxication" title="Substance intoxication">intoxication</a>, unnecessary effort on Sundays and <a href="/wiki/Sumptuary_law" title="Sumptuary law">ostentatious clothing</a>. He was initially opposed to the <a href="/wiki/Half-Way_Covenant" title="Half-Way Covenant">Half-Way Covenant</a> but later supported it. He firmly believed in the direct appearance of God's disfavor in everyday life, e.g. the weather, political situations, attacks by Native Americans, fires and floods, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UMKC_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UMKC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was strenuous in attempting to keep people to his idea of morality,<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> making strong use of <a href="/wiki/Jeremiad" title="Jeremiad">jeremiads</a> to try to prevent indifference and especially to try to get government officials to enforce public morality. During his tenure at Harvard he regularly stamped out any relaxation of Puritan strictness, such as <a href="/wiki/Latitudinarianism" class="mw-redirect" title="Latitudinarianism">latitudinarianism</a>, which had flourished during his overseas absence.<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following his acceptance of the Covenant, <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Stoddard" title="Solomon Stoddard">Solomon Stoddard</a> and others attempted to further liberalize Puritanism by <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a> of children who had nonmember parents<sup id="cite_ref-harv_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harv-2">[2]</a></sup> and admittance of all but the openly immoral to services.<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup> To try and stop this, Mather had a <a href="/wiki/Synod" title="Synod">synod</a> called to outlaw similar measures. A declaration was adopted, but never made binding.<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup> Following this, reform-minded members were sent to the body and it took on a less conservative tone, bitterly disappointing Mather.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Given_name">Given name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Increase_Mather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Given name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stated reason for his first name was "...the never-to-be-forgotten <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/increase" class="extiw" title="wikt:increase">increase</a>, of every sort, wherewith God favoured the country about the time of his nativity."<sup id="cite_ref-ref1911_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1911-6">[6]</a></sup> The name "<a href="/wiki/Increase_(given_name)" title="Increase (given name)">Increase</a>" is a literal translation of the Hebrew "Yosëf" (<a href="/wiki/Joseph" title="Joseph">Joseph</a>).</p>
