			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Fight Fiercely, Harvard</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p>"<b>Fight Fiercely, Harvard</b>" is a <a href="/wiki/Satirical" class="mw-redirect" title="Satirical">satirical</a> <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">college</a> <a href="/wiki/Fight_song" title="Fight song">fight song</a> written and originally performed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Lehrer" title="Tom Lehrer">Tom Lehrer</a> and dedicated to his <a href="/wiki/Alma_mater" title="Alma mater">alma mater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-everything2.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-everything2.com-1">[1]</a></sup> The song was written in 1945 while Lehrer was in his second year of study at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a><sup id="cite_ref-rogerdeitz.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rogerdeitz.com-2">[2]</a></sup> and "eventually made him a local hero".<sup id="cite_ref-Odell_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Odell-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fight_Fiercely,_Harvard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was Lehrer's earliest—and for a while his only—musical work and was included on <i><a href="/wiki/Songs_by_Tom_Lehrer" title="Songs by Tom Lehrer">Songs by Tom Lehrer</a></i>, a debut album recorded at Trans Radio Studios, <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, on January 22, 1953.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Starting with a <a href="/wiki/Press_run" class="mw-redirect" title="Press run">press run</a> of 400 copies of the then-novel <a href="/wiki/LP_album" class="mw-redirect" title="LP album">10" LP record</a> format produced by Lehrer at his own expense, these records were sold in stores around <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Square" title="Harvard Square">Harvard Square</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In speaking about "Fight Fiercely, Harvard" and the rest of his satirical repertoire that brought him a certain level of fame as an <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate" class="mw-redirect" title="Undergraduate">undergraduate</a>, Lehrer says he did not write to gain popularity but rather:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I mostly thought these songs weren’t of interest to anyone! I wrote ‘Fight Fiercely, Harvard’ in 1945. That’s the earliest song on any of my records. The others were written a little later. I wrote a lot of songs for special occasions ... silly songs, songs about math, and so on, but nothing else that would be recordable until 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-Odell_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Odell-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Lehrer has also said of his undergraduate <a href="/wiki/Songwriting" class="mw-redirect" title="Songwriting">songwriting</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>The only one of those songs that eventually made it into my repertoire was 'Fight Fiercely, Harvard,' which was written in 1945 and shows it. I started singing these songs at parties, but never with the intention of their becoming commercial.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Lehrer later responded to rumors that he had stopped performing because the displeasure of Harvard University's administration over the satirical lyrics of "Fight Fiercely, Harvard" threatened to ruin his career, by saying:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>...my stimulus came from humor however grim the humor may have been at the base. I wasn't burning when I wrote these songs...it amazes me that anyone would think a song making fun of the football team was grounds for expulsion.<sup id="cite_ref-Odell_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Odell-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Today "Fight Fiercely, Harvard" lives on in the repertoire of the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University_Band" title="Harvard University Band">Harvard University Band</a><sup id="cite_ref-hcs.harvard.edu_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hcs.harvard.edu-7">[7]</a></sup> and was included on their 75th anniversary album released in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The song remains fairly well known in the Harvard community.<sup id="cite_ref-everything2.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-everything2.com-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Odell_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Odell-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On visiting Harvard for the first time in decades, Lehrer was delighted to discover that his song had been performed at every home football game for many years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theme">Theme</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fight_Fiercely,_Harvard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Theme">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The humor of "Fight Fiercely, Harvard" revolves around the supposedly refined and <a href="/wiki/Gentleman" title="Gentleman">gentlemanly</a> nature of Harvard <a href="/wiki/College_athletics" title="College athletics">athletes</a>. As it appears on <i>Tom Lehrer Revisited</i>, a live album, it is preceded by banter almost as long as the song itself. In talking to his audience, Lehrer explains that he wanted to create a college fight song different from those performed, as he says with a <a href="/wiki/Hypercorrection" title="Hypercorrection">hypercorrective</a> use of a <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <a href="/wiki/Pluralization" class="mw-redirect" title="Pluralization">plural</a> for a common <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> word, "in comparable <i><a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadia</a></i>." This preamble also says, in part:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>...I was reminded not too long ago, upon returning from my lesson with the Scrabble pro at the Harvard Club in Boston, of the days of my undergraduacy long ago when there used to be these very long Saturday afternoons in the fall with nothing to do - the library was closed - just waiting around for the cocktail parties to begin. And on occasions like that...did come to realize that the football fight songs that one hears...have a tendency to be somewhat uncouth, and even violent, and that it would be refreshing, to say the least, to find one that was a bit more genteel. And here it is, dedicated to my own alma mater, and called Fight Fiercely, Harvard.<sup id="cite_ref-everything2.com_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-everything2.com-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The lyrics are written with an exaggeratedly <a href="/wiki/Erudition" title="Erudition">erudite</a> style of <a href="/wiki/Diction" title="Diction">diction</a> and say in part:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Fight fiercely, Harvard!<br />
Fight, fight, fight!<br />
Demonstrate to them our skill.<br />
Albeit they possess the might,<br /></p>
Nonetheless we have the will.<sup id="cite_ref-hcs.harvard.edu_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hcs.harvard.edu-7">[7]</a></sup></blockquote>
<p>The lyrics also refer to celebrating victory by inviting the "whole team up for <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a>" and very conscientiously warn "let's try not to injure them." Lehrer, who is actually <a href="/wiki/Jew" class="mw-redirect" title="Jew">Jewish</a> and from <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, performed the song in the <a href="/wiki/Stereotype" title="Stereotype">stereotypical</a> <a href="/wiki/Boston_Brahmin_accent" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Brahmin accent">Boston Brahmin accent</a> of his on-stage <a href="/wiki/Persona" title="Persona">persona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pronunciation" title="Pronunciation">pronounced</a> the words "fiercely" and "Harvard" with a <a href="/wiki/Rhotic_and_non-rhotic_accents" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhotic and non-rhotic accents">non-rhotic</a> <a href="/wiki/Drawl" title="Drawl">drawl</a>. He also injected exclamations such as "...and <i>do</i> fight fiercely!"<sup id="cite_ref-rogerdeitz.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rogerdeitz.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hcs.harvard.edu_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hcs.harvard.edu-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fight_Fiercely,_Harvard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Harvardiana" title="Harvardiana">Harvardiana</a>", a song which refers to <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(Harvard-Yale)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Game (Harvard-Yale)">Harvard's rivalry with Yale</a></li>
</ul>
<p>"Sports Song," written by <a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic" title="&quot;Weird Al&quot; Yankovic">"Weird Al" Yankovic</a>, also gives a satirical spin to the traditional football fight song (the video, choreographed and directed by Yankovic himself, even uses a real college band). Unlike Lehrer's song, this one unashamedly "levels" with football fans, and states the true point of fight songs using blunt statements such as "We're great, and you suck."</p>
