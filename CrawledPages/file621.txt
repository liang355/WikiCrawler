<p><b>Harvard Square</b> is a triangular plaza at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Avenue_(Boston)" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts Avenue (Boston)">Massachusetts Avenue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brattle_Street_(Cambridge,_Massachusetts)" title="Brattle Street (Cambridge, Massachusetts)">Brattle Street</a>, and John F. Kennedy Street, near the center of <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, United States. The term "Harvard Square" is also used to delineate the business district and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> surrounding that intersection, which is the historic center of Cambridge.<sup id="cite_ref-urlMACRIS.CAM.AB_Details_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlMACRIS.CAM.AB_Details-2">[2]</a></sup> Adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a>, the historic heart of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-urlMACRIS.CAM.AD_Details_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlMACRIS.CAM.AD_Details-3">[3]</a></sup> the Square (as it is sometimes called locally) functions as a commercial center for Harvard students, as well as residents of western Cambridge and the inner western and northern suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>. These residents use the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_(MBTA_station)" title="Harvard (MBTA station)">Harvard station</a>, a major <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Transportation_Authority" title="Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority">MBTA</a> <a href="/wiki/Red_Line_(MBTA)" title="Red Line (MBTA)">Red Line</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">subway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bus" title="Bus">bus</a> <a href="/wiki/Transportation_hub" class="mw-redirect" title="Transportation hub">transportation hub</a>.</p>
<p>In an extended sense, the name "Harvard Square" can also refer to the entire neighborhood surrounding this intersection for several blocks in each direction. The nearby <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Common" title="Cambridge Common">Cambridge Common</a> has become a park area with a playground, baseball field, and a number of monuments, several relating to the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">Revolutionary War</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Square&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Harvard_Square_aerial_1.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Harvard_Square_aerial_1.JPG/300px-Harvard_Square_aerial_1.JPG" width="300" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Harvard_Square_aerial_1.JPG/450px-Harvard_Square_aerial_1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Harvard_Square_aerial_1.JPG/600px-Harvard_Square_aerial_1.JPG 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>

<p>The heart of Harvard Square is the junction of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Avenue_(Boston)" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts Avenue (Boston)">Massachusetts Avenue</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brattle_Street_(Cambridge,_Massachusetts)" title="Brattle Street (Cambridge, Massachusetts)">Brattle Street</a>. Massachusetts Avenue enters from the southeast (a few miles after crossing the <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles River</a> from Boston at <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a>), and turns sharply to the north at the intersection, which is dominated by a large pedestrian space incorporating the MBTA subway entrance, an international newsstand, a visitor information kiosk, and a small open-air performance space ("The Pit"). Brattle Street and John F. Kennedy Street merge from the southwest, joining Massachusetts Avenue at "Nini's Corner", where another newsstand is located. The <a href="/wiki/Harvard/MIT_Cooperative_Society" title="Harvard/MIT Cooperative Society">Harvard/MIT Cooperative Society</a> main building forms the western streetwall at the intersection, along with a bank and some retail shops.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nearby_places">Nearby places</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Square&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nearby places">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The walled enclosure of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a> is adjacent, with <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Extension_School" title="Harvard Extension School">Harvard Extension School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Art_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard Art Museum">Harvard Art Museums</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Semitic_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard Semitic Museum">Semitic Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Museum_of_Archaeology_and_Ethnology" title="Peabody Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology">Peabody Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="Harvard Museum of Natural History">Museum of Natural History</a> just short walks away.</p>
<p>Other institutions in the general neighborhood include the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Public_Library" title="Cambridge Public Library">Cambridge Public Library</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lesley_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Lesley College">Lesley College</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Longy_School_of_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Longy School of Music">Longy School of Music</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Divinity_School" title="Episcopal Divinity School">Episcopal Divinity School</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Rindge_and_Latin_School" title="Cambridge Rindge and Latin School">Cambridge Rindge and Latin School</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Repertory_Theater" title="American Repertory Theater">American Repertory Theater</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cooper-Frost-Austin_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Cooper-Frost-Austin House">Cooper-Frost-Austin House</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hooper-Lee-Nichols_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Hooper-Lee-Nichols House">Hooper-Lee-Nichols House</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Longfellow_House%E2%80%93Washington%27s_Headquarters_National_Historic_Site" title="Longfellow House–Washington's Headquarters National Historic Site">Longfellow House–Washington's Headquarters National Historic Site</a>.</p>
<p>The high pedestrian traffic makes Harvard Square a gathering place for street musicians and <a href="/wiki/Busker" class="mw-redirect" title="Busker">buskers</a>, who must obtain a permit from the Cambridge Arts Council. Singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Tracy_Chapman" title="Tracy Chapman">Tracy Chapman</a>, who attended nearby <a href="/wiki/Tufts_University" title="Tufts University">Tufts University</a>, is known to have played here during her college years. <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Palmer" title="Amanda Palmer">Amanda Palmer</a>, of <a href="/wiki/The_Dresden_Dolls" title="The Dresden Dolls">The Dresden Dolls</a>, regularly performed here as a "<a href="/wiki/Living_statue" title="Living statue">living statue</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Lotman_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lotman-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:145</sup> Another frequent performer over the years has been indie rock guitarist <a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Lord" title="Mary Lou Lord">Mary Lou Lord</a>. A small bronze statue of "Doo Doo" (a puppet created by <a href="/wiki/Igor_Fokin" title="Igor Fokin">Igor Fokin</a>) sits at the corner of Brattle and Eliot streets, in honor of Fokin and all the street performers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>Until 1984, the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_(MBTA_station)" title="Harvard (MBTA station)">Harvard Square stop</a> was the northern terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Line_(MBTA)" title="Red Line (MBTA)">Red Line</a>, and it still functions as a major transfer station between subway, bus, and trackless trolley. Automobile traffic can be heavy, and parking is difficult. Most of the bus lines serving the area from the north and west run through a tunnel adjacent to the subway tunnel. Originally built for <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">streetcars</a> (which last ran in 1958) and still used by <a href="/wiki/Trolleybus" title="Trolleybus">trackless trolleys</a> as well as ordinary buses, the tunnel lessens bus traffic in central Harvard Square, and lets buses cross the Square without encountering automobile traffic. The tunnel also allows safer and covered access between the subway and the buses.</p>
<p>At the center of the Square is the old <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Square_Subway_Kiosk" title="Harvard Square Subway Kiosk">Harvard Square Subway Kiosk</a>, now a newsstand, <a href="/wiki/Out_of_Town_News" class="mw-redirect" title="Out of Town News">Out of Town News</a>, stocking newspapers and magazines from around the world. A video of it appears in transitional clips used on <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (April 2015)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> A public motion art installation, <i><a href="/wiki/Lumen_Eclipse" title="Lumen Eclipse">Lumen Eclipse</a></i>, has been introduced at the Tourist Information Booth showing monthly exhibitions of local, national and international artists.</p>

<p>The office of <a href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="National Public Radio">NPR</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Car_Talk" title="Car Talk">Car Talk</a></i> radio show faces the square, with a stencil in the window that reads "<a href="/wiki/Dewey,_Cheatem_%26_Howe" title="Dewey, Cheatem &amp; Howe">Dewey, Cheetham &amp; Howe</a>," the fictional law firm often referenced on the show. The popular show has referenced this by asking its viewers to send in answers to the "Puzzler" to "Puzzler Tower, Car Talk Plaza, Harvard Square, Cambridge (our fair city), MA 02138".</p>
<p>The sunken region next to the newsstand and the subway entrance is called "The Pit". Its arena-like appearance attracts skateboarders and, more generally, young, high-school aged people from surrounding neighborhoods who are associated with countercultural movements such as the <a href="/wiki/Punk_subculture" title="Punk subculture">punk</a>, hardcore, <a href="/wiki/Straight_edge" title="Straight edge">straight edge</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Goth_subculture" title="Goth subculture">goth</a> subcultures. The contrast between these congregants, and the often older and more conservatively dressed people associated with nearby Harvard University and the businesses in the Square, occasionally leads to tension. Also, Harvard sports teams and clubs, including the track teams and all-male social clubs, are known to make use of this contrast through encouraging or sometimes forcing their newest members to engage in humorous or humiliating performances in "The Pit" as part of these members' initiations into the group. The overall scene was later fondly recalled by singer songwriter <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Palmer" title="Amanda Palmer">Amanda Palmer</a> as a hangout for "Cool People".<sup id="cite_ref-Lotman_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lotman-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:173</sup></p>

<p>Across the street to the east of The Pit, an outdoor <a href="/wiki/Au_Bon_Pain" title="Au Bon Pain">cafe</a> features always-busy tables for <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> players, including US Federation Life Master Murray Turnbull, who has displayed his "Play the Chessmaster" sign here since 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-Lotman_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lotman-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:134,204</sup></p>
<p>In the southwest area of the Square neighborhood, on Mount Auburn St, stands the <a href="/wiki/Igor_Fokin" title="Igor Fokin">Igor Fokin</a> Memorial.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This memorial, created by sculptor <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Simun" title="Konstantin Simun">Konstantin Simun</a>, pays tribute not only to the late "beloved puppeteer," but to all street performers that are an integral part of the square, especially during summer months.</p>
<p>A number of other public squares dot the surrounding streets, notably Brattle Square and Winthrop Square,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[note 1]</a></sup> with a wide variety of street performers throughout the year. Brattle Street itself is home to the <a href="/wiki/Brattle_Theater" class="mw-redirect" title="Brattle Theater">Brattle Theater</a> (a non-profit <a href="/wiki/Art_film" title="Art film">arthouse theater</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/American_Repertory_Theater" title="American Repertory Theater">American Repertory Theater</a>. The John F. Kennedy Memorial Park, one block further down JFK Street, is on the bank of the Charles River. <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Common" title="Cambridge Common">Cambridge Common</a> is two blocks north.</p>
<p>The Square often attracts activists for unconventional political factions and has its share of <a href="/wiki/Panhandler" class="mw-redirect" title="Panhandler">panhandlers</a>. Although <a href="/wiki/Tom_Magliozzi" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Magliozzi">Tom Magliozzi</a> has derided it as "the bum capital of the world", it's also very likely one of the world's best places to people-watch, having many benches, terraces, and sidewalk restaurants and cafes dedicated for that purpose, and an affluent, ambient shopping population to sustain most anyone's interest.</p>
<p>"The Garage" is a small, multi-story <a href="/wiki/Shopping_mall" title="Shopping mall">shopping mall</a>, named thus because it was formerly a <a href="/wiki/Multi-story_car_park" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-story car park">parking garage</a>. The original car ramp has been preserved, and is a central feature of this <a href="/wiki/Adaptive_reuse" title="Adaptive reuse">adaptive reuse</a> project. One of the main attractions in The Garage is a <a href="/wiki/Newbury_Comics" title="Newbury Comics">Newbury Comics</a> store.</p>

<p>Although today a commercial center, the Square had famous residents in earlier periods, including the colonial poet <a href="/wiki/Anne_Bradstreet" title="Anne Bradstreet">Anne Bradstreet</a>.</p>

<p>Discussions of how the Square has changed in recent years usually center on the <a href="/wiki/Gentrification" title="Gentrification">gentrification</a> of the Harvard Square neighborhood and Cambridge in general. The Square also used to be a neighborhood shopping center, including a grocery store (Sages) and a <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">Woolworth's</a> <a href="/wiki/Variety_store" title="Variety store">five and ten</a>. Although a hardware store (Dickson Hardware at 26 Brattle Street) survives amid chain drug stores and bank branches, the Square is mainly a regional rather than neighborhood shopping destination, serving students and commuters.</p>
<p>In 1981 and 1987 the Harvard Square Theater was converted into a <a href="/wiki/Multiplex_(movie_theater)" title="Multiplex (movie theater)">multiplex</a> cinema; it later became part of the <a href="/wiki/Loews_Cineplex_Entertainment" title="Loews Cineplex Entertainment">Loews Cineplex Entertainment</a> chain and then closed on July 8, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup> During the late 1990s, some locally run businesses with long-time shopfronts on the Square—including the unusual <a href="/wiki/Tasty_Sandwich_Shop" title="Tasty Sandwich Shop">Tasty Diner</a>, a tiny sandwich shop open long hours, and the Wursthaus, a German restaurant with an extensive beer menu—closed to make way for national chains. Elsie's Lunch, a long-popular deli, has also closed; what remained of its small corner storefront space facing <a href="/wiki/Lowell_House" title="Lowell House">Lowell House</a> on Mount Auburn Street is now occupied by an <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" title="Automated teller machine">ATM</a>. Another long-time restaurant, the 64-year-old Leo's Place, closed in December 2013 when the landlord of the property terminated their lease. The owners are hoping to reopen in a new location.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The student co-op, the <a href="/wiki/Harvard/MIT_Cooperative_Society" title="Harvard/MIT Cooperative Society">Harvard/MIT Cooperative Society</a> ("The Coop", founded in 1882) is now managed by <a href="/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble" title="Barnes &amp; Noble">Barnes &amp; Noble</a>, though it is still overseen by a board elected by its membership of Harvard and MIT students and staff. <a href="/wiki/Schoenhof%27s_Foreign_Books" title="Schoenhof's Foreign Books">Schoenhof's Foreign Books</a> is owned by the French <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ditions_Gallimard" title="Éditions Gallimard">Éditions Gallimard</a>. Major bookstores Paperback Booksmith, Reading International, and Barilari Books had closed by the end of the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> WordsWorth Books at 30 Brattle Street closed in 2004, after 29 years as a fixture in the Square.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> In the same year, the famous <a href="/wiki/Grolier_Poetry_Bookshop" title="Grolier Poetry Bookshop">Grolier Poetry Bookshop</a> announced that it would be sold (although it survived under new management). <a href="/wiki/Globe_Corner_Bookstore" title="Globe Corner Bookstore">Globe Corner Bookstore</a> converted to an exclusively online business, serving its last walk-in customer on July 4, 2011.</p>
<p>Following national trends, the former Harvard Trust Company has been absorbed into the national <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a> through a series of mergers. The iconic <a href="/wiki/Out_of_Town_News" class="mw-redirect" title="Out of Town News">Out of Town News</a> is owned by the UK-based <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Group" title="Hudson Group">Hudson Group</a>. Still, several establishments remain as longstanding, locally-run businesses with unique styles. Examples include <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leavitt_%26_Pierce&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leavitt &amp; Pierce (page does not exist)">Leavitt &amp; Pierce</a> tobacconists (est. 1883), Laflamme Barber Shop (est. 1898), <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Book_Store" title="Harvard Book Store">Harvard Book Store</a> (est. 1932), Cardullo's Gourmet Shoppe (est. 1950), <a href="/wiki/Charlie%27s_Kitchen" title="Charlie's Kitchen">Charlie's Kitchen</a> (est. 1951), the <a href="/wiki/Brattle_Theater" class="mw-redirect" title="Brattle Theater">Brattle Theater</a> (est. 1953), the Hong Kong Chinese restaurant (est. 1954), <a href="/wiki/Club_Passim" title="Club Passim">Club Passim</a> (est. 1958), <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Pamplona" title="Café Pamplona">Café Pamplona</a> (est. 1959), Mr. Bartley's Burger Cottage (est. 1960), Dickson Brothers Hardware (est. 1961), Million Year Picnic comics (est. 1970), Algiers Coffee House (est. 1970), and <a href="/wiki/Grendel%27s_Den" title="Grendel's Den">Grendel's Den</a> (est. 1971).</p>
<p>Raven Used Books arrived in 2005, helping to preserve Harvard Square's reputation as a center for used and new book browsing and sales.</p>
<p>A 2012 proposal suggested major changes to the pedestrian island in the heart of Harvard Square, including possible removal of the landmark Out of Town News kiosk.<sup id="cite_ref-Flynn_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Flynn-12">[11]</a></sup> Reaction to the proposal was mixed, and no changes have been approved as of April 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Square&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_film_and_other_media">In film and other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Square&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In film and other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1969 film <i><a href="/wiki/Goodbye,_Columbus_(film)" title="Goodbye, Columbus (film)">Goodbye, Columbus</a></i> takes place in Harvard Square near the film's conclusion, after the <a href="/wiki/Richard_Benjamin" title="Richard Benjamin">Richard Benjamin</a> character learns that his girlfriend, Brenda Potimkin (played by <a href="/wiki/Katharine_Ross" title="Katharine Ross">Katharine Ross</a>), an undergraduate at <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_College" title="Radcliffe College">Radcliffe College</a>, had left her diaphragm in the top drawer of her bureau at home for her mother to discover.</p>
<p>The 1970 film <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Story_(1970_film)" title="Love Story (1970 film)">Love Story</a></i>, by the late Harvard University alumnus and <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> professor of <a href="/wiki/Classics" title="Classics">classics</a> <a href="/wiki/Erich_Segal" title="Erich Segal">Erich Segal</a>, takes place almost entirely in and around Harvard Square during its first two-thirds, while Harvard undergraduates <a href="/wiki/Love_Story_(1970_film)" title="Love Story (1970 film)">Oliver Barrett</a> and Jenny Cavalieri meet; finish college; get married; and Oliver goes to <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Law_School" title="Harvard Law School">Harvard Law School</a> while Jenny teaches school, living in a second-story walk-up in what looks like <a href="/wiki/Watertown,_Massachusetts" title="Watertown, Massachusetts">Watertown</a>. The film continues to be screened annually to incoming freshmen at Harvard College during orientation week.</p>
<p>The 1973 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Paper_Chase_(film)" title="The Paper Chase (film)">The Paper Chase</a></i>, set at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Law_School" title="Harvard Law School">Harvard Law School</a>, features Harvard Square landmarks of its era, including the old Out of Town Newsstand, the old MBTA Harvard station kiosk with its "8 Minutes to Park Street" sign, and the now-defunct Kupersmith's Florists.</p>
<p>The 1977 film <i><a href="/wiki/Between_the_Lines_(1977_film)" title="Between the Lines (1977 film)">Between the Lines</a></i> features similar Harvard Square footage as well as aerial footage of the Back Bay.</p>
<p>The 1994 film <i><a href="/wiki/With_Honors_(film)" title="With Honors (film)">With Honors</a></i> has a scene filmed in Harvard Square in which the Out of Town Newsstand is featured. In the scene, Monty approaches Simon as he (Simon) is attempting to sell newspapers he took out of a vending machine.</p>
<p>Various parts of the 1997 film <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Will_Hunting" title="Good Will Hunting">Good Will Hunting</a></i> were filmed in and around Harvard Square, most notably at the former <a href="/wiki/Tasty_Sandwich_Shop" title="Tasty Sandwich Shop">Tasty Sandwich Shop</a> and the outdoor seating area of the square's largest <a href="/wiki/Au_Bon_Pain" title="Au Bon Pain">Au Bon Pain</a> café.</p>
<p>The 2005 <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary film</a> <i>Touching History; Harvard Square, the Bank, and The Tasty Diner</i> chronicles the changing face of the Square, as a small diner (The Tasty) closes its doors to make way for a large retail space.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a> shot portions of his film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Town_(2010_film)" title="The Town (2010 film)">The Town</a></i> (2010) in <a href="/wiki/Grendel%27s_Den" title="Grendel's Den">Grendel's Den</a> on Winthrop Street, locally famous in the 1970s for its chocolate <a href="/wiki/Fondue" title="Fondue">fondue</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Social_Network" title="The Social Network">The Social Network</a></i>, about the Harvard student origins of <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, featured aerial footage of the area near <a href="/wiki/Smith_Campus_Center" title="Smith Campus Center">Holyoke Center</a>.</p>
