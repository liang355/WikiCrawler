<p><b>Cambridge Common</b> is a public park in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It is located near <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Square" title="Harvard Square">Harvard Square</a> and borders on several parts of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>. This park is a popular place to play kickball, softball, soccer, and frisbee. The north end of the park has a large playground. The park is maintained by the Cambridge Department of Public Works.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>General <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> gathered troops on Cambridge Common during the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>. A commemorative plaque marks the location of the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Elm" title="Washington Elm">Washington Elm</a>, a tree under which legend claims Washington stood as he first assumed command of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_Army" title="Continental Army">Continental Army</a>. Nearby is a trio of <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> cannons, a plaque for <a href="/wiki/Henry_Knox" title="Henry Knox">Henry Knox</a>, and another for <a href="/wiki/Tadeusz_Ko%C5%9Bciuszko" title="Tadeusz Kościuszko">Tadeusz Kościuszko</a>.</p>
<p>Slightly southeast of the center of the Common is a memorial to the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> with a statue of <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> in a covered area near the base of the memorial. On top of the memorial is a statue of a soldier.</p>
<p>Cambridge Common is also the site of an Irish Famine Memorial, dedicated on July 23, 1997 by then <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ireland" title="President of Ireland">President of Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_Robinson" title="Mary Robinson">Mary Robinson</a>, and unveiled to an audience of 3,000 people. The Memorial <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a> was created by <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Harron" title="Maurice Harron">Maurice Harron</a>, a sculptor from <a href="/wiki/Derry" title="Derry">Derry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>. There is a similar memorial in downtown <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>.</p>
