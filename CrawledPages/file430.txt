<p><b>Neuss</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈnɔʏs]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Neuss.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/87/De-Neuss.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Neuss.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>; spelled <i>Neuß</i> until 1968; <a href="/wiki/Limburgish_language" title="Limburgish language">Limburgish</a>: <i>Nüss</i>; <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>: <i>Novaesium</i>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is located on the west bank of the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> opposing <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a>. Neuss is the largest city within the <a href="/wiki/Rhein-Kreis_Neuss" title="Rhein-Kreis Neuss">Rhein-Kreis Neuss</a> district. It is primarily known for its historic Roman sites, as well as the annual <a href="/wiki/Neuss_Sch%C3%BCtzenfest" title="Neuss Schützenfest">Neusser Bürger-Schützenfest</a>. In 1984 Neuss celebrated 2000 years since its foundation. It therefore holds the title of "Germany's oldest city" alongside the city of <a href="/wiki/Trier" title="Trier">Trier</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_Rome">Ancient Rome</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neuss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ancient Rome">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Neuss was founded by the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a> in 16 BC as a military fortification (<i><a href="/wiki/Castrum" class="mw-redirect" title="Castrum">castrum</a></i>) with the current city to the north of the castrum, at the confluence of the rivers <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erft" title="Erft">Erft</a>, with the name of <b>Novaesium</b>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Legio_XVI_Gallica" title="Legio XVI Gallica">Legio XVI Gallica</a> ("Gallic 16th Legion") of the Roman army was stationed here in 43-70 AD. It was disbanded after surrendering during the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_rebellion" class="mw-redirect" title="Batavian rebellion">Batavian rebellion</a> (AD 70).<sup id="cite_ref-Bunson2009_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bunson2009-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later a civil settlement was founded in the area of today's centre of the town during the 1st century AD. Novaesium, together with <a href="/wiki/Treves" class="mw-redirect" title="Treves">Trier</a> (<i>Augusta Treverorum</i>), is one of the three oldest Roman settlements in Germany.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages">Middle Ages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neuss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Middle Ages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Neuss grew during the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> because of its prime location on several routes, by the crossing of the great Rhine valley, and with its <a href="/wiki/Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="Harbour">harbour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a>. During the 10th century, the remains of the <a href="/wiki/Martyr" title="Martyr">martyr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tribune" title="Tribune">tribune</a> <a href="/wiki/Saint_Quirinus_of_Neuss" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Quirinus of Neuss">Saint Quirinus</a>, not to be confused with the Roman god <a href="/wiki/Quirinus" title="Quirinus">Quirinus</a>, had been relocated to Neuss. This resulted in <a href="/wiki/Pilgrimage" title="Pilgrimage">pilgrimage</a> to the shrine of St. Quirinus even from countries beyond the borders of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>. Neuss was first documented as a town in 1138.</p>
<p>One of the main events in the town's history is the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Neuss" title="Siege of Neuss">siege of the town in 1474–75</a> by <a href="/wiki/Charles_the_Bold" title="Charles the Bold">Charles the Bold</a>, Duke of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Burgundy" title="Duchy of Burgundy">Burgundy</a>, that lasted for nearly a year. The citizens of Neuss withstood the siege and were therefore rewarded by the Holy Roman Emperor <a href="/wiki/Frederick_III,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick III, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick III</a>. The town was granted the right to mint its own coins and to carry the imperial <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>, the imperial eagle and the crown, in the town's own coat of arms. Neuss became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hanseatic_League" title="Hanseatic League">Hanseatic League</a>, although it was never accepted by the other members of the League.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Modernity">Early Modernity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neuss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early Modernity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1586, <a href="/wiki/Destruction_of_Neuss" title="Destruction of Neuss">more than two-thirds of the city was destroyed by fire</a>, and several wars during the reign of King <a href="/wiki/Louis_XIV_of_France" title="Louis XIV of France">Louis XIV</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">France</a> resulted in worsening finances for Neuss. Its importance as a place for trading declined rapidly, and from the mid-17th century onwards, Neuss became a place only important for its agriculture.</p>
<p>Until the late 18th century, Neuss belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Cologne" title="Electorate of Cologne">Electorate of Cologne</a>. From 1794 to 1814, Neuss was part of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon</a>. In 1815 after the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>, Neuss became part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a>, and was reorganized as a district with the municipalities of Neuss, <a href="/wiki/Dormagen" title="Dormagen">Dormagen</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nettesheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nettesheim (page does not exist)">Nettesheim</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nievenheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nievenheim (page does not exist)">Nievenheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rommerskirchen" title="Rommerskirchen">Rommerskirchen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zons" title="Zons">Zons</a>. The town had a population of 6,333 at that time. It was part of the Prussian <a href="/wiki/Province_of_J%C3%BClich-Cleves-Berg" title="Province of Jülich-Cleves-Berg">Province of Jülich-Cleves-Berg</a> (1815–22) and its successor, the <a href="/wiki/Rhine_Province" title="Rhine Province">Rhine Province</a> (1822–1946).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century_-_present">19th century - present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neuss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 19th century - present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Neuss regained its economic power in the 19th century, with expansion of the harbour in 1835, and increasing industrial activity. The city's boundaries were expanded in 1881. Neuss became part of the new state of <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a> in 1946.</p>
<p>In 1968 the spelling of the name was changed from <i>Neuß</i> to <i>Neuss</i>. In 1975 the town of Neuss and the district of <a href="/wiki/Grevenbroich" title="Grevenbroich">Grevenbroich</a> were joined to form the district of <a href="/wiki/Rhein-Kreis_Neuss" title="Rhein-Kreis Neuss">Rhein-Kreis Neuss</a> with a population of 440,000 and its seat of government in Neuss. Neuss is also home to <a href="/wiki/Toshiba" title="Toshiba">Toshiba's</a> European headquarters.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neuss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One sports club is Neusser Schlittschuh-Klub. Their sections are <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating" title="Figure skating">figure skating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_stock_sport" title="Ice stock sport">ice stock sport</a> and, as the only club in Germany, <a href="/wiki/Bandy" title="Bandy">bandy</a>. With the lack of a large ice surface, the variety <a href="/wiki/Rink_bandy" title="Rink bandy">rink bandy</a> is practiced.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> And there is also a football club in the city of Neuss, the VfR Neuss Football Club. There is also a fieldhockey club, HTC Schwarz-Weiss Neuss, and TC Blau-Weiss Neuss, a famous tennis club where Nadal began his career. And an American Football Club, the Neuss Frogs. Besides Neuss owns an all-weather racecourse called "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Galopprennbahn_Neuss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Galopprennbahn Neuss (page does not exist)">Galopprennbahn Neuss</a>".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Points_of_interest">Points of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neuss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Points of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Botanischer_Garten_der_Stadt_Neuss" title="Botanischer Garten der Stadt Neuss">Botanischer Garten der Stadt Neuss</a></i>, the city's <a href="/wiki/Botanical_garden" title="Botanical garden">botanical garden</a></li>
<li>Saint Quirinus Minster: a 13th-century late <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">romanesque</a> church, dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_of_Neuss" title="Quirinus of Neuss">the city's patron saint</a> and housing a shrine with his relics. Its dome-shaped eastern tower is one of the city's landmarks. In 2009 it was granted the title of <a href="/wiki/Minor_basilica" title="Minor basilica">minor basilica</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li><i>Obertor</i> (<i>Upper Gate</i>): southern city gate, built c.1200; today part of the <i>Clemens-Sels-Museum</i>. It is the only remaining of originally six gates that were part of the medieval town fortification.</li>
<li><i>Blutturm</i> (<i>Bloody Tower</i>): built in the 13th century, the only remaining round tower of the historic town fortification.</li>
<li><i>Zum "Schwatte Päd"</i> (<i>The Black Horse</i>): the oldest <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">public house</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lower_Rhine" title="Lower Rhine">Lower Rhine</a> region, established 1604</li>
<li>Saint Sebastianus Church</li>
