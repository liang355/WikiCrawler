			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Harvard Graduate School of Design</h1>

<p>The <b>Harvard Graduate School of Design</b> (also known as <b>The GSD</b>) is a professional <a href="/wiki/Graduate_school" title="Graduate school">graduate school</a> at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, located at Gund Hall, <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>. The GSD offers masters and doctoral programs in <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">landscape architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">urban planning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_design" title="Urban design">urban design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Graduate_real_estate_education" title="Graduate real estate education">real estate</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> design engineering, and design studies.</p>
<p>The GSD has over 13,000 alumni and has graduated many famous <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_planner" title="Urban planner">urban planners</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architect" title="Landscape architect">landscape architects</a>. The school is considered a global academic leader in the design fields.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (February 2017)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The GSD has the world's oldest <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">landscape architecture</a> program (founded in 1893), and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>'s oldest <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">urban planning</a> program (founded in 1900). Architecture courses were first taught at Harvard University in 1874.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The Graduate School of Design was officially established in 1936, combining the three fields of architecture, urban planning, and landscape architecture under one graduate school.<sup id="cite_ref-harvard.edu_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harvard.edu-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The market value of the school's endowment for the fiscal year 2013 was approximately $396 million.<sup id="cite_ref-harvard.edu1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harvard.edu1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Charles_Eliot_Norton" title="Charles Eliot Norton">Charles Eliot Norton</a> brought the first <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a> classes to Harvard University in 1874.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Urban_planning">Urban planning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Urban planning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1900, the first <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">urban planning</a> courses were taught at Harvard University, and by 1909, urban planning was added into Harvard's design curriculum. In 1923, North America's first urban planning degree was established at Harvard. In 1980, the program was temporarily moved to the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Kennedy_School_of_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard Kennedy School of Government">Harvard Kennedy School of Government</a> until it returned to the GSD in 1984.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Landscape_architecture">Landscape architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Landscape architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1893, the nation's first professional course in <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">landscape architecture</a> was offered at Harvard University. In 1900, the world's first landscape architecture program was established by <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Law_Olmsted,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick Law Olmsted, Jr.">Frederick Law Olmsted, Jr.</a> and Arthur A. Shurcliff. The School of Landscape Architecture was established in 1913.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment">Establishment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Establishment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The three major design professions (architecture, urban planning, and landscape architecture) were officially united in 1936 to form the Harvard Graduate School of Design. In 1937, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Gropius" title="Walter Gropius">Walter Gropius</a> joined the GSD faculty as chair of the Department of Architecture and brought modern designers, including <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Breuer" title="Marcel Breuer">Marcel Breuer</a> to help revamp the curriculum.</p>
<p>In 1960, <a href="/wiki/Josep_Llu%C3%ADs_Sert" title="Josep Lluís Sert">Josep Lluís Sert</a> established the nation's first Urban Design program. George Gund Hall, which is the present iconic home GSD, opened in 1972 and was designed by Australian architect and GSD graduate John Andrews. The school's now defunct Laboratory for Computer Graphics and Spatial Analysis (LCGSA) is widely recognized as the research/development environment from which the now-commercialized technology of <a href="/wiki/GIS" class="mw-redirect" title="GIS">geographic information systems</a> (GIS) emerged in the late 1960s and 1970s. More recent research initiatives include the Design Robotics Group, a unit that investigates new material systems and fabrication technologies in the context of architectural design and construction.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-harvard.edu_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harvard.edu-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The degrees granted in the masters programs include the Master of <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">Architecture</a> (M.Arch.), Master in <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">Landscape Architecture</a> (MLA), Master of Architecture in <a href="/wiki/Urban_Design" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban Design">Urban Design</a> (MAUD), Master of Landscape Architecture in Urban Design (MLAUD), Master in <a href="/wiki/Urban_Planning" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban Planning">Urban Planning</a> (MUP), Master in Design Engineering (MDE), Master in Design Studies (M.Des.) in more than eight concentrations. The school offers a doctoral degree, Doctor of Design (D.Des.), and jointly administers a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> (PhD) degree in architecture, urban planning, and landscape architecture with the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Harvard Graduate School of Arts and Sciences">Graduate School of Arts and Sciences</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rankings">Rankings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rankings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2016, the program's ten-year <a href="/wiki/Arithmetic_mean" title="Arithmetic mean">average</a> ranking, places it 1st, overall, on <i>DesignIntelligence's</i> ranking of programs accredited by the National Architectural Accrediting Board.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Education">Executive Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Executive Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Executive Education operates within GSD providing continuing education classes, they are located at 7 Sumner Rd.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Advanced Management Development Program in Real Estate (AMDP) is a six-week executive development course. The program is open to established professionals with 15+ years of experience in real estate. Upon graduating from AMDP, participants are full-fledged <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> Alumni. As of 2013, AMDP is in its 13th year.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The other large program organized by Executive Education is summer Open Enrollment. In 2013, Executive Education held 18 classes throughout the month of July. Each class lasts from 1 to 3 days and is eligible for continuing education credits through <a href="/wiki/American_Institute_of_Architects" title="American Institute of Architects">American Institute of Architects</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Society_of_Landscape_Architects" title="American Society of Landscape Architects">American Society of Landscape Architects</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/American_Planning_Association" title="American Planning Association">American Planning Association</a>. Open Enrollment classes are open to everyone, though basic knowledge of the subject is recommended.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of 2012–2013, there were 878 students enrolled. 362 students or 42% were enrolled in architecture, 182 students or 21% in landscape architecture, 161 students or 18% in urban planning, and 173 students or 20% in doctoral or design studies programs. Approximately, 65% of students were <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">Americans</a>. The average student is 27 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_publications">Research and publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Research and publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to its degree programs, the GSD administers the Loeb Fellowship, and numerous research initiatives such as the Zofnass Program for Sustainable Infrastructure. The school publishes the bi-annual <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Design_Magazine" title="Harvard Design Magazine">Harvard Design Magazine</a>, Platform, and other design books and studio works.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_Research_Labs">Design Research Labs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Design Research Labs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The GSD Design Labs synthesize theoretical and applied knowledge through research with the intent to enable design to be an agent of change in society. There are seven current labs: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://research.gsd.harvard.edu/maps/">Material Processes and Systems Group</a>; Energy, Environments and Design; New Geographies Lab; Responsive Environments and Artifacts Lab; Social Agency Lab; Urban Theory Lab; Geometry Lab.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The GSD campus is located northeast of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a> and across the street from <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Hall_(Harvard_University)" title="Memorial Hall (Harvard University)">Memorial Hall</a>. Gund Hall is the main building of the GSD, and it houses most of the student space and faculty offices. Other nearby buildings include space for the school's Design Research Labs, faculty offices, the Loeb Fellowship program office, and research space for students, including those in the M.Des. and D.Des. programs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gund_Hall">Gund Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Gund Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gund Hall is the main building, which has studio spaces and offices for approximately 800 students and more than 100 faculty and staff, lecture and seminar rooms, workshops and darkrooms, an audiovisual center, computer facilities, Chauhaus, the cafeteria, a project room, Piper Auditorium, and the Frances Loeb Library. The central studio space, also known as the Trays, extends through five levels under a stepped, clear-span roof. Gund Hall has a yard that comprises a basketball court and is often used for events, as an exhibition area for class projects, and as the setting for commencement ceremonies.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Frances_Loeb_Library">Frances Loeb Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Frances Loeb Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Frances Loeb Library, is the main library of the Graduate School of Design. The library has a collection of over 300,000 books and journals. It also has a Materials and Visual Resources Department, and the Special Collections Department, which houses the GSD's rare books and manuscript collection.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fabrication_Lab">Fabrication Lab</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Fabrication Lab">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Fabrication Lab has both traditional tools and state-of-the-art technology available for model making and prototyping to faculty research and student course work. The Fabrication Lab has a full wood shop, metals shop, printing labs, 3D printing, CNC tools, robotic machines, <a href="/wiki/Laser_cutter" class="mw-redirect" title="Laser cutter">laser cutter</a> machines, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distinguished_alumni_and_faculty">Distinguished alumni and faculty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Distinguished alumni and faculty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2013, the GSD had over 13,000 alumni in 96 countries. The GSD had 77 faculty members and 129 visiting faculty members. 45% of the faculty members were born outside of the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-harvard.edu1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harvard.edu1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
