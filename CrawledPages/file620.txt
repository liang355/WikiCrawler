			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">University-preparatory school</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"College Prep" and "College preparatory school" redirect here. For the high school in Oakland, California, see <a href="/wiki/The_College_Preparatory_School" title="The College Preparatory School">The College Preparatory School</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>university-preparatory school</b> or <b>college-preparatory school</b> (shortened to <b>preparatory school</b>, <b>prep school</b>, or <b>college prep</b>) is a type of <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a>. The term can refer to <a href="/wiki/State_school" title="State school">public</a>, <a href="/wiki/Private_school" title="Private school">private independent</a> or <a href="/wiki/Parochial_school" title="Parochial school">parochial</a> schools primarily designed to prepare students for <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the United States, there are <a href="/wiki/State_school" title="State school">public</a>, <a href="/wiki/Private_school" title="Private school">private</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charter_school" title="Charter school">charter</a> college preparatory schools and they can be either <a href="/wiki/Parochial_school" title="Parochial school">parochial</a> or <a href="/wiki/Secular" class="mw-redirect" title="Secular">secular</a>. Admission is sometimes based on specific <a href="/wiki/Selective_school" title="Selective school">selection criteria</a>, usually academic, but some schools have open enrollment.<sup id="cite_ref-greatkids_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greatkids-1">[1]</a></sup> Fewer than 1% of students enrolled in school in the United States attend an independent, private prep school, compared to 9% who attend <a href="/wiki/Parochial_school" title="Parochial school">parochial schools</a> and 88% who attend <a href="/wiki/Public_school_(government_funded)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public school (government funded)">public schools</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Public and charter college prep schools are typically connected to a local <a href="/wiki/School_district" title="School district">school district</a> and draw from the entire district instead of the closest <a href="/wiki/School_zone" title="School zone">school zone</a>. Some offer specialized courses or <a href="/wiki/Curricula" class="mw-redirect" title="Curricula">curricula</a> that prepare students for a specific field of study, while others use the label as a promotional tool without offering programs that differ from a conventional high school.<sup id="cite_ref-greatkids_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greatkids-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term "prep school" in the U.S. is usually associated with private, elite institutions that have very <a href="/wiki/Selective_school" title="Selective school">selective</a> admission criteria and high tuition fees.<sup id="cite_ref-laneri_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laneri-2">[2]</a></sup> Prep schools can be <a href="/wiki/Day_school" title="Day school">day schools</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding schools</a>, or both, and may be <a href="/wiki/Co-educational" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-educational">co-educational</a> or <a href="/wiki/Single-sex_education" title="Single-sex education">single-sex</a>. Currently day schools are more common than boarding, and since the 1970s co-educational schools are more common than single-sex.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Unlike the public schools which are free, they charge tuition ($10,000 to 40,000+ a year in 2014)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Some prep schools are affiliated with a particular religious denomination. Unlike parochial schools, independent preparatory schools are not governed by a religious organization, and students are usually not required to receive instruction in one particular religion. While independent prep schools in the United States are not subject to government oversight or regulation, they are <a href="/wiki/Educational_accreditation" title="Educational accreditation">accredited</a> by one of the six <a href="/wiki/Regional_accreditation" title="Regional accreditation">regional accreditation agencies</a> for educational institutions.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>In most parts of Europe, such as <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>, there are state-funded secondary schools specializing in university-preparatory education. These go by many names depending on the country but may be called <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Voorbereidend_wetenschappelijk_onderwijs" title="Voorbereidend wetenschappelijk onderwijs">athenaea</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Lycee" class="mw-redirect" title="Lycee">lycee</a> or a liceo, depending on the nation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, certain private or public secondary schools offer special post-secondary classes called <a href="/wiki/Classes_Pr%C3%A9paratoires" class="mw-redirect" title="Classes Préparatoires">classes préparatoires</a>, equivalent in level to the first years of <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a>, for students who wish to prepare for the competitive exams for the entrance in the <a href="/wiki/Grandes_%C3%A9coles" title="Grandes écoles">Grandes écoles</a>. Unlike American prep schools then, they begin after high-school graduation. The most famous French classes préparatoires are exceptionally intensive and selective, taking only the very best students graduating from high schools but generally not charging fees. Nevertheless, there exist many less prestigious classes préparatoires. As a result, 90% of the students in the scientific classes préparatoires eventually become engineers or scientists.</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(Germany)" title="Gymnasium (Germany)">Gymnasium (Germany)</a></div>
<p>A Gymnasium (plural: Gymnasien) is a particular type of school in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and other countries in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, with the goal to prepare its pupils to enter a university.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(ancient_Greece)" title="Gymnasium (ancient Greece)">γυμνάσιον (gymnasion)</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Ancient Greece</a> was a place for physical and eventually also intellectual <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a> of young men. The later meaning of intellectual education persisted in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> and other languages, whereas in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, the older meaning of physical education was retained. The German Gymnasien are selective and competitive schools. They enroll students after completing 4th or 6th grade (depending on the "Bundesland", i.e., state) and prepare them for college. The vast majority of Gymnasien is public and does not charge tuition fees. Article 7, Paragraph 4 of the German constitution, forbids segregation of students according to the means of their parents (the so-called Sondierungsverbot). Therefore, most private Gymnasien have rather low tuition fees and/or offer scholarships.</p>
<p>Recently, there has been some debate about the Gymnasium and some people put forward the opinion that the Gymnasien are not enrolling enough students from non-east-Asian immigrant families and from working class and lower-class families. As a result of that discussion, the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Berlin" title="Senate of Berlin">Senate of Berlin</a> ruled that the Gymnasien in the jurisdiction of Berlin should only be able to pick 70% to 65% of their students, the other places at the Gymnasien are to be allocated by lottery. Every child will be able to enter the lottery, no matter how he or she performed in primary school. It is hoped that this policy will increase the number of lower and working class students attending a Gymnasium.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, according to a 2012 Forsa study for the Berlin Newspaper, the majority of the people in Berlin think that the performance in primary school should dictate whether or not someone can be enrolled at a Gymnasium. A total of zero percent was in favour of the lottery.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Germany's oldest Gymnasien include <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_Paulinum" title="Gymnasium Paulinum">Gymnasium Paulinum</a> (founded around 797), Gymnasium Theodorianum (founded in 799) and <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_Carolinum_(Osnabr%C3%BCck)" title="Gymnasium Carolinum (Osnabrück)">Gymnasium Carolinum</a> (founded in 804).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Italy, there are several kinds of high schools, both public and private, whose curriculum has as a primary aim the preparation for university. These are called "Liceo", plural "Licei". The name comes from "Lyceum", the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> rendering of the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> Λύκειον ("Lykeion"), the name of a <i><a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(ancient_Greece)" title="Gymnasium (ancient Greece)">gymnasium</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Classical_Athens" title="Classical Athens">Classical Athens</a> dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Lyceus" class="mw-redirect" title="Apollo Lyceus">Apollo Lyceus</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lyceum_(Classical)" title="Lyceum (Classical)">This original Lyceum</a> is remembered as the location of the <a href="/wiki/Peripatetic_school" title="Peripatetic school">peripatetic school</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a>.</p>
<p>Until 1969, the <a href="/wiki/Liceo_Classico" class="mw-redirect" title="Liceo Classico">Liceo Classico</a> was the only secondary education track that allowed a student access to any kind of Italian university, while other secondary education tracks allowed only a restricted access path; nowadays it carries the reputation of being a highly formative school, one of the few European secondary school types where the study of ancient languages and their literature are compulsory.</p>
<p>There are four main types of Liceo: <a href="/wiki/Liceo_Classico" class="mw-redirect" title="Liceo Classico">Liceo Classico</a> (focusing on classical subjects, such as <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and Ancient Greek studies, and traditionally divided in two years of "Ginnasio" plus three years of "Liceo"), <a href="/wiki/Liceo_Scientifico" class="mw-redirect" title="Liceo Scientifico">Liceo Scientifico</a> (lacking Greek to devote approximately equal time to the remaining classical subjects and scientific subjects), Liceo Artistico (focusing on artistic subjects as <a href="/wiki/Art_History" class="mw-redirect" title="Art History">Art History</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drawing" title="Drawing">Drawing</a> and Liceo Linguistico (focusing on foreign languages such as <a href="/wiki/French_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="French Language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="German Language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Language">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: two of these are added to the study of the mandatory language, <a href="/wiki/English_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="English Language">English</a>).</p>
<p>Other kind of high schools, usually referred to as "technical institutes", also offer the possibility to attain university after graduation, although they also form students to have some kind of professional prospective after graduation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands">Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_Netherlands" title="Education in the Netherlands">Education in the Netherlands</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the official terminology is <i><a href="/wiki/Voorbereidend_wetenschappelijk_onderwijs" title="Voorbereidend wetenschappelijk onderwijs">voorbereidend wetenschappelijk onderwijs</a></i> (or <i>vwo</i>) meaning "preparatory academic education". The vwo is divided into the <a href="/wiki/Atheneum_(school)" title="Atheneum (school)">atheneum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasium</a>. These are identical in duration (six years) and level of education, except that the gymnasium includes <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> as compulsory subjects in the first few years, and a pupil must include at least one of these classical languages in his final exams. In the Netherlands, education is usually public.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Slovakia">Slovakia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Slovakia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Slovakia" title="Education in Slovakia">Education in Slovakia</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Slovak Republic">Slovak Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnázium</a> is one of the school types providing secondary education that leads to the <i><a href="/wiki/Maturita" class="mw-redirect" title="Maturita">maturita</a></i> exam, a prerequisite for <a href="/wiki/Higher_education_in_Slovakia" class="mw-redirect" title="Higher education in Slovakia">higher education</a>. Gymnáziums are the main school type to prepare students for tertiary education (<i><a href="/wiki/Vysok%C3%A1_%C5%A1kola" title="Vysoká škola">vysoká škola</a></i>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Baccalaureate" title="International Baccalaureate">International Baccalaureate</a>'s Diploma Programme in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> was created in 1968. It is a demanding pre-university course of study that leads to examinations. It is designed for highly motivated secondary school students aged 16 to 19. It recognizes the IB diploma as academically equivalent to "Titulo de bachillerato español". The programme has earned a reputation for rigorous assessment, giving IB diploma holders access to the world’s leading universities. The Diploma Programme is rigorous and is world-renowned. Each student’s performance is measured against well-defined levels of achievement and high standards of academics. These are consistent from one examination session to the next and are applied equally to all schools. The International Baccalaureate has shown that students are well prepared for university work. They are accepted by universities in more than 110 countries.</p>
<p>One school is the Academy School in the <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, which is a member of the National Association of British Schools in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It is inspected regularly both by British Inspectors and Inspectors from the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Ministry_of_Education_and_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Ministry of Education and Science">Spanish Ministry of Education and Science</a>. Among other prestigious schools are the <a href="/wiki/Hastings_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Hastings School">Hastings School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caxton_College" title="Caxton College">Caxton College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valencia,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Valencia, Spain">Valencia</a>, and the Bellver International College in <a href="/wiki/Mallorca" class="mw-redirect" title="Mallorca">Mallorca</a>. These are internationally recognized by the IB Diploma Programme and academically ranked accordingly.</p>
<p>The International Preparatory Schools are ranked and recognised by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Ministry_of_Education_and_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Ministry of Education and Science">Spanish Ministry of Education and Science</a> (MEC) and all teach a minimum level of Spanish language, science, literature, geography, biology and history. The curriculum also varies from one international school to another.</p>
<p>In addition to disciplinary and interdisciplinary study, the Diploma Programme features three core elements that broaden students’ educational experience and challenge them to apply their knowledge and skills. Students can study and take examinations, in English, French or Spanish.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd><i>Notes: In the UK, <a href="/wiki/Preparatory_school_(United_Kingdom)" title="Preparatory school (United Kingdom)">"prep school"</a> refers to a tuition-based school for children ages 8 to 13. In England and Wales, <a href="/wiki/Public_school_(United_Kingdom)" title="Public school (United Kingdom)">"public school"</a> refers to exclusive and private, tuition-based secondary school for children ages 13 to 18.</i></dd>
</dl>
<p><a href="/wiki/Comprehensive_school" title="Comprehensive school">Comprehensive schools</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sixth_form_college" title="Sixth form college">sixth form colleges</a>, which are both funded by the state, prepare students for university, as do fee-paying <a href="/wiki/Public_school_(United_Kingdom)" title="Public school (United Kingdom)">public schools</a> (so named as they are open to the public, as opposed to being restricted to a religious denomination). Historically <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">grammar schools</a> would prepare some or most of their pupils for university entrance. Most have since been converted to comprehensive schools although 233 remain in England and Northern Ireland. The only other institutions specialising in university entrance are <a href="/wiki/Cram_school" title="Cram school">crammers</a> for (usually) privately educated pupils who have failed to gain entrance to their university of choice directly from school.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Turkey">Turkey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Turkey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Turkey, university-preparatory schools are private schools and called <b>basic lyceum</b>(<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>temel lise</i></span>).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[note 1]</a></sup> They are formed in 2016 as a result of <a href="/wiki/Cram_school#Turkey" title="Cram school">anti-cramming movement initiated by Turkish government</a>. They are subsidised by Turkish government for conversion and to boost student enrolment.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Malaysia">Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Malaysian Higher School Certificate, or more popularly known as the <a href="/wiki/Sijil_Tinggi_Persekolahan_Malaysia" title="Sijil Tinggi Persekolahan Malaysia">Sijil Tinggi Persekolahan Malaysia</a> (STPM) is one of the two major pre-university systems for admission to Malaysian public universities. STPM students are housed at selected regular secondary schools and are known as Sixth Formers. The other Ministry programme is a one-year <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Matriculation_Programme" title="Malaysian Matriculation Programme">Malaysian Matriculation Programme</a>, where students attend Matriculation Colleges. Both fall under the Ministry of Education. Candidates technically may apply for admission to degree-level courses with a variety of pre-university examinations considered equivalent with STPM, including A-Level.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Japan, prep schools are called "shin-gakkou" (進学校), which literally means a school used to progress into another school. Prep schools in Japan are usually considered prestigious and are often difficult to get into. However, there are many tiers of prep schools, the entry into which depends on the university that the school leads into.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> Japanese prep schools started as "chu-gakkou" (中学校), secondary schools for boys which were founded after the secondary school law in 1886. Later, "koutou-jo-gakkou" (高等女学校), secondary school for girls (1891) and "jitsugyo-gakkou" (実業学校), vocational schools (1924) were included into "chutou-gakkou" and were legally regarded as schools on the same level as school for boys, but the pupils graduated from those two types of schools were required much more criteria on college entrance. Such Japanese secondary schools in modern period were five-year schools except for a short term from 1943 to 1946. The social status of "chu-gakkou", or "kyusei chu-gakkou" (旧制中学校), secondary schools for boys under the old system didn't disappear even after the new system (6-3-3) took effect in 1947. A plenty number of "shin-gakkou" are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Six-year_schools&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Six-year schools (page does not exist)">six-year schools</a> these days, and many of them has their origin in "kyusei chu-gakkou" and "kotou jo-gakkou", or ones attached to universities. Japanese pupils who aspire prep school education take written examinations when they are in sixth grade in each prep school. Other than six-year prep schools, the top municipal senior high school (three-year schools) in each school zone and some high-ranked private senior high schools (ditto) are also regarded as "shin-gakkou". In the 21st Century, some trial cases that connect public junior and senior high schools are seen in each region, too, which brodens education for college entrance. As Japanese government provides grant-in-aid to private schools, the tuition is 5,000 - 10,000 US dollar per a year even if it is a private school.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University-preparatory_school&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some of the elite private and public schools in New Zealand, for example <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College,_Auckland" title="King's College, Auckland">Kings College</a> (an <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Church_in_Aotearoa,_New_Zealand_and_Polynesia" title="Anglican Church in Aotearoa, New Zealand and Polynesia">Anglican</a> independent school) and <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Grammar_School" title="Auckland Grammar School">Auckland Grammar School</a> (a prestigious state-school in the elite central Auckland suburb of Epsom), profess their purpose to be the education of their students to attend university in preparation for a career in the professions or in the service of the nation.</p>
