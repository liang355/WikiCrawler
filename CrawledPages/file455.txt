<p><b>Landshut</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bavarian_language" title="Bavarian language">Bavarian</a>: <span lang="bar" xml:lang="bar"><i>Landsad</i></span>) is a town in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> in the south-east of Germany, belonging to both Eastern and Southern Bavaria. Situated on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Isar" title="Isar">River Isar</a>, Landshut is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Bavaria" title="Lower Bavaria">Lower Bavaria</a>, one of the seven <a href="/wiki/Administrative_region" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative region">administrative regions</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Free_state_(government)" title="Free state (government)">Free State</a> of Bavaria. It is also the seat of the surrounding district, and has a population of more 70,000. Landshut is the largest city in Lower Bavaria, followed by <a href="/wiki/Passau" title="Passau">Passau</a> and <a href="/wiki/Straubing" title="Straubing">Straubing</a>, and Eastern Bavaria's second biggest city.</p>
<p>Owing to its characteristic coat of arms, the town is also often called "City of the three Helmets" (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Dreihelmenstadt</span>). Furthermore, the town is popularly known for the Landshuter Hochzeit (<a href="/wiki/Landshut_Wedding" title="Landshut Wedding">Landshut Wedding</a>), a full-tilt medieval festival.</p>
<p>Due to its proximity and easy access to <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Franz_Josef_Strauss_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Franz Josef Strauss International Airport">Franz Josef Strauss International Airport</a>, Landshut became a powerful and future-oriented investment area. The town is one of the richest industrialized towns in Bavaria and has East Bavaria's lowest unemployment rate (ca.&#160;1.1% in October 2013), which represents full employment.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Landshut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Settings">Settings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Landshut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Settings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Landshut lies in the centre of Lower Bavaria, and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Alpine_foothills" title="Alpine foothills">Alpine foothills</a>. The <a href="/wiki/River_Isar" class="mw-redirect" title="River Isar">River Isar</a> runs through the city centre. Landshut is about 72 kilometres (45&#160;mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Landshut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city of Landshut and Trausnitz castle were founded in 1204 by <a href="/wiki/Louis_I_Wittelsbach,_Duke_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Louis I Wittelsbach, Duke of Bavaria">Duke Louis I</a>. Landshut was already a <a href="/wiki/Wittelsbach" class="mw-redirect" title="Wittelsbach">Wittelsbach</a> residence by 1231, and in 1255, when the duchy of Bavaria was split in two, Landshut also became the capital of Lower Bavaria. Duke <a href="/wiki/Henry_XVI_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry XVI of Bavaria">Henry XVI</a> was the first of the three famous rich dukes who ruled <a href="/wiki/Bayern-Landshut" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayern-Landshut">Bayern-Landshut</a> in the 15th century. The wedding of Duke <a href="/wiki/George_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="George of Bavaria">George</a> with the Polish Princess Royal <a href="/wiki/Jadwiga_Jagiellon_(1457%E2%80%931502)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jadwiga Jagiellon (1457–1502)">Jadwiga Jagiellon</a> in 1475 was celebrated in Landshut with one of the most splendid festivals of the Middle Ages (called "<a href="/wiki/Landshuter_Hochzeit" class="mw-redirect" title="Landshuter Hochzeit">Landshuter Hochzeit</a>"). After his death and the <a href="/wiki/Landshut_War_of_Succession" class="mw-redirect" title="Landshut War of Succession">Landshut War of Succession</a>, Bavaria-Landshut was reunited with <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Munich" title="Bavaria-Munich">Bavaria-Munich</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Louis_X,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Louis X, Duke of Bavaria">Louis X, Duke of Bavaria</a> built the Landshut Residence 1537–1543 after his visit to Italy. Louis built the first <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> palace constructed north of the Alps after the <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Te" class="mw-redirect" title="Palazzo Te">Palazzo Te</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mantua" title="Mantua">Mantua</a>. <a href="/wiki/William_V,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="William V, Duke of Bavaria">William V, Duke of Bavaria</a> ordered to upgrade <a href="/wiki/Trausnitz_Castle" title="Trausnitz Castle">Trausnitz Castle</a> from a gothic fortification into a renaissance complex when he lived in Landshut as crown prince for ten years until 1579. Afterwards Landshut lost most of its importance until the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Ingolstadt" title="University of Ingolstadt">University of Ingolstadt</a> was moved to Landshut in 1800. But already in 1826 the university was transferred to Munich.</p>
<p>In 1634, during the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>, the city was taken and plundered by Swedish forces under the command of <a href="/wiki/Bernard_of_Saxe-Weimar" title="Bernard of Saxe-Weimar">Bernard of Saxe-Weimar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, a subcamp of <a href="/wiki/Dachau_concentration_camp" title="Dachau concentration camp">Dachau concentration camp</a> was located in the city to provide <a href="/wiki/Slave_labour" class="mw-redirect" title="Slave labour">slave labour</a> for local industry.</p>
<p>The U.S. Army maintained facilities in Landshut, including Pinder Kaserne and a dependent housing area, until 1968.</p>
<p>Since the opening of <a href="/wiki/Munich_Airport" title="Munich Airport">Munich Airport</a> close to Landshut in 1992, the town has become an attractive business location.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights_and_culture">Main sights and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Landshut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main sights and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:91px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Siebmacher220mod.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d6/Siebmacher220mod.jpg" width="89" height="104" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="89" data-file-height="104" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Siebmacher220mod.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Coat of arms, depicted in 1605</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The town is of national importance because of its predominantly <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic architecture</a> within the historic town centre, especially <i><a href="/wiki/Trausnitz_Castle" title="Trausnitz Castle">Trausnitz Castle</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/St._Martin,_Landshut" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Martin, Landshut">Church of Saint Martin</a></i> featuring the world's tallest brick tower. Among other Gothic architecture are the churches of <i>St. Jodok</i> and <i>Holy Spirit</i>, but also the <i>Town Hall</i> and the <i>Ländtor</i>, the only still existing gate of the medieval fortification.</p>
<p>Landshut is also known for a festival celebrated every four years called the <a href="/wiki/Landshuter_Hochzeit" class="mw-redirect" title="Landshuter Hochzeit">Landshuter Hochzeit</a>, commemorating the 1475 marriage of George of Bavaria and Jadwiga Jagiellon.</p>
<p>The renaissance era produced in particular the decorated inner courtyard of the <i>Trausnitz Castle</i> and the ducal <i><a href="/wiki/Landshut_Residence" title="Landshut Residence">Landshut Residence</a></i> in the inner town. Baroque churches are represented by the Jesuit church <i>St. Ignatius</i>, the Dominican church <i>St. Blasius</i> and the church of <i>St. Joseph</i>. Also the medieval churches of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Seligenthal_convent&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Seligenthal convent (page does not exist)">Seligenthal convent</a> and of the Cistercians were redesigned in baroque style. Many old middle-class houses of the past in the Old Town still represent the history of the town from the Gothic times to the Neo-Classicism.</p>
