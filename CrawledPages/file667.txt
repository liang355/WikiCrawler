<p><b>The Observatory of Economic Complexity</b> is a data visualization site for international trade data created by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Macro_Connections&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Macro Connections (page does not exist)">Macro Connections</a> group at the <a href="/wiki/MIT_Media_Lab" title="MIT Media Lab">MIT Media Lab</a>. The goal of the observatory is to distribute international trade data in a visual form. At present the observatory serves more than 20 million interactive visualizations, connecting hundreds of countries to their export destinations and to the products that they trade.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Source_data">Source data</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Observatory_of_Economic_Complexity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Source data">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Observatory of Economic Complexity combines a number of international trade data sets, including data from Feenstra, Lipset, Deng, Ma, and Mo’s <i>World Trade Flows: 1962-2000</i> dataset,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> cleaned and made compatible through a <a href="/wiki/National_Bureau_of_Economic_Research" title="National Bureau of Economic Research">National Bureau of Economic Research</a> (NBER) project and HS4 aggregated from the HS6 classification cleaned by the BACI team at <a href="/wiki/Centre_d%27Etudes_Prospectives_et_d%27Informations_Internationales" title="Centre d'Etudes Prospectives et d'Informations Internationales">Centre d'Etudes Prospectives et d'Informations Internationales</a> (CEPII).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The dataset contains exports and imports both by country of origin and by destination. Products are disaggregated according to the Standardized International Trade Code at the four-digit level (SITC-4) and the Harmonized Systems at the four digit level (HS-4).</p>
<p>Currently, data is available from 1962 through 2015.</p>

<p>Products, represented by rectangles, are drawn from the 775 individual product classes found in the Standardized International Trade Code at the four-digit level (SITC-4). The rectangle's color corresponds to the 34 product communities found in <a href="/wiki/The_Product_Space" title="The Product Space">the Product Space</a> and other visualizations from The Observatory of Economic Complexity. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://atlas.media.mit.edu/explore/tree_map/hs/export/usa/all/show/2009/">Product Exports Treemaps</a> in The Observatory of Economic Complexity graphically represent product's share of a nation's trade (imports or exports). The larger the size of the rectangle, the larger the product's share of that country's imports or exports.</p>
<p>An example from <a href="/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a> in 2009 is shown on the right.</p>
