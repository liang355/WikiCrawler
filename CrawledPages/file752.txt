<p>The <b>Thames River</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/θ/: 'th' in 'thigh'">θ</span><span title="/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'">eɪ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span></span>/</a></span></span>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a short river and tidal <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>. It flows south for 15 miles (24&#160;km)<sup id="cite_ref-NHD_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NHD-2">[2]</a></sup> through eastern Connecticut from the junction of the <a href="/wiki/Yantic_River" title="Yantic River">Yantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shetucket_River" title="Shetucket River">Shetucket</a> rivers at <a href="/wiki/Norwich,_Connecticut" title="Norwich, Connecticut">Norwich</a>, to <a href="/wiki/New_London,_Connecticut" title="New London, Connecticut">New London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton</a>, which flank its mouth at <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Sound" title="Long Island Sound">Long Island Sound</a>. The Thames River watershed includes a number of smaller basins and the 80-mile-long <a href="/wiki/Quinebaug_River" title="Quinebaug River">Quinebaug River</a>, which rises in southern Massachusetts and joins the Shetucket River about four miles northeast of Norwich.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thames_River_(Connecticut)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PostcardSleighingRiverThames1906.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/PostcardSleighingRiverThames1906.jpg/250px-PostcardSleighingRiverThames1906.jpg" width="250" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/PostcardSleighingRiverThames1906.jpg/375px-PostcardSleighingRiverThames1906.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/PostcardSleighingRiverThames1906.jpg/500px-PostcardSleighingRiverThames1906.jpg 2x" data-file-width="589" data-file-height="387" /></a>

Sleighing on the frozen river, winter of 1903-1904</div>

<p>The river has provided important harbors since the mid-17th century. It was originally known as the <b>Pequot River</b> after the <a href="/wiki/Pequot" title="Pequot">Pequot</a> <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Indians</a> who dominated the area. Other early names for the river have included Frisius, Great, Great River of Pequot, Little Fresh, Mohegan, New London, and Pequod. The town was officially named New London in 1658 and the estuary river was renamed Thames to match.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard_Academy" title="United States Coast Guard Academy">United States Coast Guard Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_College" title="Connecticut College">Connecticut College</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Naval_Submarine_Base_New_London" title="Naval Submarine Base New London">U.S. Navy submarine base</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Electric_Boat_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Electric Boat Corporation">Electric Boat</a> submarine shipyard are located on the river at New London and Groton. The <a href="/wiki/USS_Nautilus_(SSN-571)" title="USS Nautilus (SSN-571)">USS <i>Nautilus</i> (SSN-571)</a> was launched into the river on January 21, 1954 from Electric Boat, becoming the world's first <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_submarine" title="Nuclear submarine">nuclear-powered submarine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two historic forts overlook the mouth of the river at New London harbor, now <a href="/wiki/List_of_Connecticut_state_parks" title="List of Connecticut state parks">Connecticut State Parks</a>: <a href="/wiki/Fort_Griswold" title="Fort Griswold">Fort Griswold</a> on the eastern <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Groton_Heights" title="Battle of Groton Heights">Groton Heights</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fort_Trumbull" title="Fort Trumbull">Fort Trumbull</a> on the New London side.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thames_River_(Connecticut)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Yale-Harvard_Boat_Race" class="mw-redirect" title="Yale-Harvard Boat Race">Yale-Harvard Boat Race</a> is held annually in New London.</p>
<p>New London's <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailfest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sailfest (page does not exist)">Sailfest</a> is an annual event which includes <a href="/wiki/OpSail" class="mw-redirect" title="OpSail">OpSail</a>, a gathering of large sailing vessels, including the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a> training ship <i><a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">Eagle</a></i>.</p>
