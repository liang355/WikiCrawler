<p><b>Straubing</b> is an independent city in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Bavaria" title="Lower Bavaria">Lower Bavaria</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is seat of the <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany">district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Straubing-Bogen" title="Straubing-Bogen">Straubing-Bogen</a>. Annually in August the <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4ubodenvolksfest" title="Gäubodenvolksfest">Gäubodenvolksfest</a>, the second largest <a href="/wiki/Fair" title="Fair">fair</a> in Bavaria, is held.</p>
<p>The city is located on the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> forming the centre of the <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4uboden" title="Gäuboden">Gäuboden</a>.</p>

<p>The area of Straubing has been continuously settled since the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a>. The conquest by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a> in 16–14 BC had a dramatic impact on the whole region. Even today many traces of the 400-year Roman occupation can be found: for example, the famous 'Römerschatz' (Roman treasure) which is shown in the Gäubodenmuseum. <i>Sorviodurum</i>, as the Romans called it, was an important military support base.</p>
<p>After the fall of the Roman Empire Straubing became a centre of settlement of the <a href="/wiki/Bavarii" class="mw-redirect" title="Bavarii">Bavarii</a>, mostly around <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Church,_Straubing" title="St. Peter's Church, Straubing">St. Peter's Church</a> (built in the 9th century) between Allachbach and Danube. According to the customs of the Bavarii the settlement was named after their leader <i>Strupinga</i>, which later evolved into the name Straubing.</p>
<p>In 1218 a new part of the city (called 'new town') was founded by Duke <a href="/wiki/Louis_I_Wittelsbach,_Duke_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Louis I Wittelsbach, Duke of Bavaria">Ludwig I Wittelsbach</a> of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Bavaria" title="Duchy of Bavaria">Bavaria</a>. Straubing became the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Bavaria-Straubing" class="mw-redirect" title="Duchy of Bavaria-Straubing">Duchy of Bavaria-Straubing</a> under <a href="/wiki/William_V,_Count_of_Hainaut" class="mw-redirect" title="William V, Count of Hainaut">Duke Wilhelm I</a> when Bavaria was divided among the sons of <a href="/wiki/Louis_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Louis IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Louis IV, Holy Roman Emperor</a> in 1349. In 1429 Straubing passed to <a href="/wiki/Ernest,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Ernest, Duke of Bavaria">Ernest, Duke of Bavaria</a>-<a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, who ordered the murder of <a href="/wiki/Agnes_Bernauer" title="Agnes Bernauer">Agnes Bernauer</a> in Straubing. The grave of Agnes Bernauer cannot be found. But in the graveyard of St. Peter's Church is a chapel built by Duke Ernest.</p>
<p>In 1633, during the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>, the Swedish army successfully besieged the city.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nowadays, this new town is the centre of Straubing with many shops, offices, restaurants and a pedestrian area. Most buildings there still have medieval style. The nightlife of Straubing, with many pubs and discothèques, is concentrated in this area.</p>
<p>The most important buildings are the beautiful Gothic cathedral-like Basilica of St. Jacob, the Romanesque St. Peter's Church, the <a href="/wiki/Carmelite" class="mw-redirect" title="Carmelite">Carmelite</a> monastery with its Baroque church and library, St. Vitus's, where you can find a life-size personification of "state and church" joined in holy matrimony.</p>
<p>Between 1933 and 1945 most of the members of the then small Jewish community of Straubing were murdered or forced to emigrate. In 2006, Straubing had a lively Jewish community with around 950 members.</p>
<p>During a rally in June 1940, when Straubing and Bogen held its Kriegskreistag, some 20,000 people gathered at the <i>Großdeutschlandplatz</i>. Among the speakers were <a href="/wiki/Gauleiter" title="Gauleiter">Gauleiter</a> <a href="/wiki/Fritz_W%C3%A4chtler" title="Fritz Wächtler">Wächtler</a> and <i>Gauamtsleiter</i> Erbersdobler.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In July 1940, the <i>Donau-Zeitung</i> reported that the Straubing <i>Kreisleiter</i>, Anton Putz, had flown toward <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and not returned.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1944 and 1945, Straubing suffered from several American air raids. The local military hospital was destroyed to the extent of 80 percent with a loss of 45 patients.</p>
<p>In November 2016 a fire destroyed a greater part of the medieval town hall.</p>
<p>Straubing has many industrial areas and a port at the river Danube with access to the <a href="/wiki/Rhine-Main-Danube_Canal" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhine-Main-Danube Canal">Rhine-Main-Danube Canal</a>, a connection from the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>. It is the centre of the Bavarian high tech offensive in <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a>.</p>
