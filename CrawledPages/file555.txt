<p>The <b>Universities Research Association, Inc.</b> (<b>URA</b>) is a consortium of 90 leading research-oriented universities primarily in the United States, with members also in Canada, Japan, Italy, and the United Kingdom. The not-for-profit URA corporation was founded in 1965 for management and operation of research facilities in the national interest.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_purpose">History and purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universities_Research_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The URA was founded in 1965, under the directive of the U.S. <a href="/wiki/President%27s_Science_Advisory_Committee" title="President's Science Advisory Committee">Presidential Advisory Committee</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_National_Academy_of_Sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="United States National Academy of Sciences">National Academy of Sciences</a>. Its charter involves the management and operation of research facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-URA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-URA-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1965 and 2007, URA was the prime contractor to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">U.S. Department of Energy</a> (and its predecessor organizations) for the creation and operation of <a href="/wiki/Fermi_National_Accelerator_Laboratory" class="mw-redirect" title="Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory">Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory</a> near Batavia, Illinois. As of January 1, 2007, Fermilab is operated by the Fermi Research Alliance, LLC, a limited liability corporation with two members, the University of Chicago and Universities Research Association (URA). <a href="/wiki/Fermilab" title="Fermilab">Fermilab</a> is home to the <a href="/wiki/Tevatron" title="Tevatron">Tevatron</a>, which, until the recent initiation of the <a href="/wiki/Large_Hadron_Collider" title="Large Hadron Collider">Large Hadron Collider (LHC)</a> at <a href="/wiki/CERN" title="CERN">European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN)</a>, was for many years the world's most important highest-energy accelerator for elementary particle physics research.<sup id="cite_ref-URA_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-URA-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the fiscal year 2010, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">Department of Energy</a> funding for URA is approximately $400 million. <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a> and <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> grant funding is about $1.0 million. <a href="/wiki/Fermilab" title="Fermilab">Fermilab</a> also received an additional one-time allocation of $114 million over FY 2009 and FY 2010 under the <a href="/wiki/American_Recovery_and_Reinvestment_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="American Recovery and Reinvestment Act">American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-URA_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-URA-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Universities_Research_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The corporation acts under the authority of its governing body, the Council of Presidents of its 89 member universities. A board of trustees appoints governing boards for each major research activity. The Washington headquarters office of URA coordinates the activities of the Council and boards. It is responsible for oversight and governance of the URA's enterprises and for corporate relations with the federal government, industry, academe, and the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-URA_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-URA-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
