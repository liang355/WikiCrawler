<p><b>Weiden in der Oberpfalz</b> (official name: Weiden i.d.OPf.) is a district-free city in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is located 100&#160;km east of Nuremberg and 35&#160;km west of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic%E2%80%93Germany_border" title="Czech Republic–Germany border">Czech border</a>. A branch of the <a href="/wiki/German_Army" title="German Army">German Army</a> is located here.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weiden_in_der_Oberpfalz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weiden in der Oberpfalz was first mentioned in a document in 1241 as Weiden. It is assumed that the first settlements in Weiden are dated the year 1000. Located at the intersection of two major trading routes (Goldene Strasse and Magdeburger Strasse) Weiden soon became an important trading center with a population of 2,200 in 1531. An economic boom came along in 1863 when Weiden was connected to the railroad network. Some major companies of the glass and china industry settled in Weiden and the population increased.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weiden_in_der_Oberpfalz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_buildings">Historical buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weiden_in_der_Oberpfalz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Historical buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i>St. Josef</i>. Catholic church, built 1899–1900, Neo <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> style with <a href="/wiki/Art_nouveau" class="mw-redirect" title="Art nouveau">art nouveau</a> interior.</li>
<li><i>Marktplatz</i>, the market square stretched between Upper and Lower Gates with numerous <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">renaissance</a> buildings.</li>
<li><i>Altes Rathaus</i> ("Old Town Hall"), built between 1539 and 1545 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Nopl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hans Nopl (page does not exist)">Hans Nopl</a>.</li>
<li><i>Stadtpfarrkirche St. Michael</i>. A <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> church, rebuilt several times. The church got its present baroque appearance from 1627, the tower from 1761.</li>
<li><i>Alte Stadtmauer</i>, partially preserved old city walls from the 14th century.</li>
<li><i>St. Sebastian Kirche</i>. Catholic chapel, built around 1480.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weiden_in_der_Oberpfalz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
