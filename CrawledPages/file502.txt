<p><b>Miltenberg</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is bounded by (from the north and clockwise) the city of <a href="/wiki/Aschaffenburg" title="Aschaffenburg">Aschaffenburg</a>, the districts of <a href="/wiki/Aschaffenburg_(district)" title="Aschaffenburg (district)">Aschaffenburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Main-Spessart" title="Main-Spessart">Main-Spessart</a>, and the states of <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a> (districts of <a href="/wiki/Main-Tauber_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Main-Tauber (district)">Main-Tauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neckar-Odenwald" class="mw-redirect" title="Neckar-Odenwald">Neckar-Odenwald</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a> (districts of <a href="/wiki/Odenwaldkreis" title="Odenwaldkreis">Odenwaldkreis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darmstadt-Dieburg" title="Darmstadt-Dieburg">Darmstadt-Dieburg</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miltenberg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the Middle Ages there was continuous fighting between the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Mainz" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishop of Mainz">Archbishop of Mainz</a> and the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Rieneck" title="County of Rieneck">Counts of Rieneck</a>. Both attempted to rule the region and erected castles in the <a href="/wiki/Spessart" title="Spessart">Spessart</a> hills. Later other small fiefs became involved in these fights as well.</p>
<p>During the 13th century the towns along the river <a href="/wiki/Main_(river)" title="Main (river)">Main</a> emerged. Due to the trade on the river their wealth grew, and this became a very prosperous region. Prosperity ended abruptly in the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>, when the area was devastated and depopulated.</p>
<p>In 1803, the ecclesial states of Germany were dissolved, among them the Archbishopric of Mainz. By 1816, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a> had annexed the entire region.</p>
<p>The district of Miltenberg was established in 1972 by merging the former districts of Miltenberg and Obernburg.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miltenberg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district is located in a hilly area on both banks of the river Main. On the western bank the <a href="/wiki/Odenwald" title="Odenwald">Odenwald</a> hills are rising, and on the eastern bank lies the <a href="/wiki/Spessart" title="Spessart">Spessart</a> range.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miltenberg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013 (latest data available) the <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP</a> per inhabitant was €29,224. This places the district 50th out of 96 districts (rural and urban) in Bavaria (overall average: €39,691).<sup id="cite_ref-Stat2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stat2-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miltenberg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district's <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> might be described thus: Gules a pallet wavy argent, dexter a wheel spoked of six of the same, sinister dancetty of three of the first and second, a chief bendy lozengy argent and azure.</p>
<table>
<tr valign="top">
<td><a href="/wiki/File:Wappen_Landkreis_Miltenberg.svg" class="image" title="Coat of arms"><img alt="Coat of arms" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Wappen_Landkreis_Miltenberg.svg/130px-Wappen_Landkreis_Miltenberg.svg.png" width="130" height="139" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Wappen_Landkreis_Miltenberg.svg/195px-Wappen_Landkreis_Miltenberg.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Wappen_Landkreis_Miltenberg.svg/260px-Wappen_Landkreis_Miltenberg.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="544" data-file-height="581" /></a></td>
<td>The coat of arms displays:
<ul>
<li>the blue and silver tilted diamond pattern ("bendy lozengy") of Bavaria's flag in the chief (band at the top)</li>
<li>a silver wheel, the <a href="/wiki/Wheel_of_Mainz" title="Wheel of Mainz">Wheel of Mainz</a>, symbolising the Archbishopric of Mainz</li>
<li>a wavy pallet (narrow vertical stripe), symbolising the Main</li>
<li>three silver points over a red field, the "<a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconian</a> Rake", symbolising the <a href="/wiki/Prince-Bishopric_of_W%C3%BCrzburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince-Bishopric of Würzburg">Prince-Bishopric of Würzburg</a></li>
</ul>
</td>
