<p><b>Recklinghausen</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˌʁɛklɪŋˈhaʊzn̩]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Recklinghausen.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/56/De-Recklinghausen.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Recklinghausen.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>) is the northernmost city in the Ruhr-Area and the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Recklinghausen_(district)" title="Recklinghausen (district)">Recklinghausen district</a>. It borders the rural Münsterland and is characterized by large fields and farms in the north and industry in the south. Recklinghausen is the 60th-largest city in Germany and the 22nd-largest city in North Rhine-Westphalia.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Recklinghausen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First mentioned in 1017 as <i>Ricoldinchuson</i>, in 1150 the city was the center of the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Vest_Recklinghausen" title="Vest Recklinghausen">Vest Recklinghausen</a>. In 1236, the city of Recklinghausen received <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">town privileges</a>. As part of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=County_of_Vest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="County of Vest (page does not exist)">County of Vest</a>, ownership of Recklinghausen changed several times in the 15th and 16th century, and in 1576, the entire county was pawned to the <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Cologne" title="Electorate of Cologne">Elector of Cologne</a>. In 1582-83, again in 1586, and again in 1587, the city was plundered by partisan armies during the <a href="/wiki/Cologne_War" title="Cologne War">Cologne War</a>, a feud over religious parity in <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Cologne" title="Electorate of Cologne">Electorate of Cologne</a> and electoral influence in the Holy Roman Empire.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Recklinghausen-Merian.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Recklinghausen-Merian.png/260px-Recklinghausen-Merian.png" width="260" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Recklinghausen-Merian.png/390px-Recklinghausen-Merian.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Recklinghausen-Merian.png/520px-Recklinghausen-Merian.png 2x" data-file-width="1425" data-file-height="697" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Recklinghausen-Merian.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Seventeenth-century Recklinghausen</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Recklinghausen was also the site of more than 100 <a href="/wiki/Witch-hunt" title="Witch-hunt">witchcraft trials</a> (1514–1710)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The trial activity reached a climax twice: In the time period of 1580/81 and again in 1588/89. The last person to be convicted of <a href="/wiki/Witchcraft" title="Witchcraft">witchcraft</a> was Anna Spickermann; after spending 16&#160;months in prison, she was sentenced to death by sword and burned afterward.</p>
<p>Circa 1600, the administration of the Vest Recklinghausen was divided into two parts, with the eastern part administered by Recklinghausen. The town of Recklinghausen including the parish of Recklinghausen and the parishes <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahsen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ahsen (page does not exist)">Ahsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Datteln" title="Datteln">Datteln</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Flaesheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Flaesheim (page does not exist)">Flaesheim</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm-Bossendorf&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hamm-Bossendorf (page does not exist)">Hamm-Bossendorf</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrichenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Henrichenburg (page does not exist)">Henrichenburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herten" title="Herten">Herten</a>, Horneburg, <a href="/wiki/Oer" title="Oer">Oer</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Suderwich&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Suderwich (page does not exist)">Suderwich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waltrop" title="Waltrop">Waltrop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Westerholt" title="Westerholt">Westerholt</a>. Circa 1815, the Vest was made a <a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%BCrgermeisterei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bürgermeisterei (page does not exist)">Bürgermeisterei</a>, with the town becoming the seat. In 1819, <a href="/wiki/Herten" title="Herten">Herten</a> joined the Recklinghausen Bürgermeisterei, and <a href="/wiki/Oer-Erkenschwick" title="Oer-Erkenschwick">Erkenschwick</a> followed in 1821.</p>
<p>As a target of the <a href="/wiki/Oil_Campaign_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil Campaign of World War II">Oil Campaign of World War II</a>, oil production at Recklinghausen/Forstezung<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was bombed by the RAF on 15 January 1945; and South Recklinghausen (<i>Recklinghausen Süd</i>) was captured by the US <a href="/wiki/35th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="35th Infantry Division (United States)">137th Infantry</a> on 1 April 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Recklinghausen is home to a museum of <a href="/wiki/Icons" class="mw-redirect" title="Icons">icons</a>, which includes more than 1,000 <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Orthodox</a> works from <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Balkan" class="mw-redirect" title="Balkan">Balkan</a> countries, as well as early <a href="/wiki/Copt" class="mw-redirect" title="Copt">Coptic</a> <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> art from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. The icon museum - the largest outside the Orthodox world - was founded in 1956 and reopened after renovation in February 2006 for its 50th anniversary.</p>
<p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruhrfestpielhaus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ruhrfestpielhaus (page does not exist)">Ruhrfestpielhaus</a>, whose remodeling in 2001 won the German Architecture Award is home of "Die Liegende Nr 5", a famous sculpture by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Moore" title="Henry Moore">Henry Moore</a>. At the Lohtor in front of a memorial for the victims of World War I, there is a large sculpture made of more than 30,000 bricks by <a href="/wiki/Per_Kirkeby" title="Per Kirkeby">Per Kirkeby</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_events">Annual events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Recklinghausen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Annual events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Recklinghausen hosts the annual <a href="/wiki/Ruhrfestspiele" title="Ruhrfestspiele">Ruhrfestspiele</a>, a cultural festival with an international reputation. Every year there is a cultural programme with many national and international theatrical productions starting on 1 May. In 2008 the programme included the play <i>Speed the Plow</i> starring <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Spacey" title="Kevin Spacey">Kevin Spacey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Goldblum" title="Jeff Goldblum">Jeff Goldblum</a> as one of the major productions. The main theatrical stage is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruhrfestspielhaus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ruhrfestspielhaus (page does not exist)">Ruhrfestspielhaus</a> but other theatres in and around Recklinghausen participate.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Recklinghausen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The two major motorways crossing the area of the city are the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_2" title="Bundesautobahn 2">A2</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_43" title="Bundesautobahn 43">A43</a>. The city is connected to the larger waterways by the <a href="/wiki/Rhein-Herne-Kanal" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhein-Herne-Kanal">Rhein-Herne-Kanal</a>. Recklinghausen has two railway stations. The <i>Central Station</i> (<a href="/wiki/Recklinghausen_Hauptbahnhof" title="Recklinghausen Hauptbahnhof">Recklinghausen Hauptbahnhof</a>), which is served by <a href="/wiki/Intercity_(Deutsche_Bahn)" title="Intercity (Deutsche Bahn)">Intercity</a> and <a href="/wiki/EuroCity" title="EuroCity">EuroCity</a> services, and the <i><a href="/wiki/Recklinghausen_S%C3%BCd_station" title="Recklinghausen Süd station">South Station</a></i> (Recklinghausen Südbahnhof).</p>
