<p><b>Hamm</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈham]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Hamm.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d8/De-Hamm.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Hamm.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>: <i>Hammona</i>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is located in the northeastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Ruhr_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhr area">Ruhr area</a>. As of December 2003 its population was 180,849. The city is situated between the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_1" title="Bundesautobahn 1">A1 motorway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_2" title="Bundesautobahn 2">A2 motorway</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hamm_(Westfalen)_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamm (Westfalen) railway station">Hamm railway station</a> is an important hub for rail transport and renowned for its distinctive station building.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The coat of arms has been in use in its present form for about 750 years. It shows the markish chessboard ("märkischen Schachbalken") in red and silver on a golden field. Originally it was the founders' coat of arms, i. e. the Counts of Mark. The chessboard and the colours are often displayed in the coats of arms of further towns founded by that family line. Similarly, the colours of the city are red and white.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name <i>Ham</i> means "corner" in the old <a href="/wiki/Low_German" title="Low German">Low German</a> dialect spoken at that time. In the old times the name <i>thom Hamme</i> would be used, which evolved slowly into its modern form <i>Hamm</i>. The name derives from the description of the Hamm's location in the corner of the Lippe river and the narrow Ahse affluent, where it was founded on Ash Wednesday in March 1226 by Count Adolf I of the <a href="/wiki/Mark_(county)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mark (county)">Mark</a>.</p>

<ul>
<li>1350 The <a href="/wiki/Black_death" class="mw-redirect" title="Black death">Black death</a> killed nearly all of the citizens. Only seven families survived.</li>
<li>1469 Hamm became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hanseatic_League" title="Hanseatic League">Hanseatic League</a>. It was one of the most powerful towns in the region, while the large cities of the today's Ruhr area still were only tiny villages.</li>
<li>1614 The Treaty of Xanten ends the conflict about the heritage of Cleve-Mark, the <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">Electorate</a> Brandenburg (later <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a>) inherited the Ducal Cleve and the counties Ravensburg and Mark (with Hamm)</li>
<li>1618-1648 <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>, Hamm was taken several times by different armed forces and had to endure changing garrisons. Almost all buildings were destroyed, except for the main church St. Georg (today: Pauluskirche) and St. Agnes church.</li>
<li>1657 Establishment of the Gynasium illustre (later named Gymnasium Hammonense) with three faculties (<a href="/wiki/Theology" title="Theology">theology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jurisprudence" title="Jurisprudence">jurisprudence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>).</li>
<li>1753 Establishment of the regional court (Landgericht)</li>
<li>1767 "Märkische Kammerdeputation" established</li>
<li>1769 Brewery Isenbeck founded</li>
<li>1787 Changing of the "Märkische Kammerdeputation" into the "Märkische Kriegs- und Domänenkammer" by the Prussian "Generaldirektorium".</li>
<li>1818 Hamm has 4,688 inhabitants.</li>
<li>1820 The regional appeal court moves from Cleve to Hamm.</li>
<li>1847 First train stops at the main station Hamm</li>
<li>1853 Westfälische Union (later Thyssen Draht AG) was founded</li>
<li>1856 Westfälische Draht Industrie was founded (later Klöckner Draht GmbH, today Westfälische Draht Industrie (WDI))</li>
<li>1901 30,000 inhabitants, the district Hamm is split up into the urban district of Hamm (City) and the district of Unna.</li>
<li>1901 Coal-mine de Wendel in Herringen starts mining (later Heinrich-Robert, now Bergwerk Ost) (first coal output 1904)</li>
<li>1902 Coal-mine Maximilian in Werries/Ostwennemar starts mining (first coal output 1907)</li>
<li>1905 Coal-mine Radbod in Bockum-Hövel starts mining (first coal output 1905)</li>
<li>1912 Coal-mine Sachsen in Heessen starts mining (first coal output 1914)</li>
<li>1914 Datteln-Hamm-Canal is completed including the new city port</li>
<li>1938 The A2 (motorway) reaches Hamm</li>
<li>1939-1945 55 air raids destroy nearly 60% of the old city and leave only a few historical buildings.</li>
<li>1944 Coal-mine Maximillian closes after several problems with water drainage of the hole mine (completely flooded in 1914).</li>
<li>1945 First meeting of the city council after the war</li>
<li>1946 Establishment of the industrial court and the industrial court of appeal by the Allied Control Council.</li>
<li>1953 Windsor Boys' School opens for the children on British Service personnel</li>
<li>1956 Sport airfield founded in the Lippe meadows.</li>
<li>1965 A1 (motorway) reaches Hamm.</li>
<li>1976 Coal-mine Sachsen closes</li>
<li>1983 Windsor Boys' School closes</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_Structure">Politics and Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Politics and Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_council">City council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: City council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city council has 58 members</p>
<ul>
<li>Pirate Party Germany: 1 seat</li>
<li>Alliance 90/The Greens: 4 seats</li>
<li>SPD: 20 seats</li>
<li>Free voters: 2 seats</li>
<li>FDP: 2 seats</li>
<li>CDU: 25 seats</li>
<li>RECHTE (The right): 1 seat</li>
<li>THE LEFT: 3 seats</li>
</ul>
<p>Head of the city Council is the "<a href="/wiki/Oberb%C3%BCrgermeister" class="mw-redirect" title="Oberbürgermeister">Oberbürgermeister</a>" (<a href="/wiki/Lord_mayor" title="Lord mayor">Lord mayor</a>) the chief executive (since 1999) of the administration. The "Oberbürgermeister" is elected directly for a five years term, together with the city council. The "Oberstadtdirektor" was the non-elected chief executive before the reform of the Northrhine-Westphalian local administration in 1999, the "Oberbürgermeister" in the period before 1999 was only the elected representative of a larger town or city.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Incorporations">Incorporations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Incorporations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1939, 1968 and 1975 Hamm incorporated several towns and municipalities: in 1939 the village Mark (which the Counts and the county took the name of) and in 1968 the villages of Berge and Westtünnen. In the reorganisation of 1975, the following towns and municipalities were incorporated into the City of Hamm:<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<ol>
<li>The town of <b>Bockum-Hövel</b>, Lüdinghausen district</li>
<li>The town of <b>Heessen</b>, Beckum district</li>
<li>The municipality of <b>Uentrop</b>, Unna district, formed in 1968, including the municipalities of Braam-Ostwennemar, Frielinghausen, Haaren, Norddinker, Schmehausen, Uentrop, Vöckinghausen and Werries</li>
<li>The municipality of <b><a href="/wiki/Rhynern" title="Rhynern">Rhynern</a></b>, (Unna district, without Hilbeck incorporated by Werl), formed in 1968, including the municipalities of Allen, Freiske, Hilbeck, Osterflierich, Osttünnen, Rhynern, Süddinker and Wambeln</li>
<li>The municipality of <b>Pelkum</b>, (Unna district), formed in 1968, including the municipalities of Herringen, Lerche, Pelkum, Sandbochum, Weetfeld and parts of Wiescherhöfen.</li>
</ol>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Miscellaneous">Miscellaneous</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Miscellaneous">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006 Hamm was the first city to accomplish a "Ratsbürgerentscheid" (citizens decision). Subject of the plebiscite was a plan to build a 43 ha city lake (2007–2010) near the city centre. 136,521 citizens were entitled to vote, 57,563 used that possibility and 56.9% refused their approbation for the project. As minimal vote 20% (of the total 136,521 voters) had to decide between one of the possibilities (20% for Yes or 20% for NO). The city council accepted the voting and stopped the plans. This procedure is planned for future projects in NRW. Also in Hamm established was the "Baugerichtstag e. V." a society organizing a congress about the German building law. The congress is held in a two years term.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The largest health facility is the Marien Hospital with its two separate buildings, Marien Hospital I the old building within the centre of the city and Marien Hospital II together 587 beds. Then there is the (EVK Hamm) Evangelisches Krankenhaus Hamm (Protestant Hospital) combined with the children's hospital south of the centre together 493 beds, the St. Barbara Klinik (Clinic) in Heessen with 422 beds and the Malteser Krankenhaus (Hospital) St. Josef in Bockum-Hövel with 260 beds. Additional there are the Klinik für manuelle Therapie (Clinic for manual therapy) within the quarter Bad Hamm (138 beds), the cure district. The Westfälisches Institut Hamm für Kinder- und Jugendpsychiatrie und psychotherapie (Westphalian Institute Hamm for Children's- and Youth psychiatry and psychotherapy) as an Institute of the federal state (158 beds) and the private Klinik (clinic) am Bärenbrunnen. (All <a href="/wiki/Hospital" title="Hospital">Hospitals</a> together have 2058 beds.) Former Hospitals are:</p>
<ul>
<li>The BWK <a href="/wiki/Bundeswehr" title="Bundeswehr">Bundeswehrkrankenhaus</a> Hamm (Hospital of the Federal Defence Forces) closed in 2007 after a reform of the German forces</li>
<li>Knappschaftskrankenhaus (Hospital operated by the health fund for miners (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Bundesknappschaft&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bundesknappschaft (page does not exist)">Bundesknappschaft</a>) now Marien Hospital II)</li>
<li>Märkische Kinderklinik (Children's Hospital combined with the Elisabeth Kinderklinik and now part of the EVK Hamm as "Klinik für Kinder- und Jugendmedizin")</li>
<li>The St. Elisabeth Kinderklinik (Children's Hospital combined with the Märkische Kinderklinik and now part of the EVK Hamm as "Klinik für Kinder- und Jugendmedizin")</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hamm has six <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">Gymnasien</a> (grammar schools), two comprehensive schools and several <a href="/wiki/Realschule" title="Realschule">Realschulen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hauptschule" title="Hauptschule">Hauptschulen</a> and Grundschulen (<a href="/wiki/Elementary_school" title="Elementary school">elementary schools</a>). The oldest Gymnasium in Hamm is the Gymnasium Hammonense which was established as academic school (small university) with three faculties in 1657. The school declined in its importance and in 1781 merged with the local Latin school and got reformed by Prussia. The new combined school was humanist Gymnasium. In 1867 the "Märkisches Gymnasium" followed as the second Gymnasium of Hamm, in 1902 the "<a href="/wiki/Freiherr_vom_Stein" class="mw-redirect" title="Freiherr vom Stein">Freiherr vom Stein</a> Gymnasium", in 1924 the "Beisenkamp Gymnasium" – first as "Oberlyceum" (Gymnasium for girls) – and in 1968 the "<a href="/wiki/Galilei" title="Galilei">Galilei</a>-Gymnasium" were established. Additionally in Schloss Heessen there is a private school including the Gymnasium. Both comprehensive schools in Hamm are younger foundations by the city during school reforms of NRW. Hamm is also well known for its many <a href="/wiki/Vocational_school" title="Vocational school">vocational schools</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li>"Eduard Spranger Berufskolleg für Technik" a vocational school for techniques</li>
<li>"Elisabeth Lüders Berufskolleg für Sozialwesen, Gesundheit, Hauswirtschaft und Kinderpflege" a vocational school for social welfare, health, home economics and child care, and several other schools.</li>
<li>"Friederich List Berufskolleg für Wirtschaft" a trade school</li>
</ul>
<p>In 2005 a small private university of applied sciences was established, the "SRH Fachhoschule Hamm". The private university of applied sciences started with two study programs ending with degrees of Bachelor and Master of Science for <a href="/wiki/Logistic_engineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Logistic engineering">logistic engineering</a>. The "Klinik für Psychatrie und Psychotherapie of the Marienhospital" cooperates with the University Witten-Herdecke in education and science. During the summer 2009 another university of applied sciences was founded by the federal state NRW, named Hochschule Hamm-Lippstadt. The University is divided in two departments, each with its own small Campus area, one in Hamm and another in <a href="/wiki/Lippstadt" title="Lippstadt">Lippstadt</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_and_economy">Industry and economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Industry and economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schloss_Heessen.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Schloss Heessen</div>

<p>Major industrial branches are the <a href="/wiki/Coal-mining" class="mw-redirect" title="Coal-mining">coal-mining</a> industry, <a href="/wiki/Steel_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Steel industry">steel industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_industry" title="Chemical industry">chemical industry</a> and the car component supplier industry. In the last century there were four coal-mines within the urban district. Today the Bergwerk Ost in Herringen is the last operating coal mine with about 3,000 employees. Mannesmann Hoesch Präzisrohr, Westfälische Draht Industrie (WDI) and Böhler Thyssen Welding are the major representatives of the steel industry, the chemical industry is represented by <a href="/wiki/DuPont" title="DuPont">DuPont</a> in Uentrop and the car supplier industry by Hella KGaA Hueck &amp; Co. factory 4 in Bockum-Hövel with about 1000 employees. The <a href="/wiki/Energy_industry" title="Energy industry">energy industry</a> is represented by a <a href="/wiki/RWE" title="RWE">RWE</a> coal power plant and a further power plant (Trianel) in Uentrop. A new coal power plant is currently under construction. The <a href="/wiki/THTR-300" title="THTR-300">THTR-300</a>, also in Uentrop-Schmehhausen, was decommissioned in 1989. Alongside the A2, in the southern part of the urban district, a new business park inhabits the growing <a href="/wiki/Logistics" title="Logistics">logistics</a> business. Hamm is also known as "City of Law" (Stadt des Rechts) because of the greatest German regional appeal court (Oberlandesgericht), the local court (Amtsgericht), the industrial court (Arbeitgericht) and the industrial appeal court (Landesarbeitsgericht). The Chamber of Notaries and bar association of the regional appeal court Hamm and the courts are of greater influence on the appearance of the city. Several hospitals in the urban district are also important employers, for example the EVK Hamm has about 1000 employees.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The only daily newspaper of Hamm printed there, is the "Westfälischer Anzeiger". At first, a weekly, newspaper in Hamm was the "Kreis Hammsches Wochenblatt" founded by Heinich Jakob Grote in 1822. Its name was changed in 1848 to "Westfälischer Anzeiger". A second newspaper appeared, founded by the Thiemann family, the "Westfälische Kurier". Both co-existed until the end of the Second World War. After the War both newspapers were combined and named "Westfälischer Anzeiger und Kurier". In the 1960s another name-change made the "Westfälischer Anzeiger". This newspaper is the mantle of several smaller regional newspapers, together they had a total 153,428 copies in 2004. In the 1970s the "Westdeutsche Allgemeine Zeitung" unsuccessfully tried to establish its own daily newspaper. Two weekly newspapers appear in Hamm the "Stadtanzeiger" from the "Westfälischer Anzeiger" with a total of 384.000 copies and the Sonntags-Rundblick by a medium-sized local company. Since 1990 the local radio station "Radio Lippewelle Hamm" is On Air and number one radio station of the local radios in NRW. On 3 October 1993 the "Offener Kanal Hamm" started broadcasting, it is a small TV project by people for the people, started through the federal state.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>Waldbühne Hamm-Heessen</b> in Heessen is one of the most active open-air theatres in Germany. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=St%C3%A4dtische_Musikschule_Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Städtische Musikschule Hamm (page does not exist)">Städtische Musikschule Hamm</a> is one of the oldest music schools in Germany. Hamm also has several active choirs and a jazz club and has been the home of many bands.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hamm is linked to three <a href="/wiki/Motorway" class="mw-redirect" title="Motorway">motorways</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_1" title="Bundesautobahn 1">Bundesautobahn 1</a> (BAB 1 or A1, Puttgarden-Saarbrücken) named Hansaline with two connections No.81 and 82., the (BAB 2 or A2, Oberhausen-Berlin) with three connections No.17,18 and 19 and the <a href="/wiki/A445" class="mw-redirect" title="A445">A445</a> (Hamm-Arnsberg) which is connected by the B63 until the planned construction between Hamm and <a href="/wiki/Werl" title="Werl">Werl</a> is completed. The <a href="/wiki/Kamener_Kreuz" title="Kamener Kreuz">Kamener Kreuz</a> is situated in the southwest of Hamm. Two "federal roads" (Bundesstrassen) the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bundesstrasse_61&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bundesstrasse 61 (page does not exist)">B61</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bundesstrasse_61&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bundesstrasse 61 (page does not exist)">B63</a> intersect in the city centre. Several state roads (Landesstrassen) are connecting Hamm with its neighbouring towns and municipalities.</p>

<p>Hamm has three stations, the main railway station <a href="/wiki/Hamm_(Westfalen)_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamm (Westfalen) railway station">Hamm (Westfalen)</a> and two minor stations, one in Bockum-Hövel and the other one in Heessen. The main station is one of the biggest railway hubs in Germany, and connected with one of the greatest <a href="/wiki/Marshalling_yard" class="mw-redirect" title="Marshalling yard">marshalling yards</a> of Europe, the latter now only partly operating. Notable is the railway station for its <a href="/wiki/Art_deco" class="mw-redirect" title="Art deco">Art deco</a></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%BCnderzeit" title="Gründerzeit">Gründerzeit</a> inspired building styles. Hamm is connected to the rail since 2 May 1847. Its huge <a href="/wiki/Classification_yard" title="Classification yard">railroad yard</a>—Europe's biggest at the time<sup id="cite_ref-Germany_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Germany-3">[3]</a></sup>—was bombed repeatedly during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> as was the city itself (in December 1944 it was hit by eleven raids on one day).<sup id="cite_ref-Germany_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Germany-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Buses">Buses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Buses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/City_bus" class="mw-redirect" title="City bus">city bus</a> net Hamm is served by the "Stadtwerke Hamm", with 65 buses, and the "Verkehrsgesellschaft Breitenbach". A regional bus service is served by different companies and both nets serve the central <a href="/wiki/Bus_station" title="Bus station">bus station</a>, which is situated in front of the main railway station in the centre of Hamm. Hamm is part of the Verkehrsgemeinschaft Ruhr-Lippe. About 12 million people are using the bus net every year, transported by 50 bus lines with 500 bus stops within the city.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canal">Canal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Canal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hamm is the end of the "<a href="/wiki/Datteln-Hamm-Kanal" class="mw-redirect" title="Datteln-Hamm-Kanal">Datteln-Hamm-Kanal</a>", three ports are situated in the urban district. The city port, the canal end port Uentrop, and the port of "Gersteinwerk". The ports of Hamm are the second biggest canal port by freight transact, 1.4 million tons a year by <a href="/wiki/Ship" title="Ship">ships</a> and 0.5 million tons by train. The city <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a> allows for ships up to 110 metres length 11.45 meters width and 2.7 metres draft. It is linked with the railway by a track to the near main railway station and the marshaling yard. <a href="/wiki/File:Karte_des_Datteln-Hamm-Kanals.png" class="image" title="Datteln-Hamm-Kanal"><img alt="Datteln-Hamm-Kanal" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Karte_des_Datteln-Hamm-Kanals.png/800px-Karte_des_Datteln-Hamm-Kanals.png" width="800" height="271" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Karte_des_Datteln-Hamm-Kanals.png/1200px-Karte_des_Datteln-Hamm-Kanals.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Karte_des_Datteln-Hamm-Kanals.png/1600px-Karte_des_Datteln-Hamm-Kanals.png 2x" data-file-width="2672" data-file-height="906" /></a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airfield_and_Airports">Airfield and Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Airfield and Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Near city centre, in the meadows of the River Lippe, the sport airfield Hamm is situated. Its runway is 900 m long and 30 m wide. The <a href="/wiki/Airfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Airfield">airfield</a> is operated by the Luftsportclub Hamm e. V.. Hamm is situated in the middle of a triangle of three smaller International <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">Airports</a>, in the north the <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnster_Osnabr%C3%BCck_International_Airport" title="Münster Osnabrück International Airport">Münster Osnabrück International Airport</a> (FMO) in the south-west <a href="/wiki/Dortmund_Airport" title="Dortmund Airport">Dortmund Airport</a> and in the east <a href="/wiki/Paderborn_Lippstadt_Airport" title="Paderborn Lippstadt Airport">Paderborn Lippstadt Airport</a>. The nearest greater <a href="/wiki/International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="International Airport">International Airport</a> is <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf_Airport" title="Düsseldorf Airport">Düsseldorf Airport</a>.</p>
