<p>The <b>Cornell Big Red men's ice hockey</b> team is a <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA) <a href="/wiki/Division_I_(NCAA)" class="mw-redirect" title="Division I (NCAA)">Division I</a> <a href="/wiki/College_ice_hockey" title="College ice hockey">college ice hockey</a> program that represents <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a>. Cornell competes in the <a href="/wiki/ECAC_Hockey" title="ECAC Hockey">ECAC Hockey</a> conference and plays its home games at <a href="/wiki/Lynah_Rink" title="Lynah Rink">Lynah Rink</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ithaca,_New_York" title="Ithaca, New York">Ithaca</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. Six of the eight <a href="/wiki/Ivy_League" title="Ivy League">Ivy League</a> schools sponsor men's hockey and all six teams play in the twelve-team ECAC. The Ivy League crowns a champion based on the results of the games played between its members during the ECAC season.</p>
<p>Cornell has won the ECAC Tournament Championship a record 12 times and has won the Ivy League Title 21 times, second to Harvard's 24.</p>
<p>The 1970 Cornell Hockey team, coached by <a href="/wiki/Ned_Harkness" title="Ned Harkness">Ned Harkness</a> was the first (and currently only team) in NCAA hockey history to win a national title while being undefeated and untied with a <a href="/wiki/Perfect_season" title="Perfect season">perfect</a> 29-0-0 record.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Big Red's archrival is the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Crimson_men%27s_ice_hockey" title="Harvard Crimson men's ice hockey">Harvard Crimson</a>. The teams meet at least twice each season for installments of the historic <a href="/wiki/Cornell-Harvard_hockey_rivalry" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornell-Harvard hockey rivalry">Cornell-Harvard hockey rivalry</a>.</p>
