			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Forbes Magazine's List of America's Best Colleges</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes.com</a> began publishing an annual list, prepared by the <i>Center for College Affordability and Productivity</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> of "America's Best Colleges".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <b>Student satisfaction</b> (evaluations from <a href="/wiki/RateMyProfessors.com" title="RateMyProfessors.com">RateMyProfessors.com</a>, <a href="/wiki/Retention_rate" title="Retention rate">retention rates</a> and targeted student satisfaction surveys on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>) constitutes 75% of the score. <b>Post-graduate success</b> (self-reported salaries of alumni from <a href="/wiki/PayScale" title="PayScale">PayScale</a>, alumni appearing on the CCAP's <i>America's Leaders List</i>) constitutes 32.5% of the score. <b>Student debt loads</b> constitute 25% of the score. The <b>graduation rate</b> (the proportion of students who complete four-year degrees in four years) constitutes 7.5% of the score. <b>Academic success</b> (the proportion of students receiving nationally competitive awards) constitutes 10% of the score. Public <a href="/wiki/Reputation" title="Reputation">reputation</a> is not considered, which causes some colleges to score lower than in other lists. A three-year <a href="/wiki/Moving_average" title="Moving average">moving average</a> is used to smooth out the scoring.</p>
<p>Starting in 2013, four schools that had admitted to misreporting admissions data were removed from the list for two years. The four removed colleges were <a href="/wiki/Bucknell_University" title="Bucknell University">Bucknell University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Claremont_McKenna_College" title="Claremont McKenna College">Claremont McKenna College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emory_University" title="Emory University">Emory University</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Iona_College_(New_York)" title="Iona College (New York)">Iona College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
