<p>The <b>athletic nickname</b>, or equivalently <b>athletic moniker</b>, of a <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> or <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">college</a> within the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> is the name officially adopted by that institution for at least the members of its <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">athletic</a> <a href="/wiki/Team" title="Team">teams</a>. Typically as a matter of engendering <a href="/wiki/School_spirit" title="School spirit">school spirit</a>, the institution either officially or unofficially uses this <a href="/wiki/Moniker" class="mw-redirect" title="Moniker">moniker</a> of the institution's athletic teams also as a <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a> to refer to people associated with the institution, especially its current <a href="/wiki/Student" title="Student">students</a>, but also often its <a href="/wiki/Alumni" class="mw-redirect" title="Alumni">alumni</a>, its <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(university)" class="mw-redirect" title="Faculty (university)">faculty</a>, and its <a href="/wiki/Academic_administration" title="Academic administration">administration</a> as well. This practice at the university and college tertiary higher-education level has proven so popular that it extended to the high school secondary-education level in the USA and in recent years even to the primary-education level as well.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletic_nickname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the USA, multiple recurring themes have appeared over time for choosing a school's athletic nickname. In almost all cases, the institution chooses an athletic nickname with an overtly positive goal in mind, where that goal reflects the character of the institution—either a previously established characteristic or a characteristic hoped for as a goal henceforth.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Abstract_concept">Abstract concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletic_nickname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Abstract concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Often by choosing an abstract concept as its athletic moniker, the institution wants to inspire its student-athletes on and off the field to achieve success that the abstract concept represents. Examples: <a href="/wiki/Cornell_Big_Red" title="Cornell Big Red">Cornell Big Red</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanford_Cardinal" title="Stanford Cardinal">Stanford Cardinal</a>, <a href="/wiki/UIC_Flames" title="UIC Flames">UIC Flames</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tulane_Green_Wave" title="Tulane Green Wave">Tulane Green Wave</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animal">Animal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletic_nickname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Animal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Often by choosing an <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">animal</a>, the school wants to emphasize the instillation of <a href="/wiki/Fear" title="Fear">fear</a> of losing athletic competitions to the institution's teams, such as through an especially fierce or stealthy animal. When the school chooses an animal as its athletic nickname, usually in the plural or as a collective noun for a group of that animal, then typically, the school has that animal (in the singular) as its <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">mascot</a>, either specifically named with a proper noun or generically referred to without a proper noun. Examples: <a href="/wiki/Michigan_Wolverines" title="Michigan Wolverines">Michigan Wolverines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Ducks" title="Oregon Ducks">Oregon Ducks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princeton_Tigers" title="Princeton Tigers">Princeton Tigers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iowa_Hawkeyes" title="Iowa Hawkeyes">Iowa Hawkeyes</a>, <a href="/wiki/California_Golden_Bears" title="California Golden Bears">California Golden Bears</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Collection">Collection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletic_nickname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Collection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Often by choosing a collection that represents a summary of the institution's students or of its history. Such a collection may refer to an <a href="/wiki/Ethnicity" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnicity">ethnicity</a>; a <a href="/wiki/Profession" title="Profession">profession</a>; religious designation, such as saints; or other groupings of people. A portion of athletic monikers that fall into this collection category started originally as <a href="/wiki/Derogatory" class="mw-redirect" title="Derogatory">derogatory</a> <a href="/wiki/Epithet" title="Epithet">epithets</a> from others, but as an act of defiance, the school embraced the term as a rallying cry to overcome the term's negative origin. Because a collection is hard to represent or <a href="/wiki/Icon" title="Icon">iconify</a>, when a school chooses a collection as its athletic nickname, the school typically chooses a related but different <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">mascot</a> that symbolizes that collection. Examples: <a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_Fighting_Irish" title="Notre Dame Fighting Irish">Notre Dame Fighting Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_Sooners" title="Oklahoma Sooners">Oklahoma Sooners</a>, <a href="/wiki/Purdue_Boilermakers" title="Purdue Boilermakers">Purdue Boilermakers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois_Fighting_Illini" title="Illinois Fighting Illini">Illinois Fighting Illini</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hero_or_archetype">Hero or archetype</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletic_nickname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hero or archetype">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A small number of schools choose an <a href="/wiki/Archetype" title="Archetype">archetypical</a> heroic person as their official athletic nickname. This person may be a graduate of the school who is viewed as embodying the school's mission or an archetypal person who is symbolic of the school's area, such as the <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia_University" title="West Virginia University">West Virginia University</a> Mountaineer. In religiously affiliated schools, this person may be a historical person in the religion who has been bestowed an official designation in that religion, such as a <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saint</a> in <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Orthodox Church">Orthodox Christianity</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Native_American_likeness">Native American likeness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletic_nickname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Native American likeness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Native_American_mascot_controversy" title="Native American mascot controversy">Native American mascot controversy</a></div>
<p>Likenesses to <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a> were at one time widely popular athletic monikers, especially for schools that adopted them in the 19th or early 20th century. In recent years, some Native American organizations have protested the unlicensed use of likenesses of Native Americans related to team names, team <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logos</a>, athletic monikers, <a href="/wiki/Cheerleader" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheerleader">cheerleaders</a>, and cheering techniques. The granting of overtly expressed written licenses by Native American organizations to use likenesses of Native Americans in these ways is rare, although not unheard of. In one notable example, two major groups of the <a href="/wiki/Seminole" title="Seminole">Seminole</a> nation, the Seminole Tribe of Florida and Seminole Nation of Oklahoma, have expressly given <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">Florida State University</a> permission to use the nickname "Seminoles" and certain Seminole imagery. <a href="/wiki/Central_Michigan_University" title="Central Michigan University">Central Michigan University</a> has a similar arrangement with the <a href="/wiki/Saginaw_Chippewa_Indian_Tribe" class="mw-redirect" title="Saginaw Chippewa Indian Tribe">Saginaw Chippewa Indian Tribe</a> to use the name "Chippewas".</p>
<p>Because of protests from some Native American organizations, some schools have changed their athletic moniker and mascot and cheering practices without significant objection once the issue was raised, especially if such offense toward a group of people was viewed as incompatible with that school's stated mission or if the threat of legal action was too burdensome. Other schools or their student bodies have defended their use of Native American likenesses, especially if the institution views the use of Native American likenesses as respectful or so intimately tied with history to be inseparable from the institution, such as if the name of institution derives from the name of a tribe. Still other schools have embarked on a series of failed attempts to find a replacement.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_and_uncommon_names">Common and uncommon names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletic_nickname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Common and uncommon names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Often, certain nicknames (animals and some abstract concepts, such as <a href="/wiki/Giant_(mythology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Giant (mythology)">Giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Broncos" class="mw-redirect" title="Broncos">Broncos</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Wildcats" class="mw-redirect" title="Wildcats">Wildcats</a>) become very common. However, some nicknames are unique to that school/team such as <a href="/wiki/Illinois_Fighting_Illini" title="Illinois Fighting Illini">Illini</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wake_Forest_Demon_Deacons" title="Wake Forest Demon Deacons">Demon Deacons</a>.</p>
