<p><b>DoubleTree by Hilton</b> is an American hotel chain and a part of <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Worldwide" title="Hilton Worldwide">Hilton Worldwide</a>. Most DoubleTree hotels are independently owned and operated by <a href="/wiki/Franchising" title="Franchising">franchisees</a>, but some are owned and/or managed by Hilton Worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> DoubleTree has been the fastest growing Hilton brand by number of properties since 2007, and by number of rooms from 2007-2015, until in 2016 <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Garden_Inn" title="Hilton Garden Inn">Hilton Garden Inn</a> opened more rooms.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2016_10k_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2016_10k-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DoubleTree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first DoubleTree hotel opened in <a href="/wiki/Scottsdale,_Arizona" title="Scottsdale, Arizona">Scottsdale, Arizona</a>, in 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Doubletree Corporation merged with Promus Hotel Corporation in December 1997, bringing together the Doubletree, <a href="/wiki/Red_Lion_Hotels" title="Red Lion Hotels">Red Lion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Inn" class="mw-redirect" title="Hampton Inn">Hampton Inn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Embassy_Suites_Hotels" class="mw-redirect" title="Embassy Suites Hotels">Embassy Suites</a> brands.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In December 1999, Hilton Hotels Corporation acquired <a href="/wiki/Promus_Hotel_Corporation" title="Promus Hotel Corporation">Promus Hotel Corporation</a> which brought Doubletree Hotels and other Promus hotel brands under the umbrella of the newly renamed Hilton Worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In early 2011, Hilton Worldwide launched a logo and name rebranding for the chain, replacing the name "Doubletree" with "DoubleTree by Hilton".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>DoubleTree's strategy to grow the brand has been to convince operators of other brands to switch flags. This is in contrast to brands like Marriott or Sheraton which rely on new construction to grow their footprint. DoubleTree also provides lower cost options to improve properties, which help operators remain in the brand system.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The world's largest DoubleTree, a 1,094 room resort, opened in <a href="/wiki/Orlando" class="mw-redirect" title="Orlando">Orlando</a> on <a href="/wiki/International_Drive" title="International Drive">International Drive</a> in 2009 after a renovation that cost $35 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
