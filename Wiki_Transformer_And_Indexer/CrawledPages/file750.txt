<p><b>Varsity</b> is an alteration and shortening of the term <i>university</i>. The meaning differs depending on the region, but is usually related to sporting activity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Varsity_in_the_United_Kingdom">Varsity in the United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varsity_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Varsity in the United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Varsity_match" title="Varsity match">Varsity match</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <b>varsity team</b> or varsity club refers to groups participating in <a href="/wiki/Varsity_match" title="Varsity match">varsity matches</a> in sport or other competitions between <a href="/wiki/University_rivalry#United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="University rivalry">rival universities</a>, most famously <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxford University">Oxford University</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge University">Cambridge University</a>; also <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College_London" title="King's College London">King's College London</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/University_College_London" title="University College London">University College London</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bristol" title="University of Bristol">University of Bristol</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_West_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="University of the West of England">University of the West of England</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Sheffield" title="University of Sheffield">University of Sheffield</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Hallam_University" title="Sheffield Hallam University">Sheffield Hallam University</a>, among others. The term originally referred strictly to university-sponsored teams, and dates from the 1840s.<sup id="cite_ref-DictDotCom_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DictDotCom-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Varsity_in_North_America">Varsity in North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varsity_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Varsity in North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> <b>varsity teams</b> are the principal athletic teams representing a <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">college</a>, <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a>, <a href="/wiki/High_school" class="mw-redirect" title="High school">high school</a>, or other <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> the term "Varsity" is rarely used amongst high school teams. Such teams compete against similar teams at corresponding educational institutions. Groups of varsity sports teams are often organized into <a href="/wiki/Athletic_conference" title="Athletic conference">athletic conferences</a>, which are groups of teams that regularly play each other during a given athletic season. In recognition of their high level of performance, athletes on varsity teams are often given <a href="/wiki/Varsity_letter" title="Varsity letter">varsity letters</a>. They are in contrast to the institution's <a href="/wiki/Collegiate_club_sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Collegiate club sports">club sports</a>. A major difference between varsity and club sports is the source for allocated funds. Varsity teams receive financial support, equipment, and facilities from college and university athletic department budgets.<sup id="cite_ref-UNBcuts_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNBcuts-2">[2]</a></sup> Universities often allocate club sport budgets through student life departments similar to other clubs on campus. Because club sports cost more than other clubs, many club student-athletes must <a href="/wiki/Pay_to_play" title="Pay to play">pay to play</a> and also engage in team fundraising efforts to pay for facilities time, equipment, and other team expenses.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> At various levels of collegiate sports, varsity student athletes are eligible for <a href="/wiki/Scholarships" class="mw-redirect" title="Scholarships">scholarships</a> solely or partially based on athletic skills.<sup id="cite_ref-UNBcuts_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNBcuts-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Varsity can be compared with the <a href="/wiki/Junior_varsity" class="mw-redirect" title="Junior varsity">junior varsity</a> level, which is typically for less-experienced <a href="/wiki/Underclassmen" class="mw-redirect" title="Underclassmen">underclassmen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Players may be able to go up a level to varsity playing by performing well.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Varsity_in_the_Netherlands">Varsity in the Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varsity_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Varsity in the Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the Varsity is the oldest and most prestigious rowing race. It was held for the first time in 1878, and was started as a Dutch equivalent for the Boat Race between Oxford and Cambridge.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Varsity_in_South_Africa">Varsity in South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varsity_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Varsity in South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Varsity_Football_(South_Africa)" title="Varsity Football (South Africa)">Varsity Football (South Africa)</a></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> and some other <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth countries">Commonwealth countries</a>, varsity is often still used in place of the word university, in contexts unrelated to sporting activity.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
