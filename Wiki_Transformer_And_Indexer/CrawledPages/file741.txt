<p>The <b>Yale Bulldogs</b> are the athletic teams of <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>. The school sponsors 35 varsity sports. The school has won two <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a> national championships in <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Fencing_team_championship" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA Fencing team championship">women's fencing</a>, four in <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Swimming_and_Diving_Championships" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA Men's Swimming and Diving Championships">men's swimming and diving</a>, 21 in <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_Men%27s_Golf_Championships" title="NCAA Division I Men's Golf Championships">men's golf</a> and one in men's hockey.</p>

<h2><span id="Men's_sports"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_sports">Men's sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Men's sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baseball">Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yale_Bulldogs_baseball" title="Yale Bulldogs baseball">Yale Bulldogs baseball</a></div>
<p>Major leaguers pitcher <a href="/wiki/Craig_Breslow" title="Craig Breslow">Craig Breslow</a> (Oakland A's and Boston Red Sox) and catcher <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lavarnway" title="Ryan Lavarnway">Ryan Lavarnway</a> (Boston Red Sox/Los Angeles Dodgers), among others, played baseball for the Bulldogs. Breslow led the Ivy League with a 2.56 ERA in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Lavarnway led the NCAA in <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting average</a> (.467) and <a href="/wiki/Slugging_percentage" title="Slugging percentage">slugging percentage</a> (.873) in 2007, set the Ivy League hitting-streak record (25), and through 2010 held the Ivy League record in career <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home runs</a> (33).<sup id="cite_ref-yalebulldogs1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yalebulldogs1-3">[3]</a></sup> In August 2012, Breslow and Lavarnway, playing for the Red Sox, became the first Yale grads to be Major League teammates since 1949.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Men's_basketball"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_basketball">Men's basketball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Men's basketball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yale_Bulldogs_men%27s_basketball" title="Yale Bulldogs men's basketball">Yale Bulldogs men's basketball</a></div>
<h3><span id="Men's_crew"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_crew">Men's crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Men's crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Harvard%E2%80%93Yale_Regatta" title="Harvard–Yale Regatta">Harvard–Yale Regatta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goldthwait_Cup" title="Goldthwait Cup">Goldthwait Cup</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Association_of_Rowing_Colleges" title="Eastern Association of Rowing Colleges">Eastern Association of Rowing Colleges</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yale_Bulldogs_football" title="Yale Bulldogs football">Yale Bulldogs football</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a> team has competed since 1876. They have won nineteen <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_FBS_National_Football_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA Division I FBS National Football Championship">national championships</a> when the school competed in what is now known as the <a href="/wiki/Football_Bowl_Subdivision" class="mw-redirect" title="Football Bowl Subdivision">FBS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> They are perhaps best known for their <a href="/wiki/Harvard%E2%80%93Yale_football_rivalry" title="Harvard–Yale football rivalry">rivalry</a> with <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard</a>, known as "The Game". Twenty one former players have been inducted into the <a href="/wiki/College_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="College Football Hall of Fame">College Football Hall of Fame</a>.</p>
<h3><span id="Men's_golf"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_golf">Men's golf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Men's golf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The men's golf team has won 21 <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_Men%27s_Golf_Championships" title="NCAA Division I Men's Golf Championships">collegiate team championships</a> (all except 1943 were bestowed by the National Intercollegiate Golf Association): 1897, 1898 (spring),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> 1902 (spring), 1905–13, 1915, 1924–26, 1931–33, 1936, 1943. They have crowned 13 individual champions: John Reid, Jr. (1898, spring), Charles Hitchcock, Jr. (1902, fall), Robert Abbott (1905), W. E. Clow, Jr. (1906), Ellis Knowles (1907), <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hunter_(golfer)" title="Robert Hunter (golfer)">Robert Hunter</a> (1910), George Stanley (1911), Nathaniel Wheeler (1913), Francis Blossom (1915), <a href="/wiki/Jess_Sweetser" title="Jess Sweetser">Jess Sweetser</a> (1920), Dexter Cummings (1923, 1924), Tom Aycock (1929). Both are records. They have won nine <a href="/wiki/Ivy_League" title="Ivy League">Ivy League</a> championships since the League championship was started in 1975: 1984–85, 1988, 1990–91, 1996–97, 2003, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Men's_ice_hockey"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_ice_hockey">Men's ice hockey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Men's ice hockey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yale_Bulldogs_men%27s_ice_hockey" title="Yale Bulldogs men's ice hockey">Yale Bulldogs men's ice hockey</a></div>
<p>The Yale Men's Ice Hockey team is the oldest existing <a href="/wiki/College_athletics" title="College athletics">intercollegiate</a> hockey program, having played its first game in 1896 against Johns Hopkins (a 2–2 tie).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The team competes in the <a href="/wiki/ECAC_Hockey" title="ECAC Hockey">ECAC Hockey League</a> (ECACHL); in addition the <a href="/wiki/Ivy_League" title="Ivy League">Ivy League</a> also crowns a champion for its members that field varsity ice hockey. The Bulldogs (coached by <a href="/wiki/Keith_Allain" title="Keith Allain">Keith Allain</a>) won the 2013 NCAA National Championship in Pittsburgh with a 4–0 shutout of Quinnipiac University.</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yale_Bulldogs_men%27s_lacrosse" title="Yale Bulldogs men's lacrosse">Yale Bulldogs men's lacrosse</a></div>
<h3><span id="Men's_soccer"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_soccer">Men's soccer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Men's soccer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before the NCAA began its tournament in 1959, the annual national champion was declared by the <a href="/wiki/Intercollegiate_Soccer_Football_Association" title="Intercollegiate Soccer Football Association">Intercollegiate Association Football League</a> (IAFL) — from 1911 to 1926 — and then the <a href="/wiki/Intercollegiate_Soccer_Football_Association" title="Intercollegiate Soccer Football Association">Intercollegiate Soccer Football Association</a> (ISFA), from 1927 to 1958. From 1911 to 1958, Yale won four national championships.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby">Rugby</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Bulldogs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Rugby">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yale_Rugby" title="Yale Rugby">Yale Rugby</a></div>
<p>Yale rugby plays <a href="/wiki/College_rugby" class="mw-redirect" title="College rugby">college rugby</a> in Division 1 in the <a href="/wiki/Ivy_Rugby_Conference" title="Ivy Rugby Conference">Ivy Rugby Conference</a>. Yale Rugby was founded in 1875, making it one of the oldest rugby teams in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> played rugby for Yale during his student days.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
