<p><b>Würzburg</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in the northwestern part of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Neighboring districts are (from the north, clockwise) <a href="/wiki/Main-Spessart" title="Main-Spessart">Main-Spessart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schweinfurt_(district)" title="Schweinfurt (district)">Schweinfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kitzingen_(district)" title="Kitzingen (district)">Kitzingen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neustadt_(Aisch)-Bad_Windsheim" title="Neustadt (Aisch)-Bad Windsheim">Neustadt (Aisch)-Bad Windsheim</a>, and the district <a href="/wiki/Main-Tauber_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Main-Tauber (district)">Main-Tauber</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>. The city <a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Würzburg">Würzburg</a> is not part of the district, although it is completely enclosed by it.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=W%C3%BCrzburg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1852, districts were created in the region, including the two precursor districts of Würzburg and Ochsenfurt. In 1972 the previous district Würzburg was merged with the former district Ochsenfurt, and several municipalities from the districts Marktheidenfeld, Karlstadt, Kitzingen and Gerolzhofen were added to form the district with today's borders.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=W%C3%BCrzburg_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> might be described thus: Per fess in chief gules dancetty of three argent, in base per pale argent two lions passant guardant sable armed and langued of the first and azure a fleur-de-lis Or.</p>
<table>
<tr valign="top">
<td><a href="/wiki/File:De_wuerzburg_coat.png" class="image" title="Coat of arms"><img alt="Coat of arms" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a8/De_wuerzburg_coat.png" width="179" height="172" data-file-width="179" data-file-height="172" /></a></td>
<td>In chief (the upper part of the coat of arms) is the "Franconian Rake", the symbol of the former Electoral State of Würzburg. In base, on the dexter (armsbearer’s right, viewer’s left) side, the <a href="/wiki/Fleur-de-lis" title="Fleur-de-lis">fleur-de-lis</a> is the symbol of the St. Burkhard monastery in Würzburg, while the lions on the sinister (armsbearer’s left, viewer’s right) side derive from the coat of arms of the former Ochsenfurt district.</td>
</tr>
</table>
