<p><b>Harvard Undergraduate Television (HUTV)</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> <a href="/wiki/Student_television_station" title="Student television station">Student television station</a> broadcasting to the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>.</p>
<p>HUTV carries original, student-produced content from eleven shows<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and from individual Harvard students.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> HUTV shows include <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Tower_(Harvard_Undergraduate_Television)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivory Tower (Harvard Undergraduate Television)"><i>Ivory Tower</i></a>, <i>On Harvard Time</i> (an award-winning comedy news show<sup id="cite_ref-On_Harvard_Time_award_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-On_Harvard_Time_award-3">[3]</a></sup>), and video reports by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Harvard_Crimson" title="The Harvard Crimson">The Harvard Crimson</a></i> (Harvard's daily student newspaper).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The network has a full production studio and post-production editing facilities in <a href="/wiki/Pforzheimer_House" title="Pforzheimer House">Pforzheimer House</a>, a Harvard dormitory.<sup id="cite_ref-Voice_Article_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Voice_Article-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>HUTV, under the guidance of co-President Derek Flanzraich, replaced then-defunct Harvard-Radcliffe Television (HRTV) on April 6, 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> inheriting HRTV's shows and staff.<sup id="cite_ref-Voice_Article_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Voice_Article-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Undergraduate_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Harvard-Radcliffe_Film_Workshop_(HRFW)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Harvard-Radcliffe_Film_Workshop_.28HRFW.29">Harvard-Radcliffe Film Workshop (HRFW)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Undergraduate_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Harvard-Radcliffe Film Workshop (HRFW)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1975, Bob Doyle who was then working as a <a href="/wiki/Research_fellow" title="Research fellow">research fellow</a> in Harvard's Department of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College#Concentrations" title="Harvard College">Visual and Environmental Studies</a>, founded the Harvard-Radcliffe Film Workshop (HRFW), which offered <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">filmmaking</a> instruction and <a href="/wiki/Film_screening" title="Film screening">film screenings</a> in the Morse Music Library in the basement of <a href="/wiki/Pforzheimer_House" title="Pforzheimer House">Pforzheimer House</a>, which was then known as North House.<sup id="cite_ref-Desktop_Video_Group_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Desktop_Video_Group-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1980s, Doyle helped form the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.dtvgroup.com/index.html">Desktop Video Group</a> to "support undergraduate <a href="/wiki/Video_production" title="Video production">video production</a> and television distribution" at <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard</a> Universities.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Harvard-Radcliffe_Television_(HRTV)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Harvard-Radcliffe_Television_.28HRTV.29">Harvard-Radcliffe Television (HRTV)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Undergraduate_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Harvard-Radcliffe Television (HRTV)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:121px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Harvard_Radcliffe_Television_logo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/0f/Harvard_Radcliffe_Television_logo.jpg" width="119" height="139" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="119" data-file-height="139" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Harvard_Radcliffe_Television_logo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>In 1992, Emily Brodsky founded Harvard-Radcliffe Television (HRTV).<sup id="cite_ref-Desktop_Video_Group_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Desktop_Video_Group-7">[7]</a></sup> That same year, <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Tower_(Harvard_Undergraduate_Television)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivory Tower (Harvard Undergraduate Television)"><i>Ivory Tower</i></a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ivy_League" title="Ivy League">Ivy League's</a> oldest <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a>, became one of HRTV's first shows.<sup id="cite_ref-Oldest_Soap_Opera_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oldest_Soap_Opera-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until 1996, HRTV's shows were <a href="/wiki/Video_editing" title="Video editing">edited</a> using Desktop Video Group equipment. In 1996, the Morse Music Library, which had previously been the site of HRFW's instruction sessions and film screenings, was re-organized into a <a href="/wiki/Television_studio" title="Television studio">television studio</a> for HRTV, overseen by Doyle and aided financially by <a href="/wiki/Pforzheimer_House" title="Pforzheimer House">Pforzheimer House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Desktop_Video_Group_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Desktop_Video_Group-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In its early years, HRTV screened its shows in dormitory common rooms and dining halls,<sup id="cite_ref-Desktop_Video_Group_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Desktop_Video_Group-7">[7]</a></sup> as well as on various <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge</a> <a href="/wiki/Public-access_television" title="Public-access television">Public-access television</a> <a href="/wiki/Cable_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="Cable TV">cable TV</a> channels.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2006, HRTV began posting all of its shows exclusively online, though episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Ivory_Tower_(Harvard_Undergraduate_Television)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivory Tower (Harvard Undergraduate Television)">Ivory Tower</a></i> had been posted online before then.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Several prominent Harvard <a href="/wiki/Alumni" class="mw-redirect" title="Alumni">alumni</a> in the film and television industries have been members of the HRTV Honorary Board of Advisers, including <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conan_O%27Brien" title="Conan O'Brien">Conan O'Brien</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mira_Sorvino" title="Mira Sorvino">Mira Sorvino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Lemmon" title="Jack Lemmon">Jack Lemmon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_Shue" title="Elisabeth Shue">Elisabeth Shue</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Lithgow" title="John Lithgow">John Lithgow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Desktop_Video_Group_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Desktop_Video_Group-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Harvard_Undergraduate_Television_(HUTV)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Harvard_Undergraduate_Television_.28HUTV.29">Harvard Undergraduate Television (HUTV)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Undergraduate_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Harvard Undergraduate Television (HUTV)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 6, 2009, HRTV relaunched as Harvard Undergraduate Television, under the direction of co-president Derek Flanzraich.<sup id="cite_ref-Voice_Article_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Voice_Article-5">[5]</a></sup> The transformation included a new <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hutvnetwork.com/">website</a> and a short <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hutvnetwork.com/video/hutv-promo-get-with-the-times-feat-steven-pinker">promotional video</a> featuring Harvard professor and prominent psychologist <a href="/wiki/Steven_Pinker" title="Steven Pinker">Steven Pinker</a> smashing a television and telling viewers to "get with the times" by watching television online.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_Shows">Current Shows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Undergraduate_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Current Shows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="HUTV_Productions">HUTV Productions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Undergraduate_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: HUTV Productions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>HUTV currently produces eight shows.<sup id="cite_ref-Home_Page_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Home_Page-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>Ivory Tower</b></i> - Created in 1992 by Sara Alexandra Bibel and Andrea N. Moore, <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Tower_(Harvard_Undergraduate_Television)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivory Tower (Harvard Undergraduate Television)"><i>Ivory Tower</i></a> is HUTV's longest-running show and the <a href="/wiki/Ivy_League" title="Ivy League">Ivy League's</a> oldest <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a>, and according to the show's staff the oldest college soap opera. In fall 2008, <i>Ivory Tower</i> switched from a soap opera format to a <a href="/wiki/Situation_comedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Situation comedy">situation comedy</a> format. It has been popular not only on the Harvard campus but also, since being posted online, in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Desktop_Video_Group_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Desktop_Video_Group-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oldest_Soap_Opera_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oldest_Soap_Opera-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-About_Ivory_Tower_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About_Ivory_Tower-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>Respectably French!</b></i> - A <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch comedy</a> show created by Nicholas Krasney, Ho Tuan, and Matthew Tai, <i>Respectably French!</i> is currently in its fifth season. In addition to popular sketch comedy programming, the show has interviewed and been endorsed by <a href="/wiki/Celebrities" class="mw-redirect" title="Celebrities">celebrities</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Nealon" title="Kevin Nealon">Kevin Nealon</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Idle" title="Eric Idle">Eric Idle</a> of <a href="/wiki/Monty_Python" title="Monty Python">Monty Python</a>, <a href="/wiki/Homestar_Runner" title="Homestar Runner">Homestar Runner</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pleasureman_Gunther&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pleasureman Gunther (page does not exist)">Pleasureman Gunther</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2008, Respectably French! has also covered <a href="/wiki/ROFLCon" title="ROFLCon">ROFLCon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2008_Republican_National_Convention" title="2008 Republican National Convention">2008 Republican National Convention</a>.</li>
<li><i><b>On Harvard Time</b></i> - HUTV's <a href="/wiki/News_satire" title="News satire">satirical news</a> show, modeled after <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Show" title="The Daily Show">The Daily Show</a></i>, presents and comments on news at Harvard in a <a href="/wiki/Comedic" class="mw-redirect" title="Comedic">comedic</a> fashion. Currently in its fourth season, <i>On Harvard Time</i> produces weekly episodes as well as periodic humorous interviews with both Harvard and national public figures, such as Harvard <a href="/wiki/Dean_(education)" title="Dean (education)">Dean</a> of <a href="/wiki/University_and_college_admissions" class="mw-redirect" title="University and college admissions">Admissions</a> William Fitzsimmons, <a href="/wiki/Deputy_White_House_Chief_of_Staff" class="mw-redirect" title="Deputy White House Chief of Staff">Deputy White House Chief of Staff</a> <a href="/wiki/Karl_Rove" title="Karl Rove">Karl Rove</a>, and 2008 <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">U.S. Presidential</a> <a href="/wiki/Candidate" title="Candidate">candidate</a> <a href="/wiki/Mike_Gravel" title="Mike Gravel">Mike Gravel</a>. <i>On Harvard Time's</i> November 2008 <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hutvnetwork.com/shows/onharvardtime/video/harvard-yale-aid-on-harvard-time">"Harvard Yale Aid" video</a>, mocking <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> before the annual <a href="/wiki/Harvard-Yale_football_rivalry" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard-Yale football rivalry">Harvard-Yale football game</a>, was named 2008 <a href="/wiki/Ivy_League" title="Ivy League">Ivy League</a> Video of the Year by <a href="/wiki/IvyGate" title="IvyGate">IvyGate Blog</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-On_Harvard_Time_award_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-On_Harvard_Time_award-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-About_On_Harvard_Time_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About_On_Harvard_Time-21">[21]</a></sup> Founded and created by Derek Flanzraich in 2006, today it is "HUTV's most popular show." <sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>Crimson Edition</b></i> - A long-form news <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary</a> show, <i>Crimson Edition</i> made its debut in 1995. It was relaunched with a more serious focus in 2006 by Executive Producer Eric Paternot, and is inspired by television <a href="/wiki/News_magazines" class="mw-redirect" title="News magazines">news magazines</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/60_Minutes" title="60 Minutes">60 Minutes</a></i>. Past topics have included Harvard's <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> community and the situation of the <a href="/wiki/Homeless" class="mw-redirect" title="Homeless">homeless</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Square" title="Harvard Square">Harvard Square</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Desktop_Video_Group_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Desktop_Video_Group-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-About_Crimson_Edition_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About_Crimson_Edition-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>HUTV News</b></i> - Founded in 2007, <i>HUTV News</i> is HUTV's short-form <a href="/wiki/News" title="News">news</a> show, producing shorter reports than <i>Crimson Edition</i> on a wider range of topics. Before HRTV became HUTV, <i>HUTV News</i> was called <i>HRTV News</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>Harvard Hooligans</b></i> - This <a href="/wiki/Comedic" class="mw-redirect" title="Comedic">comedic</a> show follows a group of college roommates. Most <i>Harvard Hooligans</i> episodes depict them speaking into a <a href="/wiki/Webcam" title="Webcam">webcam</a> and portraying a <a href="/wiki/Stereotypically" class="mw-redirect" title="Stereotypically">stereotypically</a> <a href="/wiki/Nerdy" class="mw-redirect" title="Nerdy">nerdy</a> students commenting on Harvard events. However, members have also interviewed <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a> <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">Will Ferrell</a>, covered the <a href="/wiki/2008_Democratic_National_Convention" title="2008 Democratic National Convention">2008 Democratic National Convention</a>, and run for president of the Harvard student body over the course of the show.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>Love@Harvard</b></i> - Modeled after <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dating_Game" title="The Dating Game">The Dating Game</a></i>, <i>Love@Harvard</i> is a show in which three students compete to win a <a href="/wiki/Dating_(activity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dating (activity)">date</a> with another student contestant. It debuted in November 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>H-Biz Tonight</b></i> - Formerly <i>HBS TV Market Minute with <a href="/wiki/Mia_Saini" class="mw-redirect" title="Mia Saini">Mia Saini</a></i>, <i>H-Biz Tonight</i> features short daily reports from <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_School" title="Harvard Business School">Harvard Business School</a> student <a href="/wiki/Mia_Saini" class="mw-redirect" title="Mia Saini">Mia Saini</a> on <a href="/wiki/Economic" class="mw-redirect" title="Economic">economic</a> news.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Content_Partners">Content Partners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harvard_Undergraduate_Television&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Content Partners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>HUTV also currently features three "content partners" on its site.<sup id="cite_ref-Home_Page_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Home_Page-15">[15]</a></sup> These shows are not produced by HUTV staff, but according to HUTV's website "HUTV distributes their content as part of [their] mission to connect media groups on the Harvard campus."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>The Harvard Crimson Video</b></i> - Video reports from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Harvard_Crimson" title="The Harvard Crimson">The Harvard Crimson</a></i>, Harvard University's daily student <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspaper</a>. <i>The Crimson</i> began producing videos in March 2009 as part of its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.thecrimson.com/blog_summary.aspx?blogID=56">"Project Sorrento"</a>, an effort to modernize the paper's website through the use of <a href="/wiki/Multimedia" title="Multimedia">multimedia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>UCTV</b></i> - Biweekly addresses from the President of the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Undergraduate_Council" title="Harvard Undergraduate Council">Harvard Undergraduate Council</a> (UC), Harvard's <a href="/wiki/Students%27_union" title="Students' union">student government</a>, to the Harvard <a href="/wiki/Student_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Student body">student body</a>, modeled on the weekly <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_address" class="mw-redirect" title="United States presidential address">United States presidential address</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><b>The Yard</b></i> - An <a href="/wiki/Improvisation" title="Improvisation">improvised</a> "docu-soap" about six Harvard undergraduates, premiered in 2009 and produced jointly by students at Harvard and <a href="/wiki/Emerson_College" title="Emerson College">Emerson College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
