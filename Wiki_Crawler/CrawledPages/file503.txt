<p><b>Mühldorf</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany"><i>Landkreis</i> (district)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Bavaria" title="Upper Bavaria">Upper Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is bounded by (from the north and clockwise) the districts of <a href="/wiki/Landshut_(district)" title="Landshut (district)">Landshut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rottal-Inn" title="Rottal-Inn">Rottal-Inn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alt%C3%B6tting_(district)" title="Altötting (district)">Altötting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Traunstein_(district)" title="Traunstein (district)">Traunstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosenheim_(district)" title="Rosenheim (district)">Rosenheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ebersberg_(district)" title="Ebersberg (district)">Ebersberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erding_(district)" title="Erding (district)">Erding</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%BChldorf_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most parts of the district have belonged to <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> from the 13th century on. The archbishopric of <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a> possessed some estates in the region as well from the year 798.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1803, when the clerical states of Holy Empire were dissolved, Bavaria annexed these estates.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%BChldorf_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district is located in generally plain countryside on either bank of the <a href="/wiki/Inn_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Inn River">Inn River</a>.</p>
