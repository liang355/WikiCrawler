<p>The <b>Radcliffe Quadrangle</b> at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, formerly the residential campus of <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_College" title="Radcliffe College">Radcliffe College</a>, is part of Harvard's undergraduate campus, in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, United States. Generally just called <b>the Quad</b>, it is a traditional college <a href="/wiki/Quadrangle_(architecture)" title="Quadrangle (architecture)">quad</a> slightly removed from the main part of campus. It should not be confused with <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_College" title="Radcliffe College">Radcliffe Yard</a> or with <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a> — where most classes are conducted.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quadrangle_(Harvard)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term "the Quad" can refer to the rectangular green field bounded by <a href="/wiki/Cabot_House" title="Cabot House">Cabot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pforzheimer_House" title="Pforzheimer House">Pforzheimer Houses</a>, or it can refer to the entire section of campus bounded by Garden, Linnaean, Walker, and Shepard Streets, plus <a href="/wiki/Pforzheimer_House" title="Pforzheimer House">the Jordans</a>, which are east of Walker Street. This larger area consists of the Quad green itself as well as Hilles, which formerly contained the Quad Library, and all of <a href="/wiki/Pforzheimer_House" title="Pforzheimer House">Pforzheimer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cabot_House" title="Cabot House">Cabot</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Currier_House_(Harvard_College)" title="Currier House (Harvard College)">Currier Houses</a>, including the Cabot Masters' Residence and the Jordans.</p>
<p>Currier and Hilles are separated from the rest of the Quad by a landscaped walk and paved road, a private way used mainly by campus shuttlebuses, that runs north-south through the Quad.</p>
<p>Other adjacent portions of campus, such as the Botanic Gardens, Kittredge, the Quadrangle Recreational Athletic Center (Q-RAC), and the Observatory, are often also grouped as part of the Quad.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quadlings">Quadlings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quadrangle_(Harvard)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Quadlings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Residents of the three <i>Quad Houses</i> are called <i>Quadlings</i> (after the <a href="/wiki/Quadling_Country" title="Quadling Country">Quadlings</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Land_of_Oz" title="Land of Oz">Oz</a> books); they tend to be extremely loyal to their Houses and to each other as an outgrowth of their shared relative separation from the main campus.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>All nine other Houses (called <i>River Houses</i> for their proximity to the <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles River</a>), all freshman dorms, and almost all undergraduate classrooms and department offices are located in the main part of campus surrounding <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Square" title="Harvard Square">Harvard Square</a>, which is located about half a mile from the Quad, and which is often called <i>the River</i> by Harvard students. Although not all the houses are equidistant from Harvard Yard, shuttles run to the Quad and the more distant river houses to transport students to class.</p>
<p>Freshmen, who have no control over which upperclass Houses they will be randomly assigned to, often fear being assigned to the Quad (also known as being "quadded"). However, many find a sense of community in the Quad and come to love it.</p>
<p>The benefits of Quad life include fervent spirit and Quad community; better housing arrangements than the River, with larger modern rooms and more common spaces; and the suburban and residential atmosphere of the Quad neighborhood as an area distinct from the academic parts of the Harvard campus. The drawbacks are also often exaggerated, as the Quad is no farther from the Science Center than <a href="/wiki/Mather_House_(Harvard_University)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mather House (Harvard University)">Mather House</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dunster_House" title="Dunster House">Dunster House</a>, and shuttlebuses run to the Yard throughout the day and to the entire campus at night.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_nomenclature">History and nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quadrangle_(Harvard)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History and nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Quad began as housing for (female) <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_College" title="Radcliffe College">Radcliffe College</a> students in 1901 with the construction of <a href="/wiki/Bertram_Hall_at_Radcliffe_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Bertram Hall at Radcliffe College">Bertram Hall</a>; male students first moved in around 1970 as part of an exchange program between Radcliffe and Harvard (Women from Radcliffe moved into Winthrop House at about the same time). The Quad became fully coresidential in 1972, when Radcliffe College and Harvard University agreed to let their respective students live on the other institution's campus.</p>
<p>Early in its history, Radcliffe College was unofficially called "the Harvard Annex," and its dorms were called "Annex housing" by Harvard students. Within Radcliffe culture, however, the collection of dormitories was officially called the "Radcliffe Quadrangle". Because of the terms of the merger agreement between Harvard University and Radcliffe College, the Radcliffe Quadrangle and the Radcliffe Yard are designated as "Radcliffe" in perpetuity. For more on Radcliffe's shifting role in the University, see <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_College" title="Radcliffe College">Radcliffe College</a>.</p>
