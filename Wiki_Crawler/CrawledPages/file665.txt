<p><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Harvard%E2%80%93MIT_Program_of_Health_Sciences_and_Technology&amp;params=42.3592539_N_71.0953229_W_type:edu"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">42°21′33″N</span> <span class="longitude">71°05′43″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">42.3592539°N 71.0953229°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">42.3592539; -71.0953229</span></span></span></a></span></span></span></p>
<p>The <b>Harvard–MIT Program of Health Sciences and Technology</b>, or <b>HST</b>, is one of the oldest and largest <a href="/wiki/Biomedical_engineering" title="Biomedical engineering">biomedical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physician-scientist" title="Physician-scientist">physician-scientist</a> <a href="/wiki/Medical_Scientist_Training_Program" title="Medical Scientist Training Program">training programs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It was founded in 1970 and is the longest-standing functional collaboration between <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> (MIT).</p>
<p>HST's <a href="/wiki/Interdisciplinary" class="mw-redirect" title="Interdisciplinary">interdisciplinary</a> educational program brings engineering as well as the <a href="/wiki/Physical_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical sciences">physical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biological_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological sciences">biological sciences</a> from the scientist's bench to the patient's bedside. Conversely, it brings clinical insight from the patient's bedside to the laboratory bench. In this way, HST students are trained to have deep understanding of <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physical_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical sciences">physical sciences</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Biological_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological sciences">biological sciences</a>, complemented with hands-on experience in the clinic or in industry; and they become conversant with the underlying quantitative and molecular aspects of <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biomedical_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Biomedical science">biomedical science</a>. Within the division, more than 300 graduate students work with eminent faculty and affiliated faculty members from throughout the <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harvard" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard">Harvard</a> communities.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> HST is also the home of the Laboratory of Computational Physiology (LCP) which hosts the <a href="/w/index.php?title=MIMIC_II&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="MIMIC II (page does not exist)">MIMIC II</a> database and <a href="/w/index.php?title=PhysioNet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="PhysioNet (page does not exist)">PhysioNet</a>.</p>
<p>HST is a part of MIT's <a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Medical_Engineering_and_Science" title="Institute for Medical Engineering and Science">Institute for Medical Engineering and Science</a> and 1 of 5 societies at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Medical_School" title="Harvard Medical School">Harvard Medical School</a>.</p>
