			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Billionaire</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Billionaires&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Billionaires">Billionaires</a>)</span></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Billionaire_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Billionaire (disambiguation)">Billionaire (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>billionaire</b>, in countries that use the <a href="/wiki/Long_and_short_scales" title="Long and short scales">short scale</a> number naming system, is a person with a <a href="/wiki/Net_worth" title="Net worth">net worth</a> of at least <a href="/wiki/1000000000_(number)" class="mw-redirect" title="1000000000 (number)">one billion</a> (1,000,000,000, i.e. a thousand million) units of a given <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a>, usually major currencies such as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">United States dollar</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euro</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">pound sterling</a>. The American business magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> produces <a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Billionaires" title="The World's Billionaires">a complete global list of known U.S. dollar billionaires</a> every year and updates an Internet version of this list in real time.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The American <a href="/wiki/Oil_magnate" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil magnate">oil magnate</a> <a href="/wiki/John_D._Rockefeller" title="John D. Rockefeller">John D. Rockefeller</a> became the world's first confirmed U.S. dollar billionaire in 1916.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As of 2017, there are over 2,000 U.S. dollar billionaires worldwide, with a combined wealth of over US$7.6 trillion.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes-2017_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-2017-3">[3]</a></sup> According to a 2017 <a href="/wiki/Oxfam" title="Oxfam">Oxfam</a> report, the <a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Billionaires#2017" title="The World's Billionaires">top eight richest billionaires</a> own as much combined wealth as "half the human race".<sup id="cite_ref-OXF-20170116_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OXF-20170116-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYT-20170116_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20170116-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_U.S._dollar_billionaires">Current U.S. dollar billionaires</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billionaire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current U.S. dollar billionaires">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Billionaires" title="The World's Billionaires">The World's Billionaires</a></div>
<p>According to the <i>Forbes</i> report released in March 2017, there are currently 2,043 U.S. dollar billionaires worldwide, from 66 countries, with a combined net worth of $7.67 trillion, which is more than the combined <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP</a> of 152 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes-2017_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-2017-3">[3]</a></sup> The majority of billionaires are male, but as of 2015 there are 197 <a href="/wiki/Female_billionaires" class="mw-redirect" title="Female billionaires">female billionaires</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BehindNumbers_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BehindNumbers-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2015, there were ten <a href="/wiki/LGBT_billionaires" title="LGBT billionaires">LGBT billionaires</a>. The United States has the largest number of billionaires of any country, with 536 as of 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-BehindNumbers_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BehindNumbers-6">[6]</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> are home to 213, 90 and 88 billionaires respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes2015_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes2015-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IndiaBillionaires2015_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndiaBillionaires2015-8">[8]</a></sup> Among American billionaires, the average age is 66 years,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> but as of 2015 there are globally also 46 billionaires under the age of 40.<sup id="cite_ref-BehindNumbers_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BehindNumbers-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Billionaires_who_own_the_same_wealth_as_half_the_world">Billionaires who own the same wealth as half the world</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billionaire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Billionaires who own the same wealth as half the world">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Billionaires_who_own_the_same_wealth_as_half_the_world.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/df/Billionaires_who_own_the_same_wealth_as_half_the_world.png/250px-Billionaires_who_own_the_same_wealth_as_half_the_world.png" width="250" height="194" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/d/df/Billionaires_who_own_the_same_wealth_as_half_the_world.png 1.5x" data-file-width="358" data-file-height="278" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Billionaires_who_own_the_same_wealth_as_half_the_world.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Number of billionaires who together own the same wealth as half the world population and what transport they would fit on between years 2010 to 2016<sup id="cite_ref-oxfam_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxfam-10">[10]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>According to a 2016 <a href="/wiki/Oxfam" title="Oxfam">Oxfam</a> report, the wealth of the poorest 50% dropped by 41% between 2010 and 2015, despite an increase in the <a href="/wiki/Global_population" class="mw-redirect" title="Global population">global population</a> of 400 million.<sup id="cite_ref-oxfam_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxfam-10">[10]</a></sup> In the same period, the wealth of the richest 62 people between <a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Billionaires" title="The World's Billionaires">the World's Billionaires</a> increased by $500bn (£350bn) to $1.76tn. Runaway <a href="/wiki/Income_inequality" class="mw-redirect" title="Income inequality">income inequality</a> has created a world where 62 people own as much as the poorest half of the world's population. This number has fallen dramatically from 388 as recently as 2010. More recently, in 2017 an Oxfam report noted that just eight billionaires own as much combined wealth as "half the human race".<sup id="cite_ref-OXF-20170116_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OXF-20170116-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYT-20170116_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20170116-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
