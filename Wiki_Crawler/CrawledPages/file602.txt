			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Hollis Chair of Divinity</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Hollis Chair of Divinity</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Financial_endowment" title="Financial endowment">endowed chair</a> at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Divinity_School" title="Harvard Divinity School">Harvard Divinity School</a>. It was established in 1721 by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hollis_(1659%E2%80%931731)" title="Thomas Hollis (1659–1731)">Thomas Hollis</a>, a wealthy English merchant and benefactor of the university, at a salary of £80 per year.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the oldest endowed chair in the United States, the first professorship in theology in the country,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and in the early 19th century it was considered to be "the most prestigious endowed professorship in America".<sup id="cite_ref-dorrien_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dorrien-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span id="History,_denominational_issues"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="History.2C_denominational_issues">History, denominational issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hollis_Chair_of_Divinity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History, denominational issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The terms for the new position were drawn up in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> on 22 August 1721.<sup id="cite_ref-1840history_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1840history-4">[4]</a></sup> Requirements for the professor were not very sectarian, although Hollis made a requirement of character: "That he should be a man of solid learning in divinity, of sound, or orthodox principles, one well gifted to teach, of a sober and pious life, and of a grave conversation."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Traditionally, the chair's occupant has the right to graze a cow on the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Yard" title="Harvard Yard">Harvard Yard</a>, but until 2009 none but the first two Hollis professors had done so;<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> in 2009, upon his retirement, theologian <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Cox" title="Harvey Cox">Harvey Cox</a> restored the tradition and chose Faith, a <a href="/wiki/Jersey_cattle" title="Jersey cattle">Jersey cow</a><sup id="cite_ref-walsh_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-walsh-7">[7]</a></sup> belonging to the Farm School<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Athol,_Massachusetts" title="Athol, Massachusetts">Athol, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cox_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cox-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Hollis was a <a href="/wiki/Baptists" title="Baptists">Baptist</a>, he had enough faith in the liberal and tolerant atmosphere at Harvard to endow the chair and allow the president and faculty of the university to appoint theologians to the chair, with the condition "that none be refused on account of his belief and practice of adult baptism." Hollis's "sound and orthodox principles" initially meant <a href="/wiki/Congregationalist_polity" title="Congregationalist polity">Congregationalist</a> or <a href="/wiki/Calvinism" title="Calvinism">Calvinist</a>. The chair's first occupant, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Wigglesworth" title="Edward Wigglesworth">Edward Wigglesworth</a>, had to swear allegiance to the <i>Medulla Theologiae</i>, a Calvinist theological manual by <a href="/wiki/William_Ames" title="William Ames">William Ames</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hoeveler_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hoeveler-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-observatory_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-observatory-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The chair was first unoccupied, briefly, from 1803 to 1805, when the <a href="/wiki/Puritan" class="mw-redirect" title="Puritan">Puritans</a> at Harvard ceded power to the <a href="/wiki/Unitarianism" title="Unitarianism">Unitarians</a>; in 1805, Unitarian Henry Ware assumed the post.<sup id="cite_ref-smith_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smith-13">[13]</a></sup> Proponents of the Unitarian faction pointed out that it would be impossible to find a man orthodox enough for the 1720s in the early nineteenth century; "orthodox" they interpreted as following "the general sentiment of the country."<sup id="cite_ref-morison_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-morison-14">[14]</a></sup> In the 1830s, Harvard found itself in financial trouble and at the same time was moving away from the teaching of religion. <a href="/wiki/Josiah_Quincy_III" title="Josiah Quincy III">Josiah Quincy III</a>, then-president of Harvard, refused to nominate a successor for Henry Ware, and the post was left unoccupied a second time.<sup id="cite_ref-shoemaker_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shoemaker-15">[15]</a></sup> It also seems that the original endowment had dried up.<sup id="cite_ref-morison_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-morison-14">[14]</a></sup> In the meantime, to lessen the possible charge of a "narrowly sectarian education" the chair was moved to the Divinity School,<sup id="cite_ref-shoemaker_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shoemaker-15">[15]</a></sup> which had been formed in 1816.</p>
