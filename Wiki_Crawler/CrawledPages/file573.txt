<p><b>Harvard Yard</b>, in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>, is a grassy area of 22.4 acres (9.1&#160;ha) enclosed by fences with twenty-seven gates.<sup id="cite_ref-hammond_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hammond-2">[2]</a></sup> It is the oldest part of the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> campus, its historic center, and its modern crossroads.</p>
<p>Bounded principally by <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Avenue_(Boston)" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts Avenue (Boston)">Massachusetts Avenue</a>, Cambridge Street, Broadway, and Quincy Street, it contains <a href="/wiki/List_of_Harvard_College_freshman_dormitories" title="List of Harvard College freshman dormitories">most of the freshman dormitories</a>; Harvard's most important libraries; <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Church_of_Harvard_University" title="Memorial Church of Harvard University">Memorial Church</a>; several classroom and departmental buildings; and the offices of the Dean of the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Faculty_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Harvard Faculty of Arts and Sciences">Faculty of Arts and Sciences</a>, the Dean of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>, and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Harvard_University" title="President of Harvard University">President of Harvard University</a>. <span id="Tercentenary_Theater"></span> The center of the Yard—a wide grassy area bounded by <a href="/wiki/Widener_Library" title="Widener Library">Widener Library</a>, <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Church_of_Harvard_University" title="Memorial Church of Harvard University">Memorial Church</a>, University Hall, and <a href="/wiki/Sever_Hall" title="Sever Hall">Sever Hall</a> known as <b>Tercentenary Theatre</b>‍—‌is the site of annual <a href="/wiki/History_and_traditions_of_Harvard_commencements" title="History and traditions of Harvard commencements">commencement</a> exercises and other <a href="/wiki/Convocation" title="Convocation">convocations</a>.</p>
<p>The western third of Harvard Yard, which opens onto Peabody Street (often mistaken for nearby Massachusetts Avenue) at <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Gate" title="Johnston Gate">Johnston Gate</a>, is known as the Old Yard,<sup id="cite_ref-urlMACRIS.CAM.AD_Details_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlMACRIS.CAM.AD_Details-3">[3]</a></sup> and around it cluster most of the freshman dormitories. Among these is <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Hall_(Harvard_University)" title="Massachusetts Hall (Harvard University)">Massachusetts Hall</a> (1720), Harvard's oldest building and the second-oldest academic building in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The original Harvard Hall on this site housed the College library, including the books donated by <a href="/wiki/John_Harvard_(clergyman)" title="John Harvard (clergyman)">John Harvard</a>—​all but one of which were destroyed when the building burned in 1764. Rebuilt in 1766, Harvard Hall now houses classrooms. <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Square" title="Harvard Square">Harvard Square</a> is adjacent to the Old Yard.</p>
<p>Across the Old Yard from Johnston Gate is <a href="/wiki/University_Hall_(Harvard_University)" title="University Hall (Harvard University)">University Hall</a> (1815), whose white-granite facade was the first to challenge the red-brick Georgian style until then ascendant;<sup id="cite_ref-gazart_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazart-5">[5]</a></sup> between its twin west staircases stands the <a href="/wiki/John_Harvard_(statue)" title="John Harvard (statue)">John Harvard statue</a>.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Harvard_University_Library" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard University Library">Libraries</a> in the Yard are <a href="/wiki/Widener_Library" title="Widener Library">Widener Library</a>, its connected Pusey Library annex, <a href="/wiki/Houghton_Library" title="Houghton Library">Houghton Library</a> for rare books and manuscripts, and <a href="/wiki/Lamont_Library" title="Lamont Library">Lamont Library</a>, the main undergraduate library. Classroom and departmental buildings include Emerson Hall, <a href="/wiki/Sever_Hall" title="Sever Hall">Sever Hall</a>, Robinson Hall, and Boylston Hall. The <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Bixi" title="Harvard Bixi">Harvard Bixi</a>, a Chinese stele with inscribed text, is located near Widener.</p>
<p>The freshman dormitories of Harvard Yard include the upper levels of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Hall,_Harvard_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts Hall, Harvard University">Massachusetts Hall</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wigglesworth_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Wigglesworth Hall">Wigglesworth Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weld_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Weld Hall">Weld Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grays_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Grays Hall">Grays Hall</a>, Matthews Hall, <a href="/wiki/Straus_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Straus Hall">Straus Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mower_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Mower Hall">Mower Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hollis_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Hollis Hall">Hollis Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stoughton_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Stoughton Hall">Stoughton Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Lionel Hall">Lionel Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holworthy_Hall" title="Holworthy Hall">Holworthy Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canaday_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Canaday Hall">Canaday Hall</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thayer_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Thayer Hall">Thayer Hall</a>.</p>
<p>Nestled among Mower, Hollis, Lionel, and Stoughton Halls is <a href="/wiki/Holden_Chapel" title="Holden Chapel">Holden Chapel</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Holden_Choirs" class="mw-redirect" title="Holden Choirs">Holden Choirs</a>. Nearby is Phillips Brooks House, dedicated to student service to the community. Wadsworth House houses the Office for Scholarly Communication, the Harvard University Librarian, the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University_Marshal" title="Harvard University Marshal">Office of the University Marshal</a>, the Office of Commencement, and those of several professors. Loeb House is the home of Harvard's governing bodies: the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvard Corporation">Harvard Corporation</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Board_of_Overseers" title="Harvard Board of Overseers">Board of Overseers</a>.</p>
